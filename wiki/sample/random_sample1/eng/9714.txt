<p><b>Evan David Meek</b> (born May 12, 1983) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">relief pitcher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_Barnstormers" title="Lancaster Barnstormers">Lancaster Barnstormers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_League_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Atlantic League of Professional Baseball">Atlantic League of Professional Baseball</a>. He has played for the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a> in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) and was selected to one <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star Game</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meek attended <a href="/wiki/Inglemoor_High_School" title="Inglemoor High School">Inglemoor High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kenmore,_Washington" title="Kenmore, Washington">Kenmore, Washington</a>. Afterwards, he played for <a href="/wiki/Midland_College" title="Midland College">Midland College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Midland,_Texas" title="Midland, Texas">Midland, Texas</a>. Then he transferred at semester to play at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bellevue_Community_College" title="Bellevue Community College">Bellevue Community College</a> also in <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pro_career">Pro career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pro career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minnesota_Twins">Minnesota Twins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minnesota Twins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After being selected by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2002_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2002 Major League Baseball draft">2002 Major League Baseball draft</a>, Meek began playing for the Twins affiliate in the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_League" title="Appalachian League">Appalachian League</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Elizabethton_Twins" title="Elizabethton Twins">Elizabethton Twins</a>. In 2004, Meek played for Elizabethton and the <a href="/wiki/Quad_Cities_River_Bandits" title="Quad Cities River Bandits">Quad Cities River Bandits</a>. Meek started the 2005 season with the <a href="/wiki/Beloit_Snappers" title="Beloit Snappers">Beloit Snappers</a>. Meek was released by the Twins in June 2005.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Diego_Padres">San Diego Padres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: San Diego Padres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meek signed with the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a> in September 2005. He started the 2006 season with the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Elsinore_Storm" title="Lake Elsinore Storm">Lake Elsinore Storm</a>. In August 2006, Meek and a <a href="/wiki/Player_to_be_named_later" title="Player to be named later">player to be named later</a>, (<a href="/wiki/Dale_Thayer" title="Dale Thayer">Dale Thayer</a>), were traded to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Devil_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Devil Rays">Tampa Bay Devil Rays</a> for <a href="/wiki/Russell_Branyan" title="Russell Branyan">Russell Branyan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trade_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-trade-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tampa_Bay_Devil_Rays">Tampa Bay Devil Rays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tampa Bay Devil Rays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meek finished the 2006 season with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visalia_Oaks" title="Visalia Oaks">Visalia Oaks</a>. He pitched the 2007 season for the <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_Biscuits" title="Montgomery Biscuits">Montgomery Biscuits</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pittsburgh_Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pittsburgh Pirates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Rule_5_draft" title="Rule 5 draft">Rule 5 draft</a>, Meek was selected from the Devil Rays by the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>. This required that Meek remain on the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_rosters" title="Major League Baseball rosters">active roster</a> of the Pirates for the entire <a href="/wiki/2008_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2008 Major League Baseball season">2008 season</a> or else the Pirates would have to offer him back to the Devil Rays for half the amount they paid to acquire him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meek made his major league debut on April 2, 2008 with the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>. He would record his first career loss in extra innings against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> on April 7, 2008. Meek made nine relief appearances, going 0–1 with a 6.92 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a>, before being designated for assignment on May 4, 2008. He was offered back per Rule 5 guidelines on May 14. The Pirates paid cash to keep him and sent him down to their minor league teams. After pitching 9 games with the Double-A <a href="/wiki/Altoona_Curve" title="Altoona Curve">Altoona Curve</a>, Meek was promoted to the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Indians" title="Indianapolis Indians">Indianapolis Indians</a>.</p>

<p>Meek spent the 2009 season in the bullpen with the Pirates. He finished the season with a 1–1 record in 41 appearances, had a 3.45 ERA, gave up 2 home runs, 0 hit batsmen, 29 walks, 42 strikeouts, a .209 average against, and a 1.34 WHIP, in 47.0 innings pitched.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2010 Major League Baseball season">2010 season</a> was Meek's first full season in the Majors. He was often asked by manager <a href="/wiki/John_Russell_(catcher)" title="John Russell (catcher)">John Russell</a> to pitch multiple innings in relief. Meek's performance rivaled other excellent <a href="/wiki/Closer_(baseball)" title="Closer (baseball)">closers</a> like <a href="/wiki/Joakim_Soria" title="Joakim Soria">Joakim Soria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Wilson_(baseball)" title="Brian Wilson (baseball)">Brian Wilson</a> despite not being one himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Early in the season when Dotel had given up runs in six straight games as the closer, Meek had an opportunity in the role and earned his first Major League <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">save</a> on April 29, 2010 to close out a 2–0 win over the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On May 14, 2010 against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wrigley_Field" title="Wrigley Field">Wrigley Field</a>, Meek entered the game in the 6th inning with the score tied 6–6, and he pitched two scoreless innings of relief, striking out four of the seven Cubs hitters he faced including <a href="/wiki/Derrek_Lee" title="Derrek Lee">Derrek Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xavier_Nady" title="Xavier Nady">Xavier Nady</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Soriano" title="Alfonso Soriano">Alfonso Soriano</a> in the bottom of the 7th. The Pirates rallied behind his strong pitching to win the game 10–6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meek delivered one of his finest performances of the season against the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a> on June 10, 2010, coming in to relieve Pirates starter <a href="/wiki/Zach_Duke" title="Zach Duke">Zach Duke</a> with the bases loaded and no outs. He quickly forced <a href="/wiki/Willie_Harris" title="Willie Harris">Willie Harris</a> to line into a double play before retiring <a href="/wiki/Cristian_Guzm%C3%A1n" title="Cristian Guzmán">Cristian Guzmán</a>, needing only six pitches to get out of the inning with no runs in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Due to his excellent work, he was named to the <a href="/wiki/2010_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2010 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">2010 MLB All Star Game</a>. At the time of his selection on July 4, 2010, Meek carried a 4–2 record with a 0.96 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a>, a mark that lead all <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> relievers. "It's surprising", Meek said. "A lot of starters and closers go to the game. I've always said there are a lot of great middle relievers out there who deserve to go to the game. I think they're overlooked a little bit. It's amazing to be selected, an overwhelming feeling."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Meek was the first Pirate reliever selected who was not a <a href="/wiki/Closer_(baseball)" title="Closer (baseball)">closer</a> since <a href="/wiki/Mace_Brown" title="Mace Brown">Mace Brown</a> in 1938 – before the closer position emerged. The Pirates celebrated his nomination at <a href="/wiki/PNC_Park" title="PNC Park">PNC Park</a> with an 8–5 victory over the visiting <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a>, a game where Meek was the winning pitcher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 3, 2010, Meek recorded his first career base hit, off fellow reliever <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Smith_(baseball)" title="Jordan Smith (baseball)">Jordan Smith</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>. Meek finished the 2010 season with a 2.14 ERA, 70 strikeouts, 4 saves, 15 <a href="/wiki/Hold_(baseball)" title="Hold (baseball)">holds</a>, and a 5–4 record, all career-highs.</p>
<p>After several injuries in 2011, Meek saw a decrease in fastball velocity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hmeekhmeek_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-hmeekhmeek-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meek started the 2012 season with Pittsburgh, but on May 1, Meek was optioned to Triple-A Indianapolis. Meek was 0-0 with a 5.59 ERA in 9 games with a .293 opposing batting average. He was recalled to the Pirates on July 21, 2012, after an injury to reliever <a href="/wiki/Juan_Cruz_(baseball)" title="Juan Cruz (baseball)">Juan Cruz</a>. But was sent back down on July 26, to make room for highly anticipated prospect <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starling_Mart%C3%A9" title="Starling Marté">Starling Marté</a>. Meek was <a href="/wiki/Designated_for_assignment" title="Designated for assignment">designated for assignment</a> by the Pirates on September 10, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In October 2012, Meek elected minor league free agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Texas_Rangers">Texas Rangers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Texas Rangers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meek spent the 2013 season in the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> organization with the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock_Express" title="Round Rock Express">Round Rock Express</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baltimore_Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Baltimore Orioles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meek signed a minor league deal with the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a> in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> After a strong spring training, he made the Opening Day roster. On May 2, he was optioned to the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Tides" title="Norfolk Tides">Norfolk Tides</a>. He was recalled on May 15, and then designated for assignment on May 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Instead, the next day Meek was optioned back to Norfolk. On May 28, he was assigned outright to Norfolk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On September 25, 2014, Meek gave up a walk-off single to <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> all-star <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jeter" title="Derek Jeter">Derek Jeter</a> in Jeter's final game at <a href="/wiki/Yankee_Stadium" title="Yankee Stadium">Yankee Stadium</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Washington_Nationals">Washington Nationals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Washington Nationals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meek signed a minor league contract with the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a> on January 21, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kia_Tigers">Kia Tigers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Kia Tigers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 20, 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">Korea Baseball Organization</a> announced that they had signed Meek to a one-year deal worth $150,000. Meek filled a roster vacancy created by the club's release of former MLB pitcher <a href="/wiki/Philip_Humber" title="Philip Humber">Philip Humber</a> on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Somerset_Patriots">Somerset Patriots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Somerset Patriots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 5, 2016, Meek signed with the <a href="/wiki/Somerset_Patriots" title="Somerset Patriots">Somerset Patriots</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_League_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Atlantic League of Professional Baseball">Atlantic League of Professional Baseball</a>. He was released on July 20, 2016.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lancaster_Barnstormers">Lancaster Barnstormers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Lancaster Barnstormers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 26, 2016, Meek signed with the <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_Barnstormers" title="Lancaster Barnstormers">Lancaster Barnstormers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_League_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Atlantic League of Professional Baseball">Atlantic League of Professional Baseball</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scouting_report">Scouting report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_Meek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Scouting report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meek's <a href="/wiki/Four-seam_fastball" title="Four-seam fastball">four-seam fastball</a> normally sits around 95 mph, although he is capable of reaching 97–98 mph on occasion. It is his primary pitch, throwing it slightly over half the time. Despite missing the final two months of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2009 Major League Baseball season">2009 Major League Baseball season</a>, he added 8 pounds of muscle during the off-season and promised that he would be able to hit 100 mph in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Since his command has improved throughout his career – 5.55 walks per 9 innings in 2009 to around 2.10 in 2010 – opposing batters have shown more willingness to chase the pitch, even out of the strike zone to due his ability throw his other pitches for strikes.</p>
<p>However, after several injuries in 2011, Meek saw his fastball velocity decrease into the low to mid 90s, ranging from 90-95 mph.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hmeekhmeek_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-hmeekhmeek-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meek's <a href="/wiki/Curveball" title="Curveball">curveball</a> is his primary off-speed pitch, with the velocity sitting around 79–82 mph.</p>
<p>Meek throws a <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(baseball)" title="Cutter (baseball)">cutter</a> that was rated in August 2009 as the 3rd most effective cutter in Major League Baseball because of its runs above the MLB average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The pitch has "unfair biting" movement, even against left-handed batters, according to Dejan Kovacevic of the <i><a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette" title="Pittsburgh Post-Gazette">Pittsburgh Post-Gazette</a></i>. Average velocity is 92–93 mph and he normally likes to use it when he gets ahead in a count as a strikeout pitch. It is normally a very difficult pitch to hit well, helping him limit the number of extra base hits he allows.</p>
<p>Meek also throws a fourth pitch, an off-speed <a href="/wiki/Slider" title="Slider">slider</a> that is seldom used in pressure situations. The pitch normally sits around 80–82 mph, mainly incorporated to keep hitters out of a rhythm. His ability to throw four pitches well however, makes him rare as a relief pitcher as the normal reliever only possesses a three-pitch arsenal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
