<p><i><b>Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance.</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ヱヴァンゲリヲン新劇場版: 破</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Evangerion Shin Gekijōban: Ha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Evangelion: The New Movie: Break")</span> is a 2009 <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">animated</a> film directed by <a href="/wiki/Kazuya_Tsurumaki" title="Kazuya Tsurumaki">Kazuya Tsurumaki</a> and Masayuki, and written by <a href="/wiki/Hideaki_Anno" title="Hideaki Anno">Hideaki Anno</a>. It is the second of four films released in the <i><a href="/wiki/Rebuild_of_Evangelion" title="Rebuild of Evangelion">Rebuild of Evangelion</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Tetralogy" title="Tetralogy">tetralogy</a> based on the original <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion_(TV_series)" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion (TV series)">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a></i>. It was produced and co-distributed by <a href="/wiki/Hideaki_Anno" title="Hideaki Anno">Hideaki Anno</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_Khara" title="Studio Khara">Studio Khara</a> in partnership with Gainax.</p>
<p>The film continues the story of <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_1.0_You_Are_(Not)_Alone" title="Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone">Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Ikari" title="Shinji Ikari">Shinji Ikari</a> continuing his role as a pilot of one of the gigantic "Evangelion" <a href="/wiki/Mecha" title="Mecha">mecha</a> as part of NERV's ongoing fight against the mysterious creatures known as Angels. While replicating many scenes and plot elements from the original series, the film also introduces new ones, including newly designed creatures and new characters, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mari_Illustrious_Makinami" title="Mari Illustrious Makinami">Mari Illustrious Makinami</a>, and integrates newly available <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">3D CG technology</a>. Its ending paves the way for the significant storyline departures from the original series in <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_3.0_You_Can_(Not)_Redo" title="Evangelion: 3.0 You Can (Not) Redo">Evangelion: 3.0 You Can (Not) Redo</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The resuscitated skeleton of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shamshel" title="Shamshel">Third Angel</a> escapes its confinement and tries to break out of NERV's arctic <a href="/wiki/Bethany_(biblical_village)" title="Bethany (biblical village)">Bethany</a> Base, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mari_Illustrious_Makinami" title="Mari Illustrious Makinami">Mari Illustrious Makinami</a>, a new pilot, launches <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#EVA_Provisional_Unit_05" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Evangelion Provisional Unit-05</a> and destroys the Angel by self-destructing the EVA and safely ejecting at the last moment.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Ikari" title="Shinji Ikari">Shinji Ikari</a> and his father <a href="/wiki/Gendo_Ikari" title="Gendo Ikari">Gendo</a> visit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yui_Ikari" title="Yui Ikari">Yui's</a> grave. Shortly after, the Seventh Angel attacks, but is quickly dispatched by the newly arrived <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eva_Unit-02" title="Eva Unit-02">Unit-02</a> and its headstrong pilot, <a href="/wiki/Asuka_Langley_Soryu" title="Asuka Langley Soryu">Asuka Langley Shikinami</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryoji_Kaji" title="Ryoji Kaji">Ryoji Kaji</a> delivers to Gendo a suitcase containing the mysterious "Key of Nebuchadnezzar" and notes that the destruction of Unit-05 went as planned. Gendo and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kozo_Fuyutsuki" title="Kozo Fuyutsuki">Kozo Fuyutsuki</a> then visit NERV's lunar compound, <a href="/wiki/Tabgha" title="Tabgha">Tabgha</a> Base, to view the construction of <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#Evangelion_Mark.06" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Evangelion Mark.06</a>, which is noted as being different from the other EVAs. They are denied permission to land, but briefly see <a href="/wiki/Kaworu_Nagisa" title="Kaworu Nagisa">Kaworu Nagisa</a> sitting in space without a space suit. During Gendo's absence, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Angels_in_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion#Sahaquiel" title="List of Angels in Neon Genesis Evangelion">Eighth Angel</a> attacks Tokyo-3. Shinji, Asuka, and Rei kill it in an improvised plan devised by <a href="/wiki/Misato_Katsuragi" title="Misato Katsuragi">Misato Katsuragi</a>. Both <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#Evangelion_Unit_00_.28Zerog.C5.8Dki:_.E9.9B.B6.E5.8F.B7.E6.A9.9F.29" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Unit-00</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eva_Unit-01" title="Eva Unit-01">Unit-01</a> are damaged. Gendo returns and asks to have them both repaired, but SEELE only allows the repair of Unit-01.</p>
<p>In an attempt to heal the relationship between Gendo and Shinji, Rei prepares a dinner party. At the same time, Mari arrives in Tokyo-3. Later, <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#EVA_Unit_04" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Unit-04</a> and NERV's U.S. branch are destroyed during an experiment, prompting the U.S. government to send <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#EVA_Unit_03" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Unit-03</a> to Tokyo-3. Due to an international agreement that allows no more than three functioning Evangelions per country at a time, NERV seals away Unit-02 and schedules a test of Unit-03 on the same day as Rei's dinner party; Asuka volunteers to take Rei's place as the test pilot. Once Asuka activates Unit-03, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bardiel" title="Bardiel">Ninth Angel</a> possesses the EVA and goes on a rampage. Shinji pilots Unit-01, but refuses to fight the Angel for fear of harming Asuka, who is still trapped inside. Gendo orders the activation of the Dummy System, which makes Unit-01 autonomous, and allows it to savagely destroy Unit-03, crushing the cockpit in its teeth. Emotionally wounded by his father's cold decision, Shinji leaves NERV.</p>
<p>Asuka survives, but is grievously injured and placed in quarantine for fear of mental contamination by the Angel. Shinji heads out of the city, but the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zeruel" title="Zeruel">Tenth Angel</a> attacks, so he flees to an emergency shelter. Gendo fails to activate Unit-01 without Shinji, so Mari hijacks Unit-02 and heads out to fight the Angel alone. She removes Unit-02's restraints and launches an all-out attack, but the Angel severely damages Unit-02. Rei joins the fight with the damaged Unit-00 and charges at the Angel with a missile. The missile detonates, but fails to destroy the Angel. The Angel consumes Unit-00 and integrates it with its own body, allowing it to bypass NERV's defenses.</p>
<p>Upon seeing Rei consumed along with Unit-00, Shinji rushes to Gendo and asks to pilot Unit-01 again. Shinji fends off the Angel, but before he can defeat it, Unit-01 runs out of power and shuts down. Unit-01 reactivates in berserk mode in response to Shinji's rage at the loss of Rei. It easily takes down the Angel, and Shinji uses the EVA to reach inside the Angel's core to save Rei. Unit-01 then starts transcending its physical boundaries to grant Shinji's wish. At the moment Shinji retrieves Rei, Unit-01 pulls Unit-00's core out of the Angel. The Angel explodes and its remains, along with Unit-00's core, are absorbed into Unit-01, fusing them all into one being. Unit-01 then sprouts giant wings of light, and Misato, who has been watching the battle, sees that they are identical to the ones she saw during Second Impact. Ritsuko realizes that the EVA has become a divine being, triggering Third Impact and declares this to be the end of the world.</p>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/Post-credits_scene" title="Post-credits scene">post-credits scene</a>, a spear shoots down from the Moon and impales Unit-01 in the middle of its <a href="/wiki/Apotheosis" title="Apotheosis">apotheosis</a>, neutralizing it and stopping Third Impact. Kaworu then descends from the Moon in Mark.06, saying that this time, he will show Shinji true happiness.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2006, it was confirmed the second film produced as part of the <i>Rebuild of Evangelion</i> series, with a release date tentatively set for January 2008 release and a 90-minute running time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-September_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-September-2">[2]</a></sup> In November 2006, the December edition of the Japanese anime magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Newtype" title="Newtype">Newtype</a></i> confirmed the second film was written during <a href="/wiki/Post-production" title="Post-production">post-production</a> on the first film. Anno stated the introduction of new characters and Evangelion units would begin from the second film onwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-November_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-November-3">[3]</a></sup> The release date was pushed back several times from the original announcement of January 2008:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-September_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-September-2">[2]</a></sup> first, to a December 2008 release<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-December_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-December-4">[4]</a></sup> before an update on the official website on October 6, 2008 announced the official English title and a "early summer 2009" release date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Summer_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Summer-5">[5]</a></sup> A final postponement revealed that the film would be released on June 27, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-June_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-June-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Neon_Genesis_Evangelion#Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_.28Not.29_Advance_Original_Soundtrack" title="Music of Neon Genesis Evangelion">Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance Original Soundtrack</a></div>
<p>In May 2009, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hikaru_Utada" title="Hikaru Utada">Hikaru Utada</a> returned to the series and provide the theme song for the film, "Beautiful World (Planitb Acoustica Mix)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Utada previously released "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beautiful_World_(Hikaru_Utada_song)" title="Beautiful World (Hikaru Utada song)">Beautiful World</a>" for <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_1.0_You_Are_(Not)_Alone" title="Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone">Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</a></i> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Theme_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Theme-8">[8]</a></sup> A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance_Original_Sound_Track" title="Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance Original Sound Track">soundtrack album</a> featuring the film's score by <a href="/wiki/Shir%C5%8D_Sagisu" title="Shirō Sagisu">Shirō Sagisu</a> was released on July 8, 2009 in both the regular and special editions. The special edition contains a bonus disc featuring score selections not edited to fit into the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The film is also inserted popular Japanese folk songs, "<a href="/wiki/Tsubasa_o_Kudasai" title="Tsubasa o Kudasai">Tsubasa o Kudasai</a>" and "Kyō no Hi wa Sayōnara", performed by Megumi Hayashibara.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film's initial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teaser_trailer" title="Teaser trailer">teaser trailer</a>, shown after the end credits of <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_1.0_You_Are_(Not)_Alone" title="Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone">Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</a></i> in the style of the "next episode" previews in the original TV series, hinted at the adoption of Unit-02 and its pilot <a href="/wiki/Asuka_Langley_Soryu" title="Asuka Langley Soryu">Asuka Langley Shikinami</a> and showed scenes inspired by the original series (the disappearance of Unit-04 upon the startup of its S² engine, the possession of Unit-03 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angel_(Neon_Genesis_Evangelion)#Bardiel" title="Angel (Neon Genesis Evangelion)">Bardiel</a>, Evangelion Units 01 and 02 fighting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angel_(Neon_Genesis_Evangelion)#Zeruel" title="Angel (Neon Genesis Evangelion)">Zeruel</a>) as well as new material (the newly designed Units 05 and 06 and the unintroduced new character of Mari Makinami). A full trailer was released on April 2, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The character Makinami Mari Illustrious, teased in the teaser trailer and appearing in the Nintendo DS game <i>Petit Eva: Evangelion@Game</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ぷちえゔぁ～EVANGELION@GAME～</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Puchi Eva ~Evangelion@Game~</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In its opening weekend in Japan, the film reached number-one at the box office with a revenue of 510 million yen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The film subsequently grossed the equivalent of over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">US</a> $40 million at the Japanese box office, making it the third highest grossing Japanese anime of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The film's North American box office take was over $130,000, an improvement over <i>1.0</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchandise">Merchandise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Merchandise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Character designer <a href="/wiki/Yoshiyuki_Sadamoto" title="Yoshiyuki Sadamoto">Yoshiyuki Sadamoto</a> designed three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Be@rbrick" title="Be@rbrick">Be@rbrick</a> figures of Asuka, Rei, and Mari, which went on sale on March 14, 2009 and were also available as a bundle with advance film ticket purchases. <a href="/wiki/NTT_DoCoMo" title="NTT DoCoMo">NTT DoCoMo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sharp_Corporation" title="Sharp Corporation">Sharp</a> released a production model of the <i>"SH-06A NERV"</i> phone featured in the film, which Hideaki Anno and Studio Khara supervised in designing. The phone, only available in Japan between June and July 2009, was originally set for 20,000 pre-order units and 10,000 for retail sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-May-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phone_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-Phone-56">[56]</a></sup> After selling out all pre-orders in only five hours, NTT docomo announced they would be manufacturing an additional 7,500 units to meet all orders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-58">[58]</a></sup> In August 2009, ABC-Mart, King Records, and Achilles released sneakers based on the design of <a href="/wiki/Evangelion_(mecha)#Evangelion_Unit_01_.28Shog.C5.8Dki:_.E5.88.9D.E5.8F.B7.E6.A9.9F.29" title="Evangelion (mecha)">Evangelion Unit-01</a> as part of "The Footstep Instrumentality Project."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Theme_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Theme-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/UCC_Ueshima_Coffee_Co." title="UCC Ueshima Coffee Co.">Ueshima Coffee Co.</a>, as with <i>Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone</i>, released coffee cans decorated with <i>Evangelion</i> characters and their Evangelion units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UCC_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-UCC-59">[59]</a></sup> Medicon released "Real Action Heroes" action figures of Shinji, Rei, Asuka, and Kaworu in restyled plugsuits in August, September, and November 2009 and May 2010, while <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">Bandai</a> released its own line of action figures with Mari Illustrious Makinami and two separate sets of <a href="/wiki/Gashapon" title="Gashapon">Gashapon</a> figures in May and July 2009. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Justin Sevakis of <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> praised the new character interactions and development, stating "Evangelion 2.0 feels as though the project has been put on mood stabilizers: it's not any happier, but it's more stable and certainly more focused. And in the end, it's a vast improvement."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eva_ann_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-eva_ann-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Reviewing the US Blu-ray release for ANN, Theron Martin<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> wrote that <i>2.0'</i>s Asuka is "distinct from the get-go, too, as she is less bratty and more aggressively antisocial than the first one. She may have initially seemed to be the most socially adjusted Eva pilot in the TV series, but here she makes no pretenses about liking anyone – not even Kaji, whom she hopelessly mooned over in the TV series but doesn't give a second thought here – and seems motivated as much by establishing herself in a future career path in NERV as she is by her personal pride. " and praised Anno's technique where "the peak moments of the most gruesome [scene] are set to a gentle, vocalized children's song which, paradoxically, makes the scenes all the more horrific".</p>
<p>Mania.com reviewer Chris Beveridge wrote that "watching the second installment of Evangelion is more rewarding and more difficult than the first. It's more rewarding because you're seeing this interpretation taking on its own life fully, from the way Asuka is introduced, the new character of Mari and the larger scope of how the world works with Evangelion units...They go big here with a lot of changes, changes that could annoy and irk the faithful, but it's an interpretation that is doing something different at this point and it does it well, giving it all the kind of smoothness it needs, even if it does feel rushed sometimes when there are so many Angel fights throughout it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Many reviews praised the action scenes (the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Globe" title="Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i> criticizing them as "exhausting"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup>); The <i><a href="/wiki/Toronto_Star" title="Toronto Star">Toronto Star</a></i> writes: "Sparing no time to get newbies up to speed, <i>Evangelion 2.0</i> will undoubtedly confound some viewers with its jam-packed narrative and jargon-heavy dialogue but the intricately designed, hyper-kinetic visuals more than compensate, especially when seen at the scale they deserve."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> likewise praised the action, but criticized the <a href="#Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> for being "a weird array of elevator-style music"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Kansas_City_Star" title="The Kansas City Star">The Kansas City Star</a></i>'s reviewer wrote "The peripheral characters are hard to keep track of, and a medieval theologian would be weirded out by all the mystical gibberish", but also praised the "terrific battle scenes and gorgeous animation. Those elements don’t compensate for every shortcoming, but they make a challenging genre much easier for novices to enjoy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> (<i><a href="/wiki/Northwest_Asian_Weekly" title="Northwest Asian Weekly">NAW</a></i>: "Having introduced Mari so vividly, though, the film forgets about her for about 40 minutes....She doesn’t reappear until halfway through the film. And her purpose remains elusive."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup>) <i><a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth_Weekly" title="Fort Worth Weekly">Fort Worth Weekly</a>'</i>s reviewer, on the other hand, praised the mystical gibberish and otaku references: "this movie trafficks in <a href="/wiki/Judeo-Christian" title="Judeo-Christian">Judeo-Christian</a> symbolism and references that often lean toward the obscure. This, along with its meta-commentary on anime itself and the ways its fans relate to it, helps set the movie apart from its fellows."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The film currently holds a 75% "fresh" rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
