<p><b>Everett McGill</b> (born <b>Charles Everett McGill III</b>, October 21, 1945) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> actor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> best known for mostly playing supporting roles in films like <i><a href="/wiki/Licence_to_Kill" title="Licence to Kill">Licence to Kill</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Silver_Bullet_(film)" title="Silver Bullet (film)">Silver Bullet</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_People_Under_the_Stairs" title="The People Under the Stairs">The People Under the Stairs</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbreak_Ridge" title="Heartbreak Ridge">Heartbreak Ridge</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dune_(film)" title="Dune (film)">Dune</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Yanks" title="Yanks">Yanks</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Under_Siege_2:_Dark_Territory" title="Under Siege 2: Dark Territory">Under Siege 2: Dark Territory</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/My_Fellow_Americans" title="My Fellow Americans">My Fellow Americans</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> as well as starring in the <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult classic</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Quest_for_Fire_(film)" title="Quest for Fire (film)">Quest for Fire</a></i> (1981).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everett_McGill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McGill was born <b>Charles Everett McGill III</b> in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach,_Florida" title="Miami Beach, Florida">Miami Beach, Florida</a>. He graduated from Rosedale High School in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Kansas" title="Kansas City, Kansas">Kansas City, Kansas</a>, in 1963.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everett_McGill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McGill has a relatively short filmography, but has managed to garner some level of fame by appearing in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Brubaker" title="Brubaker">Brubaker</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Robert_Redford" title="Robert Redford">Robert Redford</a>. With <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult followings</a>, including a stint as Chad Richards on the <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Guiding_Light" title="The Guiding Light">The Guiding Light</a></i> in 1975 and 1976. After coming into the public eye in 1981 for his role as the rugged <a href="/wiki/Caveman" title="Caveman">caveman</a> leader Naoh in <i><a href="/wiki/Quest_for_Fire_(film)" title="Quest for Fire (film)">Quest for Fire</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> McGill appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Silver_Bullet_(film)" title="Silver Bullet (film)">Silver Bullet</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> a 1985 <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> film inspired by a <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a> short story; the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> battle epic <i><a href="/wiki/Field_of_Honor_(1986_film)" title="Field of Honor (1986 film)">Field of Honor</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a> war film <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbreak_Ridge" title="Heartbreak Ridge">Heartbreak Ridge</a></i> in 1986; and in the 1989 installment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Bond_(film_series)" title="James Bond (film series)">James Bond</a> franchise <i><a href="/wiki/Licence_to_Kill" title="Licence to Kill">Licence to Kill</a></i>. In 1988, McGill played the titular role in <i><a href="/wiki/Iguana_(film)" title="Iguana (film)">Iguana</a></i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Monte_Hellman" title="Monte Hellman">Monte Hellman</a>. In later 1996, he starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/My_Fellow_Americans" title="My Fellow Americans">My Fellow Americans</a></i> starring his two late co-stars <a href="/wiki/James_Garner" title="James Garner">James Garner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Lemmon" title="Jack Lemmon">Jack Lemmon</a>. In 1991, McGill would reunite with his <i>Twin Peaks</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Robie" title="Wendy Robie">Wendy Robie</a>. The two appeared as the villains of the <a href="/wiki/Wes_Craven" title="Wes Craven">Wes Craven</a> feature <i><a href="/wiki/The_People_Under_the_Stairs" title="The People Under the Stairs">The People Under the Stairs</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_with_David_Lynch">Work with David Lynch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everett_McGill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Work with David Lynch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The actor is most widely recognized for his work with director <a href="/wiki/David_Lynch" title="David Lynch">David Lynch</a>. McGill first worked with Lynch in the 1984 adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Frank_Herbert" title="Frank Herbert">Frank Herbert</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dune_(film)" title="Dune (film)">Dune</a></i>, in which he played <a href="/wiki/Fremen" title="Fremen">Fremen</a> leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stilgar" title="Stilgar">Stilgar</a>. McGill later appeared as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Big_Ed_Hurley" title="Big Ed Hurley">Ed Hurley</a> on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks" title="Twin Peaks">Twin Peaks</a></i>. McGill also appeared in Lynch's 1999 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Straight_Story" title="The Straight Story">The Straight Story</a></i>.</p>
<p>After Lynch searched for him in his country home, the actor and director reunited, and McGill decided to come out of retirement specifically to reprise the role of Big Ed Hurley, in the <a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks_(2017_TV_series)" title="Twin Peaks (2017 TV series)">2017 revival of <i>Twin Peaks</i></a>.</p>
