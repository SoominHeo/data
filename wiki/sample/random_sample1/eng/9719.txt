<p><b>Evan</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masculine" title="Masculine">masculine</a> <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> derived from "lefen", a <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> form for the name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_(name)" title="John (name)">John</a>. In other languages it could be compared to "<a href="/wiki/Ivan_(name)" title="Ivan (name)">Ivan</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Ian" title="Ian">Ian</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Juan" title="Juan">Juan</a>"; the name John itself is derived from the ancient Hebrew name <span class="script-hebrew" dir="rtl" style="font-size: 115%; font-family: Alef, 'SBL BibLit', 'SBL Hebrew', 'David CLM', 'Frenk Ruehl CLM', 'Hadasim CLM', Cardo, Shofar, David, 'Ezra SIL', 'Ezra SIL SR', 'Noto Sans Hebrew', FreeSerif, 'Times New Roman', FreeSans, Arial;">יְהֹוחָנָן</span>‎ Yəhôḥānān, which means "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YHWH" title="YHWH">YHWH</a> is gracious". Evan also comes from Gaelic Eóghan meaning "youth" or "young warrior" and Scots for "right-handed".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In Hebrew, the actual non-Proper noun, "evan/even/eban/eben", literally means "rock". It can also be the shortened version of the Greek name "<a href="/wiki/Evangelos" title="Evangelos">Evangelos</a>" (meaning messenger, or "<a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Evander" title="Evander">Evander</a>" (meaning good man). The old English translation of the name "Evan" could also be interpreted as "Heir of the Earth" or "The King". The name is also occasionally given to females, as with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rachel_Wood" title="Evan Rachel Wood">Evan Rachel Wood</a>. It may be encountered as a surname, but <a href="/wiki/Evans_(surname)" title="Evans (surname)">Evans</a> is usual.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popularity">Popularity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Popularity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The popularity of the name Evan for males in the United States rose steadily over the last several decades, going from the 440th-ranked male name in 1957 to peaking at the 35th-ranked male name in 2009. It has since declined somewhat, and dropped out of the top 50 male names in the US in 2013.</p>
