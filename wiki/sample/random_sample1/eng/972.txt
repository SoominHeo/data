<p><b>FC Viktoria Plzeň</b> (<small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Czech" title="Help:IPA for Czech">[ˈvɪktorja ˈpl̩.zɛɲ]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_club" title="Football club">football club</a> based in the city of <a href="/wiki/Plze%C5%88" title="Plzeň">Plzeň</a> (Pilsen). They currently play in the <a href="/wiki/Czech_First_League" title="Czech First League">Czech First League</a>, the top division of football in the country.</p>
<p>As runners up in the 1970–71 <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_Cup" title="Czechoslovak Cup">Czechoslovak Cup</a> they gained the right to play in the following season's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a> as winners <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spartak_Trnava" title="Spartak Trnava">Spartak Trnava</a> also won the championship and played in the European Cup. In 2010, they played in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> after winning the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Czech_Cup" title="2009–10 Czech Cup">2009–10 Czech Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club won the Czech league for the first time in 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011-12_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011-12 UEFA Champions League">2011-12 UEFA Champions League</a> group stage, during which they won their first Champions League match,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> earning five points and qualifying for the Round of 32 in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Europa League">2011–12 UEFA Europa League</a>. The club won their second Czech league title in the 2012–13 season.</p>
<p>In 2013–14, the club participated in the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Champions League">2013–14 UEFA Champions League</a> group stage finishing third. Then reached the Round of 16 in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Europa League">2013–14 UEFA Europa League</a> before being eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viktoria_Plze%C5%88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viktoria_Plze%C5%88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1911 Jaroslav Ausobský, an official of the state railways, filed a request for the establishment of a new football club in Plzeň. In August of that year the newly formed team Viktoria played their first match, losing 7–3 against Olympia Plzeň.</p>
<p>For the first eighteen years of its existence, Viktoria Plzeň was a purely amateur club, although in 1922–1923 they took their first foreign trip to Spain, where they won six out of nine games. In June 1929 an extraordinary meeting of members agreed to go professional and enter the national professional league. Viktoria finished their first season in seventh place, but the very next season, improved and reached second place, which meant a first-ever promotion to the First League. In the <a href="/wiki/1934%E2%80%9335_Czechoslovak_First_League" title="1934–35 Czechoslovak First League">1934–35 Czechoslovak First League</a>, Viktoria finished in fourth place and subsequently played in the 1935 <a href="/wiki/Mitropa_Cup" title="Mitropa Cup">Mitropa Cup</a>, the top European club competition at the time. Two matches against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juventus" title="Juventus">Juventus</a> of Italy brought Viktoria to the attention of European football at large. They drew 3–3 at home, but lost 5–1 in Italy.</p>
<p>Viktoria played without success in the First League, being relegated for the 1938 season but returning to top competition the next year. The outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> interrupted competition, notably through the absence of teams from <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>. In 1942 Viktoria fell again into the divisions, but again returned to the top league the next year, where they would remain until 1952. That same year the club changed its name to Sokol Škoda Plzeň. For nine years they remained in the divisions, struggling to return to the First League, and in 1961, now under the name of Spartak Plzeň, achieved that promotion. The club was relegated and promoted frequently between the top two tiers until 1972, when as Škoda Plzeň they settled in the First League for eight years.</p>
<p>In 1971, Viktoria won the Czech Cup by drawing lots after the two-legged final ended 4–4 on aggregate and 5–5 in a limited penalty shootout against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparta_Prague" title="Sparta Prague">Sparta Prague</a> B.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf-4">[4]</a></sup> They lost 7–2 on aggregate in the Czechoslovak Cup final against Slovak Cup winners <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spartak_Trnava" title="Spartak Trnava">Spartak Trnava</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf2-5">[5]</a></sup> but as Trnava had won the league title that season, Viktoria was the country's entrant to the next season's Cup Winners' Cup. The club was eliminated in the first round by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> of West Germany, 7–2 on aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf3-6">[6]</a></sup> From 1980 until the division of Czechoslovakia 13 years later, Viktoria moved frequently between the top two tiers again.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Viktoria_Plze%C5%88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In 1992, the club returned to its historical name FC Viktoria Plzeň and the very next season, advanced to the first league, where it remained until 1999.</p>
<p>In the first years of the new millennium, Viktoria was owned by a foreign investor – Italian Football Company Ltd EAST. This situation lasted until March 2005, when 100% of the club's shares were bought by local interests. The Summer of 2005 also brought back relations with the Czech motor company <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0koda_Works" title="Škoda Works">Skoda</a>, who had previously been the club's name sponsors.</p>
<p>On 18 May 2010, Viktoria won the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Cup" title="Czech Cup">Czech Cup</a> final 2–1 against <a href="/wiki/FK_Jablonec" title="FK Jablonec">FK Jablonec</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf4_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf4-7">[7]</a></sup> and returned to European competitions (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010-11_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2010-11 UEFA Europa League">2010-11 UEFA Europa League</a>). Viktoria entered in the third qualifying round against <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş</a> of Turkey, and held them 1–1 at home before losing 3–0 away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Viktoria won its first ever league championship in 2010–11, finishing with 69 points to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparta_Prague" title="Sparta Prague">Sparta Prague</a>'s 68.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf5_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf5-9">[9]</a></sup> The club therefore qualified for a play-off to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011-12_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011-12 UEFA Champions League">2011-12 UEFA Champions League</a>, in which they beat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Copenhagen" title="FC Copenhagen">FC Copenhagen</a> of Denmark 5–2 on aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa2-10">[10]</a></sup> Viktoria were placed in Group H with reigning champions <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Milan" title="AC Milan">AC Milan</a>, and reached third place in the group by recording a victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_BATE_Borisov" title="FC BATE Borisov">BATE Borisov</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa3_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa3-11">[11]</a></sup> This saw the club drop into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011-12_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011-12 UEFA Europa League">2011-12 UEFA Europa League</a> in the last 32, where they lost 4–2 on aggregate to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schalke_04" title="Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> of Germany after extra time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa4_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa4-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadion_m%C4%9Bsta_Plzn%C4%9B" title="Stadion města Plzně">Stadion města Plzně</a> was also rebuilt in 2011. On 11 June 2011 Viktoria celebrated together with fans in the courtyard of the <a href="/wiki/Pilsner_Urquell" title="Pilsner Urquell">Pilsner Urquell</a> brewery for a centennial anniversary. In January 2012, the club held a festive gala for its centenary, and voted current midfielder <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Horv%C3%A1th" title="Pavel Horváth">Pavel Horváth</a> as its greatest player of all time.</p>
<p>The 2011–12 season saw Viktoria finish in third place in the league, three points behind champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slovan_Liberec" title="Slovan Liberec">Slovan Liberec</a>, to qualify for a third consecutive Europa League campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsssf6_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-rsssf6-13">[13]</a></sup> Starting in the second qualifying round, the club advanced past <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metalurgi_Rustavi" title="Metalurgi Rustavi">Metalurgi Rustavi</a> of Georgia and <a href="/wiki/Ruch_Chorz%C3%B3w" title="Ruch Chorzów">Ruch Chorzów</a> of Poland to set up a play-off against the Belgian club Lokeren, in which Viktoria advanced on away goals after a 2–2 aggregate draw. The club finished first in Group B, above the tournament's reigning champions, <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>. In the round of 32, Viktoria were drawn against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoli" title="Napoli">Napoli</a> of Italy, and won 3–0 away and 2–0 at home to advance to the last 16, where they played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football_team)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football team)">Fenerbahçe</a> of Turkey. Viktoria lost the home leg 1–0, and in the away leg (which was played behind closed doors due to the Istanbul club's fans' recent conduct) drew 1–1, nevertheless being eliminated from the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa5_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa5-14">[14]</a></sup> Viktoria won the Czech league for a second time at the end of the 2012–13 season.</p>
