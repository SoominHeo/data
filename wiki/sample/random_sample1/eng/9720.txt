<p><b>David Evan McMullin</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born April 2, 1976) is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a> operations officer who ran as an independent during the 2016 United States presidential election. McMullin served as a chief policy director for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Republican_Conference" title="House Republican Conference">House Republican Conference</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._House_of_Representatives" title="U.S. House of Representatives">U.S. House of Representatives</a> and a senior adviser on national security issues for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Committee_on_Foreign_Affairs" title="House Committee on Foreign Affairs">House Committee on Foreign Affairs</a>. He also worked briefly as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investment_banker" title="Investment banker">investment banker</a> after graduating from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wharton_School" title="Wharton School">Wharton School</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a>.</p>
<p>In August 2016, McMullin launched a <a href="/wiki/Evan_McMullin_presidential_campaign,_2016" title="Evan McMullin presidential campaign, 2016">presidential campaign</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2016" title="United States presidential election, 2016">2016 election</a> for <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, as an independent candidate backed by the organization <a href="/wiki/Better_for_America" title="Better for America">Better for America</a>.</p>
<p>McMullin lost the election, receiving 0.53% of the national popular vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LeipAtlas2016PrezResults_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-LeipAtlas2016PrezResults-2">[2]</a></sup> His best performance came in his native <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>, where he received slightly over 21% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McMullin was born in <a href="/wiki/Provo,_Utah" title="Provo, Utah">Provo, Utah</a>, the oldest of four children of David McMullin and Lanie Bullard. At a young age, his family moved to a rural area outside <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HH_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-HH-4">[4]</a></sup> where his father worked as a computer scientist and his mother sold bulk foods to neighbors from the family's garage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SLTrib_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SLTrib-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> After graduating in 1994 from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auburn_Senior_High_School" title="Auburn Senior High School">Auburn Senior High School</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> McMullin spent two years in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Missionary_(LDS_Church)" title="Missionary (LDS Church)">LDS missionary</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HH_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-HH-4">[4]</a></sup> Upon returning, he spent a summer working on an Alaskan fishing vessel.</p>
<p>In 1997, McMullin began attending <a href="/wiki/Brigham_Young_University" title="Brigham Young University">Brigham Young University</a>, where he did a summer internship with the CIA every year he was in college.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>He spent a year living in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> and volunteered as a refugee resettlement officer for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-launch-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2001, McMullin graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in International Law and Diplomacy and began formal training with the CIA to become an operative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>After working for the CIA, McMullin attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wharton_School" title="Wharton School">Wharton School</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a> where he received a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> degree in 2011.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Soon after McMullin joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a>, the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> occurred, leading to an accelerated training and deployment. He spent the next decade working overseas on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counterterrorism" title="Counterterrorism">counterterrorism</a> and intelligence operations as an operations officer with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Clandestine_Service" title="National Clandestine Service">National Clandestine Service</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> in the Middle East, North Africa, and South Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-launch-8">[8]</a></sup> He was first deployed in 2003 and left the agency in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the details of his missions remain classified, former CIA officers who worked with McMullin were complimentary of his work, noting his talent for recruiting members of extremist organizations through building trust, and willingness to engage in <a href="/wiki/Human_intelligence_(intelligence_gathering)" title="Human intelligence (intelligence gathering)">human intelligence</a> outside the confines of the embassy. Some of his missions included information gathering for efforts against the Taliban and al-Qaeda, and collaborating with British intelligence agencies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Near the end of his CIA career, he worked undercover in Iraq.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He has stated that his work involved meeting with business and government leaders, as well as collecting information from terrorist operatives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After leaving the CIA and graduating from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wharton_School" title="Wharton School">Wharton School</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a> in 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> McMullin began working for the Investment Banking Division at <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2012, he volunteered for <a href="/wiki/Mitt_Romney_presidential_campaign,_2012" title="Mitt Romney presidential campaign, 2012">Mitt Romney's presidential campaign</a>, which indirectly led to him being recruited by Republicans on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Committee_on_Foreign_Affairs" title="House Committee on Foreign Affairs">House Committee on Foreign Affairs</a> looking for an adviser with counter-terrorism experience. In 2013, McMullin was an International Advisory Board member for the Kennedy Center for International Studies at <a href="/wiki/Brigham_Young_University" title="Brigham Young University">Brigham Young University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, McMullin became a senior adviser on national security issues for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Committee_on_Foreign_Affairs" title="House Committee on Foreign Affairs">House Committee on Foreign Affairs</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/113th_Congress" title="113th Congress">113th Congress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swalshe1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-swalshe1-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2015, McMullin became the chief policy director of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Republican_Conference" title="House Republican Conference">House Republican Conference</a> under Chairwoman <a href="/wiki/Cathy_McMorris_Rodgers" title="Cathy McMorris Rodgers">Cathy McMorris Rodgers</a> (<a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">R</a>-<a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Wash.</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It was from this position that he watched the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_presidential_primaries,_2016" title="Republican Party presidential primaries, 2016">2016 Republican primaries</a>, and when he began to speak out against Trump he was urged by some Republicans to stay out of the fray.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SLTrib_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-SLTrib-5">[5]</a></sup> McMullin resigned as chief policy director shortly before declaring his run for the presidency in August 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swalshe1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-swalshe1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2016, McMullin launched a <a href="/wiki/Evan_McMullin_presidential_campaign,_2016" title="Evan McMullin presidential campaign, 2016">presidential campaign</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2016" title="United States presidential election, 2016">2016 election</a> for <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, as an independent candidate backed by the organization <a href="/wiki/Better_for_America" title="Better for America">Better for America</a>. McMullin described himself as a <a href="/wiki/Conservatism_in_the_United_States" title="Conservatism in the United States">conservative</a> alternative to the two major political parties' candidates, <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> nominee <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> nominee <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>.</p>
<p>McMullin lost the election, receiving 725,902 votes nationwide (0.53%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LeipAtlas2016PrezResults_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-LeipAtlas2016PrezResults-2">[2]</a></sup> His best performance came in his native <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>, where he received about 20% of the vote and came in third place behind Trump and Clinton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Since the election, McMullin has said he is starting a "new conservative movement" reaching out to "non-traditional conservative voters... who feel disaffected." It may, he says, form a new political party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On January 25, 2017, McMullin announced the formation of Stand Up Republic, a nonpartisan watchdog organization meant to ensure that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trump_administration" title="Trump administration">Trump administration</a> upholds democracy and tells the truth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>On August 8, 2016, McMullin announced that he would run as a candidate for <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2016" title="United States presidential election, 2016">2016 presidential election</a> as an independent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-politico.com_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-politico.com-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter-24">[24]</a></sup> McMullin decided to run due to his belief that Republican nominee <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> was unfit for the office and his strong opposition to Democratic nominee <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>'s liberalism. He had personally lobbied several Congresspeople to run under the <a href="/wiki/Better_for_America" title="Better for America">Better for America</a> banner, but when none would run and it was suggested to him that he should run himself, he decided to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin had the support of several anti-Trump Republican donors<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and his presidential bid was also backed by several former members of <a href="/wiki/Better_for_America" title="Better for America">Better for America</a>, a 501(c)(4) organization dedicated to getting nationwide <a href="/wiki/Ballot_access" title="Ballot access">ballot access</a> for an independent candidate for President in the 2016 election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-launch-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> McMullin's campaign is supported by some members of the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Never_Trump" title="Never Trump">Never Trump</a>" movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin's late entrance into the race caused him to miss several state ballot deadlines,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rleonard1_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-rleonard1-29">[29]</a></sup> and ultimately he was only able to <a href="/wiki/Evan_McMullin_presidential_campaign,_2016#Ballot_status" title="Evan McMullin presidential campaign, 2016">appear on the ballot</a> in eleven states, with write-in eligibility in many other states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HowToVote_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-HowToVote-30">[30]</a></sup> As such he did not appear on enough ballots to win the necessary <a href="/wiki/Electoral_College_(United_States)" title="Electoral College (United States)">Electoral College</a> majority of 270 electoral votes. Instead McMullin hoped to deny a majority of the electoral vote from either of the two major party candidates. In such a scenario, under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Twelfth Amendment to the United States Constitution">Twelfth Amendment</a>, the House of Representatives would select the new president from the top-three electoral vote winners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bjacobs1_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-bjacobs1-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The same day that McMullin launched his independent bid it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Kahlil_Byrd" title="Kahlil Byrd">Kahlil Byrd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Ashby" title="Chris Ashby">Chris Ashby</a>, Republican strategists with expertise in third-party ballot access, would form a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_PAC" title="Super PAC">super PAC</a> called <a href="/wiki/Stand_Up_America" title="Stand Up America">Stand Up America</a> to support McMullin's campaign. Byrd is a former adviser for <a href="/wiki/Better_for_America" title="Better for America">Better for America</a>. The PAC would be used for TV and digital ads, live events, and grass-roots organizing, but would not sue for ballot access.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 6, McMullin named <a href="/wiki/Mindy_Finn" title="Mindy Finn">Mindy Finn</a> as his running mate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finn1_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-finn1-33">[33]</a></sup> Finn had previously worked for <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> and as a digital strategist for the RNC and the <a href="/wiki/National_Republican_Senatorial_Committee" title="National Republican Senatorial Committee">National Republican Senatorial Committee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finn1_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-finn1-33">[33]</a></sup> Because Finn's selection came after the ballot paperwork deadlines, Finn did not appear on any of the state ballots. Instead McMullin's friend's name, Nathan Johnson, was submitted as a placeholder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HowToVote_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-HowToVote-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin's support surged in Utah in October after the release of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donald_Trump_and_Billy_Bush_recording_controversy" title="Donald Trump and Billy Bush recording controversy">a 2005 audio recording</a> in which Donald Trump was heard bragging in lewd terms about making unwanted sexual advances on women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On October 19, an Emerson College poll showed McMullin leading the race in Utah by 4 points over Donald Trump and 7 points over Hillary Clinton. McMullin's popularity in Utah – and Trump's unpopularity – appears owing to an unusual shift of <a href="/wiki/Mormons" title="Mormons">Mormons</a> away from the Republican candidate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Had McMullin won Utah, it would have been the first time since <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1968" title="United States presidential election, 1968">1968</a> that a non-major-party candidate won a state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> His strong polling in Utah led Benjamin Morris, writing for <a href="/wiki/FiveThirtyEight" title="FiveThirtyEight">FiveThirtyEight</a>, to call him the "third-most likely person to be the next president of the United States" as of October 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ultimately, McMullin came in third place in Utah, receiving 21.54% of the state's popular vote, behind both Donald Trump (who received 45.54% and the state's six electoral votes) and Hillary Clinton (who received 27.46%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> McMullin also took third in Idaho with 6.7%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> McMullin promised the "fight would continue" for conservative values despite his defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_stances">Political stances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Political stances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_issues">Social issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Social issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McMullin opposes abortion and identifies himself as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-life" title="Pro-life">pro-life</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Life_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-Life-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBUR_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-WBUR-42">[42]</a></sup> McMullin supports overturning <i><a href="/wiki/Roe_v._Wade" title="Roe v. Wade">Roe v. Wade</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBUR_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-WBUR-42">[42]</a></sup> According to his campaign website, he opposes "a culture that subsidizes abortion on demand" and McMullin calls for action to reduce unintended pregnancies and promote adoption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Life_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-Life-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_the_United_States" title="Same-sex marriage in the United States">same-sex marriage</a>, McMullin said in 2016 that he believes in the "traditional marriage between a man and a woman" but he "respects" the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court</a>'s decision in <i><a href="/wiki/Obergefell_v._Hodges" title="Obergefell v. Hodges">Obergefell v. Hodges</a></i> (which found that same-sex couples have a constitutional right to marry) and thinks it is "time to move on" from the issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg-43">[43]</a></sup> McMullin's mother is in a same-sex marriage, having married another woman after separating from his father, and McMullin has said that "As far as my mother's marriage is concerned, I believe in the sanctity of traditional marriage. It is an important part of my faith. My mother has a different view. That is OK. I love her very much."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SLTrib_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-SLTrib-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_issues">Economic issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economic issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McMullin supports <a href="/wiki/Free_trade" title="Free trade">free trade</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RubinInterview_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-RubinInterview-44">[44]</a></sup> pointing to the economic benefits of international trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBUR_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-WBUR-42">[42]</a></sup> He supports <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NAFTA" title="NAFTA">NAFTA</a> and the proposed <a href="/wiki/Trans-Pacific_Partnership" title="Trans-Pacific Partnership">Trans-Pacific Partnership</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBUR_42-3"><a href="#cite_note-WBUR-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin supports the Republican House plan on tax reform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RubinInterview_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-RubinInterview-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin supports a reduction in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporate_income_tax" title="Corporate income tax">corporate income tax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Income_tax_in_the_United_States" title="Income tax in the United States">individual income tax</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RomboyRoche_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-RomboyRoche-45">[45]</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Estate_tax_in_the_United_States" title="Estate tax in the United States">estate tax</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin favors cuts to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entitlement_program" title="Entitlement program">entitlement programs</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_(United_States)" title="Social Security (United States)">Social Security</a>, and has proposed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Means-testing" title="Means-testing">means-testing</a> the program and raising the retirement age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RomboyRoche_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-RomboyRoche-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_the_environment">Science and the environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Science and the environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McMullin accepts the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_consensus_on_climate_change" title="Scientific consensus on climate change">scientific consensus on climate change</a>, saying: "I do believe that the climate is changing, and I do believe that human activity is contributing to it. If I were president, I would increase investment in technologies that can help us limit and decrease our carbon emissions."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBUR_42-4"><a href="#cite_note-WBUR-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_issues">Domestic issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Domestic issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McMullin has said that if elected president, he would appoint <a href="/wiki/Originalism" title="Originalism">originalist</a> judges to the Supreme Court,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WBUR_42-5"><a href="#cite_note-WBUR-42">[42]</a></sup> "in the mold of <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Scalia" title="Antonin Scalia">Antonin Scalia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clarence_Thomas" title="Clarence Thomas">Clarence Thomas</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>On immigration, McMullin supports border security but not mass deportation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RubinInterview_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-RubinInterview-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>On health care, McMullin supports the provision of the <a href="/wiki/Patient_Protection_and_Affordable_Care_Act" title="Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act">Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act</a> that blocks health insurance companies from denying coverage or discriminating against people with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preexisting_condition" title="Preexisting condition">preexisting conditions</a>. However, he also stated that "we also need to do better than ObamaCare."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_and_national_security_policy">Foreign and national security policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Foreign and national security policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syrian Civil War</a>, McMullin described himself as a "vocal advocate for international action that would stop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bashir_al-Assad" title="Bashir al-Assad">Assad</a>'s slaughter of innocent Syrians, and eventually set the stage for a negotiated departure from the country."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cupp_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cupp-49">[49]</a></sup> McMullin also has said: "We should have done more to support the moderate Syrian opposition, and we still need to do that. They haven't received sufficient support or training, and we know how to do that very well."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cupp_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cupp-49">[49]</a></sup> McMullin supports imposing a <a href="/wiki/No-fly_zone" title="No-fly zone">no-fly zone</a> over <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> "to stop the aerial bombardment of Syrian population centers."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cupp_49-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cupp-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin supports keeping the <a href="/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay_detention_camp" title="Guantanamo Bay detention camp">Guantanamo Bay detention camp</a> open.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RubinInterview_44-3"><a href="#cite_note-RubinInterview-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin has harshly criticized the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Comprehensive_Plan_of_Action" title="Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action">international nuclear agreement with Iran</a>. He stated: "We've got to certainly enforce the deal as it is, but I believe in strengthening sanctions on Iran to force them to make further concessions. I also believe in putting the military option clearly back on the table if Iran decides to not keep its end of the deal and if it ultimately decides to pursue nuclear weapons."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin told <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">ABC News</a> that he believed <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>'s public comments were frustrating U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counterterrorism" title="Counterterrorism">counterterrorism</a> efforts. "What he doesn't realize is that we actually depend on Muslims to do counterterrorism, to wage war against terrorists." Trump's remarks concerning Muslims "decreases their willingness to work with us, with other Muslims, and that impedes our ability to destroy ISIS."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> McMullin also criticized Trump's "allegiance to [Russian President] <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>" and accused Putin of engaging in a campaign to destabilize European and North American countries "through fomenting discord between different racial groups, different ethnic groups and different religious groups."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bjacobs1_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-bjacobs1-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, McMullin helped to bring Caesar, a defected Syrian military photographer who leaked 55,000 images depicting abuses by the regime (which formed the basis for the <a href="/wiki/2014_Syrian_detainee_report" title="2014 Syrian detainee report">2014 Syrian detainee report</a>), to speak before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Foreign_Affairs_Committee" title="House Foreign Affairs Committee">House Foreign Affairs Committee</a>, according to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Christian_Science_Monitor" title="The Christian Science Monitor">The Christian Science Monitor</a></i>. McMullin clashed with State Department officials he suspected were holding up the hearing. McMullin claimed that State Department officials wanted to have a closed hearing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>McMullin opposes the use of <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">torture</a> and has criticized Trump's support for it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evan_McMullin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McMullin is a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Church_of_Jesus_Christ_of_Latter-day_Saints" title="The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints">The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints</a>. He is unmarried.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
