<p><b>Evgenia Radanova</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Евгения Раданова</span>) (born 4 November 1977) is a <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> female <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skater" title="Short track speed skater">short track speed skater</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racing_cyclist" title="Racing cyclist">racing cyclist</a> who has participated in both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" title="Summer Olympics">Summer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She was the world record holder in the 500 m short track distance with 43.671s, which she set in Calgary, Canada on 19 October 2001. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City_2002" title="Salt Lake City 2002">Salt Lake City 2002</a> Winter Olympic games she won a silver medal at the same distance and a bronze at 1500m. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athens_2004" title="Athens 2004">Athens 2004</a> Summer games she took part in cycling, but did not get a medal. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_Vancouver_Olympics" title="2010 Vancouver Olympics">2010 Vancouver Olympics</a> in the women's 500 m she ended up 7th.</p>
<p>Radanova currently trains in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, although most of her career she spent with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavia_Sofia_Sports_Club" title="Slavia Sofia Sports Club">Slavia Sofia Sports Club</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Sports_Academy" title="National Sports Academy">National Sports Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>. In the Academy she studied coaching. Jeni's coach is Ivan Pandov. She weighs 65 kg and is 170 cm tall. Radanova has been severely injured at least twice, but has overcome the injuries to come back. In August 2014, Radanova was appointed as Bulgarian Minister of Youth and Sports on a temporary basis as part of the <a href="/wiki/Bliznashki_Government" title="Bliznashki Government">caretaker government of Georgi Bliznashki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mediapool101.bg_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mediapool101.bg-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
