<p><b>Ebraucus</b> (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>: <span lang="cy" xml:lang="cy"><i><b>Efrawg</b></i></span>/<b>Efrog</b>) was a legendary king of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britons_(historic)" title="Britons (historic)">Britons</a>, as recounted by <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_of_Monmouth" title="Geoffrey of Monmouth">Geoffrey of Monmouth</a>. He was the son of King <a href="/wiki/Mempricius" title="Mempricius">Mempricius</a> before he abandoned the family.</p>
<p>Following the death of his father, Mempricius, he became king and reigned 39 years. According to Geoffrey he was admired, tall, and remarkably strong. He was the first to wage war on the <a href="/wiki/Gauls" title="Gauls">Gauls</a> since the time of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brutus_of_Britain" title="Brutus of Britain">Brutus</a>. By pillaging the cities and shores and slaughtering many men, he became extremely wealthy and enriched the lands of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>.</p>
<p>He founded two settlements: Kaerebrauc, the City of Ebraucus (<a href="/wiki/Eboracum" title="Eboracum">Eboracum</a>), north of the <a href="/wiki/Humber" title="Humber">Humber</a> (this later became <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> name is <i><a class="extiw" href="https://cy.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efrog" title="cy:Efrog">Efrog</a></i>); and Alclud in <a href="/wiki/Alba" title="Alba">Albany</a> (now part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dunbarton" title="Dunbarton">Dunbarton</a>, capital of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Strathclyde" title="Kingdom of Strathclyde">Strathclyde</a>). According to <a href="/wiki/Polydore_Vergil" title="Polydore Vergil">Polydore Vergil</a> he "builded the town of Maidens, now called Edinburgh Castle, being planted in the uttermost part of Britain, now called Scotland".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <i>Registrum Malmesburiense</i> also says he built the "castle of Montrose".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He had twenty wives who produced twenty sons and thirty daughters. All his daughters he sent to his cousin Silvius Alba in <a href="/wiki/Alba_Longa" title="Alba Longa">Alba Longa</a> (Italy) to be married to the other <a href="/wiki/Troy" title="Troy">Trojan</a> descendants. Except for <a href="/wiki/Brutus_Greenshield" title="Brutus Greenshield">Brutus Greenshield</a>, all of Ebraucus's sons, led by Assaracus,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> went to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, creating a kingdom there. Brutus thus succeeded Ebraucus upon his death.</p>
