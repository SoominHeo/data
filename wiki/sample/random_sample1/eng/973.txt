<p><b>Football Club Shakhter</b> (<a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh</a>: <span lang="kk">«Шахтер» футбол клубы</span>), commonly referred to as <b>FC Shakhter Karagandy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh</a>: <span lang="kk">Қарағанды/Qarağandı</span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Kazakh" title="Help:IPA for Kazakh">[qɑɾɑˈʁɑndə]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Kk-karagandy.ogg" title="File:Kk-karagandy.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/63/Kk-karagandy.ogg" title="Kk-karagandy.ogg">listen</a>)</small>), is a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karagandy" title="Karagandy">Karagandy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>. They have been members of the <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan_Premier_League" title="Kazakhstan Premier League">Kazakhstan Premier League</a> since its foundation in 1992.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Shakhter_Karagandy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After reaching third place in 1995 and 2007 their first championship was won in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Before this they were one of the leading Kazakh teams in the football of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.</p>
<p>On 19 September 2008, Shakhter and <a href="/wiki/FC_Vostok" title="FC Vostok">Vostok</a> were disqualified from the <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan_Premier_League" title="Kazakhstan Premier League">Kazakhstan Premier League</a> for playing a <a href="/wiki/Match_fixing" title="Match fixing">fixed match</a>, club coaches and management involved were banned from football for 60 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 2 October 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_of_Kazakhstan" title="Football Federation of Kazakhstan">FFK</a> revisited their decisions. Shakhter were deducted nine points, Vostok were immediately expelled from the competition. The result of the game in question has been cancelled and all remaining Vostok games will be counted as won by 3–0 for their opponents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dec2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-dec2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 20 August 2013, Shakhter defeated Scottish champions <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> by 2–0 at home in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round" title="2013–14 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">2013–14 UEFA Champions League play-off round</a> first leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, Celtic won the return leg by 3–0 and Shakhter dropped into the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> - the first time a Kazakh club has progressed to the group stage of a European competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Shakhter were eliminated from the Europa League by finishing fourth in a group containing <a href="/wiki/PAOK_FC" title="PAOK FC">PAOK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maccabi_Haifa_F.C." title="Maccabi Haifa F.C.">Maccabi Haifa</a> and <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_culture">Club culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Shakhter_Karagandy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shakhter sacrifice a sheep the day before matches, as a tradition. This has led the <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals</a> (PETA) to petition UEFA President <a href="/wiki/Michel_Platini" title="Michel Platini">Michel Platini</a> for a ban on animal slaughter in European football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Laws regarding animal slaughter have made the custom impossible when playing away in some countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
