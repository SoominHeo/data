<p>The <b>Ramón Sánchez Pizjuán Stadium</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Estadio Ramón Sánchez Pizjuán</i></span>; <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[esˈtaðjo raˈmon ˈsantʃeθ piθˈxwan]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It is the home stadium of <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla Fútbol Club</a>. It was the venue for the <a href="/wiki/1986_European_Cup_Final" title="1986 European Cup Final">1986 European Cup Final</a> between <a href="/wiki/FC_Steaua_Bucure%C8%99ti" title="FC Steaua București">Steaua București</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1982_Football_World_Cup" title="1982 Football World Cup">1982 World Cup</a> semi-final game between <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>.</p>
<p>This stadium contains a singular legend: the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish national team</a> has never lost a game against an international team in this stadium. In European competition, Sevilla has lost only three times at home; to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AZ_(football_club)" title="AZ (football club)">AZ</a> in the 2006–07 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> group stage, to <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moskva</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2009–10 UEFA Champions League">2009–10 UEFA Champions League</a> and to <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Europa League">2013–14 UEFA Europa League</a>.</p>
<p>The stadium holds 42,500 and was built in 1957. It replaced the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_de_Nervi%C3%B3n" title="Estadio de Nervión">Estadio de Nervión</a>.</p>
<p>The stadium is nicknamed <i>"La Bombonera"</i> (<small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[la βomboˈneɾa]</a></span>; more commonly used to refer to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estadio_Alberto_J._Armando" title="Estadio Alberto J. Armando">Estadio Alberto J. Armando</a>, the home stadium of <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a>) or <i>"La Bombonera de Nervión"</i> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[la βomboˈneɾa ðe nerˈβjon]</a></span> due to the <a href="/wiki/Nervi%C3%B3n,_Seville" title="Nervión, Seville">Nervión</a> neighbourhood where the stadium is situated.</p>
