<p>The <b>Estadio Mario Alberto Kempes</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> formerly known as <b>Estadio Córdoba</b> and popularly <b>Estadio Olímpico Chateau Carreras</b>, is a stadium in the Chateau Carreras neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Argentina" title="Córdoba, Argentina">Córdoba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.</p>
<p>The stadium was built in 1976 in preparation for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_World_Cup_1978" title="Football World Cup 1978">1978 World Cup</a>, with a capacity for 46,083 spectators although it doesn't provide seating for all of them, like many <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> stadiums.</p>
<p>Most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> teams in <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Argentina" title="Córdoba, Argentina">Córdoba</a> have their own stadiums but they usually prefer playing in this stadium for its size and comfort, especially when playing important games that attract big crowds. Generally, this stadium is used for <a href="/wiki/Talleres_de_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Talleres de Córdoba">Talleres</a>' matches, but sometimes is used for the <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Belgrano" title="Club Atlético Belgrano">Belgrano</a>'s, <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Atl%C3%A9tico_Central_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Instituto Atlético Central Córdoba">Instituto</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Racing_de_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Racing de Córdoba">Racing</a>'s matches. Also, the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national football team</a> home matches have been played here.</p>
<p>The stadium, in <a href="/wiki/2006_Rally_Argentina" title="2006 Rally Argentina">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Rally_Argentina" title="2007 Rally Argentina">2007</a>, hosted some Special Stages of motorsport event named <a href="/wiki/Rally_Argentina" title="Rally Argentina">Rally Argentina</a>, a round of the <a href="/wiki/World_Rally_Championship" title="World Rally Championship">World Rally Championship</a>.</p>

<p>During 2010 and 2011 the stadium went through a remodelling process. The field was sunken 4 meters, the iconic Autotrol scoreboards installed for the 1978 World Cup were replaced with modern video screens, new bleachers were built in order to improve the poor view caused by the shallow shape of the stadium and the capacity was increased to 57,000, becoming the second largest stadium in Argentina by seating capacity. It was re-opened on June 26th, 2011, just 5 days before the start of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Copa_America" title="2011 Copa America">2011 Copa America</a>. The stands were fully covered after the cup.</p>
<p>In October, 2010 the name was changed to honor <a href="/wiki/Mario_Kempes" title="Mario Kempes">Mario Kempes</a>, top goal scorer of the <a href="/wiki/1978_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1978 FIFA World Cup">1978 FIFA World Cup</a>, native of Cordoba.</p>

<p>Mario Kempes Stadium also hosted three Group B matches and a semifinal during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1987_Copa_America" title="1987 Copa America">1987 Copa America</a>; five group stage matches, two matches during round of 16, one more for quarter finals and a semi final for the <a href="/wiki/2001_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="2001 FIFA World Youth Championship">2001 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>; and for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Copa_America" title="2011 Copa America">2011 Copa America</a> Cordoba was venue for two <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Group_B" title="2011 Copa América Group B">Group B</a>, one <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Group_A" title="2011 Copa América Group A">Group A</a> matches and held a quarter final. <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_rugby_union_team" title="Argentina national rugby union team">Argentina national rugby union team</a>, <i>Los Pumas</i>, has played several test matches here and the Cordoba Rugby Union has used the stadium for defining instances of its annual championship.</p>
<p>The 1982 <a href="/wiki/World_Boxing_Association" title="World Boxing Association">World Boxing Association</a> world Super-Bantamweight title fight between champion <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Victor_Palma" title="Sergio Victor Palma">Sergio Palma</a> and former WBA world Bantamweight champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jorge_Lujan_(boxer)" title="Jorge Lujan (boxer)">Jorge Lujan</a>, won by Palma on a fifteen round unanimous decision, was held at this stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Córdoba also held the <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Argentina_Final" title="2015 Copa Argentina Final">2015</a> and <a href="/wiki/2016_Copa_Argentina_Final" title="2016 Copa Argentina Final">2016</a> finals of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Argentina" title="Copa Argentina">Copa Argentina</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/2015_Supercopa_Argentina" title="2015 Supercopa Argentina">2015 Supercopa Argentina</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Mario_Alberto_Kempes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mario Alberto Kempes Stadium is the center of a 40 hectares park and sports complex that includes the Soledad García Provincial Field Hockey Stadium, opened in 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> that honors the two times world champion <a href="/wiki/Soledad_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Soledad García">Soledad García</a>; an <a href="/wiki/Olympic-size_swimming_pool" title="Olympic-size swimming pool">Olympic-size swimming pool</a>, opened in 2014 and named after the olympic medallist <a href="/wiki/Georgina_Bardach" title="Georgina Bardach">Georgina Bardach</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> an auxiliar field, located north of the main stadium, that hosts training and smaller football and rugby events; a BMX circuit, a running track, and basketball, volleyball and tennis facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
