<p><b>Esteban Alvarado Brown</b> (born 28 April 1989 in <a href="/wiki/Siquirres" title="Siquirres">Siquirres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lim%C3%B3n" title="Limón">Limón</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rican</a> <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">football goalkeeper</a> who plays for the Turkish club <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Esteban_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saprissa">Saprissa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Esteban_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Saprissa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Siquirres" title="Siquirres">Siquirres</a>, Costa Rica, Alvarado started his academy career at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santos_de_Gu%C3%A1piles" title="Santos de Guápiles">Santos de Guápiles</a> before moving to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.D._Saprissa" title="C.D. Saprissa">Saprissa</a>. Alvarado made his Saprissa debut, in a 3-1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipal_Liberia" title="Municipal Liberia">Municipal Liberia</a> on 29 November 2009.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> With his move to AZ finalised and expected to move in the summer, Alvarado made four appearance for the club and helped the club win the league.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AZ_Alkmaar">AZ Alkmaar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Esteban_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: AZ Alkmaar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1376" data-file-width="1105" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg/180px-Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg/270px-Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg/360px-Esteban-alvarado-brown-1313344771.jpg 2x" width="180"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>In December 2009, <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ</a> signed the Costa Rican goalkeeper from <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_Saprissa" title="Deportivo Saprissa">Deportivo Saprissa</a>, the youngster moved to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alvarado_Best_Player_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarado_Best_Player-1">[1]</a></sup> The move was later confirmed when Alvarado signed a five-year contract with the club, keeping him until 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his signing for AZ, Alvarado remained a backup goalkeeper for AZ behind <a href="/wiki/Joey_Didulica" title="Joey Didulica">Joey Didulica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Romero" title="Sergio Romero">Sergio Romero</a>. But after Romeo's injury from his international duty,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Alvarado made his AZ debut on 12 February 2011 against <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>, where it turned out to be disastrous when he conceded four goals, in a 4-0 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After the match, Alvarado said, quoting: <i>"Only Superman could have saved me."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Esteban:_Helaas_geen_Superman_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Esteban:_Helaas_geen_Superman-5">[5]</a></sup> From that on, Alvarado continued to be the first choice goalkeeper temporary, where he made six appearance for the club in the 2010-11 season, including a clean-sheet in a match against <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">Heerenveen</a> on 19 February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Alvarado also find himself left out the squad against <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> when he returned late to Netherlands from Costa Rica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ahead of the 2011-12 season, Alvardo was given number thirty-four shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Esteban:_Helaas_geen_Superman_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Esteban:_Helaas_geen_Superman-5">[5]</a></sup> Alvarado became a first choice goalkeeper for AZ after <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Romero" title="Sergio Romero">Sergio Romero</a>'s departure to <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joey_Didulica" title="Joey Didulica">Joey Didulica</a>'s unexpected retirement. Alvarado made his first AZ appearance of 2011-12 season when he played, in a 2-0 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Baumit_Jablonec" title="FK Baumit Jablonec">Baumit Jablonec</a> on 28 July 2011, which AZ went through after a 1-1 draw against them in the second leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Alvarado made his league debut of the 2011-12 season, where he helped AZ beat PSV 3-1 in the opening game of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> His performance soon led supporters voting for Alvarado awarded the club's player of the month November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 21 December 2011, Alvarado was sent off by <a href="/wiki/Bas_Nijhuis" title="Bas Nijhuis">Bas Nijhuis</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_KNVB_Cup" title="2011–12 KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> match against <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> after an Ajax supporter tried attacking him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WFP_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajax_vs_AZ_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_Esteban_Alvarado_gets_sent_off_for_kicking_pitch_invader_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ajax_vs_AZ_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_Esteban_Alvarado_gets_sent_off_for_kicking_pitch_invader-12">[12]</a></sup> A 19-year-old Ajax fan identified as Wesley van W had been drinking and entered the pitch after 36 minutes of play. He ran towards the AZ goalkeeper and attempted to kick him but slipped to the ground. Alvarado then kicked the attacker twice<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WFP_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajax_vs_AZ_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_Esteban_Alvarado_gets_sent_off_for_kicking_pitch_invader_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ajax_vs_AZ_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_Esteban_Alvarado_gets_sent_off_for_kicking_pitch_invader-12">[12]</a></sup> and was sent off for violent conduct. This prompted AZ manager <a href="/wiki/Gertjan_Verbeek" title="Gertjan Verbeek">Gertjan Verbeek</a> to order his players to the dressing rooms in protest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WFP_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajax_and_AZ_Alkmaar_match_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_kicks_fan_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ajax_and_AZ_Alkmaar_match_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_kicks_fan-13">[13]</a></sup> The game was consequently abandoned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajax_vs_AZ_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_Esteban_Alvarado_gets_sent_off_for_kicking_pitch_invader_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ajax_vs_AZ_abandoned_after_goalkeeper_Esteban_Alvarado_gets_sent_off_for_kicking_pitch_invader-12">[12]</a></sup> The KNVB subsequently rescinded the red card, and Ajax banned Wesley van W from all of their home matches for life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He was also sentenced to six months in prison, with two months suspended. He would also have to undergo treatment for an alcohol problem and report to police during all AZ, Ajax and Dutch national team matches for the following two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The KNVB ordered the match replayed in its entirety <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Behind_closed_doors_(football)" title="Behind closed doors (football)">behind closed doors</a>. On 19 January 2012, the match was replayed with Alvarado in goal. AZ beat Ajax 3–2 to progress through the quarter final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Alvarado was the ever present goalkeeper, making thirty-four appearance and earning fifteen clean sheets in the 2011-12 season. Alvarado was also named Goal.com's Eredivisie Team of the Season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2012-13 season, Alvarado was given number one shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Alvarado made his first appearance of the 2012-13 season, in a 2-2 draw against <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> in the opening game of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In both games against <a href="/wiki/FC_Anzhi_Makhachkala" title="FC Anzhi Makhachkala">Anzhi Makhackala</a> in the play-offs, Alvarado was in goal, as they proved to be too strong and failed to win either legs losing 1-0 and 5-0 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Later in the 2012-13 season, Alvarado was the ever present goalkeeper, making thirty-four appearance in 3060 minutes, as well as, earning five clean sheets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Despite this, Alvarado was in goal for the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_KNVB_Cup#Final" title="2012–13 KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup final</a>, as AZ beat PSV 2-1, making this as Alvarado's first achievement at AZ Alkmaar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2013-14 season, Alvarado hinted he may leave the club in effort for new challenges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Despite this, Alvarado made his first appearance of the season, in a 3-2 loss against Ajax in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Johan_Cruijff_Schaal" title="2013 Johan Cruijff Schaal">Johan Cruijff Schaal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Two weeks later on 11 August 2013, Alvarado help the club gained their revenge against Ajax when AZ beat them 3-2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Alvarado also help the club gained their against Anzhi Makhackala when AZ beat 1-0 on aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Alvarado was the ever present goalkeeper, making thirty-four appearance, including the play-offs, which AZ lose 3-0 on aggregate against <a href="/wiki/FC_Groningen" title="FC Groningen">Groningen</a>.</p>
<p>In the 2014-15 season, Alvarado continued to be the first choice goalkeeper until he suffered a knee injury that kept him out for months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Two spending two months on the sidelined, including having a flu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Alvarado made his return to the first team on 2 November 2014, in a 3-3 draw against <a href="/wiki/S.B.V._Excelsior" title="S.B.V. Excelsior">Excelsior</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Alvarado was featured as a first choice goalkeeper since his return for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> For the first time in his AZ career, Alvarado made less than thirty appearance, as he made twenty-six appearance in the 2014-15 season.</p>
<p>With his contract expiring at the end of the 2014-15 season, Alvarado was expected to leave the club, with newly signed <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Rochet" title="Sergio Rochet">Sergio Rochet</a> from Uruguay as his successor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> After making his last appearance against Excelsior in the last game of the season, Alvarado helped the club finished third place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> During his time at AZ, Alvarado was the club's fan favourite.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Esteban_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Trabzonspor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After leaving AZ, Alvarado joined <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a> on a free transfer in early-August, keeping him until 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The move was officially confirmed on 6 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Esteban_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> he was named the best goalkeeper of the tournament. He made his national team début in an August 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly match</a> against Paraguay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alvarado_Best_Player_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarado_Best_Player-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 March 2012, it was announced that Alvarado was barred from the national team for five months after leaving the team before a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> without communicating the staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In March 2014, he ruled out a selection for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> due to an apparent strife with national team manager <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Luis_Pinto" title="Jorge Luis Pinto">Jorge Luis Pinto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> But in May 2014, Alvarado was named in a provisional 26-man squad in the FIFA World Cup<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> By the end of May, Alvarado was not named in the 23 men squad, presumed to be a continuous strife with national team manager Pinto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following Pinto's departure as Manager of Costa, Alvarado returned to the national football team, under new management of <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Wanchope" title="Paulo Wanchope">Paulo Wanchope</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2015_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2015 CONCACAF Gold Cup">CONCACAF Gold Cup</a>, Alvarado was included in the squad and replaced <a href="/wiki/Keylor_Navas" title="Keylor Navas">Keylor Navas</a> as a first choice goalkeeper following his injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Esteban_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2011, Alvarado was accused by his girlfriend, Marcela Juárez Castillo, of alleged domestic violence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> However, two months later, charges against Alvarado were later dropped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>While growing up in Costa Rica, Alvarado made good friends with <a href="/wiki/Joel_Campbell" title="Joel Campbell">Joel Campbell</a>, having played together in childhood. Alvarado also revealed that he played as striker before switching position to become goalkeeper at fourteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
