<p>Founded in 1938, the <b>Estonian Academy of Sciences</b> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et" xml:lang="et"><i><b>Eesti Teaduste Akadeemia</b></i></span>) is <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/National_academy" title="National academy">national academy</a> of science in Tallinn. As with other national academies, it is an independent group of well-known scientists whose stated aim is to promote research and development, encourage international scientific cooperation, and disseminate knowledge to the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-a-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As of December 2012, it had 75 full members and 15 foreign members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Since November 2004, the president of the Academy is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Villems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Richard Villems (page does not exist)">Richard Villems</a>, a biologist from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tartu" title="University of Tartu">University of Tartu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-h_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-h-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy has four divisions:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Division of Astronomy and Physics (Estonian: <i>Astronoomia ja füüsika osakond)</i></li>
<li>Division of Informatics and Engineering (Estonian: <i>Informaatika ja tehnikateaduste osakond</i>)</li>
<li>Division of Biology, Geology and Chemistry (Estonian: <i>Bioloogia, geoloogia ja keemia osakond</i>)</li>
<li>Division of the Humanities and Social Sciences (Estonian: <i>Humanitaar- ja sotsiaalteaduste osakond</i>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy was established in 1938 as a learned society. When Estonia was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> the Academy was dissolved on July 17, 1940. In June 1945 it was reestablished as the <i>Academy of Sciences of the Estonian SSR</i> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et" xml:lang="et"><i>Eesti NSV Teaduste Akadeemia</i></span>). In Soviet times, it consisted of a central library and four divisions containing 15 research institutes as well as other scientific societies and museums. In April 1989, shortly before Estonian independence, the academy regained its original name of <i>Estonian Academy of Sciences</i>. At this time it was also restructured into its present form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-h_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-h-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hestonian_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-hestonian-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes">Prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy's most prestigious prize is the <i>Medal of the Estonian Academy of Sciences</i>. This is awarded "for outstanding services in development of Estonian science or in helping forward its development, as well as for services in performance of tasks of the Estonian Academy of Sciences."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy is located on Kohtu Street in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>. Its building is the so-called palace of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Von_Ungern-Sternberg" title="Von Ungern-Sternberg">Ungern-Sternberg</a>, built in 1865 by the architect <a href="/wiki/Martin_Gropius" title="Martin Gropius">Martin Gropius</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
