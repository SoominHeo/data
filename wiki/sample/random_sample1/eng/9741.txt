<p>The <b>Conservative People's Party of Estonia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et" xml:lang="et"><i>Eesti Konservatiivne Rahvaerakond</i></span>, <b>EKRE</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/National_conservatism" title="National conservatism">national conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>. Current chairman of the party is <a href="/wiki/Mart_Helme" title="Mart Helme">Mart Helme</a>, former Estonian ambassador to <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/Identitarian_movement" title="Identitarian movement">identitarian</a> youth wing, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Awakening" title="Blue Awakening">Blue Awakening</a> organizes frequent nationalist torchlight marches in Estonia. Ideologically, the party is a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_nationalism" title="Estonian nationalism">Estonian ethno-nationalist</a> camp, which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/Singing_Revolution" title="Singing Revolution">Singing Revolution</a>. Many political commentators place the EKRE on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-wing" title="Right-wing">right</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Far_right" title="Far right">far right</a> but party representatives reject this and suggest other ways of looking at the <a href="/wiki/Left%E2%80%93right_politics" title="Left–right politics">left–right axis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postimees_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-postimees-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party was founded in March 2012 when the agrarian centrist party <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Union_of_Estonia" title="People's Union of Estonia">People's Union of Estonia</a> and the nationalist pressure group <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Patriotic_Movement" title="Estonian Patriotic Movement">Estonian Patriotic Movement</a> merged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-balticworlds_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-balticworlds-4">[4]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_parliamentary_election,_2015" title="Estonian parliamentary election, 2015">parliamentary election of 2015</a>, it secured 8.1% of the votes and entered the <a href="/wiki/Riigikogu" title="Riigikogu">Riigikogu</a> with 7 seats. At the same time it had around 7600 members, being the fourth largest Estonian party by membership size.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-register_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-register-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party sees the survival of <a href="/wiki/Estonians" title="Estonians">Estonian ethnicity</a> as its main objective, and many of its policies are directed towards providing support to young Estonian families, lowering emigration of Estonians and preventing immigration from outside of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tiido_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-tiido-6">[6]</a></sup> It is also <a href="/wiki/Euroscepticism" title="Euroscepticism">eurosceptic</a> and wishes to implement <a href="/wiki/Popular_initiative_(Switzerland)" title="Popular initiative (Switzerland)">Swiss-style</a> <a href="/wiki/Direct_democracy" title="Direct democracy">direct democracy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Union_of_Estonia" title="People's Union of Estonia">People's Union of Estonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Patriotic_Movement" title="Estonian Patriotic Movement">Estonian Patriotic Movement</a></div>
<p>The party is a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_nationalism" title="Estonian nationalism">Estonian ethno-nationalist</a> camp, which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/Singing_Revolution" title="Singing Revolution">Singing Revolution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Patriotic_Movement" title="Estonian Patriotic Movement">Estonian Patriotic Movement</a> was founded in 2006. From 2008, the movement was led by Martin Helme, son of <a href="/wiki/Mart_Helme" title="Mart Helme">Mart Helme</a>. Mart Helme had formerly been a member of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Union_of_Estonia" title="People's Union of Estonia">People's Union of Estonia</a> but was forced to leave the party in 2004 because of his opposition to the relocation of <a href="/wiki/Monument_of_Lihula" title="Monument of Lihula">Monument of Lihula</a> and the suppression of protests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party was founded in March 2012 when the agrarian centrist party <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Union_of_Estonia" title="People's Union of Estonia">People's Union of Estonia</a> and the nationalist pressure group <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Patriotic_Movement" title="Estonian Patriotic Movement">Estonian Patriotic Movement</a> merged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-balticworlds_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-balticworlds-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2010, the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Union_of_Estonia" title="People's Union of Estonia">People's Union of Estonia</a> started to look for a possible merging partner. Talks with the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_(Estonia)" title="Social Democratic Party (Estonia)">Social Democratic Party</a> were concluded and a special congress was convened to approve the merger agreement. However, at the party congress, only 172 delegates out of 412 supported the deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Following the failed merger attempt, many leading members (MPs) left the party and joined the Social Democrats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_parliamentary_election,_2011" title="Estonian parliamentary election, 2011">2011 parliamentary election</a>, the People's Union did not pass the 5% threshold.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mart_Helme" title="Mart Helme">Mart Helme</a> and several members of his nationalist circle run at the election as independent candidates, none of whom passed the threshold necessary for an independent to enter the parliament. After the election, the leader of the People's Union, Margo Miljand, met with Helme. To save the party, Helme advised him to change the party name and alter the program. With links to the nationalist <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Patriotic_Movement" title="Estonian Patriotic Movement">Estonian Patriotic Movement</a>, Helme advised a deal between the two. The movement promised it would help redo the party program and elect new leaders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postimees_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-postimees-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2012, the Estonian Patriotic Movement merged into the People's Union and the latter changed its name to the Conservative People's Party of Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peoplesunion_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-peoplesunion-16">[16]</a></sup> At the assembly in <a href="/wiki/P%C3%B5ltsamaa" title="Põltsamaa">Põltsamaa</a>, where the party was founded, EKRE made its first political statement: "No political party in the <a href="/wiki/Riigikogu" title="Riigikogu">Riigikogu</a> represents the Estonian people, our national interest or traditional values. The government acts on right- and left liberal, also socialist ideas that our countrymen are simply statistical units or taxpayers, consumers at best. It is not far right or far left, just ultra-liberalism. The Conservative People's Party gives a solution to the voters who are sick of forced choice between <a href="/wiki/Andrus_Ansip" title="Andrus Ansip">Ansip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Savisaar" title="Edgar Savisaar">Savisaar</a>, East and West, left and right."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> For the first three months, EKRE’s support according to the polls was zero, than it begun to gradually rise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postimees_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-postimees-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>EKRE takes part of yearly gatherings of the veterans of the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Legion" title="Estonian Legion">Estonian Legion</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Sinim%C3%A4ed_Hills" title="Sinimäed Hills">Sinimäed Hills</a>. In 2013, the attendance of <a href="/wiki/Mart_Helme" title="Mart Helme">Mart Helme</a> was praised in Estonian media while the abstention of other parties' leaders was frown upon and seen as a result of Russian anti-fascist propaganda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the local elections in October 2013, the party gained representation in several smaller municipalities, such as the parishes of <a href="/wiki/Tudulinna_Parish" title="Tudulinna Parish">Tudulinna</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4%C3%A4demeeste_Parish" title="Häädemeeste Parish">Häädemeeste</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> A member of the party also became mayor of the town of <a href="/wiki/Saue" title="Saue">Saue</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> however he was set up independently of EKRE in a local party’s list.</p>
<p>The first party since the 1990s to politically organize Estonian diaspora, EKRE founded its Finland branch in October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the run-up to the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_parliamentary_election,_2015" title="Estonian parliamentary election, 2015">2015 parliamentary election</a>, EKRE managed to draw supporters from the mainstream right, including defectors mainly from the <a href="/wiki/Pro_Patria_and_Res_Publica_Union" title="Pro Patria and Res Publica Union">IRL</a>, but also from the liberal <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Reform_Party" title="Estonian Reform Party">Reform Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Free_Party" title="Estonian Free Party">Free Party</a>'s initiative group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In the election, EKRE won 8.1% of the vote and 7 seats in the <a href="/wiki/Riigikogu" title="Riigikogu">Riigikogu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Soon after, the winning Reform Party excluded EKRE from the coalition talks, citing as a reason a blog post by EKRE's MP <a href="/wiki/Jaak_Madison" title="Jaak Madison">Jaak Madison</a>, written in January 2012. In the post, Madison commended the economic policies of the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi Party</a> and wrote: "There is unfortunately no perfect form of government (not even democracy) but I see <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">fascism</a> as being an ideology that consists of many positive nuances necessary for preserving the nation-state."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Madison is now the vice chairman of the European Union Affairs Committee of the parliament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/President_of_Estonia" title="President of Estonia">president of Estonia</a> <a href="/wiki/Arnold_R%C3%BC%C3%BCtel" title="Arnold Rüütel">Arnold Rüütel</a> voiced his support for Mart Helme in the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_presidential_election,_2016" title="Estonian presidential election, 2016">Estonian presidential election, 2016</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology_and_political_positions">Ideology and political positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology and political positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>EKRE describes itself as "a principled and bravely patriotic Estonian party with an unshakable mission to protect Estonian national values and interests".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erakond_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-erakond-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Estonian Conservative People’s Party program states that it is founded on the continuity of the Republic of Estonia and its <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Estonia" title="Constitution of Estonia">Constitution</a>, and it unites people who fight for the nation state, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_cohesion" title="Social cohesion">social cohesion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic principles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riigikogu.ee_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-riigikogu.ee-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The activities of the Conservative People’s Party are based on three fundamental values:</p>
<ul>
<li>Endurance of the Estonian values, based on support for the language, culture, education, family, traditions and national economy</li>
<li>Participation society of equal opportunities, where open, honest and democratic governance allows all citizens to reach fulfilment and get involved in politics</li>
<li>Socially and regionally balanced development and wellbeing that are guaranteed by a fair and strong state by implementing caring and knowledge-based policies and by developing an ecologically sustainable living environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riigikogu.ee_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-riigikogu.ee-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>It has also been labelled "far-right" by Kari Käsper, the Executive Director of Estonian Human Rights Centre,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and in foreign media by <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News">BBC News</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_Christian_Science_Monitor" title="The Christian Science Monitor">the Christian Science Monitor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_News_Channel" title="Fox News Channel">Fox News Channel</a>, EKRE is a far-right party, "considered by some to have Fascist-Neo-Nazi sympathies similar to many other flourishing nationalist parties in the Baltics and Eastern Europe".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Simon_Wiesenthal_Center" title="Simon Wiesenthal Center">Simon Wiesenthal Center</a> has called EKRE youth organization's annual torchlight procession an "extreme right march".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Martin Helme, board member and the party leader's son, has said that the accusations of extremism simply reflect the unfamiliarity and discomfort of the ruling class and media with the new political rhetoric of EKRE: "The mainstream has become so orthodox, so narrow, that whatever is not immaculately, diligently, fervently more-catholic-than-pope mainstream is immediately labelled extremism."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postimees_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-postimees-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The programme of EKRE states that the citizens must actively guard against the external as well as the internal enemy in order to secure the Estonian nation, the survival of its independence and its status as an ethno-state. It also states as its objectives the creation of the environment needed for the survival of the Estonian language and culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tiido_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-tiido-6">[6]</a></sup> The party calls for implementation of <a href="/wiki/Direct_democracy" title="Direct democracy">direct democracy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-objektiiv_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-objektiiv-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Balanced_budget" title="Balanced budget">balanced state budget</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup> and strict control over immigration to Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_policies">Social policies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Social policies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_health_care">Education and health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education and health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The party strongly opposes the widespread closure of schools in countryside. Its program requires the teachers to speak high-level Estonian and be loyal to the Estonian state. To raise the quality of education, EKRE desires to raise the wages of teachers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the program of the party, the importance of health care is linked to the preservation of the Estonian nation. The party stands for free dental treatment,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onaeg_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-onaeg-36">[36]</a></sup> wants to limit the availability of tobacco, alcohol and narcotics, and does not support abortion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography_and_immigration">Demography and immigration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demography and immigration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>According to EKRE, demography is one of the most crucial aspects in the survival of Estonian state. According to Mart Helme, Estonia is in a "demographic crisis", characterized by low birth rate and emigration of more than 100,000 Estonians in recent years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> To counter the falling birth rate, the party has proposed family welfare programs such as paying back a quarter of a married couple's <a href="/wiki/Mortgage_loan" title="Mortgage loan">mortgage loan</a> with every child's birth and lowering a parent's income tax by 5% rate for every child being raised in the family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onaeg_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-onaeg-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>EKRE has actively stood against <a href="/wiki/European_migrant_crisis" title="European migrant crisis">immigration from the Middle East and Africa</a>, especially regarding the quota system proposed by EU Commission in 2015 to resettle the immigrants to all EU member states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tiido_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-tiido-6">[6]</a></sup> Citing a large number of Russians already imported during the Soviet occupation, the party has repeatedly ruled out supporting any further mass immigration into Estonia. The party upholds that the Estonian migration policies must advance the aim of "expanding the amount and precentage of Estonians in Estonia" and if the liberal government allows immigration to "alter the ethnic makeup of Estonia", it is "scandalous and undemocratic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Commenting riots in socially segregated suburbs in Sweden, Martin Helme, board member and the party leader's son said in a TV talk show in May 2013: "Estonia shouldn't allow things to go as far as in England, France and Sweden. Our immigration policy should have one simple rule: if you're black, go back. As simple as that. We shouldn't allow this problem to emerge in the first place."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Same-sex_unions">Same-sex unions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Same-sex unions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The party strictly opposed the civil partnership law on registered partnership for same-sex couples that was adopted by <a href="/wiki/Riigikogu" title="Riigikogu">Riigikogu</a> in October 2014. Pointing out that the law grants adoption rights to homosexual couples, the party claimed it essentially establishes same-sex marriage. Instead, the party proposes laws that would help to raise the birth rate and strengthen the societal attitudes towards having children, including the need to strengthen the traditional family model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>EKRE also claims that pushing through the law while opinion polls showed that the majority of Estonian people opposed it, was undemocratic. The party platform proposes a referendum on the civil partnership law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onaeg_36-2"><a href="#cite_note-onaeg-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Direct_democracy">Direct democracy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Direct democracy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>EKRE views Estonia's form of government as heavily biased towards <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">representative democracy</a>, without means for the people to have an influence on politics other than elections. To change that, the party wants to return to more traditional ways of <a href="/wiki/Direct_democracy" title="Direct democracy">direct democracy</a>, such as provided by the earlier <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Estonia" title="Constitution of Estonia">Estonian constitutions</a> of 1920 and 1934.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-otsedemokraatia_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-otsedemokraatia-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party program includes support of the right of citizens to create <a href="/wiki/Initiative" title="Initiative">initiatives</a> if at least 25,000 registered voters sign a petition to put a bill on referendum. EKRE supports public presidential elections, <a href="/wiki/Recall_election" title="Recall election">recall elections</a> and public elections of judges, prosecutors and local police prefects. The party wants to abolish <a href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_method" title="D'Hondt method">D'Hondt method</a> from the parliamentary elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-otsedemokraatia_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-otsedemokraatia-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_policies">Economic policies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economic policies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party program states that the development of the market must serve the national interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-4"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup> The party calls for creation of a national <a href="/wiki/Public_bank" title="Public bank">public bank</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> It has also supported <a href="/wiki/Cooperative_banking" title="Cooperative banking">cooperative banking</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tartu" title="Tartu">Tartu</a>, including in its program the establishment of an alternative interest-free currency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>EKRE has voiced criticism over the exexution of the <a href="/wiki/Rail_Baltica" title="Rail Baltica">Rail Baltica</a> project. According to the party, Rail Baltica could potentially bring great benefit to the economy of Estonia, but the current project has a questionable impact on the environment and local communities, as well as a doubtful economic viability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In 2016, both Mart Helme and Henn Põlluaas proposed that the current project should be replaced with a <a href="/wiki/Vactrain" title="Vactrain">vactrain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hyperloop" title="Hyperloop">Hyperloop</a> connecting Estonia to Central Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to EKRE, the "untouched natural beauty" of Estonia must be preserved more effectively. Therefore, the party supports alternative and environment-friendly sources of energy. EKRE wants to intensify the fight against littering and says the offenders must be punished at least with a sum necessary to compensate for the harm created to nature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-5"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup> The party's program includes a ban on all <a href="/wiki/Genetically_modified_food" title="Genetically modified food">genetically modified foods</a> and their import.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-6"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_and_defence_policies">Foreign and defence policies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Foreign and defence policies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Baltic_states_borders.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="593" data-file-width="721" height="181" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Baltic_states_borders.jpg/220px-Baltic_states_borders.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Baltic_states_borders.jpg/330px-Baltic_states_borders.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Baltic_states_borders.jpg/440px-Baltic_states_borders.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Baltic_states_borders.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dark green territory is, according to EKRE, illegally occupied by Russia</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Conservative People's Party of Estonia often calls to protect Estonia's independence and sovereignty from <a href="/wiki/Supranational_union" title="Supranational union">supranational unions</a>. They claim that the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> is moving towards a federal state and Estonia should veto any legislation that centralises more power to the hands of EU.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> On 30 August 2012, EKRE organised a protest at <a href="/wiki/Toompea" title="Toompea">Toompea</a> against the ratification of <a href="/wiki/European_Stability_Mechanism" title="European Stability Mechanism">European Stability Mechanism</a> treaty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the party does not advocate Estonia's exit from the EU, it believes that the union has to overgo a drastic change. According to EKRE, the EU has to become a military alliance that would defend all European ethnicities based on the concept of <a href="/wiki/Ethnopluralism" title="Ethnopluralism">ethnopluralism</a>. EKRE wants to strongly limit the amount of bureaucracy in the EU.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>EKRE strongly opposes a proposed border treaty between Estonia and Russia, which, according to the party, would cede 5.2% of Estonia's territory to the occupier once and for all without any compromise or compensation on its side. The party has called the possible signing of the treaty "treason" and the Estonian politicians who would sign it "traitors to the state".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party opposes the <a href="/wiki/Accession_of_Turkey_to_the_European_Union" title="Accession of Turkey to the European Union">accession of Turkey to the European Union</a> and has called for reconsidering Turkey's membership in NATO. After the <a href="/wiki/2016_Turkish_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt" title="2016 Turkish coup d'état attempt">2016 Turkish coup d'état attempt</a>, EKRE expressed regret over its defeat and called Turkey "no more democratic country than Russia".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>The EKRE proposes a national defence policy based on self-reliance of Estonia. The security of the country would be safeguarded by the existence of initial defence, compulsory military service, total defence, international cooperation and the membership of <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-programm_35-7"><a href="#cite_note-programm-35">[35]</a></sup> The platform of the party includes formation of two tank battalions and acquisition of medium-range anti-aircraft systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onaeg_36-3"><a href="#cite_note-onaeg-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Martin Helme has expressed sympathy for American presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump_presidential_campaign,_2016" title="Donald Trump presidential campaign, 2016">Donald Trump</a> and his alt-right supporters. According to Helme, Trump is right to point out that the Baltic countries need to make a financial contribution in exchange for the military alliance with the United States. "He can see no reason why the United States should make the national defense of other countries its duty. And I believe this question is completely justified. It's namely our party that has been saying for a long time that making initial independent defense capability strong is the most important thing, allies being the next component," said Helme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_relations">International relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>EKRE has close links with similar right-wing nationalist parties in Latvia and Lithuania. On 23 August 2013, EKRE signed the Declaration of Bauska together with political parties <a href="/wiki/National_Alliance_(Latvia)" title="National Alliance (Latvia)">National Alliance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_Nationalist_Union" title="Lithuanian Nationalist Union">Lithuanian Nationalist Union</a>. The declaration calls for a new national awakening of the Baltic states and warns about threats posed by international <a href="/wiki/Globalism" title="Globalism">globalism</a>, multiculturalism and Russian imperial ambitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, the party's congress in Tallinn was visited by a delegation from the <a href="/wiki/UK_Independence_Party" title="UK Independence Party">UK Independence Party</a>. The delegation was led by <a href="/wiki/Roger_Helmer" title="Roger Helmer">Roger Helmer</a> who gave a speech in support of Euroscepticism in Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Organizations with whom EKRE cooperates take regularly part of the annual torchlight march in Tallinn. They include all signatories of the Declaration of Bauska and the Scandinavian nationalist youth movement Nordisk Ungdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party has also contacts among Ukrainian nationalists. During <a href="/wiki/Euromaidan" title="Euromaidan">Euromaidan</a>, Mart Helme sent an address to the protestors in Kiev, urging the Ukrainian patriots not to succumb to Russian demands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Blue_Awakening">Blue Awakening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservative_People%27s_Party_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Blue Awakening">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party's affiliated nationalist youth movement is named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Awakening" title="Blue Awakening">Blue Awakening</a>. With links to the pan-European <a href="/wiki/Identitarian_movement" title="Identitarian movement">Identitarian movement</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Alt-right" title="Alt-right">alt-right</a> in North America, Blue Awakening states "a new national awakening of the Estonians" as its principal aim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegram_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegram-61">[61]</a></sup> This would come as a continuation to the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_national_awakening" title="Estonian national awakening">19th century Estonian national awakening</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Singing_Revolution" title="Singing Revolution">Singing Revolution</a>, leading the way to the establishment of "an eternal Estonian ethno-state".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UU_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-UU-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blue Awakening was founded on 30 November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UU_62-1"><a href="#cite_note-UU-62">[62]</a></sup> The young activists have been behind many of the party's protest marches. In addition to politics, the youth of Blue Awakening focus on art, music and right-wing philosophy such as the <a href="/wiki/Traditionalist_School" title="Traditionalist School">Traditionalist School</a> of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Evola" title="Julius Evola">Julius Evola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Gu%C3%A9non" title="René Guénon">René Guénon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sinised_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-sinised-63">[63]</a></sup> The movement is disciplined and its activists have formed several squads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Their activities include rituals shaped after Finno-Ugric shamanism,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UU_62-2"><a href="#cite_note-UU-62">[62]</a></sup> including celebrations of sunrise on ancient <a href="/wiki/Tumulus" title="Tumulus">tumuli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>Blue Awakening is the main organizer of the annual torchlight march through <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a> on February 24, <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Estonia)" title="Independence Day (Estonia)">Independence Day</a> of Estonia. The first Independence Day torchlight march was held in 2014. According to Blue Awakening, the torchlight march is meant to honor those who have fallen for the nation of Estonia and to signify that Estonian youth have not abandoned the nationalist principles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
