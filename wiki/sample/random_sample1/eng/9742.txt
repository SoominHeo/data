<p>The <b>Estonian Free Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>Eesti Vabaerakond</i></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonian</a> <a href="/wiki/Centre-right_politics" title="Centre-right politics">centre-right</a> political party founded in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The chairman of the party is <a href="/wiki/Andres_Herkel" title="Andres Herkel">Andres Herkel</a>. The party gained 8 seats after passing the 5-percent threshold in the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_parliamentary_election,_2015" title="Estonian parliamentary election, 2015">2015 Estonian parliamentary elections</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Free_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">nonprofit</a> Estonian Free Party Founding Group was founded in January 2014. Originally it was to be founded by two organizations, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Free_Patriotic_Citizen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Free Patriotic Citizen (page does not exist)">Free Patriotic Citizen</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Better_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Better Estonia (page does not exist)">Better Estonia</a>, but the latter decided to withdraw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Estonian Free Party was officially established on 20 September 2014, and Andres Herkel was elected as chairman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The party attracted numerous public figures, including former head of the <a href="/wiki/Estonia_Defence_Forces" title="Estonia Defence Forces">Defense Forces</a> Admiral <a href="/wiki/Tarmo_K%C3%B5uts" title="Tarmo Kõuts">Tarmo Kõuts</a>, actor <a href="/wiki/Ain_Lutsepp" title="Ain Lutsepp">Ain Lutsepp</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%BCri_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jüri Adams (page does not exist)">Jüri Adams</a>, a former government minister who was one of the main writers of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Estonia" title="Constitution of Estonia">Constitution of Estonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_parliamentary_election,_2015" title="Estonian parliamentary election, 2015">2015 Estonian parliamentary elections</a>, the Estonian Free Party received 49,883 votes, constituting 8.7 percent of the total, which garnered it 8 seats in the 101-member parliament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Estonian Free Party's candidate for <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Estonia" title="Prime Minister of Estonia">prime minister</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Artur_Talvik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Artur Talvik (page does not exist)">Artur Talvik</a>, who received the most votes of all the Estonian Free Party candidates, was not a member of the party himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soonvald_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-soonvald-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Estonian Free Party participated in coalition talks together with the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Reform_Party" title="Estonian Reform Party">Estonian Reform Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_(Estonia)" title="Social Democratic Party (Estonia)">Social Democratic Party</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pro_Patria_and_Res_Publica_Union" title="Pro Patria and Res Publica Union">Pro Patria and Res Publica Union</a>, but were left out of the coalition because their proposals to change the tax system were considered unacceptable. The Estonian Reform Party's unwillingness to reduce the state funding for political parties was cited as an issue by the Estonian Free Party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tambur_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-tambur-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_positions">Political positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estonian_Free_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Commentators have argued, that the Estonian Free Party lacks a clear ideology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It has been characterized as <a href="/wiki/Centre-right_politics" title="Centre-right politics">centre-right</a>, while chairman Andres Herkel described the party's ideology as right-wing liberal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party advocates raising the personal <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" title="Income tax">income tax</a>, while on the other hand also raising the non-taxable basic exemption to support the poorer majority of taxpayers. The party's program also includes increased taxes on alcohol, tobacco, and confectionery. In economic policy, the party supports a relatively liberal approach, especially to support small businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The party has advocated reduction of state funding of political parties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tambur_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-tambur-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Estonian Free Party has declared its opposition to the <a href="/wiki/Recognition_of_same-sex_unions_in_Estonia" title="Recognition of same-sex unions in Estonia">law recognising same-sex unions in Estonia</a>, claiming that in its current form the law does not solve any problems. The party claims that the law needs to be revised and possibly also split into multiple, separate laws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The party opposes compulsory <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a>-wide migrant quotas in regard to <a href="/wiki/European_migrant_crisis" title="European migrant crisis">European migrant crisis</a>, and insists that the number of refugees to be accepted needs to be determined through negotiations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
