<p><i><b>Mu isamaa, mu õnn ja rõõm</b></i> ("My Fatherland, My Happiness and Joy"; <small>Estonian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Estonian_and_Finnish" title="Help:IPA for Estonian and Finnish">[mu ˈisɑmɑː mu ˈɤnʲ jɑ ˈrɤːm]</a></span>) was officially adopted as the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>(riigi)hümn</i></span>) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Estonia" title="Republic of Estonia">Republic of Estonia</a> in 1920, and again in 1991.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lyrics" title="Lyrics">lyrics</a> were written by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Voldemar_Jannsen" title="Johann Voldemar Jannsen">Johann Voldemar Jannsen</a> and are set to a melody composed in 1848 by <a href="/wiki/Fredrik_Pacius" title="Fredrik Pacius">Fredrik (Friedrich) Pacius</a> which is also that of the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> of <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>: <i><a href="/wiki/Maamme" title="Maamme">Maamme</a></i> ("Vårt Land" in Swedish).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is also considered to be national anthem for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Livonian_people" title="Livonian people">Livonian people</a> with text <i><a href="/wiki/Min_iz%C4%81m%C5%8D" title="Min izāmō">Min izāmō, min sindimō</a></i>, <i>My Fatherland, my native land</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_isamaa,_mu_%C3%B5nn_ja_r%C3%B5%C3%B5m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was first presented to the public as a choral work in the Grand Song Festival of Estonia in 1869 and quickly became a symbol of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estonian_National_Awakening" title="Estonian National Awakening">Estonian National Awakening</a>.</p>
<p><i>Mu isamaa, mu õnn ja rõõm</i> was officially adopted as the national anthem of Estonia in 1920, after the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_War_of_Independence" title="Estonian War of Independence">Estonian War of Independence</a>. In contrast, Finland never passed a similar legislation about <i>Maamme</i>, although it is considered a de facto Finnish anthem.</p>
<p>During the Soviet occupation since 1944, <i>Mu isamaa, mu õnn ja rõõm</i> was banned. Between 1945 and 1990 the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic">Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthem_of_Estonian_SSR" title="Anthem of Estonian SSR">different anthem</a>. Yet, the people of Estonia could often hear the melody, as Finland's state broadcaster <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yleisradio" title="Yleisradio">Yleisradio</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> broadcasts were received in Northern Estonia, played an instrumental version of the <a href="/wiki/Maamme" title="Maamme">Finnish national anthem</a>, identical to this song (except for an additional repetition of the last verse in the Finnish version), at closedown every night.</p>

<p>My fatherland, my joy and happiness,<br/>
How beautiful you are!<br/>
I shall not find such ever<br/>
In this huge wide world<br/>
Which would be so dear to me<br/>
As you, my fatherland!</p>
<p>You have given me birth<br/>
And raised me up;<br/>
I shall thank you always<br/>
And remain faithful to you 'til death,<br/>
To me most beloved are you,<br/>
My precious fatherland!</p>
<p>May God watch over you,<br/>
My precious fatherland!<br/>

My precious fatherland!</p>

<p>My native land, my joy and delight,<br/>
How fair thou art and bright!<br/>
And nowhere in the world all round<br/>
Can ever such a place be found<br/>
So well beloved as I love thee,<br/>
My native country dear!</p>
<p>My little cradle stood on ground soil,<br/>
Whose blessings ease my toil.<br/>
With my last breath my thanks to thee,<br/>
For true to death I'll ever be,<br/>
O worthy, most beloved and fine,<br/>
Thou, dearest country mine!</p>
<p>May God in Heaven thee gave birth to me,<br/>
My best, my dearest land!<br/>
May He<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Genderless_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Genderless-2">[Note 1]</a></sup> be guard, may He be shield,<br/>
