<p><b>Espiritu Santo</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is the largest island in the nation of <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>, with an area of 3,955.5 km<sup>2</sup> (1,527.2 sq mi) and a population of around 40,000 according to the 2009 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_Hebrides" title="New Hebrides">New Hebrides</a> in the Pacific region of <a href="/wiki/Melanesia" title="Melanesia">Melanesia</a>. It is in the <a href="/wiki/Sanma_Province" title="Sanma Province">Sanma Province</a> of Vanuatu. The town of <a href="/wiki/Luganville" title="Luganville">Luganville</a>, on Espiritu Santo's southeast coast, is Vanuatu's second-largest settlement and the provincial capital. Roads run north and west from Luganville but most of the island is far from the limited road network.</p>
<p>Vanuatu's highest peak is the 1879 metre (6165 foot) <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tabwemasana" title="Mount Tabwemasana">Mount Tabwemasana</a> in west-central Espiritu Santo.</p>
<p>In 1998, Santo hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melanesia_Cup_1998" title="Melanesia Cup 1998">Melanesia Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Espiritu_Santo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Champagne_Beach.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Champagne_Beach.jpg/200px-Champagne_Beach.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Champagne_Beach.jpg/300px-Champagne_Beach.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Champagne_Beach.jpg/400px-Champagne_Beach.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Spanish_East_Indies" title="Spanish East Indies">Spanish</a> expedition led by <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> explorer <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Fernandes_de_Queir%C3%B3s" title="Pedro Fernandes de Queirós">Pedro Fernandes de Queirós</a>, established a settlement in 1606 at Big Bay on the north side of the island. Espiritu Santo takes its name from Queirós, who named the entire island group <i>La Austrialia [</i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a><i>]<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> del Espíritu Santo</i> in acknowledgment of the Spanish king's descent from the royal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hapsburg_monarchy" title="Hapsburg monarchy">House of Austria</a>, and believing he had arrived in the Great Southern Continent, <a href="/wiki/Terra_Australis" title="Terra Australis">Terra Australis</a>. During the time of the British–French <a href="/wiki/Condominium_(international_law)" title="Condominium (international law)">Condominium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hog_Harbour" title="Hog Harbour">Hog Harbour</a>, on the northeast coast, was the site of the British district administration, while <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Segond,_Vanuatu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Segond, Vanuatu (page does not exist)">Segond</a>, near Luganville was the French district administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, particularly after the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor</a>, the island was used by Allied forces as a military supply and support base, naval harbor, and airfield. In highly fictionalized form, it is the locale of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Michener" title="James Michener">James Michener</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_of_the_South_Pacific" title="Tales of the South Pacific">Tales of the South Pacific</a></i> and the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Rodgers_and_Hammerstein" title="Rodgers and Hammerstein">Rodgers and Hammerstein</a> musical, <i><a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_(musical)" title="South Pacific (musical)">South Pacific</a></i>. The presence of the Allies later contributed to the island's diving tourism, as the United States dumped most of their equipment and refuse at what is now known as 'Million Dollar Point'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Another wreck off Espiritu Santo, the <a href="/wiki/SS_President_Coolidge" title="SS President Coolidge">SS <i>President Coolidge</i></a>, is also a popular diving spot. The SS <i>President Coolidge</i> was a converted luxury liner that hit a mine during the war.</p>
<p>Between May and August 1980 the island was the site of <a href="/wiki/Coconut_War" title="Coconut War">a rebellion</a> during the transfer of power over the colonial <a href="/wiki/New_Hebrides" title="New Hebrides">New Hebrides</a> from the condominium to independent Vanuatu. <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Stevens" title="Jimmy Stevens">Jimmy Stevens</a>' <a href="/wiki/Nagriamel" title="Nagriamel">Nagriamel</a> movement, in alliance with private French interests and backed by the Phoenix Foundation and American libertarians hoping to establish a tax-free haven, declared the island of Espiritu Santo independent of the new government. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Vemerana" title="Republic of Vemerana">Republic of Vemerana</a> was proclaimed on May 28. France recognized the independence on June 3. On June 5 the tribal chiefs of Santo named the French Ambassador Philippe Allonneau "King of Vemerana", Jimmy Stevens became Prime Minister. Luganville is renamed Allonneaupolis. But negotiations with Port-Vila failed and from July 27 to August 18, British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Marines" title="Royal Marines">Royal Marines</a> and a unit of the French <i><a href="/wiki/Garde_Mobile" title="Garde Mobile">Garde Mobile</a></i> were deployed to the Vanuatu's capital island but did not invade Espiritu Santo as the soon-to-be government had hoped. The troops were recalled shortly before independence. Following independence, Vanuatu, now governed by Father <a href="/wiki/Walter_Lini" title="Walter Lini">Walter Lini</a>, requested assistance from <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>, whose forces invaded and conquered Espiritu Santo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Espiritu_Santo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:BestPoint.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2816" data-file-width="2120" height="292" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/BestPoint.JPG/220px-BestPoint.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/BestPoint.JPG/330px-BestPoint.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/BestPoint.JPG/440px-BestPoint.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Santo, with many wrecks and reefs to be explored, is a very popular tourist destination for divers. Champagne Beach draws tourists with its pink sand and clear waters. The "Western Side" of the island contains many caves which may be explored, and cruise ships often stop in at <a href="/wiki/Luganville" title="Luganville">Luganville</a>.</p>
<p>The local people make their living by supporting the tourist trade, by cash-crop farming, mostly <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a> but some <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_bean" title="Cocoa bean">cacao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kava" title="Kava">kava</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanuts</a>, or by subsistence farming and fishing.</p>
<p>Most Santo people are Christians. The largest church groups on the island are the <a href="/wiki/Presbyterian_Church_of_Vanuatu" title="Presbyterian Church of Vanuatu">Presbyterian Church of Vanuatu</a>, the Roman Catholic Church and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Church_of_Melanesia" title="Church of Melanesia">Church of Melanesia</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a>). Also active are the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Church_(denomination)" title="Apostolic Church (denomination)">Apostolic Church</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Christ" title="Church of Christ">Church of Christ</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-day Adventist Church</a>, and others. However, in many villages, particularly in Big Bay and South Santo, the people are "<a href="/wiki/Paganism" title="Paganism">heathen</a>", a term that in Vanuatu has no pejorative connotation — it simply denotes someone who has not embraced Christianity. <a href="/wiki/Mores" title="Mores">Customary</a> beliefs of a more modern sort are found among followers of the <a href="/wiki/Nagriamel" title="Nagriamel">Nagriamel</a> movement based in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fanafo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fanafo (page does not exist)">Fanafo</a>.</p>
<p>For almost all Santo's people custom plays a large part in their lives, regardless of their religion. The chief system continues strongly in most areas.</p>
<p>The people of Santo face some health problems, especially <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a> . Although there is a hospital, most locals consult either their own <a href="/wiki/Witch_doctor" title="Witch doctor">witch doctor</a> or medical clinics set up by western missionaries. <a href="/wiki/Kava" title="Kava">Kava</a> is the popular drug of the island, although alcohol is becoming more prevalent. With the rising number of adults using alcohol, there is a rising crime rate, especially involving violence toward women, and tribal warfare.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_infrastructure">Economy and infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Espiritu_Santo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy and infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Luganville" title="Luganville">Luganville</a> is the only true town on the island; the rest of the island is dotted with small villages. From <a href="/wiki/Luganville" title="Luganville">Luganville</a>, three ‘main’ roads emerge. Main Street leaves the town to the west and winds along the south coast of the island for about 40 km ending at the village of Tasiriki on the southwest coast. Canal Road runs along the south and east coast of the island, north through Hog Harbor and Golden Beach, ending at Port Olry. Big Bay Highway splits off from Canal Road near Turtle Bay on the east coast, runs generally west to the mountains, and then leads north to Big Bay. The <a href="/wiki/Santo-Pekoa_International_Airport" title="Santo-Pekoa International Airport">airport</a> is about five km east from the center of <a href="/wiki/Luganville" title="Luganville">Luganville</a>. Numerous rivers run to the coastline from the mountains of the island. The <a href="/wiki/Sarakata_River" title="Sarakata River">Sarakata River</a> is one of the largest and runs through <a href="/wiki/Luganville" title="Luganville">Luganville</a>.</p>
<p>Many people on Espiritu Santo still rely on subsistence farming for their food. The villages on the island are mostly self-sufficient with their own vegetable gardens, chickens and pigs. Taro and yam are commonly grown in these gardens, and are mainstays of the local diet.</p>
<p>Santo is home to a number of cattle farms (including the famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milai_Vanuatu_Limited" title="Milai Vanuatu Limited">Belmol Cattle Project</a>, originally established by French settlers) and exports much of its beef to Japan, Australia and other Pacific Island countries.</p>
<p>Besides beef, tin fish and rice, bought in town, Santo has many foods that locals take for granted and that tourists enjoy as delicacies. Among these are sweet pineapples, mangoes, island cabbage, flying fox and <a href="/wiki/Coconut_crab" title="Coconut crab">coconut crab</a>, as well as local nuts such as natapoa and the sweet fleshy-fruit nouse. There is a market in Luganville where local food such as manioc, taro, yam, cabbage and other freshly grown island staples are sold. Several small supermarkets such as LCM, Unity Shell and Au bon Marche, sell groceries and many packaged goods.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Espiritu_Santo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Biodiversity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island of Espiritu Santo is home to all of Vanuatu's <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> birds, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santo_mountain_starling" title="Santo mountain starling">Santo mountain starling</a>, a species restricted entirely to Santo. Two protected areas have been established to safeguard the island's biodiversity; the Loru Conservation Area on the east coast and the Vatthe Conservation area near Big Bay in the north.</p>
<p>The Loru Rainforest Protected Area is situated in the lowland rainforests of Santo. Established in 1993 by Chief Caleb Ser, the 220 hectare reserve supports a rich variety of Vanuatu's bird, bat and plant life as well as a diverse range of marine species in the two-kilometre stretch of fringing reef.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
