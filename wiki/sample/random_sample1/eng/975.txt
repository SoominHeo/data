<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>FC Seoul Reserves</b> are the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_team" title="Reserve team">Reserve team</a> of <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a>. Reserve team is member of the <a href="/wiki/R_League" title="R League">R League</a> (Reserve League).</p>
<p><b>FC Seoul Academy</b>is the <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">Youth system</a> of <a href="/wiki/FC_Seoul" title="FC Seoul">FC Seoul</a>. The academy consists of U-18 Team, U-15 Team, U-12 Team and Future of FC Seoul (Football School for hobby). U-18 Team is a member of the K League Junior (Youth League).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Seoul_Reserves_and_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FC Seoul Reserves was built in 1990 for first <a href="/wiki/R_League" title="R League">R League</a>. But <a href="/wiki/R_League" title="R League">R League</a> existed just for one year.</p>
<p>In 2000, <a href="/wiki/R_League" title="R League">R League</a> relaunched, and FC Seoul Reserves was rebuilt for competition. There are many players played for reserves including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Chung-Yong" title="Lee Chung-Yong">Lee Chung-Yong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Jin-Hyung" title="Song Jin-Hyung">Song Jin-Hyung</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ko_Myong-Jin" title="Ko Myong-Jin">Ko Myong-Jin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ahn_Sang-Hyun" title="Ahn Sang-Hyun">Ahn Sang-Hyun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Seoul_Reserves_and_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FC Seoul didn't had practical <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">youth system</a> by 2006. In 2007, FC Seoul affiliated with Dongbuk High School FC as U-18 team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 December, FC Seoul established U-18 team and U-15 team at Osan Schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 December, FC Seoul established U-12 Team. FC Seoul constructed <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">youth system</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EC%98%A4%EC%82%B0%EA%B3%A0%EB%93%B1%ED%95%99%EA%B5%90_(%EC%84%9C%EC%9A%B8)_%EC%B6%95%EA%B5%AC%EB%B6%80" title="ko:오산고등학교 (서울) 축구부">ko:오산고등학교 (서울) 축구부</a></div>
<p>FC Seoul U-18 Team is Osan High School FC in Seoul. Osan High School FC is founded on December 2012. From 2007 to 2012, FC Seoul U-18 Team was Dongbuk High School FC.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_of_FC_Seoul">Future of FC Seoul</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Seoul_Reserves_and_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Future of FC Seoul">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a class="extiw" href="https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future_of_FC_%EC%84%9C%EC%9A%B8" title="ko:Future of FC 서울">ko:Future of FC 서울</a></div>
<p>FC Seoul ran a soccer camp for hobby in 1990s and 2000s, then known as <i>LG Cheetahs Soccer Camp</i> and <i>Little FC Seoul</i>.</p>
<p>In October 2012, Renamed Future of FC Seoul, FC Seoul have been running the Future of FC Seoul (FOS) Program the Youth Football Academy to find promising young players for the future and also planning and involving in Social Contribution Events to enhance the football infrastructure and environment.</p>
<p>Outstanding players in Future of FC Seoul can transfer to U-12 team.</p>
