<p><b>AirBridgeCargo Airlines, LLC</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">ООО Авиакомпания «ЭйрБриджКарго», <i>Aviakompanija «EjrBridžKargo»</i></span>) is the largest Russian <a href="/wiki/Cargo_airline" title="Cargo airline">cargo airline</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/Volga-Dnepr_Group" title="Volga-Dnepr Group">Volga-Dnepr Group</a>. It operates scheduled cargo services on routes between Russia, Asia, Europe and North America. All of its flights are operated via Moscow hubs in <a href="/wiki/Sheremetyevo_International_Airport" title="Sheremetyevo International Airport">Sheremetyevo International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Domodedovo_International_Airport" title="Domodedovo International Airport">Domodedovo International Airport</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vnukovo_International_Airport" title="Vnukovo International Airport">Vnukovo International Airport</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AirBridgeCargo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The airline was established in 2003 and started operations on 1 April 2004. It was established by charter freight specialist Volga-Dnepr Group as its scheduled freight arm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FI-1">[1]</a></sup> April 2004 saw the delivery of one of two <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747-200F</a> freighters being acquired from <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a>. In May 2004 it started commercial operation on routes from <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> to <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and Luxembourg to <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> via Moscow and <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk" title="Novosibirsk">Novosibirsk</a>. The second Boeing 747-200 commenced scheduled services in October 2004. Airbridge Cargo began services to <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> after acquiring its third Boeing 747F in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FI-1">[1]</a></sup> The company entered the scheduled cargo market in May 2004 when the first AirBridgeCargo branded Boeing 747 made its inaugural commercial flight. This milestone for the Group was the realization of two years of preliminary work to build the airline's fleet, network, management and personnel under its Russian Scheduled Cargo Operation Development Strategy.</p>
<p>In November 2006 AirBridge Cargo received its own air operator's certificate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FI-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The airline was recognized as the largest cargo carrier at Frankfurt airport (in 2009, 2010 and 2011), and also was recognized by prizes at the Schiphol Aviation Awards in 2012.</p>
<p>In 2012 AirBridgeCargo was the first Russian cargo airline to operate transpolar flights on the Hong Kong – Chicago route with a stopover in Krasnoyarsk. Such routes save fuel and reduce flight time by 15% compared to flights routed via Sheremetyevo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AirBridgeCargo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The head office is located in Building 4 of the Krylatsky Hills Business Park in <a href="/wiki/Krylatskoye_District" title="Krylatskoye District">Krylatskoye District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Administrative_Okrug" title="Western Administrative Okrug">Western Administrative Okrug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AirBridgeCargo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Destinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The scheduled route network includes 24 destinations in 11 countries and 3 continents. The airline performs scheduled flights from cities in Asia such as <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou" title="Zhengzhou">Zhengzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chengdu" title="Chengdu">Chengdu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, to major European hubs such as <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> or <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>. The Airline currently serves <a href="/wiki/Ted_Stevens_Anchorage_International_Airport" title="Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport">Anchorage</a>, Atlanta, <a href="/wiki/O%27Hare_International_Airport" title="O'Hare International Airport">Chicago–O'Hare</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas_Fort_Worth_Airport" title="Dallas Fort Worth Airport">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_International_Airport" title="Los Angeles International Airport">Los Angeles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/George_Bush_Intercontinental_Airport" title="George Bush Intercontinental Airport">Houston–Intercontinental</a> in the United States. At present, the AirBridgeCargo route network in Europe includes <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heathrow_Airport" title="Heathrow Airport">Heathrow Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leipzig/Halle_Airport" title="Leipzig/Halle Airport">Leipzig</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ekaterinburg" title="Ekaterinburg">Ekaterinburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krasnoyarsk" title="Krasnoyarsk">Krasnoyarsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khabarovsk" title="Khabarovsk">Khabarovsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk" title="Novosibirsk">Novosibirsk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> for maintenance of their aircraft. On 3 September 2015, the airline commenced operations to Singapore, making it the first dedicated freight link between Singapore and Moscow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On 3rd November 2016, AirBridgeCargo launched a new route to <a href="/wiki/Heathrow_Airport" title="Heathrow Airport">Heathrow Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
