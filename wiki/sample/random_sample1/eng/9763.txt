<p>An <b>air taxi</b> is a small commercial aircraft which makes short flights on demand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MW_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MW-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001 air <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxi" title="Taxi">taxi</a> operations were promoted in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> by a <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> and aerospace industry study on the potential <a href="/wiki/Small_Aircraft_Transportation_System" title="Small Aircraft Transportation System">Small Aircraft Transportation System</a> (SATS) and the rise of light-jet aircraft manufacturing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NASA-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation">Regulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_taxi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, air taxi operations are regulated by <a href="/wiki/Transport_Canada" title="Transport Canada">Transport Canada</a> under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Aviation_Regulation" title="Canadian Aviation Regulation">Canadian Aviation Regulation</a> 703. The Canadian definition of air taxi includes all commercial single engined aircraft, multi-engined helicopters flown by day <a href="/wiki/Visual_flight_rules" title="Visual flight rules">visual flight rules</a> by one pilot and all multi-engined, non-turbo-jet aircraft, with a maximum take-off weight 8,618 kg (18,999 lb) or less and nine or fewer passenger seats, that are used to transport people or goods or for sightseeing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CAR70301_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CAR70301-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United States, air taxi and <a href="/wiki/Air_charter" title="Air charter">air charter</a> operations are governed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FAR_part_135" title="FAR part 135">Part 135</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Regulations" title="Federal Aviation Regulations">Federal Aviation Regulations</a> (FAR), unlike the larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scheduled_air_carriers" title="Scheduled air carriers">scheduled air carriers</a> which are governed by more stringent standards of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FAR_Part_121" title="FAR Part 121">FAR Part 121</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
