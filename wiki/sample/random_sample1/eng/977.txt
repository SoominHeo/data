<p><b>Football Club Arsenal Kyiv</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Футбо́льний Клуб Арсена́л Ки́їв</span>) is a Ukrainian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a>. The original club was established in 1925, to which today's club traces its roots.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_club">Soviet club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Soviet club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Created on July 14, 1925, as a multi-sports club of the <a href="/wiki/Kiev_Arsenal" title="Kiev Arsenal">Arsenal Factory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a>, before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the club played mostly in regional competitions or amateur competitions for factory workers. In 1936 Arsenal Kiev took part in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Cup" title="Soviet Cup">Soviet Cup</a> in football where it was eliminated after the first round of competition after a replay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the club played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Soviet_competitions" title="Ukrainian Soviet competitions">Ukrainian Soviet competitions</a> under the name of <i>FC Mashynobudivnyk Kiev</i> (the SC Arsenal Kyiv also used to have a hockey team, HC Zenit Kiev).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1958 Mashynobudivnyk won the competitions and was accepted to the Soviet Class B under the name of FC Arsenal Kiev. In 1959–1964 the club played in the Soviet Class B. During its last season in 1964 the club was renamed into <i>FC Temp Kiev</i>. After the season Arsenal was dissolved,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> while its players became part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamo_Kyiv" title="Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1958 they won the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic">Ukrainian SSR</a> Championship under that name and were accepted to the Soviet Class B under the name of <i>FC Arsenal Kiev</i>. In 1959–1964 the club played continuously in the Soviet Class B. During its last season in 1964 the club was renamed into <i>FC Temp Kiev</i>. After the season, in 1964, Arsenal was dissolved,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> while its players became part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamo_Kyiv" title="Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the club was replaced by <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo-2_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo-2 Kyiv">Dynamo-2 Kyiv</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Second_League" title="Soviet Second League">Soviet Second League</a> and ceased to exist.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Contemporary_club_origin">Contemporary club origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Contemporary club origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Boryspil" title="FC Boryspil">FC Boryspil</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_CSKA_Kyiv" title="FC CSKA Kyiv">FC CSKA Kyiv</a></div>
<p>The origin of the contemporary <i>Arsenal</i> club is disputed; some consider it to be part of the <a href="/wiki/FC_CSKA_Kyiv" title="FC CSKA Kyiv">CSKA Kyiv</a> heritage, others – of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Boryspil" title="FC Boryspil">FC Boryspil</a>. In 1994 <i>CSKA</i> merged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Boryspil" title="FC Boryspil">FC Boryspil</a> under the name <i>CSKA-Borysfen Boryspil</i> (soon thereafter – CSKA-Borysfen Kyiv). After a series of successful seasons FC Boryspil made its way from the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Third_League" title="Ukrainian Third League">Ukrainian Third League</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_First_League" title="Ukrainian First League">Ukrainian First League</a> before the merge, while CSKA prior to that was relegated in two seasons from the Ukrainian First League to the Ukrainian Third League. The original CSKA team became a farm team (CSKA-2) of the merged CSKA-Borysfen. The newly merged team advanced to the top flight (from the First League to the Higher League) in one season. Since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="1995–96 Ukrainian Premier League">1995–96</a> season, the club has continuously competed in the top flight.</p>
<p>In 1996 CSKA-Borysfen went through another transformation when it split into two different clubs: <a href="/wiki/FC_CSKA_Kyiv" title="FC CSKA Kyiv">CSKA Kyiv</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Borysfen_Boryspil" title="FC Borysfen Boryspil">Borysfen Boryspil</a>. The management of CSKA-Borysfen decided to recreate a club in <a href="/wiki/Boryspil" title="Boryspil">Boryspil</a>, while CSKA was reorganized by the Ministry of Defense as the first team of CSKA. Simultaneously, the actual CSKA that was competing at the lower division won a promotion and was later reorganized as the second team, CSKA-2. <a href="/wiki/FC_Borysfen_Boryspil" title="FC Borysfen Boryspil">Borysfen</a> after competing at the amateur level of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyiv_Oblast" title="Kyiv Oblast">Kyiv Oblast</a> applied to enter the professional competitions and gained entry to the lower divisions of the championship.</p>
<p>The army-men also managed to appear in the domestic cup's finals twice (<a href="/wiki/1998_Ukrainian_Cup_Final" title="1998 Ukrainian Cup Final">1998</a> and <a href="/wiki/2001_Ukrainian_Cup_Final" title="2001 Ukrainian Cup Final">2001</a>), where they lost both times: first against city-rivals <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a> and then against <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a>. The club's greatest achievements include a successful <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> run in the season of <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_UEFA_Cup" title="2001–02 UEFA Cup">2001–02</a>, defeating the now defunct <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Jokerit" title="FC Jokerit">Jokerit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbian</a> giants <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star Belgrade</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_epoch">New epoch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New epoch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001-2007 FC Arsenal that originally was created as the Kiev's city team was funded by the Kiev City Administration until the dismissal of <a href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Omelchenko" title="Oleksandr Omelchenko">Oleksandr Omelchenko</a>, after that the club was passed around through several private owners until it became bankrupt completely. Under the Omelchenko's guardianship Arsenal played at the main national football venue (today <a href="/wiki/Olimpiyskiy_National_Sports_Complex" title="Olimpiyskiy National Sports Complex">Olimpiyskiy National Sports Complex</a>) without any concerns, yet later it was "kicked out" of the capital and for sometime was forced to play in <a href="/wiki/Boryspil" title="Boryspil">Boryspil</a> or rent the Dynamo's home venues. Omelchenko who was a political opponent of Surkis brothers (<a href="/wiki/Hryhoriy_Surkis" title="Hryhoriy Surkis">Hryhoriy Surkis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ihor_Surkis" title="Ihor Surkis">Ihor Surkis</a>) insisted that Dynamo should be playing at its home venue <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lobanovsky_Dynamo_Stadium" title="Lobanovsky Dynamo Stadium">Lobanovsky Dynamo Stadium</a>.</p>
<p>Its European competition season in <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_UEFA_Cup" title="2001–02 UEFA Cup">2001-02</a>, the club played under the brand of CSKA while de-facto for the whole year was known as Arsenal.</p>
<p>In 2013 after selling of the club by Rabinovich to Onyshchenko, there started a promotional campaign for revival of historical heritage of another Sports Club Arsenal Kyiv that used to exist at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiev_Arsenal_Factory" title="Kiev Arsenal Factory">Kiev Arsenal Factory</a> trying to connect the old sports club of 1925 with the newly created club of 2001.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment_of_Arsenal_Kyiv">Establishment of Arsenal Kyiv</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Establishment of Arsenal Kyiv">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the 2000 financial crisis at CSKA, the club, as a state company of the Sports Committee of Ministry of Defense, had its budget cut and was on the brink of being dissolved. The Kyiv city government on the initiative of <a href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Omelchenko" title="Oleksandr Omelchenko">Oleksandr Omelchenko</a> bought the club in 2001 reorganizing it under the name of 'Arsenal', grandfathering everything from CSKA Kyiv. The First League second team CSKA-2 Kyiv continued to be affiliated with the Ministry of Defense and once again became the primary team of the Army football club, FC CSKA Kyiv.</p>
<p>While under the city government's ownership, Arsenal struggled financially, resorting to loaning many of its first team's squad players. The city had decreased funding to the club significantly as it sought to reduce its numerous sports holdings. In May 2007, it was announced that the club would be demoted due to financial issues, however soon afterwards it was revealed that <i>Arsenal</i> would be purchased by <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_oligarchs" title="Ukrainian oligarchs">Ukrainian oligarch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vadim_Rabinovich" title="Vadim Rabinovich">Vadim Rabinovich</a>. The new owner started actively financing the club and its transfers. In January 2009 the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Kiev" title="Mayor of Kiev">Mayor of Kiev</a> <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Chernovetskyi" title="Leonid Chernovetskyi">Leonid Chernovetskyi</a> bought Arsenal Kyiv for 1 <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_hryvnia" title="Ukrainian hryvnia">hryvnia</a> from Rabynovich; Chernovetskiy's 30-year-old son Stepan became the club's president. The following year Rabynovich bought the club back due to the poor management.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bankruptcy">Bankruptcy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bankruptcy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In January 2013 the owner Rabynovych stated that the club could be liquidated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> During the next month it was announced that Ukrainian oligarch <a href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Onyshchenko" title="Oleksandr Onyshchenko">Oleksandr Onyshchenko</a> was ready to finance the club and claimed that he had paid all the debts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> He also accused the previous leadership of the club of not passing the documents for signing the agreements on the transfer of corporate rights to the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> On 29 August, Rabynovych stated that he had resigned from the post of club president.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> The next day Onyschenko stated that due to the (then) present situation he could decide not to help the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> On 24 October Onyschenko stated that he had stopped financing Arsenal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> The next day Arsenal failed to appear for a <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="2013–14 Ukrainian Premier League">2013–14 Ukrainian Premier League</a> match against <a href="/wiki/SC_Tavriya_Simferopol" title="SC Tavriya Simferopol">SC Tavriya Simferopol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> On 28 October 2013 it again failed to appear for a <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Ukrainian_Cup" title="2013–14 Ukrainian Cup">2013–14 Ukrainian Cup</a> match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Nyva_Ternopil" title="FC Nyva Ternopil">FC Nyva Ternopil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> The next day general director Viktor Holovko announced that the club was filing for bankruptcy and withdrawing from competitions as it was unable to find any sponsors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-holovko_arsenal_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-holovko_arsenal-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arsenal_withdraw_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-arsenal_withdraw-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 November 2013 <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">FC Shakhtar Donetsk</a> Chairman <a href="/wiki/Rinat_Akhmetov" title="Rinat Akhmetov">Rinat Akhmetov</a> announced that after financial help from the other teams in the league; the Arsenal squad would be able to complete its 2013–14 (Ukrainian Premier League) season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AKAhIU161113_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AKAhIU161113-6">[6]</a></sup> But the next day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dnipro_Dnipropetrovsk" title="Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk">Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk</a> refused to (re)play the (16th round) match Arsenal had earlier failed to appear for (due to its bankruptcy).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AKAhIU161113_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-AKAhIU161113-6">[6]</a></sup> As a result, 29 October 2013 was the date the club lost its professional status.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reorganization_in_2014">Reorganization in 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reorganization in 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Arsenal team that was re-founded in 2001 went <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bankrupt" title="Bankrupt">bankrupt</a> in late 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-endIU111311013_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-endIU111311013-3">[3]</a></sup> but the club was soon reformed and currently plays as an amateur team in the Kiev City League competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arsenal_reforms_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-arsenal_reforms-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014 an initiative group of former club players and fans with the help of Kiev businessman and rally driver <a href="/wiki/Oleksiy_Kikireshko" title="Oleksiy Kikireshko">Oleksiy Kikireshko</a> re-established the club as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Arsenal-Kyiv" title="FC Arsenal-Kyiv">FC Arsenal-Kyiv</a>.</p>
<p>After its last game of the 2014 Kiev city championship on November 9, 2014, which was won by FC Arsenal-Kyiv, the club's president Kikireshko announced that the club submitted a preliminary application on participation in the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Second_League" title="Ukrainian Second League">Ukrainian Second League</a> for the 2015–16 <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Second_League" title="Ukrainian Second League">Ukrainian Second League</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and it was accepted. the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Second_League" title="Ukrainian Second League">Ukrainian Second League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club appointed <a href="/wiki/Andriy_Annenkov" title="Andriy Annenkov">Andriy Annenkov</a> in February 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but resigned after an unsuccessful start to a new season on 8 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters_and_Rivalries">Supporters and Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Supporters and Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arsenal supporters are very strongly tied to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-fascist" title="Anti-fascist">anti-fascist</a> movement and in the majority hold strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-wing" title="Left-wing">left-wing</a> views. They maintain friendly relations with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partizan_Minsk" title="Partizan Minsk">Partizan Minsk</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SV_Babelsberg" title="SV Babelsberg">SV Babelsberg</a> who have similar political beliefs. Their arch rivals are the majority <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationalist" title="Nationalist">nationalist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-wing" title="Right-wing">right-wing</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamo_Kiev" title="Dynamo Kiev">Dynamo Kiev</a>, with whom they contest the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiev_derby" title="Kiev derby">Kiev derby</a>. They also have a rivalry with the other Kiev team, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CSCA_Kiev" title="CSCA Kiev">CSCA Kiev</a>, not only along political lines but also due to the controversial intertwining of the two club's histories. Other fierce rivals are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karpaty_Lviv" title="Karpaty Lviv">Karpaty Lviv</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dnipro_Dnipropetrovsk" title="Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk">Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="European_competitions">European competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Arsenal_Kyiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: European competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arsenal Kyiv appeared in the European competitions for the first time as <i>CSKA Kyiv</i> in 1998 (<a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="1998–99 UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">1998–99 UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a>) in away game against the Irish <a href="/wiki/Cork_City_F.C." title="Cork City F.C.">Cork City F.C.</a> which CSKA lost 1-2. The first two qualifications to European competitions were achieved by reaching the final of <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Cup" title="Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a> in 1998 and 2001. During that time Arsenal Kyiv was known as CSKA Kyiv.</p>
<p>The first appearance in the European competitions under Arsenal brand the club made in 2012.</p>
