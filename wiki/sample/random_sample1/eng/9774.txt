<p>The <b>Eerste Divisie</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈeːrstə diˈvizi]</a></span>; English: First Division) is the second-highest <a href="/wiki/Division_(sport)" title="Division (sport)">tier</a> of <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. It is linked with the top-level <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> and with the third-level <a href="/wiki/Tweede_Divisie" title="Tweede Divisie">Tweede Divisie</a> via <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion/relegation</a> systems. It is also known as the <b>Jupiler League</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ʒypiˈleː ˈlik]</a></span> due to sponsorship, which is the same name as the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_First_Division_A" title="Belgian First Division A">top league in Belgium</a>. It is now named after <a href="/wiki/Jupiler" title="Jupiler">Jupiler</a> Pils; previously, it was most recently known as the <i>Gouden Gids Divisie</i> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈɣʌu̯də(n) ˈɣɪdz diˈvizi]</a></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> after a five-year sponsorship deal with the Dutch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yellow_Pages" title="Yellow Pages">Yellow Pages</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eerste_Divisie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Eerste Divisie consists of 20 clubs, who play each other in a double round-robin league, with each club playing the other club home and away. Each club plays every other club once in the first half of the season before the league takes a winter break around the <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year's Day">New Year's</a> holiday season. The second half of the season sees the same fixtures as the first half, with the stadiums changed, although the two halves are not played in the same order.</p>
<p>At the end of each season, the champion of the Eerste Divisie is automatically promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>. Eight other clubs enter the <i>Nacompetitie</i> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈnaː.kɔm.pə.ˌti.(t)si]</a></span>, a promotion/relegation playoff that includes the 16th- and 17th-place clubs in the Eredivisie. The following teams qualify for the Nacompetitie:</p>
<ul>
<li>The club with the best record in the "first period" of the season (after 8 rounds).</li>
<li>The club with the best record in the "second period" of the season (rounds 9–16).</li>
<li>The club with the best record in the "third period" of the season (rounds 17–24).</li>
<li>The club with the best record in the "fourth period" of the season (rounds 25–32).</li>
<li>The two remaining spots are filled at the season's end by the highest-placed clubs that have not already earned automatic promotion or qualified for the Nacompetitie.</li>
</ul>
<p>If the club that wins a period has qualified for the Nacompetitie by winning a previous period, its place is filled by the next-best club in that period that has not already qualified. Usually, the clubs that qualify for the Nacompetitie turn out to be the 2nd- through 9th-placed clubs in the final table. Clubs in the Nacompetitie face each other in a knock-out system with the numbers 16 and 17 of the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> for two places in next season's Eredivisie.</p>
<p>Between seasons 1971/1972 and 2007/2009 teams could not relegate from the Eerste Divisie. From the 2009/2010 season onwards, one team has been relegated from the Eerste Divisie to the <i><a href="/wiki/Hoofdklasse" title="Hoofdklasse">Hoofdklasse</a></i> (then the main amateur league of Dutch football). From the 2010/2011 to 2015/2016 seasons, the KNVB introduced a third and highest amateur tier called <i><a href="/wiki/Derde_Divisie" title="Derde Divisie">Topklasse</a></i>, and <i>Hoofdklasse</i> clubs have been able to get promoted to that new division.</p>
<p>Before the 2008/2009 season, Hoofdklasse clubs could promote by obtaining a professional licence. However, only a club going bankrupt or losing their license could result in clubs leaving professional football. The last clubs leaving professional football in that way were <a href="/wiki/FC_Wageningen" title="FC Wageningen">FC Wageningen</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VCV_Zeeland" title="VCV Zeeland">VCV Zeeland</a> in 1992, and more recently <a href="/wiki/HFC_Haarlem" title="HFC Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RBC_Roosendaal" title="RBC Roosendaal">RBC Roosendaal</a>, who went bankrupt in January 2010 and June 2011 respectively. The most recent additions to the league were <a href="/wiki/AGOVV_Apeldoorn" title="AGOVV Apeldoorn">AGOVV Apeldoorn</a> in 2003 and <a href="/wiki/Almere_City_FC" title="Almere City FC">FC Omniworld</a> in 2005, expanding the league to 19 and later 20 clubs. However, for the 2010/11 season, the league returned to 18 clubs, as HFC Haarlem went bankrupt and FC Oss were relegated to the newly formed Topklasse. The 2012/13 season ended with 16 teams after AGOVV and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BV_Veendam" title="BV Veendam">BV Veendam</a> went bankrupt. Four teams have been added to bring the division back up to 20 teams in 2013. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Achilles%2729" title="Achilles'29">Achilles'29</a> has been promoted from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topklasse" title="Topklasse">Topklasse</a> with the reserve teams of <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a> and <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> being added as well.</p>
<p>Since the 2016/2017 season there is automatic relegation to the third-tier, semi-professional <a href="/wiki/Tweede_Divisie" title="Tweede Divisie">Tweede Divisie</a>. Clubs in the Tweede Divisie had to announce in mid-season if they want to be eligible for promotion. Only if one of those clubs won the Tweede Divisie championship is a team relegated from the Eerste Divisie.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attendance_record">Attendance record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eerste_Divisie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Attendance record">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Clubs with larger fanbases suffered relegation in the 2000s, with <a href="/wiki/Roda_JC_Kerkrade" title="Roda JC Kerkrade">Roda JC</a> setting the Eerste Divisie attendance record at 16,150 during their home game against <a href="/wiki/N.E.C._(football_club)" title="N.E.C. (football club)">N.E.C.</a> in the 2014/15 season. <a href="/wiki/NAC_Breda" title="NAC Breda">NAC Breda</a> bettered it a season later in their home match against <a href="/wiki/Go_Ahead_Eagles" title="Go Ahead Eagles">Go Ahead Eagles</a>, which had an attendance of 17800 people.</p>
