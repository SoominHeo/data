<p><b>Amy Lynn Hartzler</b> (<a href="/wiki/Given_name#Name_at_birth" title="Given name">née</a> <b>Lee</b>; born December 13, 1981), known professionally as <b>Amy Lee</b>, is an American singer, songwriter, record producer and multi-instrumentalist. She is the co-founder and lead vocalist of the rock band <a href="/wiki/Evanescence" title="Evanescence">Evanescence</a>. Along with her contributions with the band, Lee has also participated on numerous other musical projects including <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Nightmare_Revisited" title="Nightmare Revisited">Nightmare Revisited</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets:_The_Green_Album" title="Muppets: The Green Album">Muppets: The Green Album</a></i>. Lee has performed collaborations with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Korn" title="Korn">Korn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seether" title="Seether">Seether</a>, and <a href="/wiki/David_Hodges" title="David Hodges">David Hodges</a>. Lee is also the American <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">chairperson</a> for the international <a href="/wiki/Epilepsy" title="Epilepsy">epilepsy</a> awareness foundation <i>Out of the Shadows</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OOTSwhy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OOTSwhy-2">[2]</a></sup> During Evanescence's hiatus, Lee embarked on her film score career in 2013 by composing the soundtrack to <i><a href="/wiki/War_Story_(2014_film)" title="War Story (2014 film)">War Story</a></i> (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/Indigo_Grey:_The_Passage" title="Indigo Grey: The Passage">Indigo Grey: The Passage</a></i> (2015) with cellist <a href="/wiki/Dave_Eggar" title="Dave Eggar">Dave Eggar</a>.</p>
<p>Lee cites influences ranging from composers such as <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Mozart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Elfman" title="Danny Elfman">Danny Elfman</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gurl336_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-gurl336-3">[3]</a></sup> to artists <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tori_Amos" title="Tori Amos">Tori Amos</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gurl336_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-gurl336-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Plumb_(singer)" title="Plumb (singer)">Plumb</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ct0406_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ct0406-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Manson" title="Shirley Manson">Shirley Manson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20120422_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20120422-5">[5]</a></sup> She received the Songwriter Icon Award in 2008 from the <a href="/wiki/National_Music_Publishers_Association" title="National Music Publishers Association">National Music Publishers Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nmpa08_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-nmpa08-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2012, she won Best Vocalist at the <a href="/wiki/Revolver_(magazine)" title="Revolver (magazine)"><i>Revolver</i> Golden Gods Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bb20120224_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bb20120224-7">[7]</a></sup> and also named Rock Goddess of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/Loudwire" title="Loudwire"><i>Loudwire</i> Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20130116_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20130116-8">[8]</a></sup> She won an award for Best Film Score for <i>Indigo Grey: The Passage</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Moondance_International_Film_Festival" title="Moondance International Film Festival">Moondance International Film Festival</a> in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moondance2015_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-moondance2015-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amy Lee was born in Riverside, California,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard-bio_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-bio-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whitburn2008_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Whitburn2008-11">[11]</a></sup> to parents John Lee, a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> and TV personality, and Sara Cargill. She has a brother named Robby and two sisters, Carrie and Lori. Lee had a younger sister who died in 1987 at the age of three from an unidentified illness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blender705_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-blender705-12">[12]</a></sup> The song "Hello" from <i>Fallen</i> has been reported to have been written for her late sister, as well as the song "Like You" from <i>The Open Door</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vh1songs_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-vh1songs-13">[13]</a></sup> Lee took classical piano lessons for nine years.</p>
<p>Her family moved to many places, including West Palm Beach, Florida,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indystar20040723_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-indystar20040723-14">[14]</a></sup> and Rockford, Illinois,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OOTSamybio_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-OOTSamybio-15">[15]</a></sup> and eventually settled in Little Rock, Arkansas, where Evanescence was formed. Lee graduated from <a href="/wiki/Pulaski_Academy" title="Pulaski Academy">Pulaski Academy</a>, a private school in Little Rock, in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pulaskiacademy_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-pulaskiacademy-16">[16]</a></sup> She briefly attended <a href="/wiki/Middle_Tennessee_State_University" title="Middle Tennessee State University">Middle Tennessee State University</a> in 2000 to study music theory and composition but dropped out to focus on Evanescence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evDisappearing_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-evDisappearing-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an interview on <a href="/wiki/Spinner_(website)" title="Spinner (website)">AOL Music</a>, Lee said that the first songs she remembered writing were called "Eternity of the Remorse" and "A Single Tear". The first was written when she was eleven years old and wanted to become a classical composer, and the second was for an assignment when she was in the eighth grade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOL_Ps.26Qs_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-AOL_Ps.26Qs-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lee co-founded the rock band Evanescence with guitarist <a href="/wiki/Ben_Moody" title="Ben Moody">Ben Moody</a>. The two met at a youth camp after Moody heard Lee playing <a href="/wiki/Meat_Loaf" title="Meat Loaf">Meat Loaf</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I%27d_Do_Anything_for_Love_(But_I_Won%27t_Do_That)" title="I'd Do Anything for Love (But I Won't Do That)">I'd Do Anything for Love (But I Won't Do That)</a>" on the piano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVsplit_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTVsplit-19">[19]</a></sup> Within a month, the pair were playing acoustic sets at Arkansas book stores and coffee houses,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMHleeinterview_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-SMHleeinterview-20">[20]</a></sup> and they eventually recorded two <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EPs</a>, <i>Evanescence EP</i> (1998) and <i>Sound Asleep EP</i> (1999), selling them at various local venues. In 2000, Evanescence recorded the longer EP <i><a href="/wiki/Origin_(Evanescence_album)" title="Origin (Evanescence album)">Origin</a></i>. This demo contains three songs from the debut album <i>Fallen</i> written by Lee and Moody: "Whisper", "Imaginary" and "<a href="/wiki/My_Immortal" title="My Immortal">My Immortal</a>". Whereas "Whisper" and "Imaginary" were altered before being included on <i>Fallen</i>, "My Immortal" is virtually unchanged, apart from additional string arrangements. A later band version of "My Immortal" was made available for download for those who had bought an official version of <i>Fallen</i> through their web site, but required that a CD checker program also be downloaded for verification before it would play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miband_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-miband-21">[21]</a></sup> The band version was included on later copies of <i>Fallen</i>, notably the Brazilian, Bolivian, and Argentine editions.</p>
<p>On October 22, 2003, Moody left the band citing "creative differences". In an interview several months later, Amy said: "...we'd gotten to a point that if something didn't change, we wouldn't have been able to make a second record". She also said "We're finally a real band, not just Ben and I and a few others thrown together".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMHleeinterview_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-SMHleeinterview-20">[20]</a></sup> Ex-<a href="/wiki/Cold_(band)" title="Cold (band)">Cold</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Terry_Balsamo" title="Terry Balsamo">Terry Balsamo</a> replaced Moody in the band, both on guitar and as Lee's writing partner.</p>
<p>On January 3, 2014, <a href="/wiki/TMZ" title="TMZ">TMZ</a> reported that Lee had sued Evanescence's label, <a href="/wiki/Wind-up_Records" title="Wind-up Records">Wind-up Records</a>, for $1.5 million in unpaid royalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tmz20140103_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-tmz20140103-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20140103_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20140103-23">[23]</a></sup> In March 2014, via her Twitter account, Lee announced that she had been released from her Wind-up Records contract and was now an independent artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20140320_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20140320-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2000, Lee sang on two of former Evanescence keyboardist <a href="/wiki/David_Hodges" title="David Hodges">David Hodges</a>' songs: "Breathe" (<i>The Summit Church: Summit Worship</i>) and the unreleased "Fall Into You".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-davidhodges_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-davidhodges-25">[25]</a></sup> She performed backup vocals for "Missing You", a song on <a href="/wiki/Big_Dismal" title="Big Dismal">Big Dismal</a>'s 2003 debut album <i>Believe</i>, and sang backup vocals on two songs with 'supergroup' <a href="/wiki/The_Damning_Well" title="The Damning Well">The Damning Well</a>, though her vocals were taken off the final release due to record label issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv1476854_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv1476854-26">[26]</a></sup> Lee later performed a duet with her then-boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Morgan" title="Shaun Morgan">Shaun Morgan</a> on the track "<a href="/wiki/Broken_(Seether_song)" title="Broken (Seether song)">Broken</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Seether" title="Seether">Seether</a>'s 2004 album <i><a href="/wiki/Disclaimer_II" title="Disclaimer II">Disclaimer II</a></i>. The song was also featured as part of the soundtrack for the 2004 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Punisher_(2004_film)" title="The Punisher (2004 film)">The Punisher</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2004, Lee claimed to be working on music for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" title="The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe">The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe</a></i>, but that the music was rejected by the studio for being "too dark." However, the producers of <i>Narnia</i> stated that Lee was never asked to compose any music for the film, whose score was written by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry Gregson-Williams</a>, and that "No Evanescence music was planned for the soundtrack."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv917_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv917-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nzh569_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-nzh569-28">[28]</a></sup> While there was some speculation that one of the supposed songs had been cut and used in several tracks of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Open_Door" title="The Open Door">The Open Door</a></i>, Lee stated this was not true except for part of it being used to segue into the last track of the album, "<a href="/wiki/Good_Enough_(Evanescence_song)" title="Good Enough (Evanescence song)">Good Enough</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Evboardnarnia1_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Evboardnarnia1-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lee became the American chairperson for <i>Out of the Shadows</i> in 2006. This organization is an international foundation with the goal of providing education about <a href="/wiki/Epilepsy" title="Epilepsy">epilepsy</a>. Lee's younger brother, Robby, was previously diagnosed with this condition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OOTSwhy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OOTSwhy-2">[2]</a></sup> The singer also made a brief guest appearance in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cash" title="Johnny Cash">Johnny Cash</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/God%27s_Gonna_Cut_You_Down#Johnny_Cash_version" title="God's Gonna Cut You Down">God's Gonna Cut You Down</a>" in late 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VH1cash_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-VH1cash-30">[30]</a></sup> As each celebrity featured in the shoot was allowed to choose what they would be doing for the video, Lee chose to appear laying flowers on a grave. Her scene was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Church_(Manhattan)" title="Trinity Church (Manhattan)">Trinity Church</a> in Manhattan, during which she wore a black velvet coat that previously belonged to <a href="/wiki/Tim_Burton" title="Tim Burton">Tim Burton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blend2076_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-blend2076-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2007, MTV released <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Unplugged:_Korn" title="MTV Unplugged: Korn">MTV Unplugged: Korn</a></i> to television and radio, in which Lee is featured during the song "<a href="/wiki/Freak_on_a_Leash" title="Freak on a Leash">Freak on a Leash</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv20061213_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv20061213-32">[32]</a></sup> The song was also released as the first single from the album. In November 2007, <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> produced a <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">mockumentary</a> in the style of <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Music" title="Behind the Music">Behind the Music</a></i>, titled <i>Rock Band Cometh: The Rock Band Band Story</i>, to promote the video game <i>Rock Band</i>. Lee was one of the celebrity <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameos</a> featured on the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmouth84367_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-bmouth84367-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<p>In June 2008, the <a href="/wiki/National_Music_Publishers_Association" title="National Music Publishers Association">National Music Publishers Association</a> presented Lee with their 2008 Songwriter Icon Award, which "recognizes outstanding songwriters for their personal achievement."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nmpa08_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-nmpa08-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a>' September 2008 release of <i><a href="/wiki/Nightmare_Revisited" title="Nightmare Revisited">Nightmare Revisited</a></i>, Lee sang a remake of "Sally's Song". The album contains new material and covers of songs from the original <a href="/wiki/The_Nightmare_Before_Christmas_(soundtrack)" title="The Nightmare Before Christmas (soundtrack)"><i>Nightmare Before Christmas</i> soundtrack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bb080805_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-bb080805-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-revmag1_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-revmag1-35">[35]</a></sup> Lee performed live renditions of "Sally's Song" during the October 17 <i>Nightmare Before Christmas</i> re-release premiere in Hollywood, and for an October 13 appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmouth106660_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-bmouth106660-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>During an October 2008 interview for <a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin.com</a>, Lee noted that she was writing new songs, possibly for a solo album project. Citing influences in <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Celtic_music" title="Celtic music">Celtic music</a>, she says her current writings feel like she is going back to her "really old" roots. She gave no potential release date, but said of her reason for this new direction, "I need to show that I'm more than a one trick pony."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin081017_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-spin081017-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lee stated during an October 2008 interview with <i>The Gauntlet</i> that she did not know whether or not she would begin a solo career, saying that she was "at a point where I don't know what is next." She noted that Evanescence was still together as a band but that she found touring to be monotonous. She reiterated that she was continuing to write songs, although she did not yet know what purpose they would serve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gauntlet081023_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-gauntlet081023-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> interview in March 2010, Lee stated that she was "in a very different creative space then" regarding her previous work on new material, and that while she wrote some songs that were good, nothing from those efforts would be included in the band's album <i><a href="/wiki/Evanescence_(Evanescence_album)" title="Evanescence (Evanescence album)">Evanescence</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin100305_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-spin100305-39">[39]</a></sup> which was released on October 7, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv20110711_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv20110711-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Lee covered "<a href="/wiki/Halfway_Down_(poem)" title="Halfway Down (poem)">Halfway Down the Stairs</a>" for <i><a href="/wiki/Muppets:_The_Green_Album" title="Muppets: The Green Album">Muppets: The Green Album</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nme20110627_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-nme20110627-41">[41]</a></sup> and "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_So_Lonesome_I_Could_Cry" title="I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry">I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry</a>" for the tribute album <i><a href="/wiki/We_Walk_the_Line:_A_Celebration_of_the_Music_of_Johnny_Cash" title="We Walk the Line: A Celebration of the Music of Johnny Cash">We Walk the Line: A Celebration of the Music of Johnny Cash</a></i> in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-johnnycash_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-johnnycash-42">[42]</a></sup></p>

<p>On December 2, 2013, it was announced that Lee had teamed up with American <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a> <a href="/wiki/Dave_Eggar" title="Dave Eggar">Dave Eggar</a> to create music for the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/War_Story_(2014_film)" title="War Story (2014 film)">War Story</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20131202_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20131202-43">[43]</a></sup> During an interview with <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, Lee explained, "It's going to surprise my fans. It's not what you'd expect; the film is very dark, very introspective. There's not even a lot of dialogue, which is great, because to me it makes this beautiful, sad platform for music," and that "there's a lot of blending of sounds, a lot of ominous tones. I play a lot of keyboard, and a lot of Taurus pedal. There's a lot of low drones." Lee also revealed that she penned one song for the film titled "Push the Button", explaining, "It's very different for me, it's electronic; I did it all myself, which was crazy, because I'm used to engineering and writing and mixing demos in my house, but being responsible for that being the end product was a new challenge for me. It was like 'This is it, I'm mixing this.<span style="padding-right:.2em;">'</span>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20140108_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20140108-44">[44]</a></sup> On August 6, Lee announced that the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_album" title="Soundtrack album">soundtrack album</a> would be called <i><a href="/wiki/Aftermath_(Amy_Lee_album)" title="Aftermath (Amy Lee album)">Aftermath</a></i> and that it would be released on August 25. Lee contributes to all ten tracks, which features Eggar on eight of them and American/<a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Moroccan</a> musician <a href="/wiki/Malika_Zarra" title="Malika Zarra">Malika Zarra</a> on one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20140806_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20140806-45">[45]</a></sup> Lee revealed a 36-second teaser of the album on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aftermath_Teaser_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aftermath_Teaser-46">[46]</a></sup> It is her first full-length solo album to not involve her band Evanescence.</p>
<p>In March 2015, Lee and Eggar announced that they were recording music, along with Chuck Palmer, for Hammerstep's short film <i><a href="/wiki/Indigo_Grey:_The_Passage" title="Indigo Grey: The Passage">Indigo Grey: The Passage</a></i>, which was released on September 14, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20150314_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20150314-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter576120143941640192_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter576120143941640192-48">[48]</a></sup> The film featured the songs "Between Worlds" and "Resurrection".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS11052015_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-RS11052015-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 13, 2015, Lee composed and recorded "Speak to Me" - the title song to Eric D. Howell's upcoming film <i><a href="/wiki/Voice_from_the_Stone" title="Voice from the Stone">Voice from the Stone</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20151029_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20151029-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20150813_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20150813-51">[51]</a></sup> The accompanying music video was also directed by Howell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20151029_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20151029-50">[50]</a></sup> It was filmed on October 22, 2015, on the Castle of Celsa estate near <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</p>
<p>During a radio interview in July 2015, Lee said that she had been recording <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cover_song" title="Cover song">cover songs</a> and expressed an interest in releasing them online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheEdgeJul15_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheEdgeJul15-52">[52]</a></sup> On October 27, 2015, she released the first song from the collection with <a href="/wiki/Portishead_(band)" title="Portishead (band)">Portishead</a>'s 1994 song "<a href="/wiki/Dummy_(album)" title="Dummy (album)">It's A Fire</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter659115044777631749_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter659115044777631749-53">[53]</a></sup> followed by <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>'s 1987 song "<a href="/wiki/With_or_Without_You" title="With or Without You">With or Without You</a>" on November 10, 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter664142143942889473_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter664142143942889473-54">[54]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>'s 1971 song "<a href="/wiki/Going_to_California" title="Going to California">Going to California</a>" on December 1, 2015, and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Isaak" title="Chris Isaak">Chris Isaak</a>'s 1996 song "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Did_a_Bad_Bad_Thing" title="Baby Did a Bad Bad Thing">Baby Did a Bad, Bad Thing</a>" on December 15, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-facebook1105375239514245_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-facebook1105375239514245-55">[55]</a></sup> They are featured on Lee's debut extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/Recover,_Vol._1" title="Recover, Vol. 1">Recover, Vol. 1</a></i>, which was released on February 19, 2016. The cover songs are accompanied by music videos directed by Eric Ryan Anderson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter659172979113402368_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter659172979113402368-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lee confirmed in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> that she is "definitely working and making music with every intention of people hearing it at this point," as of October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rollingstone20151030_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone20151030-57">[57]</a></sup> She also stated that she does not have "any news or plans" for new Evanescence music,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rollingstone20151030_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone20151030-57">[57]</a></sup> but assured fans on Twitter that the band has not broken up saying, "We don't follow the rules of a commerce-driven timeline. Inspiration drives us. We're wide open."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter20151031_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-twitter20151031-58">[58]</a></sup> She later stated during an interview with <i>Loudwire</i> that "there is Evanescence in the future".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20161003_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20161003-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015, Lee confirmed that she plans to complete some songs from an Evanescence album (originally produced by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lillywhite" title="Steve Lillywhite">Steve Lillywhite</a>) that was rejected by Wind-up Records in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-facebook1105375239514245_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-facebook1105375239514245-55">[55]</a></sup> She explained that she was "devastated" and "furious" over the rejection, but was determined to move forward and ended up being "angry enough to write <a href="/wiki/Evanescence_(Evanescence_album)" title="Evanescence (Evanescence album)">Evanescence's heaviest album</a>." Although three songs from the Lillywhite sessions ended up on <i>Evanescence</i>, Lee admitted, "I was still left feeling unsatisfied about what I lovingly refer to as my 'broken record.'"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-facebook1105375239514245_55-2"><a href="#cite_note-facebook1105375239514245-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Dream_Too_Much">2016–present: <i>Dream Too Much</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Dream Too Much">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 17, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> announced that Lee was scheduled to release a "family" album exclusively through <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Music" title="Amazon Music">Amazon Prime Music</a> in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-businesswire06172016_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-businesswire06172016-60">[60]</a></sup> The album, titled <i>Dream Too Much</i> (2016), features "original music for kids and families"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-businesswire06172016_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-businesswire06172016-60">[60]</a></sup> and was released on September 30, 2016.</p>
<p>In a September 14, 2016, article by <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a>, Lee was stated as having worked on music for the film <i>Blind</i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Alec_Baldwin" title="Alec Baldwin">Alec Baldwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demi_Moore" title="Demi Moore">Demi Moore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvinterview09142016_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvinterview09142016-61">[61]</a></sup> The film was premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Woodstock_Film_Festival" title="Woodstock Film Festival">Woodstock Film Festival</a> on October 13, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiewire09152016_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiewire09152016-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Lee was in a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Seether" title="Seether">Seether</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Morgan" title="Shaun Morgan">Shaun Morgan</a> from 2003 to 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contact20110614_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-contact20110614-63">[63]</a></sup> Lee wrote a song, "<a href="/wiki/Call_Me_When_You%27re_Sober" title="Call Me When You're Sober">Call Me When You're Sober</a>", about him; it was released as the first single from <i>The Open Door</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard20060828_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard20060828-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lee revealed during the live broadcast of MuchMusic's January 9, 2007, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Live_at_Much" title="Live at Much">Live at Much</a></i> that she had become engaged the prior evening. She later confirmed on EvThreads.com that Josh Hartzler, a therapist and long-time friend, proposed to her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EvThreads112785_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-EvThreads112785-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arkdemgaz20120119_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-arkdemgaz20120119-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people20070510_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-people20070510-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-westword20071121_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-westword20071121-68">[68]</a></sup> She noted in an interview that the songs "Good Enough" and "<a href="/wiki/Bring_Me_to_Life" title="Bring Me to Life">Bring Me to Life</a>" were inspired by him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blend2076_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-blend2076-31">[31]</a></sup> The couple married on May 6, 2007, and honeymooned near <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people20038444_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-people20038444-69">[69]</a></sup> She posted on EvThreads that she is "now officially Mrs. Amy Hartzler."</p>
<p>Lee announced via social media on January 18, 2014, that she was pregnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire20140122_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire20140122-70">[70]</a></sup> The couple's first child, Jack Lion Hartzler, was born on July 24, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people20140728_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-people20140728-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eonline20140728_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-eonline20140728-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Public image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee has a recognizable fashion style, marked by her occasional use of <a href="/wiki/Goth_subculture" title="Goth subculture">gothic</a> make-up and taste for <a href="/wiki/Victorian_fashion" title="Victorian fashion">Victorian</a>-styled clothing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kerrang_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-kerrang-73">[73]</a></sup> She also designs many of her own clothes, including those worn in the music video for "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Going_Under_(Evanescence_song)" title="Going Under (Evanescence song)">Going Under</a>", and the dress worn for the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Open_Door" title="The Open Door">The Open Door</a></i> and the dress she wore to the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize_Concert" title="Nobel Peace Prize Concert">Nobel Peace Prize Concert</a> in 2011. After she designed the dress she wore at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_Grammy_Awards" title="2004 Grammy Awards">2004 Grammy Awards</a>, she chose Japanese designer <a href="/wiki/H._Naoto" title="H. Naoto">H. Naoto</a> to make it for her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVleegrammys_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTVleegrammys-74">[74]</a></sup> She at one point had a piercing on her left eyebrow, which is visible on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Fallen_(Evanescence_album)" title="Fallen (Evanescence album)">Fallen</a></i>.</p>
<p>In an interview with VEVO stylized, Lee showcased and explained her fashion style, commenting that she would rather make her own clothes because it is hard to find what she exactly wants elsewhere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VEVOStylized_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-VEVOStylized-75">[75]</a></sup> In an interview with AOL, Lee stated that her daily style is very different from when she performs; pointing out that she was wearing "something floral" during the interview. She explained that her on-stage wardrobe was meant to preserve the atmosphere of the songs and complete the image. In another interview, she stated that when the band first started performing, she would wear corsets to avoid diluting the band's public image, but has gradually become more comfortable with her own style. Her current on-stage style is often characterized by boots, a simple black tank top, a long skirt and various ornaments and accessories.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Lee has stated on a number of occasions that she would never flash her breasts or engage in other <a href="/wiki/Publicity_stunt" title="Publicity stunt">publicity stunts</a> that would draw attention to herself. Further, in the music video for "<a href="/wiki/Everybody%27s_Fool" title="Everybody's Fool">Everybody's Fool</a>", she aimed to mock such artists by suggesting that celebrities who use sex to appeal to an audience are, in fact, merely peddling "lies" (the unifying theme of the music video). Many fans praise Lee for her refusal to emulate other celebrities by using <a href="/wiki/Sexual_attraction" title="Sexual attraction">sex appeal</a> in her music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VH1cheesy_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-VH1cheesy-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, <i><a href="/wiki/Blender_(magazine)" title="Blender (magazine)">Blender</a></i> listed Lee as one of the hottest women in rock alongside such singers as <a href="/wiki/Joan_Jett" title="Joan Jett">Joan Jett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Love" title="Courtney Love">Courtney Love</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liz_Phair" title="Liz Phair">Liz Phair</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blend2295_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-blend2295-77">[77]</a></sup> In 2013, Lee ranked first in NME.com's "Hottest Women in Music" award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nme20130227_78-0"><a href="#cite_note-nme20130227-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
