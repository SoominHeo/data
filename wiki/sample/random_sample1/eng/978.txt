<p><b>Fußball Club Erzgebirge Aue e.V.</b>, commonly known as simply <b>FC Erzgebirge Aue</b> or <b>Erzgebirge Aue</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Germany" title="Football in Germany">German football club</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Aue" title="Aue">Aue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>. The former East German side was a founder member of the <a href="/wiki/3._Liga" title="3. Liga">3. Liga</a> in 2008–09, after being relegated from the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> in 2007–08. The city of Aue has a population of about 18,000, making it one of the smallest cities to ever host a club playing at the second highest level of German football. However, the team attracts supporters from a larger urban area that includes <a href="/wiki/Chemnitz" title="Chemnitz">Chemnitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zwickau" title="Zwickau">Zwickau</a>, whose own football sides are among Aue's traditional rivals.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Erzgebirge_Aue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Germany.27s_dominant_side">East Germany's dominant side</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Erzgebirge_Aue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: East Germany's dominant side">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club was founded as <i>SG Aue</i> in 1945, and on 1 November 1948 became <i>BSG Pneumatik Aue</i> under the sponsorship of the local construction tool works. Changes in sponsorship led to a change in name to <i>Zentra Wismut Aue</i> in 1949 and then simply to <i>SC Wismut Aue</i> in 1951.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:112px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wismut_Aue_logo.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="267" data-file-width="264" height="111" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Wismut_Aue_logo.svg/110px-Wismut_Aue_logo.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Wismut_Aue_logo.svg/165px-Wismut_Aue_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Wismut_Aue_logo.svg/220px-Wismut_Aue_logo.svg.png 2x" width="110"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wismut_Aue_logo.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Historical logo of Wismut Aue</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The club performed well, advancing through third and second tier play to the DDR-Oberliga in 1951. <i>BSG</i> finished as national vice-champions in 1953 losing in a final to <a href="/wiki/Dynamo_Dresden" title="Dynamo Dresden">Dynamo Dresden</a> by a score of 2–3.</p>
<p>In 1954 the <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East German</a> government decided that the nearby city of <a href="/wiki/Chemnitz" title="Chemnitz">Chemnitz</a>—recently renamed <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">Karl-Marx</a>-Stadt—deserved a quality team and so, while the team was never relocated, it was renamed <i>SC Wismut Karl-Marx-Stadt</i>. It was during this time that the club became dominant in East German football. It captured the 1955 <a href="/wiki/FDGB-Pokal" title="FDGB-Pokal">East German Cup</a> and followed this up with four national titles in 1955, 1956, 1957 and 1959. It also played for the 1959 East German Cup, but lost 2–3 in a rematch versus <a href="/wiki/SC_Dynamo_Berlin" title="SC Dynamo Berlin">Dynamo Berlin</a> after the clubs had drawn 0–0 in the first game. Those successes led to <i>Aue'</i>s participation in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Champion Clubs' Cup</a> in 1958, 1959 and 1961.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="With_the_DDR-Oberliga_to_the_end">With the DDR-Oberliga to the end</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Erzgebirge_Aue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: With the DDR-Oberliga to the end">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1963, Karl-Marx-Stadt got its own team and Aue's squad recovered its identity as <i>BSG Wismut Aue</i>. The club continued to enjoy modest success by staying up in the top-tier <a href="/wiki/DDR-Oberliga" title="DDR-Oberliga">DDR-Oberliga</a>, and, although it did not win another championship, it holds the record for the most games played by any team in that league. <i>Aue</i> sits 4th on the all-time DDR-Oberliga list and over the course of thirty-eight years played more games (1,019 matches) than any other East German side. Just behind them, 6th place <a href="/wiki/FC_Rot-Wei%C3%9F_Erfurt" title="FC Rot-Weiß Erfurt">Rot-Weiß Erfurt</a> played 1,001 matches.</p>
<p>Aue also played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> tournament in 1985 and 1987, going out in the first round against <a href="/wiki/FC_Dnipro_Dnipropetrovsk" title="FC Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk">Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk</a> in their first appearance and in the second round against Albanian side <i><a href="/wiki/Flamurtari_Vlor%C3%AB" title="Flamurtari Vlorë">Flamurtari Vlorë</a></i> in their second. After <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> in 1990, the club was renamed <i>FC Wismut Aue</i> before taking on its current name, <i>FC Erzgebirge Aue</i> in 1993. The name "Erzgebirge", <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ore_Mountains_(Central_Europe)" title="Ore Mountains (Central Europe)">Ore Mountains</a> in English, recognizes that the club's home is located in the western part of these mountains. Aue was relegated to the <a href="/wiki/DDR-Liga" title="DDR-Liga">DDR-Liga</a> Staffel B in the 1989–90 season, so it was admitted to the <a href="/wiki/NOFV-Oberliga_S%C3%BCd" title="NOFV-Oberliga Süd">NOFV-Oberliga Süd</a>, which was the fourth tier of the German League between 1991–08, in the 1991–92 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Play_in_united_Germany">Play in united Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Erzgebirge_Aue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Play in united Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the combined football leagues of the newly united Germany, <i>Aue</i> began play in the <a href="/wiki/NOFV-Oberliga_S%C3%BCd" title="NOFV-Oberliga Süd">NOFV-Oberliga Süd</a> (IV). With the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_Nordost" title="Regionalliga Nordost">Regionalliga Nordost</a> (III) in 1994, the club qualified for the new league. The club was moved to the <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_Nord" title="Regionalliga Nord">Regionalliga Nord</a> in 2000, and after a surprising league title there in 2003, it advanced to the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> where it delivered mid-table performances in its first three seasons, but suffered relegation back to the third tier in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Archiv_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Archiv-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fussball_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fussball-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aue became part of the new <a href="/wiki/3._Liga" title="3. Liga">3. Liga</a> in the 2008 season. It finished runner-up in the league in its second season there, earning promotion back to the 2. Bundesliga. After a fifth-place finish in its first season back, the club has struggled against relegation, finishing in the lower third of the table each season since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fussball_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fussball-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Aue made its first appearance in the <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a>, the German Cup, in 1992 and since 2001 has been a regular participant there. They have only managed to make it into the second round on two occasions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reserve_team">Reserve team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Erzgebirge_Aue&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reserve team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The second team side of <i>Wismut Aue</i> played in the DDR-Liga (II) through the first half of the 1970s and had a single season turn there in 1985–86. They also made more than a half dozen appearances in the early rounds of FDGB Pokal (East German Cup) play between 1968 and 1991.</p>
<p>Since 2008 the club's reserve team, now the FC Erzgebirge Aue II, played in the tier five <a href="/wiki/NOFV-Oberliga_S%C3%BCd" title="NOFV-Oberliga Süd">NOFV-Oberliga Süd</a> with a fifth-place finish in 2014 as its best result. At the end of the 2014–15 season the team was withdrawn from competitive football despite finishing eight in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daten_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daten-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team also made a losing appearance in the 1991 and 2007 <a href="/wiki/Saxony_Cup" title="Saxony Cup">Saxony Cup</a> final.</p>
