<p><b>Amy Poehler</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born September 16, 1971)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvgbio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvgbio-2">[2]</a></sup> is an American actress, comedian, director, producer, voice artist and writer. After studying <a href="/wiki/Improvisational_theatre" title="Improvisational theatre">improv</a> at <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Second_City" title="The Second City">Second City</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ImprovOlympic" title="ImprovOlympic">ImprovOlympic</a> in the early 1990s, she went to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1996 to become part of the improvisational comedy troupe <a href="/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade" title="Upright Citizens Brigade">Upright Citizens Brigade</a>. The group's act became a half-hour sketch comedy series on <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a> in 1998. Along with other members of the comedy group, Poehler was a founder of the <a href="/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade_Theatre" title="Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre">Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A cast member on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> from 2001 to 2008, Poehler became co-anchor of SNL's <i><a href="/wiki/Weekend_Update" title="Weekend Update">Weekend Update</a></i> in 2004 alongside friend and colleague <a href="/wiki/Tina_Fey" title="Tina Fey">Tina Fey</a>. She is known for voicing Joy from <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_Out_(2015_film)" title="Inside Out (2015 film)">Inside Out</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sally_O%27Malley" title="Sally O'Malley">Sally O'Malley</a> from the <i><a href="/wiki/Horton_Hears_a_Who!" title="Horton Hears a Who!">Horton Hears a Who!</a></i> movie adaption, Bessie Higgenbottom in the 2008–2011 <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mighty_B!" title="The Mighty B!">The Mighty B!</a></i> and Homily Clock from the American-English dub of <i><a href="/wiki/Arrietty" title="Arrietty">The Secret World of Arrietty</a></i>. From 2009 to 2015, she starred as <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Knope" title="Leslie Knope">Leslie Knope</a> in the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation" title="Parks and Recreation">Parks and Recreation</a></i>, for which she won a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Television_Series_Musical_or_Comedy" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Musical or Comedy">Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Television Musical or Comedy Series</a> in 2014 and a <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award" title="Critics' Choice Television Award">Critics' Choice Award</a> for Best Actress in a Comedy Series in 2012.</p>
<p>Poehler served as an <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> on the Swedish-American sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Sweden_(2014_TV_series)" title="Welcome to Sweden (2014 TV series)">Welcome to Sweden</a></i>, along with her brother <a href="/wiki/Greg_Poehler" title="Greg Poehler">Greg Poehler</a>. The series aired on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>. She is also an executive producer on <i><a href="/wiki/Broad_City" title="Broad City">Broad City</a></i> which airs on Comedy Central, and appeared in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Broad_City_episodes#Season_1_.282014.29" title="List of Broad City episodes">season one</a> finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AV_Club_S01E01_review_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AV_Club_S01E01_review-4">[4]</a></sup> Since August 2015, she has served as an executive producer on the <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/Difficult_People" title="Difficult People">Difficult People</a></i>, which stars her former <i>Parks and Recreation</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Billy_Eichner" title="Billy Eichner">Billy Eichner</a> and comedian <a href="/wiki/Julie_Klausner" title="Julie Klausner">Julie Klausner</a>, the latter of whom is the creator of the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In December 2015, Poehler received a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> for her contributions in television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> An 18-time <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominee, she won the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Guest_Actress_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Poehler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Poehler was born in <a href="/wiki/Newton,_Massachusetts" title="Newton, Massachusetts">Newton, Massachusetts</a>, to school teachers Eileen and William Poehler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-biography.com-7">[7]</a></sup> Her brother, <a href="/wiki/Greg_Poehler" title="Greg Poehler">Greg Poehler</a>, is a producer and actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She grew up in nearby <a href="/wiki/Burlington,_Massachusetts" title="Burlington, Massachusetts">Burlington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography.com_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-biography.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_bio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo_bio-9">[9]</a></sup> Her father is of <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> ancestry, while her mother is of Irish descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She was raised as a <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" title="Catholicism">Catholic</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-actors_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-actors-11">[11]</a></sup> and graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burlington_High_School_(Burlington,_Massachusetts)" title="Burlington High School (Burlington, Massachusetts)">Burlington High School</a> in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>While attending <a href="/wiki/Boston_College" title="Boston College">Boston College</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvgbio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvgbio-2">[2]</a></sup> Poehler was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Improvisational_theatre" title="Improvisational theatre">improv</a> comedy troupe My Mother's Fleabag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography.com_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-biography.com-7">[7]</a></sup> She graduated from Boston College with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in media and communications in 1993 and moved to <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, where she studied improv at <a href="/wiki/The_Second_City" title="The Second City">Second City</a> with friend and future co-star <a href="/wiki/Tina_Fey" title="Tina Fey">Tina Fey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_bio_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo_bio-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She also studied with <a href="/wiki/Del_Close" title="Del Close">Del Close</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ImprovOlympic" title="ImprovOlympic">ImprovOlympic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_bio_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo_bio-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Poehler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upright_Citizens_Brigade"><i>Upright Citizens Brigade</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Poehler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Upright Citizens Brigade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During her time at Second City and Improv Olympic in Chicago, Poehler studied alongside <a href="/wiki/Matt_Besser" title="Matt Besser">Matt Besser</a> under Del Close and Charna Halpern. Poehler and Besser joined with <a href="/wiki/Ian_Roberts_(American_actor)" title="Ian Roberts (American actor)">Ian Roberts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matt_Walsh_(comedian)" title="Matt Walsh (comedian)">Matt Walsh</a> to found the <i><a href="/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade" title="Upright Citizens Brigade">Upright Citizens Brigade</a></i> (UCB).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zinoman.2C_Jason.3B_Get_the_Laughs_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zinoman.2C_Jason.3B_Get_the_Laughs-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hones.2C_Ree.3B_UCB_gang_look_back_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hones.2C_Ree.3B_UCB_gang_look_back-15">[15]</a></sup> The group performed sketch and improv around Chicago before moving to New York City in 1996. Immediately after moving to New York, the group quickly scored a TV gig, appearing as sketch regulars on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="Late Night with Conan O'Brien">Late Night with Conan O'Brien</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-actors_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-actors-11">[11]</a></sup> Other early members of UCB included <a href="/wiki/Horatio_Sanz" title="Horatio Sanz">Horatio Sanz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_McKay" title="Adam McKay">Adam McKay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Roman" title="Rick Roman">Rick Roman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Flynn" title="Neil Flynn">Neil Flynn</a>.</p>
<p>In 1998, <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a> debuted the group's eponymous half-hour sketch comedy series. During the show's second season, the group opened an improv theatre/training center in New York City at 161 W. 22nd Street, occupying the space of a former strip club. The UCB theatre held shows seven nights a week in addition to offering classes in <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a> writing and improv.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-actors_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-actors-11">[11]</a></sup> In the summer of 2000, Comedy Central canceled the <i>Upright Citizens Brigade</i> program after its third season, though the UCB Theatre continues to operate. The foursome continue to work together in many projects, and frequently perform together in live improv shows at their comedy theatres in both New York and Los Angeles.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saturday_Night_Live"><i>Saturday Night Live</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Poehler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Saturday Night Live">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Poehler joined the cast of <i>SNL</i> during the <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live_(season_27)" title="Saturday Night Live (season 27)">2001–2002 season</a>, her debut episode being the first one produced after the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">9/11 attacks</a> — with host <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a>, musical guest <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, and New York City Mayor <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Giuliani" title="Rudy Giuliani">Rudy Giuliani</a> as a special guest. Poehler was promoted from featured player to full cast member in her first season on the show, making her only the third person to have earned this distinction (after <a href="/wiki/Harry_Shearer" title="Harry Shearer">Harry Shearer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Murphy" title="Eddie Murphy">Eddie Murphy</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beginning with the <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live_(season_30)" title="Saturday Night Live (season 30)">2004–2005 season</a>, she co-anchored "<a href="/wiki/Weekend_Update" title="Weekend Update">Weekend Update</a>" with Tina Fey, replacing the newly departed <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Fallon" title="Jimmy Fallon">Jimmy Fallon</a>. In a <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> interview, Fey said that with Poehler co-anchoring, there now is "double the sexual tension". When Fey left after the <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live_(season_31)" title="Saturday Night Live (season 31)">2005–2006 season</a> to devote time to the sitcom she created, <i><a href="/wiki/30_Rock" title="30 Rock">30 Rock</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Seth_Meyers" title="Seth Meyers">Seth Meyers</a> joined Poehler at the anchor desk. In 2008, Poehler was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> the first <i>SNL</i> cast member recognized in this category. She was heavily favored to win by many critics, but ultimately lost to <a href="/wiki/Jean_Smart" title="Jean Smart">Jean Smart</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Samantha_Who%3F" title="Samantha Who?">Samantha Who?</a></i>. She was nominated once again in 2009, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Chenoweth" title="Kristin Chenoweth">Kristin Chenoweth</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i>. On September 13, 2008, the <i>SNL</i> season premiere opened with Fey and Poehler as <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Palin" title="Sarah Palin">Sarah Palin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>, respectively, performing a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Nonpartisan_Message_from_Governor_Sarah_Palin_%26_Senator_Hillary_Clinton" title="A Nonpartisan Message from Governor Sarah Palin &amp; Senator Hillary Clinton">joint political campaign spot</a>".</p>
<p>It was officially announced on September 16, 2008, that Poehler would be leaving <i>SNL</i> in October to give birth to her first child. On the October 25, 2008 episode, it was announced by "Weekend Update" co-anchor <a href="/wiki/Seth_Meyers" title="Seth Meyers">Seth Meyers</a>, who anchored the segment alone, "Amy Poehler is not here because she is having a baby", to wild applause from the audience. At the end of "Weekend Update", special guest <a href="/wiki/Maya_Rudolph" title="Maya Rudolph">Maya Rudolph</a> and current cast member <a href="/wiki/Kenan_Thompson" title="Kenan Thompson">Kenan Thompson</a> sang a custom rendition of "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Take_My_Eyes_Off_You" title="Can't Take My Eyes Off You">Can't Take My Eyes Off You</a>" for Poehler, changing the words: "We love you Amy, and we just can't wait to meet your baby!" Meyers signed off: "For Weekend Update, I'm Seth Meyers — we love you Amy!"</p>
<p>Poehler returned to the show on November 3, 2008, during the "<i>SNL</i> Presidential Bash '08", "hosting" as Hillary Clinton. The Bash was pre-taped from scenes shot between September and October. Her return to <i>SNL</i> after her pregnancy was on December 6, 2008, where she stayed for two weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> During "Weekend Update", on December 13, she thanked her family, friends, and fans for the continued support and announced that it would be her last show. On April 18, 2009, a <i>Saturday Night Live</i> special, "The Best of Amy Poehler", aired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Poehler returned for "Weekend Update" (signing off with "...and he's Seth Meyers") and joined the "chorus" for <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Goodnight_Saigon" title="Goodnight Saigon">Goodnight Saigon</a>" (along with the <i>SNL</i> cast and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maya_Rudolph" title="Maya Rudolph">Maya Rudolph</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norm_Macdonald" title="Norm Macdonald">Norm Macdonald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rudd" title="Paul Rudd">Paul Rudd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artie_Lange" title="Artie Lange">Artie Lange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anne_Hathaway" title="Anne Hathaway">Anne Hathaway</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>) on the <i>SNL</i> season finale on May 16, 2009.</p>
<p>Poehler returned to the "Weekend Update" desk in the fall of 2009 with Meyers, for two "WU" <a href="/wiki/Weekend_Update#Seth_Meyers_.26_.28temporarily.29_Amy_Poehler_.282009.E2.80.93present.29" title="Weekend Update">Thursday episodes</a>, which led directly into <i><a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation" title="Parks and Recreation">Parks and Recreation</a></i>. She also returned to <i>Saturday Night Live</i> for a special <a href="/wiki/Mother%27s_Day" title="Mother's Day">Mother's Day</a> episode on May 8, 2010, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Betty_White" title="Betty White">Betty White</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poehler returned to <i>Saturday Night Live</i> on September 25, 2010, to host the Season 36 opening episode with performer <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> She returned once again for the <i>Saturday Night Live</i> special, "The Women of <i>SNL</i>". The special aired on November 1, 2010. Between the time she left and the time she hosted, she also returned several times to join Seth Meyers in their "Really!?! With Seth and Amy" segment on Weekend Update. The "Really!?!" segment has aired since with Meyers speaking doing the segment alone, although he was once accompanied by special guest <a href="/wiki/Kermit_the_Frog" title="Kermit the Frog">Kermit the Frog</a> and another time, with guest host <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart" title="Kevin Hart">Kevin Hart</a>. Poehler appeared as a guest in the December 17, 2011 Jimmy-Fallon-hosted episode, starring in the opening sketch and a Weekend Update joke-off with Fallon, Meyers, and Tina Fey. She appeared once again on the February 18, 2012 Maya Rudolph-hosted episode, reprising her role as Betty Caruso in the Bronx Beat sketch (with co-host Maya Rudolph), co-anchoring Weekend Update as well as a segment of "Really!?! With Seth and Amy", and appearing briefly as Hillary Clinton at the end of the "The Obama Show" sketch.</p>
<p>In 2015, during <i>Saturday Night Live</i>'s 40th Anniversary show, she returned to anchor "Weekend Update", this time with Tina Fey and <a href="/wiki/Jane_Curtin" title="Jane Curtin">Jane Curtin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>In July 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> magazine reported that Poehler was in final negotiations to star in a series by writers <a href="/wiki/Greg_Daniels" title="Greg Daniels">Greg Daniels</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Schur" title="Mike Schur">Mike Schur</a>, set to air on Thursdays before <i><a href="/wiki/The_Office" title="The Office">The Office</a></i>, starting January 2009 on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officespinoffvariety_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-officespinoffvariety-23">[23]</a></sup> On July 21, 2008, NBC announced Poehler's new series, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation_(TV_series)" title="Parks and Recreation (TV series)">Parks and Recreation</a></i>, saying the project would not be a direct spin-off of <i>The Office</i>, as previously speculated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcwantsboth_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-nbcwantsboth-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poehler joined an ensemble cast including <a href="/wiki/Aziz_Ansari" title="Aziz Ansari">Aziz Ansari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rashida_Jones" title="Rashida Jones">Rashida Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Pratt" title="Chris Pratt">Chris Pratt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_Plaza" title="Aubrey Plaza">Aubrey Plaza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Schneider_(actor)" title="Paul Schneider (actor)">Paul Schneider</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Offerman" title="Nick Offerman">Nick Offerman</a>, and at the end of season two, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Scott_(actor)" title="Adam Scott (actor)">Adam Scott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Lowe" title="Rob Lowe">Rob Lowe</a>. Poehler plays Deputy Director of the Parks Department, <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Knope" title="Leslie Knope">Leslie Knope</a>, in the fictional city of Pawnee, Indiana. After the <a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation_(season_1)" title="Parks and Recreation (season 1)">first season</a> received mixed reception, the show's <a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation_(season_2)" title="Parks and Recreation (season 2)">second</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation_(season_3)" title="Parks and Recreation (season 3)">third</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation_(season_4)" title="Parks and Recreation (season 4)">fourth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation_(season_5)" title="Parks and Recreation (season 5)">fifth</a> have been well received by critics, and Poehler received a <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nomination for her role. Poehler was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Advocate" title="The Advocate">The Advocate</a></i> for her role in the show.</p>
<p>Poehler has written four episodes of the series, the first being "<a href="/wiki/Telethon_(Parks_and_Recreation)" title="Telethon (Parks and Recreation)">Telethon</a>" in season two, which was the episode Poehler sent in as her <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> submission in 2010. Her second was the episode entitled "<a href="/wiki/The_Fight_(Parks_and_Recreation)" title="The Fight (Parks and Recreation)">The Fight</a>", which aired on May 12, 2011. She both wrote and directed the season four episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Debate_(Parks_and_Recreation)" title="The Debate (Parks and Recreation)">The Debate</a>", for which she received a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Writing_for_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Comedy Series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award_for_Television:_Episodic_Comedy" title="Writers Guild of America Award for Television: Episodic Comedy">Writers Guild of America Award for Television: Episodic Comedy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> She co-wrote the final episode of the series, "One Last Ride" with co-creator Mike Schur, which aired on February 24, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poehler was again nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy in 2011. She sent the episode "<a href="/wiki/Flu_Season_(Parks_and_Recreation)" title="Flu Season (Parks and Recreation)">Flu Season</a>" as her submission, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Melissa_McCarthy" title="Melissa McCarthy">Melissa McCarthy</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Mike_%26_Molly" title="Mike &amp; Molly">Mike &amp; Molly</a></i>. She was nominated an additional two times in 2012, for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy (having submitted the episode "<a href="/wiki/Win,_Lose,_or_Draw_(Parks_and_Recreation)" title="Win, Lose, or Draw (Parks and Recreation)">Win, Lose, or Draw</a>"), as well as Outstanding Writing for a Comedy Series for "<a href="/wiki/The_Debate_(Parks_and_Recreation)" title="The Debate (Parks and Recreation)">The Debate</a>". She lost the former to <a href="/wiki/Julia_Louis-Dreyfus" title="Julia Louis-Dreyfus">Julia Louis-Dreyfus</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Veep_(TV_series)" title="Veep (TV series)">Veep</a></i>, and the latter to <a href="/wiki/Louis_C.K." title="Louis C.K.">Louis C.K.</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Louie_(TV_series)" title="Louie (TV series)">Louie</a></i>. She was nominated for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy in 2013. In 2014, she won the Golden Globe for Best Actress in a Television Series – Comedy at the <a href="/wiki/71st_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="71st Golden Globe Awards">71st Golden Globe Awards</a>, which she co-hosted with <a href="/wiki/Tina_Fey" title="Tina Fey">Tina Fey</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011, the show won a <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Award" title="Peabody Award">Peabody Award</a> for "developing a hilarious venue to explore the good side of American democracy in an age when that side is so rarely on display".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Welcome_to_Sweden"><i>Welcome to Sweden</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Poehler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Welcome to Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Welcome to Sweden</i> is a Swedish sitcom that premiered on TV4 on March 21, 2014. On July 10, 2014, the series began airing on NBC in the United States, and on The Comedy Network in Canada. It is based on the experiences of Greg Poehler, who moved with his girlfriend to her native country of Sweden in 2006.</p>
<p>On April 7, 2014, TV4 picked up the show for a second season of ten episodes. NBC followed suit on August 4, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Season two premiered on July 19, 2015 in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The series was canceled by NBC on July 28, 2015, due to low ratings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Amy Poehler makes cameo appearances in multiple episodes as the former boss of her brother's character. She is also co-executive producer with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>Poehler appeared in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Sisters_(2015_film)" title="Sisters (2015 film)">Sisters</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wet_Hot_American_Summer" title="Wet Hot American Summer">Wet Hot American Summer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mean_Girls" title="Mean Girls">Mean Girls</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Deuce_Bigalow:_Male_Gigolo" title="Deuce Bigalow: Male Gigolo">Deuce Bigalow: Male Gigolo</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tenacious_D_in_The_Pick_of_Destiny" title="Tenacious D in The Pick of Destiny">Tenacious D in The Pick of Destiny</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blades_of_Glory" title="Blades of Glory">Blades of Glory</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Envy_(2004_film)" title="Envy (2004 film)">Envy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_the_Third" title="Shrek the Third">Shrek the Third</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Woodcock" title="Mr. Woodcock">Mr. Woodcock</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet_2" title="Hamlet 2">Hamlet 2</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_Out_(2015_film)" title="Inside Out (2015 film)">Inside Out</a></i>. She appeared in various comedy segments on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="Late Night with Conan O'Brien">Late Night with Conan O'Brien</a></i>, often playing her recurring role as <a href="/wiki/Andy_Richter" title="Andy Richter">Andy Richter</a>'s little sister, Stacy, as well as appearances as a recurring character in two episodes of the college dramedy, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undeclared_(TV_show)" title="Undeclared (TV show)">Undeclared</a></i>.</p>
<p>Poehler appears in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Southland_Tales" title="Southland Tales">Southland Tales</a></i>, which premiered on May 21, 2006, at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2006 Cannes Film Festival">2006 Cannes Film Festival</a>. In 2008, she appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Horton_Hears_a_Who!_(film)" title="Horton Hears a Who! (film)">Horton Hears a Who!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet_2" title="Hamlet 2">Hamlet 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Baby_Mama_(film)" title="Baby Mama (film)">Baby Mama</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Breakdown" title="Spring Breakdown">Spring Breakdown</a></i>. She also co-created an animated series for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_(TV_channel)" title="Nickelodeon (TV channel)">Nickelodeon</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mighty_B!" title="The Mighty B!">The Mighty B!</a></i>, about Bessie Higgenbottom, a "sweet, merit-badge-obsessed girl scout", to which she lends her vocal talents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2009, Poehler earned a <a href="/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award" title="Daytime Emmy Award">Daytime Emmy Award</a> nomination for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Performer_In_An_Animated_Program" title="Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Performer In An Animated Program">Outstanding Performer in an Animated Program</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2008, Poehler began producing a digital series with two of her friends, Meredith Walker and <a href="/wiki/Amy_Miles" title="Amy Miles">Amy Miles</a>, titled <i>Smart Girls at the Party</i>. Launched by the Texas-based ON Networks distributor, the official press release stated that the show "aims to help girls find confidence in their own aspirations and talents." In each episode, Poehler interviews a girl with a "unique talent, community interest or point of view." The first season of <i>Smart Girls at the Party</i> premiered online November 17, 2008, with <a href="/wiki/Mattel" title="Mattel">Mattel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Barbie" title="Barbie">Barbie</a> signed on as the lead sponsor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> <i>Smart Girls at the Party</i> returned in 2012 as part of <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>'s <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Original_Channel_Initiative" title="YouTube Original Channel Initiative">YouTube Original Channel Initiative</a> that focused upon the creation of new content. The new <i>Smart Girls at the Party</i> YouTube Channel went live on July 2, 2012, including new episodes of <i>Smart Girls at the Party</i> along with additional shows by Poehler, Walker, and Miles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amy_Poehler.E2.80.99s_.E2.80.98Smart_Girls.27_Kicks_Off_With_Mattel.E2.80.99s_Barbie_on_Board_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amy_Poehler.E2.80.99s_.E2.80.98Smart_Girls.27_Kicks_Off_With_Mattel.E2.80.99s_Barbie_on_Board-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Poehler was included on <i>TIME's</i> "<a href="/wiki/Time_100" title="Time 100">100 most influential people in the world</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She also delivered the Class Day address to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>'s class of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poehler and Fey hosted the Golden Globe Awards ceremony for the first time in 2013. Their inaugural appearance garnered attention due to a joke directed at <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>, who later responded with a <a href="/wiki/Madeleine_Albright" title="Madeleine Albright">Madeleine Albright</a> quote: "There's a special place in hell for women who don't help other women." Poehler's response to Swift's comment, made as part of a <i>Vanity Fair</i> interview, was humorous, agreeing that she will go to hell, but for "other reasons".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poehler hosted the Golden Globes ceremony with Fey again in 2014 as part of a three-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time-40">[40]</a></sup> Gilbert Cruz, of the Vulture website, wrote: "They killed it last year with their opening monologue and they did so again this year."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2014, the <i>Smart Girls at the Party</i> project, described as an "online community" in the announcement, was acquired by the Legendary Entertainment company in mid-October. Poehler said to the media: "We at Smart Girls are excited to be working with Legendary and look forward to providing funny and inspirational content for all of the goofballs out there." By the time of the deal, the terms of which were not disclosed, over five million views were registered on its YouTube channel and over 550,000 fans had liked the initiative on Facebook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On the <i>Smart Girls</i> YouTube channel, viewers have the opportunity to ask for life advice from Poehler in segments called <i>Ask Amy</i>.</p>
<p>Poehler's memoir, <i><a href="/wiki/Yes_Please" title="Yes Please">Yes Please</a></i>, was published on October 28, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> She explained in a promotional interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" title="National Public Radio">National Public Radio</a> (NPR) that she was "used to writing in characters and not really writing about myself ... it was easier to share the early parts of my life rather than my own current events." Topics covered in the book include body image, parenthood, and learning about the limitations of physical appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Poehler and Fey hosted the Golden Globe Awards ceremony for the third successive time in 2015, confirming prior to the event that the third time would be their last.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time-40">[40]</a></sup> <i>Rolling Stone</i> magazine wrote afterward that the pair "left no superstar unscathed during their riotous opening monologue," in which they "casually roasted the assembled masses". <i><a href="/wiki/The_Interview" title="The Interview">The Interview</a></i> (2014), <a href="/wiki/Bill_Cosby" title="Bill Cosby">Bill Cosby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Carell" title="Steve Carell">Steve Carell</a> were among the numerous subjects covered in the routine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2016, BBC America announced it is developing a scripted series called <i>Zero Motivation</i>. The project is being exec produced by Brooke Posch and Poehler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amy_Poehler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Poehler married actor <a href="/wiki/Will_Arnett" title="Will Arnett">Will Arnett</a> on August 29, 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> and had a recurring role on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Arrested_Development_(TV_series)" title="Arrested Development (TV series)">Arrested Development</a></i> as the wife of Arnett's character <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gob_Bluth" title="Gob Bluth">Gob Bluth</a>. They also played a quasi-<a href="/wiki/Incest" title="Incest">incestuous</a> brother-sister ice skating team in the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Blades_of_Glory" title="Blades of Glory">Blades of Glory</a></i>, and appeared together in <i><a href="/wiki/Horton_Hears_a_Who!_(film)" title="Horton Hears a Who! (film)">Horton Hears a Who!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/On_Broadway_(film)" title="On Broadway (film)">On Broadway</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Breakdown" title="Spring Breakdown">Spring Breakdown</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters_vs._Aliens" title="Monsters vs. Aliens">Monsters vs. Aliens</a></i>, along with Arnett having a guest appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/Parks_and_Recreation" title="Parks and Recreation">Parks and Recreation</a></i>. Both also did voice acting in <i><a href="/wiki/Arrietty" title="Arrietty">The Secret World of Arrietty</a></i>.</p>
<p>Together, Poehler and Arnett have two sons: Archie (born October 2008) and Abel (born August 2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Poehler and Arnett announced on September 6, 2012, that they were separating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Arnett filed for divorce on April 8, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The divorce was finalized on July 29, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
