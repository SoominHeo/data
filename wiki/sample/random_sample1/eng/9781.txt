<p><b>Aimee-Ffion Edwards</b> (born November 21, 1987) is a Welsh actress from <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-1">[1]</a></sup> She is perhaps best known for playing <a href="/wiki/Sketch_(Skins_character)" title="Sketch (Skins character)">Sketch</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Skins_(UK_TV_series)" title="Skins (UK TV series)">Skins</a></i> and Esme in <i><a href="/wiki/Peaky_Blinders_(TV_series)" title="Peaky Blinders (TV series)">Peaky Blinders</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aimee-Ffion_Edwards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aimee-Ffion Edwards was born in Newport, Wales. She attended Ysgol Gymraeg Casnewydd (Newport Welsh medium primary school) and Ysgol Gyfun Gwynllyw. She played for a local youth rugby team, the only girl in a boys' team, until she was 14. She would often go from ballet lessons to playing for the rugby team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She took a drama A-level at school, and joined the National Youth Theatre of Wales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is fluent in English and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aimee-Ffion_Edwards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aimee-Ffion_Edwards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Edwards appeared in the 2002 short film <i>DÅµr Dwfn</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was training to be a classical singer when she appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/Pop_Idol" title="Pop Idol">Pop Idol</a></i>-type <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh language</a> TV show called <i><a href="/wiki/Wawffactor" title="Wawffactor">Wawffactor</a></i> in 2006 and became runner-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Edwards made her television debut as the character Sketch in the <a href="/wiki/E4_(TV_channel)" title="E4 (TV channel)">E4</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Skins_(UK_TV_series)" title="Skins (UK TV series)">Skins</a></i> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline2_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, she appeared in the Valentine's Day episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Casualty_(TV_series)" title="Casualty (TV series)">Casualty</a></i> ("Stand By Me"), as a teenager who finds a replacement speed-date in the wards of Holby City Hospital, and ends up involved in a serious gun-related incident. In the first episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Casualty_1909" title="Casualty 1909">Casualty 1909</a></i> she played a young prostitute called Deborah Lynch, who was being abused by her father.</p>
<p>In 2010, she appeared in an episode of the supernatural BBC drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(UK_TV_series)" title="Being Human (UK TV series)">Being Human</a></i> as a theatre usher, who is also a ghost.</p>
<p>In 2011, she appeared in series 2 of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Luther_(TV_series)" title="Luther (TV series)">Luther</a></i>, playing the character Jenny Jones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline2_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, she appeared in <a href="/wiki/Sky_Atlantic" title="Sky Atlantic">Sky Atlantic</a>'s four-part series <i>Walking And Talking</i>, a spin-off from an episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_One" title="Sky One">Sky One</a>'s Christmas series of shorts <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Crackers" title="Little Crackers">Little Crackers</a></i>. Aimee played Mary, friend of Kath, played by Ami Metcalf, in these stories written by Kathy Burke and based on her own teenage years.</p>
<p>In 2013 and 2014, she appeared as Esme, the wife of John Shelby, in the BBC series <i><a href="/wiki/Peaky_Blinders_(TV_series)" title="Peaky Blinders (TV series)">Peaky Blinders</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2014, Edwards appeared in two <a href="/wiki/BBC_Cymru_Wales" title="BBC Cymru Wales">BBC Cymru Wales</a> television productions celebrating the centenary of <a href="/wiki/Dylan_Thomas" title="Dylan Thomas">Dylan Thomas</a>: as part of an all-Welsh cast in a television adaptation of Thomas' radio drama <i><a href="/wiki/Under_Milk_Wood" title="Under Milk Wood">Under Milk Wood</a></i>, playing the part 'Laugharne Voice';<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and as Marianne in <i><a href="/wiki/A_Poet_in_New_York" title="A Poet in New York">A Poet in New York</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Davies_(writer)" title="Andrew Davies (writer)">Andrew Davies</a>' dramatisation of Thomas' last days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Poet_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Poet-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, she appeared as Katy in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Harrowing_(Inside_No._9)" title="The Harrowing (Inside No. 9)">The Harrowing</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-1">[1]</a></sup> the sixth episode of the first series of <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_No._9" title="Inside No. 9">Inside No. 9</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Pemberton" title="Steve Pemberton">Steve Pemberton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reece_Shearsmith" title="Reece Shearsmith">Reece Shearsmith</a>. She also appeared as Sophie in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Detectorists" title="Detectorists">Detectorists</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, she appeared as Sian in <a href="/wiki/Death_in_Paradise_(TV_series)" title="Death in Paradise (TV series)">Death In Paradise</a> as a young girl who is murdered.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aimee-Ffion_Edwards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Edwards made her stage debut in 2008 in <i>SH*T-M*X</i> at the Trafalgar Studios in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, she performed in <a href="/wiki/Jez_Butterworth" title="Jez Butterworth">Jez Butterworth</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Jerusalem_(play)" title="Jerusalem (play)">Jerusalem</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Court_Theatre" title="Royal Court Theatre">Royal Court Theatre</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Mackenzie_Crook" title="Mackenzie Crook">Mackenzie Crook</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline2_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, she made her <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut, reprising her role in <i>Jerusalem</i> at the Music Box Theatre, New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-1">[1]</a></sup> She appeared in the London revival of <i>Jerusalem</i> later that year.</p>
<p>In 2012, she appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Recruiting_Officer" title="The Recruiting Officer">The Recruiting Officer</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Donmar_Warehouse" title="Donmar Warehouse">Donmar Warehouse</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WalesOnline_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-1">[1]</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Marius_von_Mayenburg" title="Marius von Mayenburg">Marius von Mayenburg</a>'s <i>Fireface</i> at the Young Vic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, she appeared as Avonia Bunn in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trelawny_of_the_Wells" title="Trelawny of the Wells">Trelawny of the Wells</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Donmar_Warehouse" title="Donmar Warehouse">Donmar Warehouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
