<p><b>Avery Antonio Bradley Jr.</b> (born November 26, 1990) is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). He played college basketball for <a href="/wiki/Texas_Longhorns_men%27s_basketball" title="Texas Longhorns men's basketball">Texas</a> before being drafted 19th overall by the Celtics in the <a href="/wiki/2010_NBA_draft" title="2010 NBA draft">2010 NBA draft</a>. A longtime starter for the Celtics, he has twice been recognized as an <a href="/wiki/NBA_All-Defensive_Team" title="NBA All-Defensive Team">NBA All-Defensive Team</a> member.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bradley was born on November 26, 1990 in <a href="/wiki/Tacoma,_Washington" title="Tacoma, Washington">Tacoma, Washington</a> to Alicia Jones-Bradley and Avery Bradley, Sr. He has two older brothers, one older sister, and one younger brother. His mother worked in a welfare office, while his father had a 22-year military career. After the two divorced in 2001, Bradley lived with his mother but maintained a strong relationship with his father, whose career took him all over the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He became a <a href="/wiki/Texas_Longhorns_men%27s_basketball" title="Texas Longhorns men's basketball">Texas</a> fan when his family lived in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington, Texas</a> from 2001 to 2004. He and his family moved back to Tacoma in the summer of 2004, prior to his eighth grade year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Bradley was <a href="/wiki/Amateur_Athletic_Union" title="Amateur Athletic Union">AAU</a> teammates with future Boston Celtics teammate <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Thomas_(basketball)" title="Isaiah Thomas (basketball)">Isaiah Thomas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_career">High school career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: High school career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bradley was ranked as one of the top high school basketball players in the class of 2009. He was rated #1 in the <a href="/wiki/ESPNU" title="ESPNU">ESPNU</a>100,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> #4 by <a href="/wiki/Rivals.com" title="Rivals.com">Rivals.com</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and #5 by Scout.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Bradley led <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Henderson_International_School" title="The Henderson International School">Findlay College Prep</a> to the National High School Basketball championship against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oak_Hill_Academy_(Virginia)" title="Oak Hill Academy (Virginia)">Oak Hill Academy</a>, and won 56–53. After leading Findlay to the championship, Bradley was named National High School Basketball Player of the Year by <a href="/wiki/Parade_(magazine)" title="Parade (magazine)">Parade Magazine</a>. He played in the 2009 <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s_All-American_Game" title="McDonald's All-American Game">McDonald's All-American Game</a> and won the 2009 McDonald's High School All-American Dunk Contest. Before transferring to Findlay Prep for his senior season, Bradley was a three-year starter at <a href="/wiki/Bellarmine_Preparatory_School" title="Bellarmine Preparatory School">Bellarmine Preparatory School</a> in Tacoma, Washington, and along with <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a> recruit <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Gaddy" title="Abdul Gaddy">Abdul Gaddy</a>, led Bellarmine Prep to the Class 4A state semi-finals with a 25-4 mark as a junior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bradley attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Austin" title="University of Texas at Austin">University of Texas at Austin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio-7">[7]</a></sup> Bradley found the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Longhorns_men%27s_basketball" title="Texas Longhorns men's basketball">Texas Longhorns basketball</a> program appealing in part because he had spent parts of his childhood in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington</a>, where he became a fan of <a href="/wiki/T._J._Ford" title="T. J. Ford">T. J. Ford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a freshman in 2009–10, Bradley averaged 11.6 points for the Longhorns and established himself as one of the top defensive guards in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He subsequently earned Big 12 All-Rookie Team and All-Big 12 Honorable Mention honors.</p>
<p>In April 2010, Bradley declared for the <a href="/wiki/NBA_draft" title="NBA draft">NBA draft</a>, forgoing his final three years of college eligibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boston_Celtics_.282010.E2.80.93present.29">Boston Celtics (2010–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Boston Celtics (2010–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311_season">2010–11 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010–11 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bradley was selected with the 19th overall pick in the <a href="/wiki/2010_NBA_draft" title="2010 NBA draft">2010 NBA draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a>. On July 2, 2010, he signed his rookie scale contract with the Celtics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The same day, he underwent successful ankle surgery and subsequently missed the 2010 NBA Summer League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Still just 19 years old, Bradley joined a Celtics team that was one of the best in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Conference_(NBA)" title="Eastern Conference (NBA)">Eastern Conference</a>. Thus, he did not see his first regular season action until the fourteenth game of the season, a 23-point win over the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> in which Bradley scored two points and committed two turnovers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> On January 14, 2011, Bradley was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/Maine_Red_Claws" title="Maine Red Claws">Maine Red Claws</a> of the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Development_League" title="NBA Development League">NBA Development League</a> and on the same day made his debut game for the team, playing 21 minutes and scoring 11 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Following a <a href="/wiki/Spinal_cord" title="Spinal cord">spinal cord</a> injury to <a href="/wiki/Marquis_Daniels" title="Marquis Daniels">Marquis Daniels</a> during a game against the <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a> on February 6, 2011, Bradley was recalled by the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> on February 7, and joined the team for the game against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bobcats" title="Charlotte Bobcats">Charlotte Bobcats</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the one Celtics game of Bradley's rookie season in which he played more than 15 minutes, Bradley scored 20 points to go with three rebounds, two assists and two steals. However, he played ten or more minutes in just two other NBA games and did not appear in any of the Celtics' postseason contests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> On June 30, 2011, the Celtics exercised their third-year team option on Bradley's rookie scale contract, extending the contract through the 2012–13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>In October 2011, Bradley signed with <a href="/wiki/Hapoel_Jerusalem_B.C." title="Hapoel Jerusalem B.C.">Hapoel Jerusalem</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Basketball_Premier_League" title="Israeli Basketball Premier League">Israeli Basketball Premier League</a> for the duration of the <a href="/wiki/2011_NBA_lockout" title="2011 NBA lockout">NBA lockout</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-euro_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-euro-17">[17]</a></sup> He played three games with the team, averaging 13.7 points per game.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_NBA_season" title="2011–12 NBA season">2011–12 NBA season</a>, Bradley enjoyed much more playing time and was promoted to a starting role following an injury to <a href="/wiki/Ray_Allen" title="Ray Allen">Ray Allen</a>. Bradley's scoring output increased significantly during the season, and he managed a career high 28 points against the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> on April 20, 2012. He also received praise for his tremendous hustle and defense, including memorable blocks on <a href="/wiki/Dwyane_Wade" title="Dwyane Wade">Dwyane Wade</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Westbrook" title="Russell Westbrook">Russell Westbrook</a>, among others. However, Bradley suffered a <a href="/wiki/Dislocated_shoulder" title="Dislocated shoulder">dislocated shoulder</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2012_NBA_Playoffs" title="2012 NBA Playoffs">2012 NBA Playoffs</a>. This injury, which led to season-ending surgery, was a significant setback to the Celtics, who lost in seven games to the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a> in the Conference Finals.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313_season">2012–13 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2012–13 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On October 30, 2012, the Celtics exercised their fourth-year team option on Bradley's rookie scale contract, extending the contract through the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> With Bradley still sidelined for the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NBA_season" title="2012–13 NBA season">2012–13 NBA season</a>, the aging Celtics struggled with the duo of <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Lee" title="Courtney Lee">Courtney Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Terry" title="Jason Terry">Jason Terry</a> receiving the majority of minutes at <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">shooting guard</a>. On January 2, 2013, Bradley returned to action against the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a>, regaining his spot as the starting <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">shooting guard</a> and providing a significant boost to the team, evident by their winning six out of Bradley's first seven games back. However, the season was ultimately a disappointment for both Bradley and the Celtics. Although he led the league in fewest points per play allowed on defense, at 0.697,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redemption_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-redemption-19">[19]</a></sup> he struggled offensively, shooting just 40.2 percent from the field and managing 15 or more points just five times in 50 regular season games. The Celtics lost any realistic chance of contention when point guard <a href="/wiki/Rajon_Rondo" title="Rajon Rondo">Rajon Rondo</a> went down with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torn_ACL" title="Torn ACL">torn ACL</a> on January 27,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> leaving them without their most dynamic player. After falling behind three games to none against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> in the first round of the playoffs, Boston mounted a furious comeback, winning two games and narrowly losing Game 6. Bradley, whose play in the series had mirrored the ineffectiveness of the team, provided a gutsy effort at the end of the game, making all four of his shots and stealing the ball three times in the last ten minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redemption_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-redemption-19">[19]</a></sup> The Celtics entered the offseason with an eye toward the future and Bradley a vital part of their plans to rebuild a contender.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Celtics ended an era on the day of the <a href="/wiki/2013_NBA_draft" title="2013 NBA draft">2013 NBA draft</a>, trading ageing stars <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Garnett" title="Kevin Garnett">Kevin Garnett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Pierce" title="Paul Pierce">Paul Pierce</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Jason_Terry" title="Jason Terry">Jason Terry</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets" title="Brooklyn Nets">Brooklyn Nets</a>. The Celtics also declined to sign Bradley to a contract extension before the October 31, 2013 deadline, allowing him to become a restricted free agent in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> However, new Celtics coach <a href="/wiki/Brad_Stevens" title="Brad Stevens">Brad Stevens</a> professed his trust in Bradley, who, in the absence of the injured Rondo, began the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_NBA_season" title="2013–14 NBA season">2013–14 NBA season</a> as the team's starting point guard. Bradley's time at point guard lasted just four disappointing games; he had more turnovers than assists, and the Celtics lost all four times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-br-22">[22]</a></sup> In an effort to turn the team around, Stevens named <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Crawford" title="Jordan Crawford">Jordan Crawford</a> the new point guard, allowing Bradley to move to his natural shooting guard. The move worked brilliantly, as the Celtics immediately went on a four-game winning streak and the more comfortable Bradley settled nicely into his role on the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbslocal_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbslocal-23">[23]</a></sup> Although the Celtics' lack of talent and experience began to show itself as the season went along, particularly with Rondo still out, Bradley was a rare bright spot, increasing his scoring average every month through January. He was especially effective in December, shooting a fantastic 50 percent on three-pointers and making 48.7 percent of his shots overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-br-22">[22]</a></sup> Unfortunately, on January 21, in just the third game all season that Rondo was active, Bradley sprained his right ankle<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bostonglobe_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-bostonglobe-24">[24]</a></sup> and ended up missing five contests. Shortly after returning, on February 5, he re-sprained the same ankle. Determined to be cautious, Stevens still had not set a return date for Bradley as the Celtics headed into the All-Star break more than a week later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bostonglobe2_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-bostonglobe2-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bradley ultimately returned to action on March 14. In his fourth game back, a win over the Miami Heat, he connected on a career high six three-pointers as part of a 23-point effort,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> then followed it up with 28 points, matching his career high, the next game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> When healthy, Bradley played significant minutes and played effectively in the final stretch of the season, scoring at least 18 points in the team's last five games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-br_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-br-22">[22]</a></sup> Bradley shouldered a higher percentage of the offensive workload for Celtics in 2013–14, and he responded by greatly improving his shooting from the previous season. In a rebuilding year for the team, one that saw them win only 25 games, Bradley stood out as one of their few consistent performers.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With Bradley set to become a restricted free agent in July 2014, the Celtics needed to extend a qualifying offer of $3.6 million in order to be able to match any contract offered by another team, which they did on June 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On July 15, Bradley re-signed with the Celtics<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> to a four-year, $32 million contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Although the Celtics had high hopes for their backcourt pairing of Bradley and Rajon Rondo, both now healthy, they were soon dealt a setback when Rondo broke his hand a month before the 2014–15 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Although the team planned to be cautious with Rondo's injury, Brad Stevens made the decision to keep Bradley at shooting guard even with Rondo out, with a mix of rookie <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Smart" title="Marcus Smart">Marcus Smart</a>, second-year guard <a href="/wiki/Phil_Pressey" title="Phil Pressey">Phil Pressey</a> and new acquisition <a href="/wiki/Evan_Turner" title="Evan Turner">Evan Turner</a> playing point guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the concerns, Rondo ultimately surprised many by being ready for opening night. Bradley continued to play harassing defense and scored in double digits in 13 of the team's first 15 games,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> including a career-high 32 points in a loss to the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The Celtics, however, struggled to a 4–11 start. Meanwhile, Bradley's offense cooled off in December, as he shot just 39 percent from the field for that month and made just 1-of-21 three-point attempts over a six-game stretch. Faced with a 9–14 record on December 18, Celtics President of Basketball Operations <a href="/wiki/Danny_Ainge" title="Danny Ainge">Danny Ainge</a> made the difficult decision to part with Rondo, trading him and rookie <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Powell" title="Dwight Powell">Dwight Powell</a> to the Dallas Mavericks for <a href="/wiki/Brandan_Wright" title="Brandan Wright">Brandan Wright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jae_Crowder" title="Jae Crowder">Jae Crowder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jameer_Nelson" title="Jameer Nelson">Jameer Nelson</a> and two draft picks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Pundits such as <a href="/wiki/Bill_Simmons" title="Bill Simmons">Bill Simmons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jalen_Rose" title="Jalen Rose">Jalen Rose</a> portrayed the trade as a surrender to a mediocrity, with the hopes of getting a high draft pick at season's end,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and the Celtics continued to struggle after Rondo's exit.</p>
<p>However, under the tutelage of Brad Stevens, and with the help of trade acquisition <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Thomas_(basketball)" title="Isaiah Thomas (basketball)">Isaiah Thomas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> the young Celtics gradually improved. After failing to post winning records in November, December or January, they did so in February, March and April, and won eight of the season's last nine games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Bradley's offensive output was especially effective in February, as he managed over 18 points per game in the month while shooting 47 percent from the field and over 40 percent from three-point range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Although a shoulder injury sidelined him for three games between March 6 and March 9, he returned to score 17 points against the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a> on March 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> For the regular season, Bradley led the Celtics in minutes played and points,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> although his points per game and shooting percentages declined slightly from 2013–14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avery_Bradley_Stats_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avery_Bradley_Stats-43">[43]</a></sup> A 40–42 record saw the Celtics earn the seventh seed in the Eastern Conference. Their playoff series was brief, as the eventual conference champion <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> swept the Celtics 4–0. Bradley played 40 out of the 48 minutes in the last game of the series, but his 16 points was not enough to overcome <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a> and the Cavaliers. Nevertheless, the 2014–15 season was a surprise success for the Celtics and another solid year, and a relatively healthy year, for Bradley.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Fresh off of their first playoff appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Brad_Stevens" title="Brad Stevens">Brad Stevens</a> era, the Celtics entered 2015–16 campaign eager to prove that it had not been a fluke. Acquiring veteran forwards <a href="/wiki/David_Lee_(basketball)" title="David Lee (basketball)">David Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amir_Johnson" title="Amir Johnson">Amir Johnson</a> to fortify their frontcourt, the team also hoped to see the continued improvement of their promising young players, including <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Smart" title="Marcus Smart">Marcus Smart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jared_Sullinger" title="Jared Sullinger">Jared Sullinger</a>. They started the year off slow, winning just one of their first four. Bradley provided a rare highlight, however, throwing down a tremendous dunk on reigning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_Defensive_Player_of_the_Year" title="NBA Defensive Player of the Year">Defensive Player of the Year</a> <a href="/wiki/Kawhi_Leonard" title="Kawhi Leonard">Kawhi Leonard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> After missing two games with a calf injury, Bradley returned to action as a <a href="/wiki/Sixth_man" title="Sixth man">sixth man</a> after starting 224 of 226 Celtics games in which he was healthy. He excelled in his new role, improving his scoring, efficiency and defensive rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Bradley soon returned to the starting lineup on November 22 and proved himself highly capable in either role, immediately managing two games in a row with at least 25 points and 13 in a row with at least 10 points.</p>
<p>Bradley missed three games in early January with a hip injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and upon his return, the Celtics emerged as one of the top teams in the Eastern Conference. Bradley contributed a number of memorable performances. On January 27, he scored 21 of his season-high tying 27 points in the first half of the Celtics' 111–103 win over the Denver Nuggets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On February 5, he connected on a game-winning three-pointer to give Boston a 104–103 win over the Eastern Conference-leading Cleveland Cavaliers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> On February 29, he blocked a shot from <a href="/wiki/Utah_Jazz" title="Utah Jazz">Utah Jazz</a> forward <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Hayward" title="Gordon Hayward">Gordon Hayward</a> with 23 seconds left in the game to give the Celtics a win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> For the season, Bradley was his team's second leading scorer, behind only All-Star <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Thomas_(basketball)" title="Isaiah Thomas (basketball)">Isaiah Thomas</a>.</p>
<p>With a 48–34 record, the Celtics finished the regular season in a four-way tie for the third seed in Eastern Conference and were assigned the fifth seed based on tiebreaker rules. Drawing the Atlanta Hawks in the first round of the playoffs, the Celtics narrowly lost in Game 1, as Bradley scored 18 points but went down in the fourth quarter with an apparent right hamstring injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The injury turned out to be serious enough to sideline Bradley for the rest of the series,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> which turned out to be a crippling blow for the Celtics. They were considerably outplayed by the Hawks in his absence, particularly on offense, and lost the series in six games. However, Bradley's sixth NBA season was both an individual and team success, capped off when he was included in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_All-Defensive_First_Team" title="NBA All-Defensive First Team">NBA All-Defensive First Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the Celtics' third game of the season on October 29, 2016, Bradley scored 31 points on a career-high eight three-pointers and grabbed 11 rebounds, as the Celtics defeated the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Hornets" title="Charlotte Hornets">Charlotte Hornets</a> 104–98.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On November 16, he recorded 18 points and a career-high 13 rebounds in a 90–83 win over the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Player_profile">Player profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Player profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although he has a build more typical of a <a href="/wiki/Point_guard" title="Point guard">point guard</a>, Bradley mostly plays <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">shooting guard</a>. Making up for his lack of size at the position with quickness, strength and tenacity, Bradley excels at defense. Opponents <a href="/wiki/Damian_Lillard" title="Damian Lillard">Damian Lillard</a> and <a href="/wiki/C._J._McCollum" title="C. J. McCollum">C. J. McCollum</a> have called him the best perimeter defender in NBA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Bradley has outstanding athleticism and explosiveness, having won the Slam Dunk Contest at the 2009 <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s_All-American_Game" title="McDonald's All-American Game">McDonald's All-American Game</a>. However, he rarely displays his elite leaping ability in games, occasionally blocking players at the rim or throwing down dunks but more often simply harassing his man on the perimeter and settling for layups. Bradley has nevertheless become an increasingly valuable offensive player, especially as a reliable three-point shooter. Bradley's intense and frenetic defensive style of play, probably his most valuable asset, may also be partly to blame for the series of injuries he has suffered in his career. However, his most serious injuries, which required three surgeries by the time he was 23,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> happened early in his career. Bradley has become an increasingly important member of the Celtics over his tenure with the team, and his minutes played per game has increased in each of his first six seasons, from just 5.2 to 33.4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avery_Bradley_Stats_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-Avery_Bradley_Stats-43">[43]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avery_Bradley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bradley has a son, Avery Bradley III, who was born just two weeks after Bradley's mother died, in September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> He began hosting a basketball camp, the Avery Bradley Skills Academy, for Boston-area children in the summer of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2016, Bradley shocked the Harshman family of Wesley Chapel, Florida by reaching out to them after the death of their 15-year-old son, Jaret. Bradley had been Jaret's favorite player, and touched by the tragedy, Bradley sent the family a letter and a signed jersey and invited them to a game. After meeting at the game, the Harshmans gave Bradley a bracelet to honor their son's memory, which Bradley wore in his next game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
