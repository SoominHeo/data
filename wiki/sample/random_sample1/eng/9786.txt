<p><b>Asa Maxwell Thornton Farr Butterfield</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">AY</span></span></b>-zə</i></a></span>; born 1 April 1997) is an English actor. He began his acting career at the age of 9 in the television drama <i><a href="/wiki/After_Thomas" title="After Thomas">After Thomas</a></i> (2006) and the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_Rambow" title="Son of Rambow">Son of Rambow</a></i> (2007). He became known for playing the main character Bruno in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holocaust" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_in_the_Striped_Pyjamas_(film)" title="The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas (film)">The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas</a></i> (2008), for which he received nominations for the <a href="/wiki/British_Independent_Film_Awards_2008" title="British Independent Film Awards 2008">British Independent Film Award</a> and the <a href="/wiki/London_Film_Critics_Circle_Awards_2008" title="London Film Critics Circle Awards 2008">London Film Critics Circle Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/London_Film_Critics_Circle_Awards_2008" title="London Film Critics Circle Awards 2008">Young British Performer of the Year</a> at the age of 11. He also played the young Mordred in the hit BBC TV Series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merlin_(TV_series)" title="Merlin (TV series)">Merlin</a></i> (2008-2009) and Norman in the fantasy film <i><a href="/wiki/Nanny_McPhee_and_the_Big_Bang" title="Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang">Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang</a></i> (2010).</p>
<p>For his leading performance as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hugo_Cabret" title="Hugo Cabret">Hugo Cabret</a> in <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a>'s fantasy <i><a href="/wiki/Hugo_(film)" title="Hugo (film)">Hugo</a></i> (2011) Butterfield received considerable praise and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Young_Hollywood_Awards" title="Young Hollywood Awards">Young Hollywood Award for Breakthrough Performance - Male</a> and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Movie_Award_for_Best_Young_Performer" title="Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Young Performer">Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Young Performer</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Empire_Award_for_Best_Male_Newcomer" title="Empire Award for Best Male Newcomer">Empire Award for Best Male Newcomer</a>, among other accolades. He is also known for portraying <a href="/wiki/Ender_Wiggin" title="Ender Wiggin">Ender Wiggin</a> in the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ender's Game (film)">film adaptation</a> of the science fiction novel <i><a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game" title="Ender's Game">Ender's Game</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Orson_Scott_Card" title="Orson Scott Card">Orson Scott Card</a>. He was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/British_Independent_Film_Awards_2014" title="British Independent Film Awards 2014">British Independent Film Award for Best Actor</a> for playing Nathan Ellis in <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Matthews_(filmmaker)" title="Morgan Matthews (filmmaker)">Morgan Matthews's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/X%2BY" title="X+Y">X+Y</a></i> (2014). In 2016, he starred as Jacob "Jake" Portman in <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Peregrine%27s_Home_for_Peculiar_Children_(film)" title="Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children (film)"><i>Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children</i></a></i>, and will play Gardner Elliot in <a href="/wiki/The_Space_Between_Us_(film)" title="The Space Between Us (film)"><i>The Space Between Us</i></a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asa_Butterfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Butterfield was born in <a href="/wiki/Islington" title="Islington">Islington</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, and is the son of Jacqueline Farr and Sam Butterfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production-notes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-production-notes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was born with the middle names "Maxwell Thornton", but now uses the middle name "Bopp" on his passport instead (after <a href="/wiki/Comet_Hale%E2%80%93Bopp" title="Comet Hale–Bopp">Comet Hale–Bopp</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Butterfield first started acting at the age of 7 on Friday afternoons after school at the Young Actors' Theatre Islington. Later, he secured minor roles in the 2006 television drama <i><a href="/wiki/After_Thomas" title="After Thomas">After Thomas</a></i> and the 2007 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_Rambow" title="Son of Rambow">Son of Rambow</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-production-notes_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-production-notes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvhits-interview_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvhits-interview-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2008 he had a guest role playing Donny in <i><a href="/wiki/Ashes_to_Ashes_(TV_series)" title="Ashes to Ashes (TV series)">Ashes to Ashes</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc-review_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-abc-review-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In that same year, aged 10, he played the lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_in_the_Striped_Pyjamas_(film)" title="The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas (film)">The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas</a></i>. Director <a href="/wiki/Mark_Herman" title="Mark Herman">Mark Herman</a> said that they came across Butterfield early on in the audition process. He was on the first audition tape he received and he was the third hopeful he met in person. Herman thought Butterfield's performance was outstanding, but only decided to cast him after auditioning hundreds of other boys, "so no stone was left unturned".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lastbroadcast_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-lastbroadcast-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Producer <a href="/wiki/David_Heyman" title="David Heyman">David Heyman</a> and director <a href="/wiki/Mark_Herman" title="Mark Herman">Mark Herman</a> were looking for someone who was able to portray the main character's innocence, so they asked each of the children what they knew about the Holocaust. Butterfield's knowledge was slim and it was purposely kept that way throughout filming so it would be easier for him to convey his character's innocence. The final scenes of the film were shot at the end of the production period to prepare both him and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Scanlon" title="Jack Scanlon">Jack Scanlon</a> for the dramatic ending of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mark-David-indie_London-interview_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-mark-David-indie_London-interview-7">[7]</a></sup> He beat hundreds of boys to the role and also successfully passed the auditions for a role in <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Nobody_(film)" title="Mr. Nobody (film)">Mr. Nobody</a></i> for which he auditioned at the same time. He elected not to pursue the latter role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvhits-interview_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvhits-interview-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Butterfield appeared in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merlin_(TV_series)" title="Merlin (TV series)">Merlin</a></i> episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Beginning_of_the_End_(Merlin)" title="The Beginning of the End (Merlin)">The Beginning of the End</a>"; he played a young druid boy sentenced to death by <a href="/wiki/Uther_Pendragon" title="Uther Pendragon">Uther Pendragon</a> because he feels threatened by the boy's magic. Despite the Great Dragon's advice and the warning that Arthur cannot survive if the boy does, Merlin and Arthur, with the help of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morgana_(Merlin_character)" title="Morgana (Merlin character)">Morgana</a>, help the boy escape to rejoin the Druids. When they are about to disappear into the forest, Arthur asks him for his name, which he says is <a href="/wiki/Mordred" title="Mordred">Mordred</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcone-listing_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcone-listing-8">[8]</a></sup> an important character from the Arthurian legends who is supposed to kill King Arthur. Butterfield appeared as Mordred in a number of subsequent episodes; however the role was recast, with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Vlahos" title="Alexander Vlahos">Alexander Vlahos</a> playing Mordred as an adult character.</p>
<p>In 2010, he had a small part in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Wolfman_(2009_film)" title="The Wolfman (2009 film)">The Wolfman</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timesonline_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-timesonline-9">[9]</a></sup> He starred as Norman Green at the age of 12 in <i><a href="/wiki/Nanny_McPhee_and_the_Big_Bang" title="Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang">Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang</a></i> (2010), working together with <a href="/wiki/Emma_Thompson" title="Emma Thompson">Emma Thompson</a>. The film, and his performance, both received positive reviews. At the age of 13 he played the main and title character in <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Hugo_(film)" title="Hugo (film)">Hugo</a></i>, adapted from the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Invention_of_Hugo_Cabret" title="The Invention of Hugo Cabret">The Invention of Hugo Cabret</a></i>. <i>Hugo</i> was filmed from June 2010 to January 2011, it was released on 23 November 2011, and achieved critical and box-office success.</p>
<p>Butterfield played the title role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andrew_%22Ender%22_Wiggin" title='Andrew "Ender" Wiggin'>Andrew "Ender" Wiggin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ender's Game (film)">film adaptation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Orson_Scott_Card" title="Orson Scott Card">Orson Scott Card</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game" title="Ender's Game">Ender's Game</a></i>, along with Hailee Steinfeld, Abigail Breslin, and Harrison Ford. The film completed filming in the first half of 2012, and was released in 2013. After the shooting of <i>Ender's Game</i>, Butterfield was immediately cast in coming of age British drama, <i><a href="/wiki/X%2BY" title="X+Y">X+Y</a></i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> as Nathan Ellis, a mathematical savant on the autistic spectrum selected to compete with other gifted children on a United Kingdom team in an internationally renowned mathematics competition. The film premiered on 5 September 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and was released in UK cinemas on 13 March 2015. Butterfield's performance received widespread critical acclaim,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and saw him nominated for the <a href="/wiki/BIFA_Award_for_Best_Performance_by_an_Actor_in_a_British_Independent_Film" title="BIFA Award for Best Performance by an Actor in a British Independent Film">BIFA Award for Best Actor</a>.</p>
<p>Later in 2013, Butterfield was reported to be in talks for a role in <i>King of Kastle</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and in May, he was cast in <i>The White Circus</i> with his fellow cast member from <i>Hugo</i>, <a href="/wiki/Chlo%C3%AB_Grace_Moretz" title="Chloë Grace Moretz">Chloë Grace Moretz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In early 2014, Butterfield was cast in a film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_Thousand_Saints" title="Ten Thousand Saints">Ten Thousand Saints</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Shooting began on 27 January 2014 and the film was released on 14 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2015, he joined the ensemble cast of <a href="/wiki/Shane_Carruth" title="Shane Carruth">Shane Carruth</a>'s third film, <i>The Modern Ocean</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Anne_Hathaway" title="Anne Hathaway">Anne Hathaway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keanu_Reeves" title="Keanu Reeves">Keanu Reeves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Radcliffe" title="Daniel Radcliffe">Daniel Radcliffe</a>, Chloe Grace Moretz, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Holland_(actor)" title="Tom Holland (actor)">Tom Holland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Goldblum" title="Jeff Goldblum">Jeff Goldblum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Attah" title="Abraham Attah">Abraham Attah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, he starred as Jacob "Jake" Portman in <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Peregrine%27s_Home_for_Peculiar_Children_(film)" title="Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children (film)">Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> based on the <a href="/wiki/Miss_Peregrine%27s_Home_for_Peculiar_Children" title="Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children">novel of the same name</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Eva_Green" title="Eva Green">Eva Green</a>. He will also star as Gardner in the 2017 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Space_Between_Us_(film)" title="The Space Between Us (film)">The Space Between Us</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asa_Butterfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Butterfield enjoys making and producing music, and released a <a href="/wiki/Mashup_(music)" title="Mashup (music)">mashup</a> of the songs "<a href="/wiki/Teenage_Dirtbag" title="Teenage Dirtbag">Teenage Dirtbag</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Wheatus" title="Wheatus">Wheatus</a> and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Making_Plans_For_Nigel" title="Making Plans For Nigel">Making Plans For Nigel</a>" by <a href="/wiki/XTC" title="XTC">XTC</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teen.com_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-teen.com-20">[20]</a></sup> In late 2012, Butterfield co-designed a <a href="/wiki/Turn-based_strategy" title="Turn-based strategy">turn-based</a> video game for <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">iPad</a> with his father and brother called Racing Blind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Racing_Blind_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Racing_Blind-21">[21]</a></sup> The game was released to the App Store on 7 April 2013.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asa_Butterfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Butterfield was nominated in the category "Most Promising Newcomer" of the 2008 <a href="/wiki/British_Independent_Film_Awards" title="British Independent Film Awards">British Independent Film Awards</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-award-in-metro_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-award-in-metro-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indieLondon_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-indieLondon-23">[23]</a></sup> but he lost the award to <a href="/wiki/Dev_Patel" title="Dev Patel">Dev Patel</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Slumdog_Millionaire" title="Slumdog Millionaire">Slumdog Millionaire</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slumdog-wins_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-slumdog-wins-24">[24]</a></sup> He was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NSPCC" title="NSPCC">NSPCC</a> Award (Young British Performer of the Year) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Critics_Circle" title="London Critics Circle">London Critics Circle</a> Film Awards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-award-in-guardian_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-award-in-guardian-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSPCC_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-NSPCC-26">[26]</a></sup> which was ultimately won by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Turgoose" title="Thomas Turgoose">Thomas Turgoose</a> for his roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Eden_Lake" title="Eden Lake">Eden Lake</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Somers_Town_(film)" title="Somers Town (film)">Somers Town</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-turgoose_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-turgoose-27">[27]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Networks" title="MTV Networks">MTV Networks</a>' NextMovie.com named him one of the <i>Breakout Stars to Watch for in 2011</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
