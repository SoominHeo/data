<p><b>Antoine McColister</b> (born May 11, 1988), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Ace Hood</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Port_St._Lucie,_Florida" title="Port St. Lucie, Florida">Port St. Lucie, Florida</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Deerfield_Beach,_Florida" title="Deerfield Beach, Florida">Deerfield Beach, Florida</a>. He is signed to <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s label <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music_Group" title="We the Best Music Group">We the Best Music Group</a> and was formerly signed to <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-1">[1]</a></sup> His song "Top of the World", from his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Gutta_(album)" title="Gutta (album)">Gutta</a></i>, was featured in the basketball simulation game, <a href="/wiki/NBA_2K10" title="NBA 2K10">NBA 2K10</a>. He has released four studio albums with his most successful being his last two albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood,_Sweat_%26_Tears_(Ace_Hood_album)" title="Blood, Sweat &amp; Tears (Ace Hood album)">Blood, Sweat &amp; Tears</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Trials_%26_Tribulations_(Ace_Hood_album)" title="Trials &amp; Tribulations (Ace Hood album)">Trials &amp; Tribulations</a></i> which was released on July 16, 2013. He is best known for the hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Hustle_Hard" title="Hustle Hard">Hustle Hard</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bugatti_(song)" title="Bugatti (song)">Bugatti</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ace Hood was born in Port St. Lucie, Florida, on May 11, 1988 and was raised by his mother with his younger cousin Ty Barton, Jr. in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deerfield_Beach" title="Deerfield Beach">Deerfield Beach</a>, part of <a href="/wiki/Broward_County,_Florida" title="Broward County, Florida">Broward County</a>. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Deerfield_Beach_High_School" title="Deerfield Beach High School">Deerfield Beach High School</a>. Following a football injury in the 10th grade, and after realizing he wouldn't be able to go pro, the Broward County native began to seriously consider rapping as a career. So Hood teamed up with a local group called <i>Dollaz &amp; Dealz</i> and released a single titled "M.O.E." in 2006. He also began promoting himself via open mic events and talent shows around town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, he met <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a> outside the office of the <a href="/wiki/WEDR" title="WEDR">WEDR 99 Jamz</a> (located at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Florida_metropolitan_area" title="South Florida metropolitan area">South Florida</a>) <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio station</a>, where they mainly played the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music. After Ace gave Khaled an autobiography and his demo tape, Khaled asked Ace to do a freestyle over the <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumental</a> of his song "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_So_Hood" title="I'm So Hood">I'm So Hood</a>" (which during at that time, the song was released as his <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> from his second album, <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best" title="We the Best">We the Best</a>), and later signed Hood to his <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music_Group" title="We the Best Music Group">We the Best Music Group</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">label</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reid_2008_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reid_2008-3">[3]</a></sup> Ace was named as an artist, along with several others, to be placed on the <i>Freshmen of '09</i> by <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i> magazine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008:_Gutta">2008: <i>Gutta</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008: Gutta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His first album, <i>Gutta</i>, was released in 2008. Singles included "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cash_Flow_(song)" title="Cash Flow (song)">Cash Flow</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Ride_(Ace_Hood_song)" title="Ride (Ace Hood song)">Ride</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a>. Hood also released mixtapes called <i>Ace Won't Fold</i> and <i>All Bets On Ace</i>. He made a guest performance among several rappers on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Out_Here_Grindin%27" title="Out Here Grindin'">Out Here Grindin'</a>", also featuring <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, Rick Ross, <a href="/wiki/Plies_(rapper)" title="Plies (rapper)">Plies</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lil_Boosie" title="Lil Boosie">Lil Boosie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trick_Daddy" title="Trick Daddy">Trick Daddy</a>, which peaked at #38 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_100" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a>, becoming Ace Hood's biggest hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reid_2008_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reid_2008-3">[3]</a></sup> It is from Khaled's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/We_Global" title="We Global">We Global</a></i>, and was eventually certified gold by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>. Ace Hood appeared on Wildstyle Radio on <a href="/wiki/WUAG" title="WUAG">WUAG</a> 103.1FM in <a href="/wiki/Greensboro,_North_Carolina" title="Greensboro, North Carolina">Greensboro, North Carolina</a>, on September 7, 2008, to promote his album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gutta_(album)" title="Gutta (album)">Gutta</a></i>, along with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The album debuted at #36 on the Billboard 200, selling 25,000 copies its first week.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Ruthless">2009: <i>Ruthless</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009: Ruthless">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 30, 2009, seven months later, Ace Hood released his second album, titled <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_(Ace_Hood_album)" title="Ruthless (Ace Hood album)"><i>Ruthless</i></a>, once again on Def Jam. The album's first single was <a href="/wiki/Overtime_(Ace_Hood_song)" title="Overtime (Ace Hood song)">"Overtime"</a>, which was produced by The Runners and featured Akon and T-Pain. The album's second single is "Loco Wit The Cake", which was produced by Schife, of the Palm Beach County Karbeen Mafia. The album's guest lists includes Rick Ross, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazmine_Sullivan" title="Jazmine Sullivan">Jazmine Sullivan</a>, <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birdman_(entertainer)" title="Birdman (entertainer)">Birdman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_(singer)" title="Lloyd (singer)">Lloyd</a>. The album debuted at #23 on the Billboard 200, selling 20,000 copies its first week.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Blood.2C_Sweat.2C_.26_Tears">2011: <i>Blood, Sweat, &amp; Tears</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011: Blood, Sweat, &amp; Tears">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ace Hood confirmed that his third album will be called, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood,_Sweat_%26_Tears_(Ace_Hood_album)" title="Blood, Sweat &amp; Tears (Ace Hood album)">Blood, Sweat &amp; Tears</a></i>. It was released on August 9, 2011. The first official single, "<a href="/wiki/Hustle_Hard" title="Hustle Hard">Hustle Hard</a>", became Ace's highest charting solo single, peaking at #60 on the Billboard 100. The second official single is "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go_%27N%27_Get_It" title="Go 'N' Get It">Go 'N' Get It</a>". Both tracks are produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lex_Luger_(producer)" title="Lex Luger (producer)">Lex Luger</a>. The third single is "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Body_2_Body_(song)" title="Body 2 Body (song)">Body 2 Body</a>" featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_entertainer)" title="Chris Brown (American entertainer)">Chris Brown</a> and the song is produced by <a href="/wiki/J.U.S.T.I.C.E._League" title="J.U.S.T.I.C.E. League">J.U.S.T.I.C.E. League</a>. It peaked at #65 on the Hot 100. The album features artists like <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Cossom" title="Kevin Cossom">Kevin Cossom</a>, Rick Ross &amp; <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>. The album debuted at #8 on the Billboard 200, selling 26,000 copies, becoming the rapper's most successful album so far. He promoted the album with his first concert tour, the Hustle Hard Tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He also appeared on three tracks from DJ Khaled's fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Forever" title="We the Best Forever">We the Best Forever</a></i>: "I'm Thuggin'", "Future", and the "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_My_Hood" title="Welcome to My Hood">Welcome to My Hood (Remix)</a>".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Signing_to_Cash_Money">2012: Signing to Cash Money</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012: Signing to Cash Money">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ace started the year 2012 by collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a> on a song called "We Going Hard". He will be appearing on <a href="/wiki/Maybach_Music_Group" title="Maybach Music Group">Maybach Music</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Self_Made_Vol._2" title="Self Made Vol. 2">Self Made Vol. 2</a></i> album. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birdman_(entertainer)" title="Birdman (entertainer)">Birdman</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a> signed Ace Hood and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/We_The_Best_Music_Group" title="We The Best Music Group">We The Best Music Group</a> to Cash Money Records. Now DJ Khaled's label <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/We_The_Best_Music_Group" title="We The Best Music Group">We The Best Music Group</a> has combined with YMCMB family. Ace will be recording his fourth studio album under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/We_The_Best_Music_Group" title="We The Best Music Group">We The Best</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money</a>. He has also made numerous guest appearances on tracks by other huge recording artists since he was signed, like <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Brisco_(rapper)" title="Brisco (rapper)">Brisco</a>. But the most noticed tracks he made a guest appearance on was "The Drill" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, also featuring <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, which appears on his <a href="/wiki/California_Republic_(mixtape)" title="California Republic (mixtape)">California Republic</a>. Ace Hood and Game have collaborated once before on a song called <i>Different People</i> for the <i>Starvation</i> mixtape.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Trials_.26_Tribulations">2013: <i>Trials &amp; Tribulations</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2013: Trials &amp; Tribulations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="5184" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg/220px-Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg/330px-Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg/440px-Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ace_Hood_side_profile.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Ace Hood in 2013</div>
</div>

<p>For his debut single on the We The Best &amp; Cash Money label, he reunited with <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a> after 4 years, to make the early 2000s styled love single known as <i>I Need Your Love</i>. The song is produced by Ben Billionz. Ace started the year by releasing his mixtape <i>Starvation 2</i> on January 10, 2013. The mixtape features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plies_(rapper)" title="Plies (rapper)">Plies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On January 16, 2013, Ace Hood announced his fourth studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Trials_%26_Tribulations_(Ace_Hood_album)" title="Trials &amp; Tribulations (Ace Hood album)">Trials &amp; Tribulations</a></i> and released the first single from the album titled "<a href="/wiki/Bugatti_(song)" title="Bugatti (song)">Bugatti</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> and Rick Ross. The song is produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_WiLL_Made_It" title="Mike WiLL Made It">Mike WiLL Made It</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On February 6, 2013, the music video was released for "Bugatti" featuring Future and Rick Ross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The song made a hot-shot debut on the Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at #42. "Bugatti" has since peaked at #33 on the Hot 100, becoming Ace Hood's most successful single to date as a lead artist, and his first top 40 hit since "Out Here Grindin'". On February 19, 2013, it was announced that <i>Trials &amp; Tribulations</i> would be released on July 16, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 7, 2013, While stopping by Power 99 Philly to talk to <a href="/wiki/Mina_SayWhat" title="Mina SayWhat">Mina SayWhat</a> about his "Trials and Tribulations" album, Ace Hood opens up about his family and deciding to go a different route with this album. He has showed an interest in working with <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hamilton_(musician)" title="Anthony Hamilton (musician)">Anthony Hamilton</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a> on the album. He also plans to get <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> for the album too.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On May 6, 2013, Ace released the remix to "<a href="/wiki/Bugatti_(song)" title="Bugatti (song)">Bugatti</a>" featuring all star guests <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, Future, Meek Mill, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>. On June 5, 2013, Ace Hood released the second single called "<a href="/wiki/We_Outchea" title="We Outchea">We Outchea</a>" featuring his Cash Money label-mate Lil Wayne. On June 26, 2013, the final track listing was revealed revealing <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> on the album from Meek Mill, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hamilton_(musician)" title="Anthony Hamilton (musician)">Anthony Hamilton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_entertainer)" title="Chris Brown (American entertainer)">Chris Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Betty_Wright" title="Betty Wright">Betty Wright</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_Starvation_3_.26_Body_Bag_3">2014-present: <i>Starvation 3</i> &amp; <i>Body Bag 3</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2014-present: Starvation 3 &amp; Body Bag 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ace Hood began the year with the release of his <i>Starvation 3</i> mixtape on January 17. The free project included features from We The Best label-mates <a href="/wiki/Vado_(rapper)" title="Vado (rapper)">Vado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mavado_(singer)" title="Mavado (singer)">Mavado</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Betty_Wright" title="Betty Wright">Betty Wright</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Cossom" title="Kevin Cossom">Kevin Cossom</a>. Production for the mixtape came courtesy of <a href="/wiki/Cool_%26_Dre" title="Cool &amp; Dre">Cool &amp; Dre</a>, The Renegades, StreetRunner, and The Beat Bully, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Since then, he's been dropping off freestyles left and right, most of which was gathered up for the third installment of Ace's Body Bag mixtape series called <i>Beast Mix</i>. <i>Body Bag 3</i> was released on August 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Two months after that, Ace Hood would team up with <a href="/wiki/Rich_Homie_Quan" title="Rich Homie Quan">Rich Homie Quan</a> for a return single titled "<i>We Don't</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ace_Hood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ace Hood and his longtime girlfriend, Shanice Tyria Sarratt, welcomed twin girls, Lyric and Sailor Blu McColister into the world on May 25, 2011. Lyric, however, unfortunately died shortly after birth due to health complications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
