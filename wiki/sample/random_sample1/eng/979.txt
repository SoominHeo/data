<p><b>FC Eindhoven</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Brabant" title="North Brabant">North Brabant</a>. They currently play in the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a> (second tier of Dutch football), and are one of two professional clubs which are based in the city of Eindhoven, the other one being <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>.</p>
<p>FC Eindhoven play their home games at <a href="/wiki/Jan_Louwers_Stadion" title="Jan Louwers Stadion">Jan Louwers Stadion</a>, on the southern part of the city. The club's official colours are blue and white, hence their nickname <i>Blauw-Witten</i> (The Blue-Whites).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Eindhoven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded on November 16, 1909 as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EVV_Eindhoven" title="EVV Eindhoven">EVV Eindhoven</a>. A couple of Gentleman had seen that the clubs in the North area called the Randstad were growing and felt there was a need for a new team in Eindhoven too. Decided was to found a new club under the name E.V.V. short for Eindhovense Voetbal Vereniging. Club colours were blue and white, the blue came from the crest of the city Eindhoven at that time. E.V.V. start to play in the Brabantse Voetbalbond, a regional league, but a couple of years after they start to play in the NVB, the National League. In 1921 E.V.V. fuses with <a href="/wiki/Gestel,_Netherlands" title="Gestel, Netherlands">Gestel</a> (local team) and changes its name to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=E.V.V._Eindhoven&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="E.V.V. Eindhoven (page does not exist)">E.V.V. Eindhoven</a>. At the end of the 30's Eindhoven has his first bit of success as they managed to get hold of the Dutch Cup, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KNVB_Beker" title="KNVB Beker">KNVB Beker</a> (1937). In 1939 E.V.V. Eindhoven is the new Champion of the 1st Division Region South and plays for the title of The Netherlands with teams like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Door_Wilskracht_Sterk" title="Door Wilskracht Sterk">DWS</a>, <a href="/wiki/NEC" title="NEC">NEC</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ajax_Sportman_Combinatie" title="Ajax Sportman Combinatie">Ajax</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Achilles_1894&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Achilles 1894 (page does not exist)">Achilles 1894</a>. They finish 4th.</p>
<p>In 1950 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=E.V.V._Eindhoven&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="E.V.V. Eindhoven (page does not exist)">E.V.V. Eindhoven</a> has their first Dutch international in the name of <a href="/wiki/Noud_van_Melis" title="Noud van Melis">Noud van Melis</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frans_Tebak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frans Tebak (page does not exist)">Frans Tebak</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Snoek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dick Snoek (page does not exist)">Dick Snoek</a> were to follow in his footsteps soon. In 1954, Eindhoven were the last Dutch league champions before the introduction of the professional league. After turning professional in 1954, the club played in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> until 1957, when Eindhoven were relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a>. In 1969 they were even relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Tweede_Divisie" title="Tweede Divisie">Tweede Divisie</a>. Two years later, the club secured promotion back to the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a>, and in 1975, <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a> were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>. In 1977, Eindhoven were relegated back to the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a>, where it has remained ever since. In 1997 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=E.V.V._Eindhoven&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="E.V.V. Eindhoven (page does not exist)">E.V.V. Eindhoven</a> left the professional scene and is once again an amateur club. A new professional club <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SBV_Eindhoven&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SBV Eindhoven (page does not exist)">SBV Eindhoven</a> (Stichting Betaald Voetbal Eindhoven) was founded, this name was in 2002 changed to FC Eindhoven.</p>
<p>In the 2009-2010 season FC Eindhoven qualified for playoffs to advance to the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>. Eindhoven advanced past the first round defeating <a href="/wiki/AGOVV_Apeldoorn" title="AGOVV Apeldoorn">AGOVV Apeldoorn</a> 4-2 goal aggregate. Eindhoven was pitted against possible relegation from <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> club <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a> and were narrowly defeated 3-2 aggregate. So they will remain in the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a>. In 2011/2012 FC Eindhoven had a fantastic season and finished 3rd, despite <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Faber" title="Ernest Faber">Ernest Faber</a> leaving FC Eindhoven as manager to join <a href="/wiki/Dick_Advocaat" title="Dick Advocaat">Dick Advocaat</a> at PSV to be his assistant in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The club lost 3-0 to <a href="/wiki/Helmond_Sport" title="Helmond Sport">Helmond Sport</a> over two legs, 1-0 in the first and 2-0 in the second, in Round 2 of the promotion playoffs.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Erwin_Koeman" title="Erwin Koeman">Erwin Koeman</a>, the replacement of <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Faber" title="Ernest Faber">Ernest Faber</a>, left in the summer of 2012 and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/John_Lammers_(footballer)" title="John Lammers (footballer)">John Lammers</a>. Under Lammers the 12-13 season finished in a disappointing 16th spot, only above two sides whose points totals had been set at zero due to bankruptcy. The 13-14 season was a big improvement with the club finishing 6th and qualifying for the promotion play offs. There they encountered <a href="/wiki/Sparta_Rotterdam" title="Sparta Rotterdam">Sparta Rotterdam</a> to whom they lost to 3-1 on aggregate.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Eindhoven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FC Eindhoven's biggest rivalry is with neighbours <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a>, against whom they contest the <i>Lichtstad Derby</i> ('City of Light Derby'). However, the clubs have not faced each other in league competition since the <a href="/wiki/1976%E2%80%9377_Eredivisie" title="1976–77 Eredivisie">1976–77</a> season. FC Eindhoven used to be a bigger club than <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> in the period between 1930 and 1955. PSV was a club for the workers of Phillips and seen as a club for the privileged while FC Eindhoven was seen as the people's club. In 2004, FC Eindhoven contracted a co-operation deal with neighbours PSV Eindhoven, meaning the possibility of swapping youth players between the two clubs.</p>
<p>Nowadays, <a href="/wiki/Helmond_Sport" title="Helmond Sport">Helmond Sport</a> is seen as the biggest rivals. The clubs are about 15 km apart and have been playing in the same league for years.</p>
