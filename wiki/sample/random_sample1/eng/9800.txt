<p><b>Ecatepec</b>, (<small>Spanish</small> <span class="nounderlines nowrap"><span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:EcatepecPronunciation.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3c/EcatepecPronunciation.ogg" title="EcatepecPronunciation.ogg"><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ekateˈpek]</span></a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:EcatepecPronunciation.ogg" title="File:EcatepecPronunciation.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span></span> once officially <b>Ecatepec de Morelos</b>, is a city and municipality in the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Mexico" title="State of Mexico">State of Mexico</a>. Both are usually known simply as "Ecatepec". The city is practically co-extensive with the municipality, with the city's 2005 population of 1,687,549 being 99.9% of the total municipal population of 1,688,258.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INEGI_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-INEGI-2">[2]</a></sup> The provisional population at the 2010 Census was 1,658,806. The city forms the most populous suburb of <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> (Ciudad de México) and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_suburbs_by_population" title="List of largest suburbs by population">15th-most-populous suburb in the world</a>. It's also Mexico's 2nd most populous municipality (after Itzapalapa of CDMX).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name "Ecatepec" is derived from <a href="/wiki/Nahuatl" title="Nahuatl">Nahuatl</a>, and means "windy hill" or "hill devoted to <a href="/wiki/Ehecatl" title="Ehecatl">Ehecatl</a>". It was also an alternative name or invocation to <a href="/wiki/Quetzalcoatl" title="Quetzalcoatl">Quetzalcoatl</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CitySite_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CitySite-4">[4]</a></sup> "Morelos" is the last name of <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Morelos" title="José María Morelos">José María Morelos</a>, a hero of the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">Mexican War of Independence</a>.</p>
<p>Most inhabitants commute to Mexico City for work, and recently the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexico_City_metro" title="Mexico City metro">Mexico City metro</a> subway system was extended into Ecatepec.</p>
<p>"San Cristóbal" (<a href="/wiki/Saint_Christopher" title="Saint Christopher">Saint Christopher</a>) is the city's patron saint, whose feast day is celebrated on July 25 of each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CitySite_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-CitySite-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Points of interest include the newest <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" title="Catholicism">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">Cathedral</a> in Mexico, <i><a href="/wiki/Sacred_Heart" title="Sacred Heart">Sagrado Corazón de Jesús</a></i>, several colonial era churches and the colonel edifice Casa de los virreyes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CitySite_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-CitySite-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The location of the municipality is north of the Mexico City, and is located in the low extreme geographical coordinates of <a href="/wiki/Greenwich" title="Greenwich">Greenwich</a>, north latitude 19º29'23" minimum, 19º40'28" maximum, west longitude 98°58'30" minimum, 99°08'35" maximum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tequixquiac_municipality_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tequixquiac_municipality-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_Ecatepec" title="San Cristóbal Ecatepec">San Cristóbal Ecatepec</a>, a municipal seat, has governing jurisdiction over the following communities: San Pedro Xoloxtoc, Tulpetlac, Chiconautla, Ciudad Azteca and Villa de Aragón. The total municipality extends 157.34 km<sup>2</sup> and borders with the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Tlalnepantla_de_Baz" title="Tlalnepantla de Baz">Tlalnepantla de Baz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tec%C3%A1mac" title="Tecámac">Tecámac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coacalco_de_Berrioz%C3%A1bal" title="Coacalco de Berriozábal">Coacalco de Berriozábal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaltenco" title="Jaltenco">Jaltenco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acolman" title="Acolman">Acolman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texcoco,_State_of_Mexico" title="Texcoco, State of Mexico">Texcoco</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atenco" title="Atenco">Atenco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Nezahualc%C3%B3yotl" title="Ciudad Nezahualcóyotl">Nezahualcóyotl</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gustavo_A._Madero,_Mexico_City" title="Gustavo A. Madero, Mexico City">Gustavo A. Madero</a>). The area of this municipality is 155 km² (59.85 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncMuc_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-EncMuc-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The human senttlements in Ecatepec de Morelos are an elongated valley from the Valley of Mexico to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_de_Guadalupe,_Mexico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sierra de Guadalupe, Mexico (page does not exist)">Sierra de Guadalupe</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecatepec_de_Morelos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>75% of Ecatepec de Morelos municipality is urbanized, on this territory no big animals; in the highlands of Sierra de Guadalupe live principally small mammals as mouse, rabbit, gray squirrel, bat and gopher, birds as cenzontle and sparrow. The flora in Sierra de Guadalupe is interesting, here there are oyamel pines, oaks, ocote pines, century plants, prickly pears, zacatón (mountain grass) and others. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecatepec_de_Morelos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Remains of earliest human inhabitation of the area have been found on the nearby <i>Cerro</i> (Hill) <i>de Ecatepec</i>. The area was initially settled by successive waves of <a href="/wiki/Otomi_people" title="Otomi people">Otomis</a>; however, because of the later arrival of <a href="/wiki/Toltec" title="Toltec">Toltec</a>-<a href="/wiki/Chichimeca" title="Chichimeca">Chichimecas</a> that dominated the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Valley_of_Mexico" title="Valley of Mexico">Valley of Mexico</a>, this area eventually assimilated to the rest of the Valley, ending with its domination by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aztec_empire" title="Aztec empire">Aztec empire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncMuc_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-EncMuc-6">[6]</a></sup> Ecatepec was an <a href="/wiki/Aztec" title="Aztec">Aztec</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Altepetl" title="Altepetl">altepetl</a></i> or <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-state</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Valley_of_Mexico" title="Valley of Mexico">Valley of Mexico</a>. From 1428 to 1539, Ecatepec was ruled by a <i><a href="/wiki/Tlatoani" title="Tlatoani">tlatoani</a></i> (literally "speaker"). The <i>tlatoque</i> (plural of <i>tlatoani</i>) of Ecatepec were closely related to the ruling dynasty of <a href="/wiki/Tenochtitlan" title="Tenochtitlan">Tenochtitlan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> - <a href="/wiki/Chimalpilli_I" title="Chimalpilli I">Chimalpilli I</a>, grandson of <a href="/wiki/Moctezuma_I" title="Moctezuma I">Moctezuma I</a>. - <a href="/wiki/Tezozomoc_(son_of_Chimalpopoca)" title="Tezozomoc (son of Chimalpopoca)">Tezozomoc</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Chimalpopoca" title="Chimalpopoca">Chimalpopoca</a>. - <a href="/wiki/Matlaccohuatl" title="Matlaccohuatl">Matlaccohuatl</a>, whose daughter <a href="/wiki/Teotlalco" title="Teotlalco">Teotlalco</a> married <a href="/wiki/Moctezuma_II" title="Moctezuma II">Moctezuma II</a>. - <a href="/wiki/Chimalpilli_II" title="Chimalpilli II">Chimalpilli II</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Ahuitzotl" title="Ahuitzotl">Ahuitzotl</a>. - <a href="/wiki/Diego_de_Alvarado_Huanitzin" title="Diego de Alvarado Huanitzin">Diego de Alvarado Huanitzin</a>, grandson of <a href="/wiki/Axayacatl" title="Axayacatl">Axayacatl</a>.</p>
<p>Diego Huanitzin was subsequently made tlatoani of Tenochtitlan by <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Mendoza" title="Antonio de Mendoza">Antonio de Mendoza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viceroy" title="Viceroy">viceroy</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aztec_Triple_Alliance" title="Aztec Triple Alliance">Aztec empire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mexica" title="Mexica">Mexicas</a> used the town to control trade routes going north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CitySite_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-CitySite-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ecatepec was considered an "<i>República de Indios</i>" (Indian Republic) 1560, allowing the village to maintain a certain amount of autonomy and keeping the succession of <a href="/wiki/Tlatoani" title="Tlatoani">tlatoanis</a> or chiefs. However, in the first part of the 17th century, this was changed to a mayorship, with the Spanish administrating, along with the communities of <a href="/wiki/Zumpango" title="Zumpango">Zumpango</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xalostoc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xalostoc (page does not exist)">Xalostoc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncMuc_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-EncMuc-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The municipality was officially created on October 13, 1874.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CitySite_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-CitySite-4">[4]</a></sup> On October 1, 1877, San Cristóbal Ecatepec was declared a village and "de Morelos" was added to its name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncMuc_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-EncMuc-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The national hero <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Morelos_y_Pav%C3%B3n" title="José María Morelos y Pavón">José María Morelos y Pavón</a> was executed in Ecatepec in 1815 by the Spanish during the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">Mexican War of Independence</a>. The house in which he was executed is now the <b>Museo Casa de Morelos</b> (Museum House of Morelos). Ecatepec was declared a city on December 1, 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CitySite_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-CitySite-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 1995, the remains of a <a href="/wiki/Mammoth" title="Mammoth">mammoth</a> were found in Colonia Ejidos de San Cristóbal, where the ancient lakes of Xaltocan-Ecatepec and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Texcoco" title="Lake Texcoco">Texcoco</a> came together and where the Aztecs build a dam to keep the fresh and salty waters separate. The bones have been tentatively dated to around 10,500 years B.C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncMuc_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-EncMuc-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ecatepec de Morelos had a 2010 census population of 1,656,107 inhabitants, which makes it the most populous municipality in the nation <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.inegi.gob.mx/est/contenidos/espanol/rutinas/ept.asp?t=mpob93&amp;c=3839&amp;e=15" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>, as well as in the state.</p>
<p>In February 2016, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> celebrated <a href="/wiki/Mass_(Catholic_Church)" title="Mass (Catholic Church)">Mass</a> in the city in front of a crowd of 300,000. The Pope's message was one of encouragement and opposition to the violence and drug trade that permeates the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>Ecatepec is served by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Metro_Line_B" title="Mexico City Metro Line B">Line B</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Metro" title="Mexico City Metro">Mexico City Metro</a> system, including stations <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metro_Muzquiz" title="Metro Muzquiz">Muzquiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metro_Ecatepec" title="Metro Ecatepec">Ecatepec (a.k.a. Tecnológico)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metro_Ol%C3%ADmpica" title="Metro Olímpica">Olímpica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metro_Plaza_Arag%C3%B3n" title="Metro Plaza Aragón">Plaza Aragón</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Metro_Ciudad_Azteca" title="Metro Ciudad Azteca">Ciudad Azteca</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-metro-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archambault_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-archambault-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, a new transportation system was introduced to Ecatepec's habitants: <a href="/wiki/Mexicable" title="Mexicable">Mexicable</a>. This project is a <a href="/wiki/Cable_car" title="Cable car">cable car</a> which main purpose is to help people displace faster (contrary to most cable cars which have tourists as their main users), specially in areas with complicated geography. The idea was originally developed in the South Americas, with Colombia being the first country to add a cable car system, called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metrocable_(Medellin)" title="Metrocable (Medellin)">Metrocable</a>, as part of their metro. The Mexicable, the first cable car constructed in Mexico as a public transport, has a lenght of almost 5 kilometers (3 miles), 190 cars and it runs the entire line in about 17 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ecatepec is located on <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Highway_85" title="Mexican Federal Highway 85">Mexican Federal Highway 85</a>, the Mexico City-<a href="/wiki/Pachuca" title="Pachuca">Pachuca</a> highway, <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Highway_57" title="Mexican Federal Highway 57">Mexican Federal Highway 57</a>/57D (Circuito Exterior Mexiquense), and <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Highway_132" title="Mexican Federal Highway 132">Mexican Federal Highway 132</a> (Ecatepec-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teotihuac%C3%A1n" title="Teotihuacán">Teotihuacán</a> highway).</p>
