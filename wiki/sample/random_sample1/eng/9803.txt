<p><b>Ecofascism</b> is a pejorative term used by opponents of the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmental movement</a> to accuse environmental activists of fascism;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-smh-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup> in the UK, it has also been used to refer to far-right efforts to gain influence within the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_(UK)" title="Green Party (UK)">Green Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RPDW1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RPDW1-3">[3]</a></sup> Some writers have used it to refer to the hypothetical danger of future dystopian governments, which might resort to extreme or "fascist" policies to deal with environmental issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup> Other writers have used it to refer to segments of historical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fascist" title="Fascist">fascist</a> movements that focused on environmental issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Environmental historian <a href="/wiki/Michael_E._Zimmerman" title="Michael E. Zimmerman">Michael E. Zimmerman</a> defines "ecofascism" as "a totalitarian government that requires individuals to sacrifice their interests to the well-being and glory of the 'land', understood as the splendid web of life, or the organic whole of nature, including peoples and their states".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup> Zimmerman argues that while no ecofascist government has so far existed, "important aspects of it can be found in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_National_Socialism" title="German National Socialism">German National Socialism</a>, one of whose central slogans was "<a href="/wiki/Blood_and_Soil" title="Blood and Soil">Blood and Soil</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to environmentalist <a href="/wiki/David_Orton_(deep_ecology)" title="David Orton (deep ecology)">David Orton</a>, the term is pejorative in nature and has "social ecology roots, against the <a href="/wiki/Deep_ecology" title="Deep ecology">deep ecology</a> movement and its supporters plus, more generally, the environmental movement. Thus, 'ecofascist' and 'ecofascism', are used not to enlighten but to smear."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orton_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Orton-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accusations_of_ecofascism">Accusations of ecofascism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accusations of ecofascism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Accusations of ecofascism are common but usually strenuously denied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orton_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Orton-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-smh-1">[1]</a></sup> Such accusations have come from both those on the political left who see it as an assault on <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>, as in <a href="/wiki/Social_ecology" title="Social ecology">social ecologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Murray_Bookchin" title="Murray Bookchin">Murray Bookchin</a>'s use of the term, and from those on the political right, as in <a href="/wiki/Rush_Limbaugh" title="Rush Limbaugh">Rush Limbaugh</a> and other conservative and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wise_Use" title="Wise Use">Wise Use Movement</a> commentators. In the latter case, it is sometimes a <a href="/wiki/Hyperbole" title="Hyperbole">hyperbolic</a> use of the term that is applied to all environmental activists, including more mainstream groups such as <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Club" title="Sierra Club">Sierra Club</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smh_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-smh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic_or_ideology">By topic or ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: By topic or ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bookchin.27s_critique_of_deep_ecology">Bookchin's critique of deep ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bookchin's critique of deep ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the former case, Bookchin criticizes the political position of <a href="/wiki/Deep_ecology" title="Deep ecology">deep ecologists</a> such as <a href="/wiki/David_Foreman" title="David Foreman">David Foreman</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"There are barely disguised racists, survivalists, macho <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Boone" title="Daniel Boone">Daniel Boones</a>, and outright social reactionaries who use the word ecology to express their views, just as there are deeply concerned naturalists, communitarians, social radicals, and feminists who use the word ecology to express theirs.... It was out of this former kind of crude eco-brutalism that Hitler, in the name of 'population control,' with a racial orientation, fashioned theories of blood and soil that led to the transport of millions of people to murder camps like Auschwitz. The same eco-brutalism now reappears a half-century later among self-professed deep ecologists who believe that Third World peoples should be permitted to starve to death and that desperate Indian immigrants from Latin America should be excluded by the border cops from the United States lest they burden 'our' ecological resources."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sakai_on_.27natural_purity.27">Sakai on 'natural purity'</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sakai on 'natural purity'">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Such observations among the left are not exclusive to Bookchin. In his review of Anna Bramwell's biography of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Walther_Darr%C3%A9" title="Richard Walther Darré">Richard Walther Darré</a>, J. Sakai observes the fascist ideological undertones of natural purity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sakai_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sakai-8">[8]</a></sup> Prior to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution" title="Russian Revolution">Russian Revolution</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsarist" title="Tsarist">Tsarist</a> <a href="/wiki/Intelligentsia" title="Intelligentsia">intelligentsia</a> was divided on the one hand between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_Liberalism" title="Social Liberalism">liberal</a> "<a href="/wiki/Utilitarianism" title="Utilitarianism">utilitarian</a> naturalists," who were "taken with the idea of creating a paradise on earth through scientific mastery of nature" and influenced by <a href="/wiki/Nihilist_movement" title="Nihilist movement">nihilism</a> as well Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zoologists" title="Zoologists">zoologists</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Anatoli_Petrovich_Bogdanov" title="Anatoli Petrovich Bogdanov">Anatoli Petrovich Bogdanov</a>, and "cultural-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aesthetic" title="Aesthetic">aesthetic</a>" conservationists such as Ivan Parfenevich Borodin, who were influenced in turn by <a href="/wiki/German_Romanticism" title="German Romanticism">German Romantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Idealism" title="Idealism">idealist</a> concepts such as 'Landschaftspflege' and 'Naturdenkmal'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weiner_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Weiner-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouvelle_Droite_movement">Nouvelle Droite movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nouvelle Droite movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The influential European <a href="/wiki/Nouvelle_Droite" title="Nouvelle Droite">Nouvelle Droite</a> movement, developed by <a href="/wiki/Alain_de_Benoist" title="Alain de Benoist">Alain de Benoist</a> and other individuals involved with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GRECE" title="GRECE">GRECE</a> think tank, have also combined green politics with right-wing ideas, such as European ethnonationalism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_country_or_region">By country or region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By country or region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Germany">In Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: In Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Prior to attaining political power, several Nazi ideologues, such as <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Himmler" title="Heinrich Himmler">Heinrich Himmler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Rosenberg" title="Alfred Rosenberg">Alfred Rosenberg</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walther_Darr%C3%A9" title="Walther Darré">Walther Darré</a> linked ideas of <a href="/wiki/Agrarianism" title="Agrarianism">Agrarianism</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nature_conservation" title="Nature conservation">nature conservation</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-semitic" title="Anti-semitic">anti-semitic</a>, racist and militaristic ideas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Using the doctrine of "Blood and Soil", Nazi thinkers argued that the German people had a special bond with the natural world, which had to be protected both from industrial <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a> and "inferior" ethnic groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup> These ideas remained in Nazism, despite its post-1936 emphasis on mechanical and military mobilisation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mez_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-mez-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timothy_Snyder" title="Timothy Snyder">Timothy Snyder</a>'s book <i>Black Earth</i> argues that Hitler's anti-Semitism viewed Jews as an "un-natural" plague that kept mankind from returning to its "natural" Darwinian state of fight among races for resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snyder_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Snyder-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Finland">In Finland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: In Finland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Although Finnish activist <a href="/wiki/Pentti_Linkola" title="Pentti Linkola">Pentti Linkola</a> does not endorse fascism <i>per se</i>, he has expressed admiration for the German National Socialist regime for its efficiency in killing large numbers of human beings in a short period of time, describing the massacres of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holocaust" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a> and Stalin's <a href="/wiki/Great_Purge" title="Great Purge">Great Purge</a> as "massive thinning operations."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He advocates a strong, centralised ecological dictatorship, with harsh population control measures and brutal punishment of those he considers to be environmental abusers. Linkola has attracted considerable controversy both in his home country and worldwide.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_United_Kingdom">In the United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: In the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, left-leaning watchdog groups<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AUDIENCE" title="Wikipedia:AUDIENCE"><span title="Wikipedia:AUDIENCE">example needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and magazines such as <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Pepper_(magazine)" title="Red Pepper (magazine)">Red Pepper</a></i> have accused the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Way_(UK)" title="Third Way (UK)">Third Way</a> political tendency of ecofascism,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RPDW1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-RPDW1-3">[3]</a></sup> although most academics describe the Third Way tendency as a position akin to <a href="/wiki/Centrism" title="Centrism">centrism</a> that tries to reconcile <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-wing" title="Right-wing">right-wing</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-wing" title="Left-wing">left-wing</a> politics by advocating a varying synthesis of right-wing economic and left-wing social policies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> From the 1930s onward,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-re_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-re-16">[16]</a></sup> there has been a history of environmentalist views being held by the far-right in the UK, notably by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Williamson" title="Henry Williamson">Henry Williamson</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-re_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-re-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-rg-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Gardiner" title="Rolf Gardiner">Rolf Gardiner</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dp_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-dp-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Wallop,_9th_Earl_of_Portsmouth" title="Gerard Wallop, 9th Earl of Portsmouth">Lord Lymington</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dp_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-dp-17">[17]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jorian_Jenks" title="Jorian Jenks">Jorian Jenks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_United_States">In the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: In the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The term <i>ecofascist</i> has also been used by Mark Potok of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Poverty_Law_Center" title="Southern Poverty Law Center">Southern Poverty Law Center</a> to describe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Jay_Lee" title="James Jay Lee">James Jay Lee</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecoterrorism" title="Ecoterrorism">ecoterrorist</a> who took several hostages at the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Communications" title="Discovery Communications">Discovery Communications</a> on September 1, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Potok also connects ecofascism with <a href="/wiki/Nativism_(politics)" title="Nativism (politics)">nativists</a>, who appeal to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmentalists" title="Environmentalists">environmentalists</a> by arguing that immigration causes environmental degradation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Greece">In Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecofascism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: In Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Golden_Dawn_(political_party)" title="Golden Dawn (political party)">Golden Dawn</a>, a far-right political party in Greece that is <a href="/wiki/Golden_Dawn_(political_party)#Allegations_of_Nazism" title="Golden Dawn (political party)">described as neo-Nazi</a>, has a branch known as the Green Wing. This organization takes part in volunteer activities such as firefighting, reforestation, and private investigation of animal abuse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
