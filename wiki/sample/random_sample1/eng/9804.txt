<p>The <b>Ecuadorian women's national football team</b> represents <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> in international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Women%27s_football_(soccer)" title="Women's football (soccer)">women's football</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It made its debut in the <a href="/wiki/1995_South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship" title="1995 South American Women's Football Championship">1995 Sudamericano</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/1998_South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship" title="1998 South American Women's Football Championship">next edition</a> three years later it reached the semifinals, its best result to date, losing the bronze play-off against <a href="/wiki/Peru_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Peru women's national football team">Peru</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2006_South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship" title="2006 South American Women's Football Championship">2006 edition</a> it ranked fifth, qualifying for the first time for the <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a>. It subsequently hosted the <a href="/wiki/2010_South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship" title="2010 South American Women's Football Championship">2010 Sudamericano</a>, narrowly missing the semifinals after tying at 9 points with <a href="/wiki/Argentina_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Argentina women's national football team">Argentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Chile women's national football team">Chile</a>.</p>
<p>Although football is not popular for women, Ecuador marked their first-ever participation in a <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="FIFA Women's World Cup">Women's World Cup</a> respectively, in <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2015 FIFA Women's World Cup">Canada 2015</a>, and also for the first time both men's and women's team participated in World Cup.</p>

<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The women's national football team of Ecuador began in 1995, when the FEF scrapped together a team with players from provincial selectives and some existing clubs to compete in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American_Women%27s_Football_Championship" title="South American Women's Football Championship">South American Women's Football Championship</a>. In 2005 a provincial selective was held, and teams were told that the winner would represent the national team. A team from Quito won, but Conmebol disqualified it as it was not a national selective. At this time no women's tournament existed neither professional nor amateur. As the base of relative success, club competition is the source to compete against national counterparts, and so as early as 2013 began the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Ecuatoriano_de_Futbol_Femenino" title="Campeonato Ecuatoriano de Futbol Femenino">Campeonato Ecuatoriano de Futbol Femenino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> With the Ministry of Sports impulsing such initiatives, the championship is mandating of at least 2 under 18 players, thinking of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_American_Under-17_Women%27s_Championship" title="South American Under-17 Women's Championship">Women's Sudamericano Sub 17</a>.</p>
