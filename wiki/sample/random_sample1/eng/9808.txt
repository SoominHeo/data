<p>The <b>eta</b> (<span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span>) and <b>eta prime meson</b> (<span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η′<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span>) are <a href="/wiki/Meson" title="Meson">mesons</a> made of a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Up_quark" title="Up quark">up</a>, <a href="/wiki/Down_quark" title="Down quark">down</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strange_quark" title="Strange quark">strange</a> <a href="/wiki/Quark" title="Quark">quarks</a> and their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antiquark" title="Antiquark">antiquarks</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charmed_eta_meson" title="Charmed eta meson">charmed eta meson</a> (<span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/>
c</span></span>) and <a href="/wiki/Bottom_eta_meson" title="Bottom eta meson">bottom eta meson</a> (<span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/>
b</span></span>) are forms of <a href="/wiki/Quarkonium" title="Quarkonium">quarkonium</a>; they have the same <a href="/wiki/Spin_(physics)" title="Spin (physics)">spin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parity_(physics)" title="Parity (physics)">parity</a> as the light eta but are made of <a href="/wiki/Charm_quark" title="Charm quark">charm quarks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bottom_quark" title="Bottom quark">bottom quarks</a> respectively. The <a href="/wiki/Top_quark" title="Top quark">top quark</a> is too heavy to form a similar meson, due to its very fast decay.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General">General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eta_meson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The eta was discovered in <a href="/wiki/Pion" title="Pion">pion</a>–<a href="/wiki/Nucleon" title="Nucleon">nucleon</a> collisions at the <a href="/wiki/Bevatron" title="Bevatron">Bevatron</a> in 1961 by A. Pevsner <i>et al.</i> at a time when the proposal of the <a href="/wiki/Eightfold_Way_(physics)" title="Eightfold Way (physics)">Eightfold Way</a> was leading to predictions and discoveries of new particles from symmetry considerations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kupsc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kupsc-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The difference between the mass of the <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span> and that of the <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η′<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span> is larger than the <a href="/wiki/Quark_model" title="Quark model">quark model</a> can naturally explain. This "<a href="/wiki/QCD_vacuum#The_.CE.B7.27" title="QCD vacuum">η–η′ puzzle</a>" can be resolved<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DelDebbio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DelDebbio-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luscher_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Luscher-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ce_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ce-5">[5]</a></sup> by the 't Hooft <a href="/wiki/Instanton" title="Instanton">instanton</a> mechanism,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hooft_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hooft-6">[6]</a></sup> whose 1/N realization is also known as the <b>Witten–Veneziano mechanism</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Witten_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Witten-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Veneziano_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Veneziano-8">[8]</a></sup> Specifically, in QCD, the higher mass of the η' is very significant, since it is associated with the axial <i>U<sub>A</sub>(1)</i> classical symmetry, which is <i>explicitly broken</i> through the <a href="/wiki/Chiral_anomaly" title="Chiral anomaly">chiral anomaly</a> upon quantization; thus its mass is not "protected" to be small, like that of the η.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quark_composition">Quark composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eta_meson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Quark composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span> particles belong to the "pseudo-scalar" nonet of mesons which have spin <span class="nowrap"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Total_angular_momentum" title="Total angular momentum"><i>J</i></a> = 0</span> and negative <a href="/wiki/Parity_(physics)" title="Parity (physics)">parity</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPMeson_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPMeson-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jones_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jones-10">[10]</a></sup> and <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span> and <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η′<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span> have zero total isospin, <a href="/wiki/Isospin" title="Isospin"><i>I</i></a>, and zero <a href="/wiki/Strangeness" title="Strangeness">strangeness</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hypercharge" title="Hypercharge">hypercharge</a>. Each quark which appears in an <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span> particle is accompanied by its antiquark (the particle overall is "<a href="/wiki/Flavour_(particle_physics)" title="Flavour (particle physics)">flavourless</a>") and all the main quantum numbers are zero.</p>

orthogonal linear combinations of the quark pairs <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span the main quantum numbers equal to zero.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".CE.B7.E2.80.B2_meson">η′ meson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eta_meson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: η′ meson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The η' meson (<span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η′<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span>) is essentially a different superposition of the same quarks as the eta meson (<span style="white-space:nowrap;"><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:right"><br/></span>η<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1.0em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><br/></span></span>), the only significant differences being a higher mass, a different decay state, and a shorter lifetime.</p>
