<p><b>Etterbeek</b> (French: <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ɛtəʁˈbek]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈɛtərˌbeːk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Etterbeek.ogg" title="File:Nl-Etterbeek.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/61/Nl-Etterbeek.ogg" title="Nl-Etterbeek.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is one of the nineteen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipalities_in_Belgium" title="Municipalities in Belgium">municipalities</a> located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels-Capital_Region" title="Brussels-Capital Region">Brussels-Capital Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It neighbours the municipalities of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Brussels" title="City of Brussels">City of Brussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ixelles" title="Ixelles">Ixelles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Auderghem" title="Auderghem">Auderghem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woluwe-Saint-Pierre" title="Woluwe-Saint-Pierre">Woluwe-Saint-Pierre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woluwe-Saint-Lambert" title="Woluwe-Saint-Lambert">Woluwe-Saint-Lambert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schaerbeek" title="Schaerbeek">Schaerbeek</a>.</p>
<p>The main university campus of <a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Brussel" title="Vrije Universiteit Brussel">Vrije Universiteit Brussel</a> is called <i>Campus Etterbeek</i>, although it is geographically not within Etterbeek but in the adjacent municipality of <a href="/wiki/Ixelles" title="Ixelles">Ixelles</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_and_etymology">Origins and etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etterbeek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins and etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to legend, <a href="/wiki/Gertrude_of_Nivelles" title="Gertrude of Nivelles">Gertrude</a>, daughter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pippin_of_Landen" title="Pippin of Landen">Pippin of Landen</a>, founded a chapel here in the 8th century. A document by <a href="/wiki/Otto_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Otto I, Holy Roman Emperor">Otto I</a>, dated 966, mentions the church of <i>Iatrebache</i>. The name <i>Ietrebecca</i> – possibly from the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_languages" title="Celtic languages">Celtic</a> root <i>ett</i> meaning "rapid movement" and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> word <i>beek</i> meaning "stream" – is found for the first time in a document dated 1127. The current spelling appears eleven years later in 1138, around which time a newer and larger church was built.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etterbeek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middle Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, Etterbeek was a rural hamlet mostly independent of <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, aside from taxation rights on beer given to Brussels around 1300 by <a href="/wiki/John_II,_Duke_of_Brabant" title="John II, Duke of Brabant">John II, Duke of Brabant</a>. The following two centuries counted several grievous moments: in 1489, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albert,_Duke_of_Saxe" title="Albert, Duke of Saxe">Albert, Duke of Saxe</a> ravaged Etterbeek in his pursuit of the rebels who fought against <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian of Austria</a>; in 1580, the village is destroyed once more, this time by the <a href="/wiki/Iconoclasm" title="Iconoclasm">iconoclasts</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Protestant Reformation</a> wars. Peace came back under the reigns of Archdukes <a href="/wiki/Albert_VII,_Archduke_of_Austria" title="Albert VII, Archduke of Austria">Albert</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infanta_Isabella_Clara_Eugenia_of_Spain" title="Infanta Isabella Clara Eugenia of Spain">Isabella</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barony_and_municipality">Barony and municipality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etterbeek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barony and municipality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1673, Etterbeek gained its independence from neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Sint-Genesius-Rode" title="Sint-Genesius-Rode">Sint-Genesius-Rode</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_Spain" title="Charles II of Spain">Charles II of Spain</a> promoted it into a <a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">barony</a>. The first baron was Don Diego-Henriquez de Castro, general treasurer of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> armies. The Castro house was sold in 1767 and can still be seen today as Etterbeek's oldest building.</p>
<p>Under the French regime, Etterbeek was made into a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a>, within the <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_France" title="Cantons of France">canton</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sint-Stevens-Woluwe" title="Sint-Stevens-Woluwe">Sint-Stevens-Woluwe</a>. From then on, and especially after the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Revolution" title="Belgian Revolution">Belgian Revolution</a> of 1830 and the development of Brussels as a capital city, the population of Etterbeek grew quickly. In 1876, there were more than 10,000 inhabitants, in 1900 more than 20,000, and in 1910 more than 33,000. In the 1900s (decade), under the reign of <a href="/wiki/Leopold_II_of_Belgium" title="Leopold II of Belgium">Leopold II</a>, construction boomed and changed the town's character with the addition of the broad avenues and residential areas that we know today.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etterbeek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> churches are located in Etterbeek (<i>Saint-Antoine</i> or <i>Sint-Antonius</i> church and <i>Notre-Dame-du-Sacré-Coeur</i>). A third one (<i>Sainte-Gertrude</i> or <i>Sint-Gertrude</i> church) was destroyed in the 1990s as it threatened to collapse.</li>
<li>The <i>Fondation René Carcan</i>, a foundation and museum in <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Carcan" title="René Carcan">René Carcan</a>'s old studio, was located in Etterbeek.</li>
<li>The <i>Cauchie</i> house was built in 1905 by <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> architect, painter and designer Paul Cauchie. Its façade is remarkable for its <a href="/wiki/Allegory" title="Allegory">allegorical</a> <a href="/wiki/Sgraffito" title="Sgraffito">sgraffiti</a>.</li>
<li>Of a completely different character, the <i>Barony</i> dates from 1680 and is the oldest building in the municipality.</li>
<li>The "Avenue de la Chasse/Jachtlaan" features since 27 September 2014 a series of large scale <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Chat" title="Le Chat">Le Chat</a></i> drawings by the Belgian Cartoonist <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Geluck" title="Philippe Geluck">Philippe Geluck</a>, who was born and raised in this neighborhood. There are in total 24 drawings, for a total length of 120m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Etterbeek has a few green areas, including the Jean-Felix Hap garden. The better known <a href="/wiki/Cinquantenaire" title="Cinquantenaire">Cinquantenaire</a> park lies on the territory of both the City of Brussels and Etterbeek. Park Leopold borders the municipality's territory.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etterbeek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Etterbeek hosts two <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> clubs (R.R.C. Etterbeek and Armenia) playing in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Provincial_leagues" title="Belgian Provincial leagues">Belgian Provincial leagues</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Guy_Thys" title="Guy Thys">Guy Thys</a> stadium, named after the famous Belgian manager in summer 2003. He led <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium national football team</a> to fourth place at the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 FIFA World Cup</a>.</li>
<li>In the summer of 1996, the municipal swimming pool burnt down. It has now been rebuilt and is again open to the public.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_redevelopment_.22Les_Jardins_de_la_Chasse.22">Proposed redevelopment "<i>Les Jardins de la Chasse</i>"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etterbeek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title='Edit section: Proposed redevelopment "Les Jardins de la Chasse"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A project is currently proposed to redevelop an area of Etterbeek near the <i>Avenue des Casernes</i>. This project would result in the town hall and police station being relocated to new buildings in a central administrative centre on this site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The new site is being called the <i>Jardins de la Chasse</i> (French) or <i>Tuinen van de Jacht</i> (Dutch). Demolition of the former CPAS building on the site started in 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dhjdlc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-dhjdlc-4">[4]</a></sup> and building of houses on the site started in 2016, with construction of the new town hall awaiting administrative approval.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Municipal offices are forecast to move to the new location in summer 2018. The site of the current town hall may in the future be used for further residential development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dhjdlc_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-dhjdlc-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
