<p><b>Emirates Telecommunications Corporation</b>, branded trade name <b>Etisalat</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">اتصالات</span>‎‎ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration">ittiṣālāt</span></i>, literally "communications"), is a multinational <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a> based telecommunications services provider, currently operating in 17 countries across <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. As of February 2014, Etisalat is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_mobile_network_operators" title="List of mobile network operators">13th largest</a> <a href="/wiki/Mobile_network_operator" title="Mobile network operator">mobile network operator</a> in the world, with a total customer base of more than 167 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Etisalat was named the most powerful company in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> by <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> Middle East in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 December 2015, Etisalat reported consolidated revenue of <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_dirham" title="United Arab Emirates dirham">AED</a>51.7 billion and net profits of <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_dirham" title="United Arab Emirates dirham">AED</a>8.3 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The total market capitalization of the company currently is <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_dirham" title="United Arab Emirates dirham">AED</a>87.7 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It is one of the only two telecommunications service provider companies in the country, the other one being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emirates_Integrated_Telecommunications_Company" title="Emirates Integrated Telecommunications Company">Emirates Integrated Telecommunications Company (du)</a>.</p>
<p>Etisalat is one of the Internet hubs in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> (<a href="/wiki/Autonomous_system_(Internet)" title="Autonomous system (Internet)">AS</a>8966), providing connectivity to other telecommunications operators in the region. It is also the largest carrier of international voice traffic in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and the 12th largest voice carrier in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> As of October 2008, Etisalat has 510 roaming agreements covering 186 countries and enabling <a href="/wiki/BlackBerry" title="BlackBerry">BlackBerry</a>, <a href="/wiki/3G" title="3G">3G</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GPRS" title="GPRS">GPRS</a> and voice roaming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Etisalat operates <a href="/wiki/Point_of_presence" title="Point of presence">Points of Presence (PoP)</a> in New York, London, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, Frankfurt, Paris and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>. In December 2011 Etisalat announced the commercial launch of Etisalat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4G_LTE" title="4G LTE">4G LTE</a> Network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dubaiblog.it_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-dubaiblog.it-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Emirates Telecommunication Corporation – Etisalat was founded in 1976 as a <a href="/wiki/Joint-stock_company" title="Joint-stock company">joint-stock company</a> between International Aeradio Limited, a British Company, and local partners. In 1983 the ownership structure changed – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_government" title="United Arab Emirates government">United Arab Emirates government</a> held a 60% share in the company and the remaining 40% were publicly traded.</p>
<p>In 1991 the UAE central government issued Federal Law No. 1, which gave the corporation the right to provide the telecommunications wired and wireless services in the country and between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a> and other countries. It also gave the firm the right to issue licenses for owning, importing, <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing</a>, using or operating telecommunication equipment. This practically gave Etisalat both regulatory and control powers, which completed the monopoly of the telecom giant in the UAE. In order to safeguard the country's economic development, the law made provisions for the development of the telecommunication sector in the country.</p>
<p>The increase of exchange lines from 36,000 in 1976 to more than 737,000 in 1998 was one of the important indicators of Etisalat network's growth and development. Today Etisalat stands 140th among the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a> Top 500 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporations" title="Corporations">Corporations</a> in the world in terms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Market_capitalisation" title="Market capitalisation">market capitalisation</a>, and is ranked by The <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> magazine as the 6th largest company in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> in terms of <a href="/wiki/Capitalization" title="Capitalization">capitalization</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revenues" title="Revenues">revenues</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">Corporation</a> is the largest contributor outside the oil sector to development programmes of the UAE Federal Government.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Etisalat has also won accolades from across the region for its nationalization programme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In November 2013, it was also announced that Etisalat would be the official sponsor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyprus_First_Division" title="Cyprus First Division">Cyprus First Division</a> side <a href="/wiki/Anorthosis_Famagusta_FC" title="Anorthosis Famagusta FC">Anorthosis Famagusta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simerini_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Simerini-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etisalat_UAE">Etisalat UAE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etisalat UAE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="1433" height="314" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg/220px-Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg/330px-Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg/440px-Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dubai_UAE_1301200712681.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>Etisalat UAE is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> and includes three regional offices – <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, and Northern Emirates. The Northern Emirates regional center is based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharjah_(emirate)" title="Sharjah (emirate)">Sharjah</a> and covers the telecom's operations in the emirates of <a href="/wiki/Ajman" title="Ajman">Ajman</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umm_Al_Quwain" title="Umm Al Quwain">Umm Al Quwain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujairah" title="Fujairah">Fujairah</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ras_Al_Khaimah" title="Ras Al Khaimah">Ras Al Khaimah</a>.</p>
<p>In the UAE, Etisalat operates where mobile penetration is already among the highest in the world "200%",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Etisalat became known for its efforts to roll out its Fibre-To-The-Home (FTTH) network in the UAE. By the end of 2009, Etisalat had completed the FTTH roll-out for 85% of households in Abu Dhabi, positioning the UAE’s capital as the first in the world to be covered by fibre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the Internet services for home users that Etisalat offers include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/3G" title="3G">3G</a> Mobile Internet access</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/4G" title="4G">4G</a> Mobile Internet access<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dubaiblog.it_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-dubaiblog.it-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadband_internet_access" title="Broadband internet access">Broadband Internet</a> services (Al Shamil<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and eLife<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>)</li>
<li>Prepaid and post-paid dialup Internet access</li>
</ul>
<p>Etisalat also operates iZone, a system of <a href="/wiki/Hotspot_(Wi-Fi)" title="Hotspot (Wi-Fi)">Wi-Fi hotspots</a> in central locations, such as shopping malls, restaurants, and <a href="/wiki/Hookah" title="Hookah">sheesha</a> cafes. iZone can be accessed by either purchasing prepaid cards, or if using an existing account. Dial-up and ISDN Internet access services are billed by the hour, whereas the domestic and residential cable and DSL connections have a fixed monthly rate depending on speed. Other Internet links, aimed at business users, have traffic utilization plans and relatively high rates when exceeding the allocated bandwidth quota. This has caused bad publicity for Etisalat and is a major source of criticism.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In addition to its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecommunication_service_provider" title="Telecommunication service provider">telecommunication service provider</a> and carrier units, Etisalat incorporates a number of additional non-telecom business units under the umbrella of Etisalat Services Holding LLC. These units support the company's operations and even provide services to other operators and organizations in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>, namely: training and consultancy services (Etisalat Academy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>), SIM/smart card manufacturing and payment solutions (Ebtikar<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>), data clearing house services (EDCH<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>), peering/voice and data transit (Emirates Internet Exchange – EMIX<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>), call center (The Contact Centre<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>), cable TV (eVision<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>), facilities management (EFM),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> as well as submarine cable laying services (eMarine<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>).</p>
<p>Etisalat is a major investor in <a href="/wiki/Thuraya" title="Thuraya">Thuraya</a> (34.5%),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> a satellite geo-mobile communication systems provider. In 2006 Etisalat started a major restructuring program that resulted in the de-merger of many of its non-core business units operating under the telecom's centralized and direct management; core services were consolidated and streamlined, reflecting the company's shift from a technology-driven telecom to a customer-focused services provider.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> As part of the program, Etisalat launched a re-branding campaign, releasing a new corporate logo and identity in May 2006. The restructuring culminated in the incorporation of Etisalat Services Holding LLC, which as of 2008 oversees the operation of Etisalat's non-telecom business units with huge success stories. On 27 March 2016 <a href="/wiki/Saleh_Al_Abdooli" title="Saleh Al Abdooli">Saleh Al Abdooli</a> has been appointed the Group CEO, extending his responsibility to international operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 June 2013, Etisalat introduced free local and national HD calls across the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Later that week, corporate, private, public and government sectors in the country were provided with better business IT solutions when Etisalat launched its first cloud service in the UAE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_presence">International presence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International presence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Etisalat International Investments was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_unit" title="Business unit">business unit</a> of Etisalat that operated telecom operations outside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a> and managed the corporation's stakes in telecommunications carriers in Afghanistan, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, Saudi Arabia, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. The International Investments unit, and its management team, was re-structured into Etisalat Group, and Ahmad Abdulkarim Julfar was appointed as Group CEO in 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> followed by Saleh Al Abdooli in 2016. As of October 2016, Etisalat had presence and operations in 16 countries outside the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobily_.E2.80.93_Saudi_Arabia">Mobily – Saudi Arabia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mobily – Saudi Arabia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mobily" title="Mobily">Mobily</a></div>
<p>One of Etisalat's first international investments was the bid to become the second mobile services operator in Saudi Arabia. <i><a href="/wiki/Mobily" title="Mobily">Mobily</a></i>, the brand name of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Etihad_Etisalat" title="Etihad Etisalat">Etihad Etisalat</a> founded in 2005 is currently the second largest mobile service provider in <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> with over 20 million subscribers. In less than 6 months the company launched services in 32 cities, Mobily brings coverage to 79.2 percent of the population. Mobily was also the first to build in the shortest period the fastest 3G network in the Kingdom.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etisalat_Sri_Lanka">Etisalat Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etisalat Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Etisalat_(Sri_Lanka)" title="Etisalat (Sri Lanka)">Etisalat (Sri Lanka)</a></div>
<p>Etisalat acquired the <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lankan</a> Operation of <a href="/wiki/Millicom" title="Millicom">Millicom International Cellular</a> (MIC), Tigo (Sri Lanka) on 16 October 2009. The acquisition was completed with a total <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_value" title="Enterprise value">enterprise value</a> of $207 million, out of which $155 million was in cash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tigo (Sri Lanka) under the brand name CELLTEL started operations in June 1989 on a Motorola TACS system and was the first cellular operator in Sri Lanka as well as South Asia. In January 2007, Millicom replaced the local CELLTEL brand with Tigo, their international brand. In February 2010, Tigo was rebranded as Etisalat.</p>
<p>Etisalat Lanka operates a <a href="/wiki/GSM" title="GSM">GSM</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EDGE" title="EDGE">EDGE</a> supported network using 900 / 1800 MHz. The company on 5 May 2011 launched <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HSPA%2B" title="HSPA+">HSPA+</a> services over 2100 MHz, becoming the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3GPP_Long_Term_Evolution" title="3GPP Long Term Evolution">LTE</a> ready mobile network in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Dual Carrier HSPA+ services was launched on 15 August 2012 by Etisalat Sri Lanka, the first operator in South Asia to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Etisalat Lanka was recently mentioned in the UNCTAD Information Economy Report of 2012 for the Android Eco System it had been able to develop in Sri Lanka. It was commended for its inclusive policy and several other innovations done in the market such as the AppZone (Sri Lanka's first independent 3rd party app store and the Book Hub, Sri Lanka's first eBook store) Many governments are now looking at this eco system and how it can too be implemented in their respective countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PTCL_.E2.80.93_Pakistan">PTCL – Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: PTCL – Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

PTCL leads the country in broadband subscriptions, with over 1.3 million in 200 cities while enjoying a robust fiber-optic network which provides access in over 550 locations across the country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etisalat_Egypt">Etisalat Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Etisalat Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Etisalat_Egypt" title="Etisalat Egypt">Etisalat Egypt</a></div>
<p>In July 2006, a consortium led by Etisalat was granted the rights to develop Egypt's third mobile network, with a winning bid of 16.7 billion <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_pound" title="Egyptian pound">Egyptian pounds</a> (EUR €2.29 billion euro).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The venture, <a href="/wiki/Etisalat_Egypt" title="Etisalat Egypt">Etisalat Egypt</a>, competes with existing service providers <a href="/wiki/Vodafone_Egypt" title="Vodafone Egypt">Vodafone</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mobinil" title="Mobinil">Mobinil</a>. On 12 September 2006, it was announced that the network would be built by <a href="/wiki/Ericsson" title="Ericsson">Ericsson</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Huawei" title="Huawei">Huawei</a> of China, at a cost of approximately US$1.2 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Etisalat Egypt's network covers and serves over 99% of the population in Egypt, through more than 6000 base stations, Etisalat Egypt was the first to launch 3.75G in Egypt and the first operator to launch video call services in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etisalat_Afghanistan">Etisalat Afghanistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Etisalat Afghanistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Etisalat Afghanistan was launched in 2007 after the UAE telecom operator won the license to operate as the fourth mobile services provider in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_Republic_of_Afghanistan" title="Islamic Republic of Afghanistan">Islamic Republic of Afghanistan</a>.</p>
<p>The operator rapidly became the fastest-growing telecommunications service provider of the country. Etisalat Afghanistan has invested over USD 300 million in the Afghan telecom industry, and it’s a wholly owned by Etisalat Group. In 2012, Etisalat won 3G license in Afghanistan and launched the first 3G services in history of Afghanistan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet_censorship">Internet censorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Internet censorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Etisalat operates an Internet content filtering system that blocks access to web resources. The web resources are claimed to be controversial or offensive (i.e. sexually explicit content, certain political and religious websites, anonymizers and proxies) or harmful (i.e. numeric IP addresses, known <a href="/wiki/Phishing" title="Phishing">phishing</a> or malicious websites, <a href="/wiki/Botnet" title="Botnet">botnet</a> command servers). The use of content filtering is mandated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecommunications_Regulatory_Authority,_UAE" title="Telecommunications Regulatory Authority, UAE">Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (TRA)</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.</p>
<p>There are claims that Etisalat breaks the rules of <a href="/wiki/Net_neutrality" title="Net neutrality">net neutrality</a> by throttling <a href="/wiki/Peer-to-peer" title="Peer-to-peer">peer-to-peer</a>, gaming and other types of network traffic in order to reduce the load on its oversubscribed international links. The effect of this interference is most noticeable during weekends or periods of high network use. The overall efficiency of the country-wide content filtering is unclear, as many of the technologically savvy users have discovered tools and methods to bypass the content filter, such as using <a href="/wiki/Tor_(anonymity_network)" title="Tor (anonymity network)">Tor</a>.</p>
<p>The type of content that is restricted by Etisalat includes:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Internet_pornography" title="Internet pornography">Pornography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nudity" title="Nudity">nudity</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sexually_explicit_material" title="Sexually explicit material">sexually explicit content</a>.</li>
<li>Certain media-sharing websites</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Criticism_of_Islam" title="Criticism of Islam">Anti-Islamic</a> websites.</li>
<li>Websites criticizing the United Arab Emirates (such as UAEprison and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Arab_Times_(US)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Arab Times (US) (page does not exist)">Arab Times</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Proxy_server" title="Proxy server">Anonymous proxy</a> sites (such as vtunnel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> pzeg,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> etc.)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">Gay and Lesbian Rights</a> websites (such as <a href="/wiki/Gaydar_(website)" title="Gaydar (website)">Gaydar</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mogenic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mogenic (page does not exist)">Mogenic</a> etc.)</li>
<li>Numerical <a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">IP address</a> links (for example, http://10.11.1.1/)</li>
<li>Voice over IP services providers' websites (such as <a href="/wiki/Vonage" title="Vonage">Vonage</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BlackBerry_controversy">BlackBerry controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: BlackBerry controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2009, Etisalat pushed an update to <a href="/wiki/BlackBerry" title="BlackBerry">BlackBerry</a> devices operating on the telecom's national network, citing performance improvements. However, it was later discovered<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gulf1_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-gulf1-47">[47]</a></sup> that the update contained eavesdropping software, developed by the US-based software development company SS8, which specializes in electronic surveillance. It is reported that the software enabled the company to monitor and forward communications on BlackBerry devices to their servers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Research in Motion, BlackBerry's developer, acknowledged<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gulf2_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-gulf2-50">[50]</a></sup> that the patch was a form of spyware, and issued a removal patch on 20 July.</p>
<p>On 27 December 2009, both Etisalat and <a href="/wiki/Du_(company)" title="Du (company)">du</a> have been mandated by the UAE telecom regulator to start filtering BlackBerry users' web access and block illegal content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Due to concerns with the security and the provisioning of legal interception for Blackberry non-voice services, on 1 Aug 2010, the Telecommunication Regularity Authority of the UAE instructed Etisalat that all Blackberry e-mail, internet and messenger functions must be suspended on 1 Oct 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> However, an agreement between Blackberry's developer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Research_in_Motion" title="Research in Motion">Research in Motion</a> and UAE's telecom regulator has been reached, and the announced BlackBerry services suspension has been canceled.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etisalat_Iran">Etisalat Iran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Etisalat Iran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2009 Etisalat in consortium with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tamin_Telecom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tamin Telecom (page does not exist)">Tamin Telecom</a> (a subsidiary of the Iranian Social Security Organization (SSO)) won the bid for running the third mobile services operator in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_Republic_of_Iran" title="Islamic Republic of Iran">Islamic Republic of Iran</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> The license included an exclusive two-year agreement for <a href="/wiki/3G" title="3G">3G</a> services provisioning, but in Sep 2009 the licence was revoked and given to its local partner, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tamin_Telecom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tamin Telecom (page does not exist)">Tamin Telecom</a>.</p>
<p>Etisalat had planned to invest over $5 billion (AED 18.39 billion) over a period of five years,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> but following the license suspension all plans for launching operation in Iran have been put on hold.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etisalat_India">Etisalat India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Etisalat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Etisalat India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009 Etisalat has announced that its Indian unit, erstwhile Swan Telecom (owned by <a href="/wiki/DB_Realty" title="DB Realty">Dynamix Balwas Realty</a> and Reliance Communications),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> headquartered in Mumbai, is renamed to Etisalat DB Telecom India Pvt. Ltd<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The business unit has been awarded Unified Services Access License in 15 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecom_circle" title="Telecom circle">telecom circles</a> (Andhra Pradesh, Delhi, Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Kerala, Maharashtra, Mumbai, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu (including Chennai), Uttar Pradesh (East), Uttar Pradesh (West), Madhya Pradesh and Bihar).</p>
<p>In April 2010 Etisalat began signal testing in Chennai [IND 922], Delhi &amp; NCR [IND 913], Maharashtra &amp; Goa [IND 919], Mumbai [IND 916] and Gujarat[IND 914]. In May 2010, Etisalat was in talks to buy 25% stake in <a href="/wiki/Reliance_Communications" title="Reliance Communications">Reliance Communications</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> but the deal was not finalised.</p>
<p>In 2010, following the $39 billion <a href="/wiki/2G_spectrum_scam" title="2G spectrum scam">2G spectrum scam</a>, Etisalat DB, the Indian subsidiary of the company, was stopped from buying a stake in a Chennai-based company due to objections raised by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Home_Affairs_(India)" title="Ministry of Home Affairs (India)">Ministry of Home Affairs</a> (MHA). Etisalat DB was not allowed to buy back the 5.27 per cent stake held by Chennai-based Genex Exim Ventures since the home ministry raised objections based largely on security concerns. The MHA had pointed out four issues that needed to be resolved before allowing the company to come into Etisalat DB, a company that got scarce 2G spectrum at allegedly throwaway prices.</p>
<p>It raised objections about Etisalat's presence in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and its connection with Pakistan's intelligence agency <a href="/wiki/Inter-Services_Intelligence" title="Inter-Services Intelligence">ISI</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Etisalat owns a 26% stake in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pakistan_Telecommunication_Company_Ltd" title="Pakistan Telecommunication Company Ltd">Pakistan Telecommunications</a> and has a subscriber base of 3 million in Afghanistan. The MHA has also expressed concerns about the telecom surveillance software Etisalat had used in a Blackberry service it had introduced in the UAE and recommended that the company should not be allowed to offer Blackberry services in India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 February 2012, Etisalat announced that it will cease operations in India post cancellation of its licenses by Supreme Court of India. It issued notice to its subscribers, giving them 30 days to change operator. On 5 September 2012, Etisalat announced that it would not bid for spectrum in the November 2012 2G spectrum auction. In a released statement the company said, "Etisalat Group has decided not to participate in the auction as it is not willing to re-participate after being canceled its owned licences. Following the publication by Department of Telecomm (DoT) – India’s Ministry of Communications and Information Technology of the information memorandum for the planned auction of spectrum in 1800 MHz and 800 MHz bands, the Emirates Telecommunications Corporation (Etisalat) wishes to confirm that it has decided not to participate in the auction.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
