<p><b>FC Zbrojovka Brno</b> is a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in the city of <a href="/wiki/Brno" title="Brno">Brno</a>, Moravia, Czech Republic. Founded in 1913 as <b>SK Židenice</b>, the club later became known as Zbrojovka Brno. Brno won the <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_First_League" title="Czechoslovak First League">Czechoslovak First League</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1977%E2%80%9378_Czechoslovak_First_League" title="1977–78 Czechoslovak First League">1977–78 season</a> and finished as runners-up in 1979–80.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zbrojovka_Brno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td>
<td class="mbox-text"><span class="mbox-text-span"><b>This section needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=FC_Zbrojovka_Brno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small> <small><i>(January 2013)</i></small></span></td>
</tr>

<p>The club, initially known as SK Židenice, played in the top tier of Czechoslovakian football from 1933 until suffering relegation in the <a href="/wiki/1946%E2%80%9347_Czechoslovak_First_League" title="1946–47 Czechoslovak First League">1946–47 Czechoslovak First League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup> During this period, the club entered the <a href="/wiki/Mitropa_Cup" title="Mitropa Cup">Mitropa Cup</a> three times, reaching the quarter finals in 1935 as well as taking part in the competition in 1936 and 1938.</p>
<p>Between 1950 and 1962 the club played outside the top tier, returning in the <a href="/wiki/1962%E2%80%9363_Czechoslovak_First_League" title="1962–63 Czechoslovak First League">1962–63 Czechoslovak First League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup> Five seasons elapsed before the club was again relegated, in 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup> They then spent four years in the second tier of Czechoslovak football before returning to the top flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup> In the 1970s the club was a strong force in the country, winning the <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_First_League" title="Czechoslovak First League">Czechoslovak First League</a> in 1978, finishing third the following season and being runners up in 1980.</p>
<p>The 1980s were less spectacular for Brno, as the club was relegated in 1983, playing until 1989 in the second tier. After just two seasons in the top tier, the club finished last in 1991 and was again relegated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1992 and 2011, Brno played in the top tier of the Czech Republic for 19 consecutive seasons, the longest such spell in the club's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2011, the club was relegated to the second division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011relegation_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-2011relegation-1">[1]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Czech_2._Liga" title="2011–12 Czech 2. Liga">2011–12 Czech 2. Liga</a>, the club only managed to finish fourth, missing out on the promotion places. However, due to the stadium requirements of the Czech First League, champions <a href="/wiki/FK_%C3%9Ast%C3%AD_nad_Labem" title="FK Ústí nad Labem">FK Ústí nad Labem</a> as well as third-placed <a href="/wiki/FK_Ban%C3%ADk_Sokolov" title="FK Baník Sokolov">FK Baník Sokolov</a> were ineligible for promotion. As a result, Brno won promotion immediately back to the top tier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brnopromotion_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-brnopromotion-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_names">Historical names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zbrojovka_Brno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historical names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i>SK Židenice</i> (1913–47)</li>

<li><i>Spartak ZJŠ Brno</i> (1956–68)</li>

<li><i>1.FC Brno</i> (2002–10)</li>

<p>In 1962, there was an amalgamation between <a href="/wiki/Rud%C3%A1_Hv%C4%9Bzda_Brno" title="Rudá Hvězda Brno">Rudá Hvězda Brno</a> (1956–62) and <i>Spartak ZJŠ Brno</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_competitions">European competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zbrojovka_Brno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: European competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brno have competed a number of times in European competitions, reaching the second round of the <a href="/wiki/1978%E2%80%9379_European_Cup" title="1978–79 European Cup">1978–79 European Cup</a> in their only appearance to date in the competition. The club played in the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_European_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="1993–94 European Cup Winners' Cup">1993–94 European Cup Winners' Cup</a> but lost in the first round.</p>
<p>Brno took part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> three times, reaching the quarter finals in <a href="/wiki/1979%E2%80%9380_UEFA_Cup" title="1979–80 UEFA Cup">1979–80</a> and also playing in 1980–81 and 1997–98.</p>
<p>Brno played five times in the <a href="/wiki/Inter-Cities_Fairs_Cup" title="Inter-Cities Fairs Cup">Inter-Cities Fairs Cup</a>, reaching the quarter finals in <a href="/wiki/1963%E2%80%9364_Inter-Cities_Fairs_Cup" title="1963–64 Inter-Cities Fairs Cup">1963–64</a>. They competed in the <a href="/wiki/Mitropa_Cup" title="Mitropa Cup">Mitropa Cup</a> three times before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, reaching the quarter finals in 1935.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zbrojovka_Brno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/M%C4%9Bstsk%C3%BD_fotbalov%C3%BD_stadion_Srbsk%C3%A1" title="Městský fotbalový stadion Srbská">Městský fotbalový stadion Srbská</a></div>
<p>Brno have played at <a href="/wiki/M%C4%9Bstsk%C3%BD_fotbalov%C3%BD_stadion_Srbsk%C3%A1" title="Městský fotbalový stadion Srbská">Městský fotbalový stadion Srbská</a> since 2001, when they moved from previous home <a href="/wiki/Stadion_Za_Lu%C5%BE%C3%A1nkami" title="Stadion Za Lužánkami">Stadion Za Lužánkami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the 1990s, Brno attracted record crowds to their football matches, with Za Lužánkami as the venue for all of the top ten most-attended Czech First League matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attendancerecords_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-attendancerecords-4">[4]</a></sup> The highest attendance for a Brno match is 44,120, set in a league match against <a href="/wiki/SK_Slavia_Prague" title="SK Slavia Prague">Slavia Prague</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attendancerecords_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-attendancerecords-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
