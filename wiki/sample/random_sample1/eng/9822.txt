<p><b>Epcot</b> (originally named <b>EPCOT Center</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">theme park</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World" title="Walt Disney World">Walt Disney World Resort</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bay_Lake,_Florida" title="Bay Lake, Florida">Bay Lake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>. It is owned and operated by the <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Walt Disney Company</a> through its <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Parks_and_Resorts" title="Walt Disney Parks and Resorts">Parks and Resorts</a> division. Inspired by <a href="/wiki/EPCOT_(concept)" title="EPCOT (concept)">an unrealized concept</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a>, the park opened on October 1, 1982,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and was the second of four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_park" title="Theme park">theme parks</a> built at Walt Disney World, after the <a href="/wiki/Magic_Kingdom" title="Magic Kingdom">Magic Kingdom</a>. Spanning 300 acres (120 ha), more than twice the size of the Magic Kingdom park,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Epcot is dedicated to the celebration of human achievement, namely technological <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">innovation</a> and international <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>, and is often referred to as a "permanent <a href="/wiki/World%27s_fair" title="World's fair">World's Fair</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The park is divided into two sections: Future World, made up eight pavilions, and World Showcase, themed to eleven <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">world nations</a>.</p>
<p>In 2015, the park hosted approximately 11.98 million guests, ranking it the third most visited theme park in North America and the sixth most visited theme park in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2015_attendance_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-2015_attendance-6">[6]</a></sup> The park is represented by <a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth_(Epcot)" title="Spaceship Earth (Epcot)">Spaceship Earth</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Geodesic_dome" title="Geodesic dome">geodesic sphere</a> that also serves as an attraction. Epcot was known as EPCOT Center until 1994 when it was later renamed Epcot '94, then Epcot '95, now commonly known simply as Epcot.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dedication">Dedication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Dedication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The theme park opened on October 1, 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdw200603_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wdw200603-1">[1]</a></sup> The dedication plaque near the entrance states:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>To all who come to this place of joy, hope and friendship, welcome.</p>
<p>Epcot Center is inspired by Walt Disney's creative genius. Here, human achievements are celebrated through imagination, the wonders of enterprise, and concepts of a future that promises new and exciting benefits for all.</p>
May Epcot Center entertain, inform and inspire. And, above all, may it instill a new sense of belief and pride in man's ability to shape a world that offers hope to people everywhere.
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E._Cardon_Walker" title="E. Cardon Walker">E. Cardon Walker</a>, October 24, 1982<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdw200603_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-wdw200603-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The park's name, EPCOT, is an acronym for <a href="/wiki/EPCOT_(concept)" title="EPCOT (concept)">Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Utopia" title="Utopia">utopian city</a> of the future planned by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney" title="Walt Disney">Walt Disney</a>, often interchanging "city" and "community." In Walt Disney's words: "EPCOT will take its cue from the new ideas and new technologies that are now emerging from the creative centers of American industry. It will be a community of tomorrow that will never be completed but will always be introducing and testing, and demonstrating new materials and new systems. And EPCOT will always be a showcase to the world of the ingenuity and imagination of American free enterprise."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> His original vision was for a model community which would have been home to twenty thousand residents and a test bed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_planning" title="City planning">city planning</a> as well as organization. It was to have been built in the shape of a circle with businesses and commercial areas at its center with community buildings, schools, and recreational complexes around it while residential neighborhoods would line the perimeter. This radial plan concept is strongly influenced by British planner <a href="/wiki/Ebenezer_Howard" title="Ebenezer Howard">Ebenezer Howard</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Garden_Cities_of_To-morrow" title="Garden Cities of To-morrow">Garden Cities of To-morrow</a>. Transportation would have been provided by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monorails" title="Monorails">monorails</a> and <a href="/wiki/PeopleMover" title="PeopleMover">PeopleMovers</a> (like that in Magic Kingdom's <a href="/wiki/Tomorrowland" title="Tomorrowland">Tomorrowland</a>.) Automobile traffic would be kept underground, leaving pedestrians safe above ground. The original model of EPCOT can still be seen by passengers riding the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomorrowland_Transit_Authority" title="Tomorrowland Transit Authority">Tomorrowland Transit Authority</a> attraction in the Magic Kingdom park; when the PeopleMover enters the showhouse for <a href="/wiki/Stitch%27s_Great_Escape!" title="Stitch's Great Escape!">Stitch's Great Escape!</a>, the remaining portion of the model is visible on the left (when facing forward) behind glass. Walt Disney was not able to obtain funding and permission to start work on his Florida property until he agreed to first build Magic Kingdom. He died nearly five years before Magic Kingdom opened.</p>
<p>After Disney's death, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Walt Disney Productions</a> decided that it did not want to be in the business of running a city without Walt's guidance. The model community of <a href="/wiki/Celebration,_Florida" title="Celebration, Florida">Celebration, Florida</a> has been mentioned as a realization of Disney's original vision, but Celebration is based on concepts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_urbanism" title="New urbanism">new urbanism</a> which is radically different from Disney's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modernist" title="Modernist">modernist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Futurist_architecture" title="Futurist architecture">futurist</a> visions. However, the idea of EPCOT was instrumental in prompting the state of Florida to create the <a href="/wiki/Reedy_Creek_Improvement_District" title="Reedy Creek Improvement District">Reedy Creek Improvement District</a> (RCID) and the cities of <a href="/wiki/Bay_Lake,_Florida" title="Bay Lake, Florida">Bay Lake</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reedy_Creek,_Florida" title="Reedy Creek, Florida">Reedy Creek</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Lake_Buena_Vista,_Florida" title="Lake Buena Vista, Florida">Lake Buena Vista</a>), a legislative mechanism allowing Disney to exercise governmental powers over Walt Disney World. Control over the RCID is vested in the landowners of the district, and the promise of an actual city in the district would have meant that the powers of the RCID would have been distributed among the landowners in EPCOT. Because the idea of EPCOT was never implemented, Disney remained almost the sole landowner in the district allowing it to maintain control of the RCID and the cities of Bay Lake and Lake Buena Vista; Disney further cemented this control by deannexing Celebration from the RCID.</p>
<p>The original plans for the park showed indecision over the park's purpose. Some <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Imagineering" title="Walt Disney Imagineering">Imagineers</a> wanted it to represent the cutting edge of technology, while others wanted it to showcase international cultures and customs. At one point, a model of the futuristic park was pushed together against a model of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World%27s_Fair" title="World's Fair">World's Fair</a> international theme, and the two were combined. The park was originally named EPCOT Center to reflect the ideals and values of the city. It was constructed for an estimated $800 million to $1.4 billion and took three years to build, at the time the largest construction project on Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kurtti_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kurtti-9">[9]</a></sup> The parking lot serving the park is 141 acres (57 ha) (including bus area) and can accommodate 11,211 vehicles (grass areas hold additional 500+ vehicles). Before it opened on October 1, 1982, Walt Disney World Ambassador Genie Field introduced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E._Cardon_Walker" title="E. Cardon Walker">E. Cardon Walker</a>, Disney's chairman and CEO, who dedicated EPCOT Center. Walker also presented a family with lifetime passes for the two Walt Disney World theme parks. His remarks were followed by Florida Governor <a href="/wiki/Bob_Graham" title="Bob Graham">Bob Graham</a> and William Ellinghaus, president of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Telephone_%26_Telegraph" title="American Telephone &amp; Telegraph">AT&amp;T</a>.</p>
<p>As part of the opening-day ceremony, dancers and band members performed <i>We've Just Begun to Dream</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Sherman_Brothers" title="Sherman Brothers">Sherman Brothers</a> wrote a song especially for the occasion entitled "<a href="/wiki/The_World_Showcase_March" title="The World Showcase March">The World Showcase March</a>". During the finale, doves and many sets of balloons were released. Performing groups representing countries from all over the world performed in World Showcase. Water was gathered from major rivers across the globe and emptied into the park's fountain of nations ceremonial containers to mark the opening. Located at the front of the park is a plaque bearing Walker's opening-day dedication, as seen above.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas">Areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Epcot_attractions" title="List of Epcot attractions">List of Epcot attractions</a></div>
<p>Epcot is divided into two main themed areas: Future World and World Showcase. The World Showcase usually opens two hours after park opening and remains open later than the Future World section of the park. Most major attractions in Future World including Test Track, Soarin', Mission Space, The Seas with Nemo and Friends, and Spaceship Earth remain open until the park's closing time.</p>

<p>Future World consists of a variety of pavilions that explore innovative aspects and applications including technology and science, with each pavilion featuring self-contained attractions. Future World also serves as the park's main entrance and features the park's iconic landmark, <a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth_(Epcot)" title="Spaceship Earth (Epcot)">Spaceship Earth</a>, a large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geodesic_sphere" title="Geodesic sphere">geodesic sphere</a> structure which houses a themed attraction inside. Originally, each pavilion of Future World featured a unique circular logo which was featured on park signage and the attractions themselves. The logos, including that of Epcot itself, have been phased out over recent years, but some remnants are still scattered throughout the park; the pavilions are now instead identified by name and recognized by the main attraction(s) housed inside. The various pavilions and attractions located in Future World include the following:</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_sponsorships">Corporate sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Corporate sponsorships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Each pavilion was initially <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sponsorship" title="Sponsorship">sponsored</a> by a corporation which helped fund its construction and maintenance in return for the corporation's logos and some marketing elements appearing throughout the pavilion. For example, <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_Energy" title="Universe of Energy">Universe of Energy</a> was sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Exxon" title="Exxon">Exxon</a> from 1982 to 2004, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Land_(Disney)" title="The Land (Disney)">The Land</a> was sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft</a> from 1982 to 1993, then <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a> from 1993 to 2009. Each pavilion contains a private "VIP area" for its sponsor with offices, lounges, and reception areas hidden away from regular park guests. While some pavilions still retain active sponsorships, in recent years several pavilions have lost sponsorships due to lack of interest from partner companies in renewing expiring agreements. After <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horizons_(Epcot_attraction)" title="Horizons (Epcot attraction)">Horizons</a> in 1993, it closed for a couple of years, then reopened temporarily while neighboring attractions <a href="/wiki/Universe_of_Energy" title="Universe of Energy">Universe of Energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_of_Motion" title="World of Motion">World of Motion</a> were renovated. Horizons closed permanently on January 9, 1999, and was demolished in 2000 to make room for the opening of Mission: SPACE on October 9, 2003. Metlife sponsored <a href="/wiki/Wonders_of_Life" title="Wonders of Life">Wonders of Life</a> from 1989 to 2001, until that area was closed. However, the Wonders of Life pavilion is still mostly intact and is used for both the <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Flower_%26_Garden_Festival" title="Epcot International Flower &amp; Garden Festival">Flower and Garden Festival</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Food_%26_Wine_Festival" title="Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival">Food and Wine Festival</a>. Current active sponsorships include the following:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Test_Track" title="Test Track">Test Track</a> opened in the former World of Motion pavilion and is currently sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet" title="Chevrolet">Chevrolet</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth_(Epcot)" title="Spaceship Earth (Epcot)">Spaceship Earth</a> is currently sponsored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siemens_AG" title="Siemens AG">Siemens</a> which also sponsors the park's nighttime show <a href="/wiki/IllumiNations:_Reflections_of_Earth" title="IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth">IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Living_with_the_Land" title="Living with the Land">Living with the Land</a> is currently sponsored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiquita" title="Chiquita">Chiquita</a>.</li>
<li>Individual small attractions and exhibits within or nearby the <a href="/wiki/Innoventions_(Epcot)" title="Innoventions (Epcot)">Innoventions</a> pavilions are sponsored by various companies such as <a href="/wiki/Club_Cool" title="Club Cool">Club Cool</a> presented by <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sum_Of_All_Thrills" title="The Sum Of All Thrills">The Sum Of All Thrills</a> presented by <a href="/wiki/Raytheon" title="Raytheon">Raytheon</a>.</li>
</ul>

<p>Of the 11 pavilions, only <a href="/wiki/Morocco_Pavilion_at_Epcot" title="Morocco Pavilion at Epcot">Morocco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway_Pavilion_at_Epcot" title="Norway Pavilion at Epcot">Norway</a> were not present at the park's opening, as they were added in 1984 and 1988, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-10">[10]</a></sup> Each pavilion contains themed architecture, landscapes, streetscapes, attractions, shops and restaurants representing the respective country's culture and cuisine. In an effort to maintain the authenticity of the represented countries, the pavilions are primarily staffed by citizens of the respective countries as part of the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_International_Program#Cultural_Representative_Program" title="Walt Disney World International Program">Cultural Representative Program</a> through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Q1_visa" title="Q1 visa">Q1 visa</a> agreements. Some pavilions also contain themed <a href="/wiki/List_of_Epcot_attractions#World_Showcase" title="List of Epcot attractions">rides, shows, and live entertainment</a> representative of the respective country. The only pavilion that is directly sponsored by the government of its respective country is Morocco; the remaining pavilions are primarily sponsored by private companies with affiliations to the represented countries.</p>
<p>Originally, the showcase was to include partnerships with the governments of the different countries. According to Disney's 1975 Annual Report, the Showcase would:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>...offer participating countries a permanent installation for such features as themed restaurants and shops, product exhibits, industrial displays, cultural presentations, a trade center, and even special facilities for business meetings.</p>
<p>Major sponsorships for each participating nation will be asked to provide the capital to cover the cost of designing, developing and constructing its attraction and/or ride and all exhibits, as well as the Pavilion itself. It will also have the responsibility for funding the housing for its employees in the International Village. Its land lease will cover the cost of maintaining the attraction for a minimum of ten years.</p>
<p>The Disney organization will be responsible for area development, including the construction of transportation systems and utilities. We will also build and operate the internal people moving system, the Courtyard of Nations and central theater facility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_pavilions_and_unused_locations">Proposed pavilions and unused locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Proposed pavilions and unused locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Pavilions for Puerto Rico,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puerto_Rico_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Puerto_Rico-11">[11]</a></sup> Russia, Switzerland, Costa Rica, Spain, Venezuela, United Arab Emirates, and Israel have occasionally been rumored as potential future pavilions but have never made it past the planning phases to date. The Israeli, Spanish, and an Equatorial Africa pavilion (blending elements of the cultures of countries such as Kenya and Zaire) were even announced as coming soon in 1982, but never took off. Instead, a small African themed refreshment shop known as the Outpost currently resides where Equatorial Africa was to be.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-10">[10]</a></sup> Israel, five African countries (Eritrea, Ethiopia, Kenya, Namibia, and South Africa), as well as eight other countries (Brazil, Chile, India, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia, Scotland, and Sweden), took part in the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Village" title="Millennium Village">Millennium Village</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_Millennium_Celebration" title="Walt Disney World Millennium Celebration">Millennium Celebration</a>.</p>
<p>There are currently nine undeveloped spots for countries around the World Showcase—including the space occupied by the Outpost—in between the locations of the current countries. Two of the potential locations, on either side of the United Kingdom, are currently occupied by <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Village" title="Millennium Village">World ShowPlace</a>. Two more lie on either side of the American Adventure, though this pavilion's use of reversed <a href="/wiki/Forced_perspective" title="Forced perspective">forced perspective</a> may preclude the construction of additional buildings as they would ruin the illusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-World_Showcase_theoretical_expansion-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Secondary_park_entrance">Secondary park entrance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Secondary park entrance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A secondary park gate is located between the France and United Kingdom pavilions and is known as the International Gateway. The International Gateway is directly accessible to guests staying at nearby <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epcot_Area_Resorts" title="Epcot Area Resorts">Epcot Area Resorts</a> and also guests coming from <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Hollywood_Studios" title="Disney's Hollywood Studios">Disney's Hollywood Studios</a> via "Friendship" taxi boat travel or landscaped pedestrian walkways. Guests entering via this gate prior to the opening of World Showcase are directed by staff to Future World.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alcohol_policy">Alcohol policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Alcohol policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Unlike Magic Kingdom, which up until 2012 did not serve alcohol and now only serves it in a few table service locations,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> most stores and restaurants at Epcot, especially in the World Showcase, serve and/or sell a variety of alcoholic beverages including specialty drinks, craft beers, wines, and spirits reflective of the respective countries. The park also hosts the <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Food_%26_Wine_Festival" title="Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival">Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival</a>, an annual event featuring food and drink samplings from all over the world, along with live entertainment and special exhibits.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_World_Showcase_Adventure">The World Showcase Adventure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epcot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The World Showcase Adventure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Originally based on the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Kim_Possible" title="Kim Possible">Kim Possible</a></i>, the World Showcase Adventure is an interactive mobile attraction taking place in several pavilions throughout the World Showcase. The attraction is an electronic <a href="/wiki/Scavenger_hunt" title="Scavenger hunt">scavenger hunt</a> that has guests using special "Kimmunicators" (in actuality, customized cell phones) to help teenage crime-fighters Kim Possible and Ron Stoppable solve a "crime" or disrupt an evil-doer's "plans for global domination." The "Kimmunicator" is able to trigger specific events within the pavilion grounds that provide clues to completing the adventure. Launched in January 2009 and presented by <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Wireless" title="Verizon Wireless">Verizon Wireless</a>, the Adventure is included in park admission. It was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Perry_the_Platypus" title="Perry the Platypus">Agent P</a>'s World Showcase Adventure, based on Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/Phineas_and_Ferb" title="Phineas and Ferb">Phineas and Ferb</a></i>, on June 23, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>Illuminations: Reflections of Earth is an award-winning show taking place in the World Showcase Lagoon every night at the park's closing time (usually 9:00 pm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It features <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lasers" title="Lasers">lasers</a>, fire, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_fountains" title="Water fountains">water fountains</a> timed to a musical score over the World Showcase Lagoon. A large rotating globe with curved LED screens is the centerpiece of the show and is used to display images of people and places. The current version premiered as part of the park's Millennium Celebration in 2000. The show tells the story of Earth and is divided into three movements titled "Chaos," "Order," and "Meaning." The music has an African tribal sound to it, to emphasize the idea of humanity as a single unified tribe on this planet; the lagoon is surrounded by nineteen large torches signifying the first 19 centuries of the common era, and the show culminates in the globe opening like a lotus blossom to reveal a twentieth torch, representing the now-completed 20th century.</p>

<p>Epcot hosts a number of special events during the year:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Flower_and_Garden_Festival" title="Epcot International Flower and Garden Festival">Epcot International Flower and Garden Festival</a>, inaugurated in 1994, uses specially-themed floral displays throughout the park, including <a href="/wiki/Topiary" title="Topiary">topiary</a> sculptures of Disney characters. Guests can meet gardening experts and learn new ideas they can use in their own home gardens. The 18th annual event was scheduled for March 2 – May 15. Each event takes more than a full year to plan and more than 20,000 cast member hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Epcot_International_Food_%26_Wine_Festival" title="Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival">Epcot International Food &amp; Wine Festival</a>, inaugurated in 1995, draws amateur and professional gourmets to sample delicacies from all around the world, including nations that do not have a permanent presence in World Showcase. Celebrity chefs are often on-hand to host the events. In 2008, the festival featured the <a href="/wiki/Bocuse_d%27Or_USA" title="Bocuse d'Or USA">Bocuse d'Or USA</a>, the American semifinal of the biennial <a href="/wiki/Bocuse_d%27Or" title="Bocuse d'Or">Bocuse d'Or</a> cooking competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Epcot International Festival of the Arts, new in 2017, is a festival showcasing visual, culinary, and performing arts. The inaugural year will take place on weekends from January 13 through February 20, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>Holidays Around the World is Epcot's annual holiday celebration. The World Showcase pavilions feature storytellers describing their nation's holiday traditions, and three nightly performances of the "Candlelight Processional" featuring an auditioned mass choir and a celebrity guest narrating the story of Christmas. During "Holidays Around the World", Illuminations: Reflections of Earth features a special extended ending.</li>
<li>On <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a>, the park offers a variety of additional entertainment including live DJ dance areas throughout the park and a special New Year's Eve countdown edition of <a href="/wiki/IllumiNations:_Reflections_of_Earth#New_Year.27s_Eve_Countdown_Edition" title="IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth">IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth</a>.</li>
</ul>
