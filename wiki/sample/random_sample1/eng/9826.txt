<p><i><b>Epic</b></i> (stylized as <i><b>epic</b></i>) is a 2013 American <a href="/wiki/3D_film" title="3D film">3D</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_animated" title="Computer animated">computer animated</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a>-<a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure</a> film<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR5Animators_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR5Animators-5">[5]</a></sup> loosely based on <a href="/wiki/William_Joyce_(writer)" title="William Joyce (writer)">William Joyce</a>'s children's book <i>The Leaf Men and the Brave Good Bugs</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety1-6">[6]</a></sup> It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Blue_Sky_Studios" title="Blue Sky Studios">Blue Sky Studios</a>, written by William Joyce, <a href="/wiki/James_V._Hart" title="James V. Hart">James V. Hart</a>, Daniel Shere, Tom J. Astle and Matt Ember and directed by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Wedge" title="Chris Wedge">Chris Wedge</a>, the director of <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age_(2002_film)" title="Ice Age (2002 film)">Ice Age</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/Robots_(2005_film)" title="Robots (2005 film)">Robots</a></i> (2005). The film stars the voices of <a href="/wiki/Colin_Farrell" title="Colin Farrell">Colin Farrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Hutcherson" title="Josh Hutcherson">Josh Hutcherson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Seyfried" title="Amanda Seyfried">Amanda Seyfried</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christoph_Waltz" title="Christoph Waltz">Christoph Waltz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aziz_Ansari" title="Aziz Ansari">Aziz Ansari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_O%27Dowd" title="Chris O'Dowd">Chris O'Dowd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Sudeikis" title="Jason Sudeikis">Jason Sudeikis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">Steven Tyler</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé Knowles</a>. The film was released on May 24, 2013 by <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>. <i>Epic</i> received mixed reviews from critics and earned $268 million on a $93 million budget.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>17-year-old Mary Katherine, or M.K., moves in with her eccentric <a href="/wiki/Scientist" title="Scientist">scientist</a> father Professor Bomba who has been searching for tiny humanoid soldiers called Leafmen. They protect the forest Bomba lives near from wicked creatures called Boggans and their malevolent leader Mandrake. An independent young soldier named Nod decides to quit much to the ire of the no-nonsense Leafmen leader Ronin.</p>
<p>The queen of the forest, Queen Tara, must choose an heir to her throne and goes out to a field of leaf pods, guarded by a laid-back <a href="/wiki/Slug" title="Slug">slug</a> named Mub and a wannabe Leafman <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">snail</a> named Grub. Immediately after she chooses a pod, the Boggans attack. Tara flees the area with the pod, and though her bodyguards do their best to protect her, they are soon overwhelmed by the sheer number of Boggans. Eventually, Ronin arrives for her and the pair fly off on his <a href="/wiki/Hummingbird" title="Hummingbird">hummingbird</a> mount. They are then attacked by Mandrake and his son Dagda. Dagda is killed by Ronin, but the former shoots Tara beforehand.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, M.K. decides to leave after having an argument with Bomba about his research. Before she can leave, Bomba's dog Ozzy runs into the woods. While looking for Ozzy, M.K. sees Tara falling. Dying, Tara gives her the pod and uses her magic to shrink her. She tells M.K. to take the pod to a <a href="/wiki/Glowworm" title="Glowworm">Glowworm</a> named Nim Galuu before she dies, and M.K. joins Ronin and the Leafmen, along with Mub and Grub. Ronin discovers that Nod has entered a race against other creatures and <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">bugs</a> on birds. Nod goes back on a deal with a tough <a href="/wiki/American_bullfrog" title="American bullfrog">bullfrog</a> named Bufo to <a href="/wiki/Match_fixing" title="Match fixing">throw the race</a>. Before Bufo and his two henchmen can feed Nod to a snake for not throwing the race, Ronin intervenes and orders them to leave. A reluctant Nod joins him, M.K., Mub, and Grub after hearing about Queen Tara's death which Bufo overhears.</p>
<p>Ronin, Nod, M.K., Mub, and Grub eventually track Nim Galuu down. He then leads them down to the scroll library, where M.K. discovers Tara's brief message before shrinking her, and a message that will get her back to normal size. When Ronin leaves, Nod takes M.K. on a <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">deer</a> ride and they begin to fall in love. Meanwhile, Mandrake has had the Boggans bring Bufo to him, finding out the location of the pod. Mandrake goes to Nim Galuu's place to steal the pod, which, if it blooms in darkness will help Mandrake destroy the forest. He takes the pod and kidnaps Mub and Grub. Ronin scolds Nod for not being there to protect the pod. To get into Boggan territory undiscovered, M.K., Nod, and Ronin set out to Bomba's house to get some disguises, where M.K. learns that the Leafmen have deliberately been leading Bomba off their trail. Bomba sees that he has visitors and captures M.K., fainting when he sees her. M.K. marks the location of Moonhaven on a map Bomba has made of the forest before rejoining Nod and Ronin.</p>
<p>When they reach the Boggan land Ronin distracts the Boggans while M.K. and Nod rescue Mub, Grub, and the pod. Mandrake discovers them and orders the Boggans to stop them. M.K., Nod, Mub, and Grub escape alive, but Ronin sacrifices himself to ensure their escape. Before the <a href="/wiki/Full_moon" title="Full moon">full moon</a> can sprout the pod at Moonhaven, Mandrake's <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a> block the light, causing the pod to begin sprouting in darkness. The Leafmen set out to fight the Boggans; M.K. tries to get help from her father by visiting his various cameras he had set in the forest. However, upon regaining consciousness, Bomba believes that he didn't really see M.K. and that he has been insane all these years, and shuts down all his cameras. He changes his mind when he sees a red push-pin that M.K. had put on his map.</p>
<p>Bomba is overjoyed to see that he has been right and he follows M.K. to Moonhaven. M.K. uses Bomba's <a href="/wiki/IPod" title="IPod">iPod</a> to make bat sounds, luring Mandrake's bats away. Meanwhile, Mub and Nim Galuu try to stop Mandrake from reaching the pod, but are unsuccessful. Just then, Ronin appears, bearing scars and bruises from the Boggans. Mandrake manages to outdo him, but Ronin is defended by Nod, who finally realizes the importance of teamwork. Before Mandrake can obtain his victory, the pod blooms in moonlight, defeating the Boggans, and sealing Mandrake into a nearby tree.</p>
<p>The chosen heir is the flower child who helped save Tara earlier in the film. Grub becomes a Leafman, Nod and Ronin reconcile, and Nod and M.K. kiss before M.K. is returned to her original size. After reuniting with Bomba and becoming his assistant, the human family still keeps regular contact with their small friends as they continue the research of their world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, it was reported that Chris Wedge would be directing an animated feature film based on <a href="/wiki/William_Joyce_(writer)" title="William Joyce (writer)">William Joyce</a>'s book, <i>The Leaf Men and the Brave Good Bugs</i> for Fox Animation. Joyce, who had already collaborated with Wedge as a designer and producer on the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Robots_(2005_film)" title="Robots (2005 film)">Robots</a></i>, was set to produce the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> At one point, Wedge got permission to find a new home for the film and turned to <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar Animation Studios</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/John_Lasseter" title="John Lasseter">John Lasseter</a>, a close friend that Wedge knew from working on <i><a href="/wiki/Tron" title="Tron">Tron</a></i>. When Pixar tried to close the rights for the film and start development, Fox changed their mind, and the film returned to Fox. The film was officially greenlit in 2009, under the title <i>Leaf Men</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Variety1-6">[6]</a></sup> In May 2012, Fox announced the final title for the film (<i>Epic</i>), its first cast details, and a plot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadline1_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-Deadline1-7">[7]</a></sup> According to Wedge, he was not satisfied with the renaming, which was decided by the marketing department. He also expressed dissatisfaction with subtitles given to the film in some non-English countries, including the French subtitle, <i>The Battle of the Secret Kingdom</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the film is based on and borrows many characters from Joyce's book, its plot has been significantly changed. Wedge explained: "But while Bill wrote a wonderful book, it is a quaint story. We wanted to make a gigantic action-adventure movie." To address online speculations about whether the film is similar to other films, like <i><a href="/wiki/FernGully:_The_Last_Rainforest" title="FernGully: The Last Rainforest">FernGully: The Last Rainforest</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i>, Wedge said: "I hate to associate it with other movies. It is adventure on the scale of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i>. And it does immerse the audience completely in a world like <i>Avatar</i>. But it has its own personality."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USATodaySneak_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-USATodaySneak-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was released publicly on May 16, 2013, in Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Germany, Peru, Puerto Rico and Switzerland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The film was released in the United States on May 24, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USATodaySneak_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-USATodaySneak-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Produced on a budget of $93 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarietyWeekend_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-VarietyWeekend-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeadlineWeekend_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-DeadlineWeekend-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Epic</i> grossed $107,518,682 in North America, and $160,907,952 in other countries, for a worldwide total of $268,426,634.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-4">[4]</a></sup> In North America, the film earned $9.3 million on its opening day,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeadlineWeekend_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-DeadlineWeekend-3">[3]</a></sup> and opened to number four in its first weekend, with $33,531,068, behind <i><a href="/wiki/Fast_%26_Furious_6" title="Fast &amp; Furious 6">Fast &amp; Furious 6</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hangover_Part_III" title="The Hangover Part III">The Hangover Part III</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Into_Darkness" title="Star Trek Into Darkness">Star Trek Into Darkness</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In its second weekend, the film dropped to number five, grossing an additional $16,616,310.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In its third weekend, the film stayed at number five, grossing $11,876,003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In its fourth weekend, the film dropped to number seven, grossing $6,284,905.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> While the film was overshadowed by other animated films that summer including <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters_University" title="Monsters University">Monsters University</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Despicable_Me_2" title="Despicable Me 2">Despicable Me 2</a></i>, the film finished in third out of six family films that summer, and became a moderate box office success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Epic</i> received mixed reviews from critics. <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives the film a score of 64% based on 119 reviews, with an average rating of 5.9/10. The site's critical consensus reads: "Though its narrative themes are all too familiar, <i>Epic</i> is beautifully animated and crafted with just enough flair to make for solid family entertainment."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Another review aggregation website, <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Normalization_(statistics)" title="Normalization (statistics)">normalized</a> rating out of 100 top reviews from mainstream critics, calculated a score of 52 out of 100 based on 30 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Audiences polled by the market research firm <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> gave <i>Epic</i> an "A" grade on average and an "A+" among kids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stephan Lee of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> gave the film a B+, saying "The story lacks the specialness of a <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a> movie — it retreads the same eco-battle archetypes as <i><a href="/wiki/FernGully:_The_Last_Rainforest" title="FernGully: The Last Rainforest">FernGully</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i> — but it's a perfectly appealing explosion of color for a lazy summer day."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Michael Rechtshaffen of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> gave the film a mixed review, saying "Where the animated film comes up short is on the inspiration front -- despite the intriguing terrain, its stock inhabitants lack the sort of unique personality traits that would prevent them from feeling overly familiar."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Michael Phillips of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> gave the film two stars, saying "It's difficult to keep its story and characters, or even its visual design, in your mind's eye, in part because the five credited screenwriters overload the narrative with incident and threatening complication."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Kyle Smith of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> gave the film one star and a half out of four, saying "It’s not that the plot of <i>Epic</i> is complex; it’s just untidy. There’s a lot going on that goes nowhere."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Moira Macdonald of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seattle_Times" title="The Seattle Times">The Seattle Times</a></i> gave the film three and a half stars out of four, saying "The story's simple enough to appeal to young kids (the 8-year-old with me pronounced the movie "awesome"), but adults will enjoy the beautiful animation, whether 3D or 2D."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Stephen Holden of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> gave the film two and a half stars out of five, saying "As beautiful as it is, <i>Epic</i> is fatally lacking in visceral momentum and dramatic edge."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Epic</i> was released on DVD, Blu-ray and Blu-ray 3D on August 20, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Danny_Elfman" title="Danny Elfman">Danny Elfman</a> composed the original music for the film, which was released on May 28, 2013 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Classics" title="Sony Classics">Sony Classics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> performed and co-wrote with <a href="/wiki/Sia_Furler" title="Sia Furler">Sia</a> an original song, titled "Rise Up."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The song was released as a digital single<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and as part of the digital version of the soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> This is the first time that a film produced by <a href="/wiki/Blue_Sky_Studios" title="Blue Sky Studios">Blue Sky Studios</a> has not been scored by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Powell_(composer)" title="John Powell (composer)">John Powell</a> since the first <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age_(2002_film)" title="Ice Age (2002 film)">Ice Age</a></i> installment, which was composed by <a href="/wiki/David_Newman_(composer)" title="David Newman (composer)">David Newman</a>.</p>
