<p><i><b>Epic Movie</b></i> is a 2007 American <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed and written by <a href="/wiki/Jason_Friedberg_and_Aaron_Seltzer" title="Jason Friedberg and Aaron Seltzer">Jason Friedberg and Aaron Seltzer</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Schiff" title="Paul Schiff">Paul Schiff</a>. It was the first film to be distributed by Regency Enterprises. It was made in a similar style to <i><a href="/wiki/Date_Movie" title="Date Movie">Date Movie</a></i>, Friedberg and Seltzer's previous film, but as a spoof of the "<a href="/wiki/Epic_film" title="Epic film">Epic</a>" style of films, hence the name. The film mostly references <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" title="The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe">The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a></i> films, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Burton" title="Tim Burton">Tim Burton</a>'s version of <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie_and_the_Chocolate_Factory" title="Charlie and the Chocolate Factory">Charlie and the Chocolate Factory</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men" title="X-Men">X-Men</a></i>. The song "<a href="/wiki/Ms._New_Booty" title="Ms. New Booty">Ms. New Booty</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Bubba_Sparxxx" title="Bubba Sparxxx">Bubba Sparxxx</a> gained commercial attention for being featured in <i>Epic Movie</i>.</p>
<p>The film was heavily panned by critics, and is considered one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_considered_the_worst" title="List of films considered the worst">worst of all time</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lucy (<a href="/wiki/Jayma_Mays" title="Jayma Mays">Jayma Mays</a>) finds that her adoptive father (<a href="/wiki/David_Carradine" title="David Carradine">David Carradine</a>), a museum curator, has been attacked by Silas (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart_(actor)" title="Kevin Hart (actor)">Kevin Hart</a>). Before dying, he gives clues which lead her to a "Golden Ticket" in a vending machine candy bar. During "comedic" situations, Golden Tickets are found by other orphans, which include Edward (<a href="/wiki/Kal_Penn" title="Kal Penn">Kal Penn</a>), a disillusioned monk trainee; Susan (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faune_Chambers" title="Faune Chambers">Faune Chambers</a>), a displaced adopted girl; and Peter (<a href="/wiki/Adam_Campbell_(actor)" title="Adam Campbell (actor)">Adam Campbell</a>), a <a href="/wiki/Mutant_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Mutant (Marvel Comics)">mutant</a> at Mutant Academy who is often teased for his chicken-like wings. All four meet up at Willy's <a href="/wiki/Charlie_and_the_Chocolate_Factory_(film)" title="Charlie and the Chocolate Factory (film)">Chocolate Factory</a>. Willy (<a href="/wiki/Crispin_Glover" title="Crispin Glover">Crispin Glover</a>) reveals his plot to use them all as a special ingredient in his treats.</p>
<p>In an effort to hide from the maniacal Willy, Lucy finds <a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" title="The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe">a wardrobe</a>. On the other side, in the middle of a wintry forest, she finds <a href="/wiki/Mr._Tumnus" title="Mr. Tumnus">Mr. Tumnus</a> (<a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Jim%C3%A9nez" title="Héctor Jiménez">Hector Jimenez</a>), who welcomes Lucy to Gnarnia and warns her of danger. The others follow Lucy to Gnarnia, and Edward meets the <a href="/wiki/White_Witch" title="White Witch">White Bitch</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Coolidge" title="Jennifer Coolidge">Jennifer Coolidge</a>). She convinces him to trap the other orphans in order to become the king of Gnarnia in her <a href="/wiki/Harold_%26_Kumar_Go_to_White_Castle" title="Harold &amp; Kumar Go to White Castle">White Castle</a>. All four go to Tumnus' house, where they discover that they are all related to one another, and that the White Bitch killed their parents. They ally themselves with Harry Beaver (<a href="/wiki/Katt_Williams" title="Katt Williams">Katt Williams</a>), Tumnus' life partner, to defeat the White Bitch.</p>
<p>Edward sneaks off to the White Bitch's castle and refuses to tell her where the others are. She flashes him her breasts, hypnotizing him into giving up the information on the orphans, then imprisons him. The White Bitch sends Silas after the trio; Tumnus apparently sacrifices himself to ensure their safety. Afterwards, the orphans meet a graying <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(character)" title="Harry Potter (character)">Harry Potter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kevin_McDonald" title="Kevin McDonald">Kevin McDonald</a>), along with a balding <a href="/wiki/Ron_Weasley" title="Ron Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> (George Alvarez) and a pregnant <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> (<a href="/wiki/Crista_Flanagan" title="Crista Flanagan">Crista Flanagan</a>) at Hogwarts, and other characters from famous movies. They all help Lucy, Susan, and Peter train for the war against the White Bitch. Meanwhile, the White Bitch reveals her plans to <a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">create a new continent for her followers</a> via a magic crystal. Edward escapes with the assistance of <a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_Dead_Man%27s_Chest" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest">Captain Jack Swallows</a> (<a href="/wiki/Darrell_Hammond" title="Darrell Hammond">Darrell Hammond</a>), only to find out it was a ruse as Jack, the Bitch's old enemy, needs intelligence out of Edward. Jack is then stabbed by the White Bitch as the magic crystal is thrown in the ocean and grows, putting the Bitch's plan in motion.</p>
<p>Upon finishing their training, Lucy, Susan, and Peter head to the camp of <a href="/wiki/Aslan" title="Aslan">Aslo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Fred_Willard" title="Fred Willard">Fred Willard</a>). Aslo agrees to help Edward, and he manages to kill Silas, but while breaking Edward out, Aslo is slain by the White Bitch. As the orphans have a pre-battle party with their allies, Susan gets drunk and vomits everywhere. Their army is disgusted enough that nobody shows up to help the orphans the next day. The four siblings engage Jack, who is using a giant wooden wheel, in battle and are defeated. Peter then finds <a href="/wiki/Click_(2006_film)" title="Click (2006 film)">a magic remote</a> and uses its powers to save his siblings. Together, they kill the army, defeat the White Bitch, and stop her plan. Peter declares the White Bitch will receive a fair and just trial in the new Gnarnia, but Jack's wheel accidentally crushes her. The four are crowned the new rulers of the land. Tumnus then shows up, having survived his battle. Decades later, the four now-elderly rulers find the wardrobe again and go through it. They appear moments after they had left, young again. They meet <a href="/wiki/Borat_Sagdiyev" title="Borat Sagdiyev">Borat</a> (<a href="/wiki/Danny_Jacobs_(actor)" title="Danny Jacobs (actor)">Danny Jacobs</a>), who congratulates them on a happy ending, but then, the four are accidentally run over by Jack's wheel.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extended_version">Extended version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Extended version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The unrated, longer version (released in the United Kingdom as the "Rude &amp; Crude Unseen Version") of the film features some scenes not shown in the theatrical version. In the last scene, Willy Wonka, instead of Borat, comes in and says: "I told you it was going to be an epic adventure." Willy Wonka then goes in the wardrobe and puts out a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Do_not_disturb_sign" title="Do not disturb sign">"do not disturb" sign</a> that refers to the girl in the wardrobe. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oompa-Loompa" title="Oompa-Loompa">Oompa-Loompas</a> come in and start singing the Willy Wonka theme song. The four are then crushed by the wheel. Also, during the scene where Lucy is crushed under the junk that falls out of the "Narnia" closet, the girl who runs out is nude, as opposed to wearing a bikini. In the <i><a href="/wiki/Snakes_on_a_Plane" title="Snakes on a Plane">Snakes on a Plane</a></i> scene, when the Samuel L. Jackson lookalike yells, he replaces "goddamn" with "motherfuckin'". In the sex scene with Mystique, Peter follows big hooters with the words silver dollar nipples.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Epic Movie</i> debuted at number one at the <a href="/wiki/Box_office" title="Box office">box office</a> with a gross of $18.6 million over the opening weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As of May 8, 2007, the film has grossed $86,865,564, with $39,739,367 of that amount earned domestically, despite negative reviews from critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-3">[3]</a></sup> The film was an economic success for its producers not least because it had a comparatively low budget, estimated at $20 million (the same as <i><a href="/wiki/Date_Movie" title="Date Movie">Date Movie</a></i>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Epic_Movie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Epic Movie</i> was panned by critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the film has an approval rating of 2% based on 64 reviews with an average rating of 2.3/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "A crude comedy with nothing new or insightful to say about the subjects it satirizes."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 17 out of 100 based on 17 critics, indicating "overwhelming dislike".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Audiences polled by <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> gave the film an average grade of "C-" on an A+ to F scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CinemaScore_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CinemaScore-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/A._O._Scott" title="A. O. Scott">A. O. Scott</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> called the film "irreverent and also appreciative, dragging its satiric prey down to the lowest pop-cultural denominator" and added, "The humor is coarse and occasionally funny. The archly bombastic score . . . is the only thing you might call witty. But happily, Jennifer Coolidge and Fred Willard show up . . . to add some easy, demented class."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mick_LaSalle" title="Mick LaSalle">Mick LaSalle</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i> thought "only a complete idiot could think <i>Epic Movie</i> is remotely funny or worth making at all." Describing it as "so bereft of anything resembling wit or inspiration", he wondered, "What were the perpetrators, uh filmmakers, thinking?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, Alex Chun called the film "nothing more than a disjointed series of scenes and references cobbled together as a backdrop for sophomoric humor,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and Ronnie Scheib of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> said it was "epically unfunny" and "unlikely to join the list of blockbusters it lampoons."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Radio_Times" title="Radio Times">Radio Times</a> said "There's very little that's epic about this senseless parody, but then there's very little that's funny about it, either... It's mind-numbingly, tediously unamusing and is so devoid of imagination it even parodies self-mocking films."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
