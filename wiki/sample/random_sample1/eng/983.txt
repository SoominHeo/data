<p><b>FC Zimbru Chișinău</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldovan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Chișinău">Chișinău</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a>. They play in the <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_National_Division" title="Moldovan National Division">Divizia Națională</a>, the top division in Moldovan football.</p>
<p>Founded in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moldovan_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Moldovan Soviet Socialist Republic">Moldovan Soviet Socialist Republic</a> in 1947, Zimbru entered the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Top_League" title="Soviet Top League">Soviet Top League</a> in 1956 and spent 11 seasons in it before their last relegation in 1983. The team was the early force in the independent <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_National_Division" title="Moldovan National Division">Moldovan National Division</a>, winning all of the first five championships and eight of the first nine, but have not won since.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zimbru_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_FC_Zimbru_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u_seasons" title="List of FC Zimbru Chișinău seasons">List of FC Zimbru Chișinău seasons</a></div>
<p>Zimbru Chișinău was formed in 1947 in the <a href="/wiki/Moldavian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic">Moldovan Soviet Republic</a> (present day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Moldova" title="Republic of Moldova">Republic of Moldova</a>). '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wisent" title="Wisent">Zimbru</a>' is a Romanian word for a form of European bison, but the club also previously functioned under names such as Dynamo, Burevestnik, Moldova, Avântul, and Nistru. The Soviet Era was spent mostly in Class B of the regional league until eventual promotion to Class A. The club then flitted between Class A and Class B as well as spending time in the Soviet Top League and First League. In total, Zimbru spent 11 seasons in the Top League between 1956 and 1983. Zimbru had their biggest success in <a href="/wiki/1956_Soviet_Top_League" title="1956 Soviet Top League">1956</a> when they finished in 6th place out of 12 in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Top_League" title="Soviet Top League">Soviet Top League</a> and in 1963 when they reached the quarter-finals of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Cup" title="Soviet Cup">Soviet Cup</a>.<br/>
Zimbru's fortunes changed after the fall of the USSR and the establishment of the Republic of Moldova. The club won all five of the initial seasons of the <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_National_Division" title="Moldovan National Division">Moldovan National Division</a> (1992–96), and apart from finishing as runners-up to Chișinău rivals <a href="/wiki/FC_Tiraspol" title="FC Tiraspol">Constructorul Chișinău</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1996-97_Moldovan_National_Division" title="1996-97 Moldovan National Division">1996–97</a>, won eight of the first nine championships. Zimbru have also won the <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_Cup" title="Moldovan Cup">Moldovan Cup</a> six times, including a double in 1997–98 and the <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_Super_Cup" title="Moldovan Super Cup">Moldovan Super Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/2014_Moldovan_Super_Cup" title="2014 Moldovan Super Cup">2014</a>. Zimbru's biggest rivalry is with <a href="/wiki/FC_Sheriff_Tiraspol" title="FC Sheriff Tiraspol">Sheriff Tiraspol</a>, who in the last sixteen years have won fourteen championships.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crest_and_colours">Crest and colours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zimbru_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Crest and colours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since its foundation, Zimbru's colours always was yellow and green. Throughout history, Zimbru Chișinău had many logos. Traditional colours were always present on club crests.</p>

<p>FC Zimbru's home ground is Zimbru Stadium, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football-specific_stadium" title="Football-specific stadium">football-specific stadium</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Botanica_district" title="Botanica district">Botanica district</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Chișinău">Chișinău</a>. It was opened in 2006. Stadium has a natural grass playing surface, and its capacity is 10,400.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Zimbru_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1990s (the first decade of Moldova's independence), Zimbru's rival was the other team from <a href="/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Chișinău">Chișinău</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Tiraspol" title="FC Tiraspol">Constructorul Chișinău</a>. Then Contructorul was dissolved. In 1997, a new team was founded in <a href="/wiki/Tiraspol" title="Tiraspol">Tiraspol</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Sheriff_Tiraspol" title="FC Sheriff Tiraspol">Sheriff Tiraspol</a>. In a few years, Sheriff was promoted to the first league and became a force in Moldovan football. Considering the strength of team and the tensions between <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldovans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transnistria" title="Transnistria">separatist Transnistrians</a>, the match between Zimbru and Sheriff became a derby, the most important match in country. Thus, it has been named "Derby of Moldova". Another rivalry was established in the mid 2000s when <a href="/wiki/FC_Dacia_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="FC Dacia Chișinău">Dacia Chișinău</a>, another team from <a href="/wiki/Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="Chișinău">Chișinău</a>, became one of Moldova's top teams. Zimbru also has a rivalry with <a href="/wiki/FC_Zaria_B%C4%83l%C8%9Bi" title="FC Zaria Bălți">Olimpia Bălți</a>.</p>
