<p><b>Héctor Bellerín Moruno</b> (born 19 March 1995) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right-back</a> for English club <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain national team</a>. Bellerín started his career as an attacking winger for <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>, and credits his development as a successful right-back to <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bould" title="Steve Bould">Steve Bould</a> upon his arrival at <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in the summer of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His speedy pace, skillful dribbling and ball crossing has also helped in creating chances. On 21 November 2016, he signed a long term contract that ties him to the club until 2022.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bellerín played internationally for Spain from <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-16_football_team" title="Spain national under-16 football team">under-16</a> to <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">under-21</a> levels, making his <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">senior</a> debut in 2016 and being chosen for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">the year's European Championship</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, Bellerín started his club football career in the youth teams of <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>. He moved to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in the summer of 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and signed his first professional contract with the club in July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Bellerín made his competitive debut away to <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_Cup" title="2013–14 Football League Cup">the third round</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> on 25 September 2013, coming on in the 95th minute as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Mikel_Arteta" title="Mikel Arteta">Mikel Arteta</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout" title="Penalty shootout">penalty shootout</a> victory after a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Two months later, Bellerín joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on a two-month loan deal, and made his debut against <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a> eight days after signing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The loan at Watford was extended until the end of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> but he was recalled by Arsenal in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>Following injuries to <a href="/wiki/Mathieu_Debuchy" title="Mathieu Debuchy">Mathieu Debuchy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calum_Chambers" title="Calum Chambers">Calum Chambers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nacho_Monreal" title="Nacho Monreal">Nacho Monreal</a>, Bellerín made his <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> debut on 16 September 2014 in a 2–0 defeat away to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for Arsenal on 1 February 2015, in a 5–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and added a second on 4 April to open a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> despite also conceding a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty kick</a> through a foul on <a href="/wiki/Raheem_Sterling" title="Raheem Sterling">Raheem Sterling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bellerín was selected to start for Arsenal in the <a href="/wiki/2015_FA_Cup_Final" title="2015 FA Cup Final">2015 FA Cup Final</a> on 30 May, helping the team keep a clean sheet in a 4–0 win over Aston Villa at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He signed a new long-term contract before the 2015–16 season and played the entirety of Arsenal's 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C.%E2%80%93Chelsea_F.C._rivalry" title="Arsenal F.C.–Chelsea F.C. rivalry">rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2015_FA_Community_Shield" title="2015 FA Community Shield">2015 FA Community Shield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He was the sole Arsenal player listed in <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Premier_League" title="2015–16 Premier League">the season's</a> <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year" title="PFA Team of the Year">PFA Team of the Year</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pfa_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-pfa-15">[15]</a></sup> and came in third place in Arsenal's Player of the Season voting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arsenal.com_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-arsenal.com-16">[16]</a></sup> On 21 November 2016, he signed a new long-term contract after agreeing to a deal that ties him to the club until 2022.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span></h2>
<p>Having reached the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2013 European Championship</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-19_football_team" title="Spain national under-19 football team">under-19 team</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Bellerín made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Albert_Celades" title="Albert Celades">Albert Celades</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">under-21 team</a> on 30 March 2015, playing the full 90 minutes of a 4–0 friendly win over Belarus in <a href="/wiki/Le%C3%B3n,_Spain" title="León, Spain">León</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 May 2016, after being named as a stand-by player for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">the year's European Championship</a>, Bellerín made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">the full squad</a> by starting in a 3–1 friendly win against <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFG_Arena" title="AFG Arena">AFG Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/St._Gallen" title="St. Gallen">St. Gallen</a>, Switzerland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Two days later, he was chosen for the final squad after <a href="/wiki/Dani_Carvajal" title="Dani Carvajal">Dani Carvajal</a> withdrew through injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He was unused as they reached the last 16.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span></h2>
<p>Bellerín is renowned for his speed. At the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Arsenal_F.C._season" title="2014–15 Arsenal F.C. season">2014–15 season</a>, he broke <a href="/wiki/Theo_Walcott" title="Theo Walcott">Theo Walcott's</a> 40 metre Arsenal sprint record by 1/100th of a second.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
