<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Enrique Cerezo</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Enrique Cerezo Torres</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[enˈrike θeˈɾeθo]</a></span> is a cinematographic producer and the current president of <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>, a position he has held since 2002.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cinematographic_career">Cinematographic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Cerezo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cinematographic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cerezo began in cinema in 1971 as a camera assistant in the movie <i>Vente a Alemania, Pepe</i>. Three years later, he directed his first and only film, <i>La sonrisa del sol: Almería</i>.</p>
<p>As a cinema producer, he is the owner of Enrique Cerezo – Cinematographic Productions and has produced many films. His most famous films produced include <i>El perro del hortelano</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_buena_estrella" title="La buena estrella">La buena estrella</a></i>, <i>Pídele cuentas al rey</i>, <i>Yoyes</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juana_la_Loca_(2001_film)" title="Juana la Loca (2001 film)">Juana la Loca</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_hora_de_los_valientes" title="La hora de los valientes">La hora de los valientes</a></i> and <i>La vida de nadie</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidency_of_Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid">Presidency of Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Cerezo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Presidency of Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the ascent of <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> to <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> in 2002, <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Gil" title="Jesús Gil">Jesús Gil</a> opted to leave the presidency of the organization, although his family continued to control it and decided to designate Enrique Cerezo as president, appreciating his collaboration with the club for many years.</p>
<p>In his first season, the club made several transfers of certain reputable players, such as <a href="/wiki/Javi_Moreno" title="Javi Moreno">Javi Moreno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demetrio_Albertini" title="Demetrio Albertini">Demetrio Albertini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabricio_Coloccini" title="Fabricio Coloccini">Fabricio Coloccini</a> coming from <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>, but despite this, the club finished the season in 12th position.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_season" title="2003–04 Atlético Madrid season">2003–04 season</a>, the club sacked coach <a href="/wiki/Luis_Aragon%C3%A9s" title="Luis Aragonés">Luis Aragonés</a> and replaced him with <a href="/wiki/Gregorio_Manzano" title="Gregorio Manzano">Gregorio Manzano</a>. Great part of the deals of the previous season leave the club, and in this year, players like <a href="/wiki/Diego_Simeone" title="Diego Simeone">Diego Simeone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ariel_Ibagaza" title="Ariel Ibagaza">Ariel Ibagaza</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mat%C3%ADas_Emanuel_Lequi" title="Matías Emanuel Lequi">Matías Emanuel Lequi</a> arrived. The club finished in seventh position, making it a poor season.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_season" title="2004–05 Atlético Madrid season">2004–05 season</a>, the club replaced their coach again, hiring <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Ferrando" title="César Ferrando">César Ferrando</a> and he aimed to renovate <i>Los Rojiblancos</i>. At that time, the club purchased central defenders <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Ib%C3%A1%C3%B1ez" title="Pablo Ibáñez">Pablo Ibáñez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Amaranto_Perea" title="Luis Amaranto Perea">Luis Perea</a>. Ferrando's plans did not materialize and the club finished 11th, again outside of European football.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_season" title="2005–06 Atlético Madrid season">2005–06 season</a>, veteran <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Bianchi" title="Carlos Bianchi">Carlos Bianchi</a> was appointed by the club. Atlético made great investments by bringing stars like <a href="/wiki/Martin_Petrov" title="Martin Petrov">Martin Petrov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maxi_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Maxi Rodríguez">Maxi Rodríguez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman" title="Mateja Kežman">Mateja Kežman</a>. Nevertheless, however, the team did not produce good results and Bianchi was dismissed midway through the season, replaced by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Murcia" title="José Murcia">Pepe Murcia</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_season" title="2009–10 Atlético Madrid season">2009–10 season</a>, the arrival of head coach <a href="/wiki/Quique_S%C3%A1nchez_Flores" title="Quique Sánchez Flores">Quique Sánchez Flores</a> saw a huge change of fortunes. Though they continued to lag somewhat in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>, finishing the year in ninth, they managed to get third place in the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2009–10 UEFA Champions League group stage">2009–10 UEFA Champions League group stage</a>, and subsequently entered the season's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> in the round of 32 and winning the Europa League title after beating English teams <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the semi-finals and eventually <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2010 UEFA Europa League Final">final</a>. <a href="/wiki/Diego_Forl%C3%A1n" title="Diego Forlán">Diego Forlán</a> scored twice, the second being an extra-time winner in the 116th minute, as Atlético Madrid won 2–1.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_season" title="2010–11 Atlético Madrid season">2010–11 season</a>, Atlético had a comparatively disappointing performance, finishing only seventh in La Liga and being eliminated in the quarter-finals of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> and the group stage of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Europa League">2010–11 UEFA Europa League</a>. This ultimately led to the departure of manager Sánchez Flores before the conclusion of the season, who was replaced with ex-Sevilla manager <a href="/wiki/Gregorio_Manzano" title="Gregorio Manzano">Gregorio Manzano</a>, and who secured the final Europa League place for Atlético. Manzano himself was replaced with Diego Simeone in December 2011 after a poor run of form in La Liga.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_season" title="2011–12 Atlético Madrid season">2011–12 season</a>, former player Diego Simeone led Atlétio to their second Europa League title in the three years since its creation after they beat <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a> 3–0 in the <a href="/wiki/2012_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2012 UEFA Europa League Final">final</a> on 9 May 2012 in <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Radamel_Falcao" title="Radamel Falcao">Radamel Falcao</a> scoring twice and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diego_Ribas_da_Cunha" title="Diego Ribas da Cunha">Diego</a> once. Again, by winning the Europa League, they qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2012_UEFA_Super_Cup" title="2012 UEFA Super Cup">2012 UEFA Super Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>, winner of the previous season's Champions League, which was played in Monaco on 31 August 2012. Atlético won 4–1, including a hat-trick scored by Falcao in the first half. On 16 May 2013, Atlético beat <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> 2–1 in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Copa_del_Rey_Final" title="2013 Copa del Rey Final">Copa del Rey final</a>, in a tense match where both teams finished with ten men. This ended a 14-year and 25-match winless streak in the Madrid derby.</p>
<p>The 2012–13 season saw the club finish with three trophies in a little over a year. As the undefeated winners of their Champions League group against FC Zenit, FC Porto and Austria Wien, and later defeating AC Milan, FC Barcelona and Chelsea in the knock-out rounds, Atlético played their first Champions League final since 1974, in Lisbon's Estádio da Luz against city rivals Real Madrid. Atlético took a first-half lead through Diego Godín and survived Real's pressure until the third minute of injury time, when Sergio Ramos scored an equaliser from a corner by <a href="/wiki/Luka_Modri%C4%87" title="Luka Modrić">Luka Modrić</a>. The match went into extra time and ended in a 4–1 loss. However, Atlético did have the consolation of winning La Liga for the first time since 1996, with Godín's headed equaliser against Barcelona on the last day of the season, a week before the Champions League final.</p>
