<p><b>Enrique Miguel Iglesias Preysler</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[enˈrike miˈɣel iˈɣlesjas pɾeizˈleɾ]</a></span>; born 8 May 1975), known professionally as <b>Enrique Iglesias</b>, is a Spanish singer, songwriter, actor and record producer. He is widely regarded as the <a href="/wiki/Honorific_nicknames_in_popular_music" title="Honorific nicknames in popular music">King of Latin Pop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Iglesias started his career in the mid-1990s on an American Spanish-language record label <a href="/wiki/Fonovisa_Records" title="Fonovisa Records">Fonovisa Records</a> under the name Enrique Martinez, before switching to the stage name Enrique Iglesias<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and becoming one of the biggest stars in Latin America and the Hispanic market in the United States. By the turn of the millennium, he had made a successful crossover into the mainstream market and signed a multi-album deal with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a> for US $68 million with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Latin_Entertainment" title="Universal Music Latin Entertainment">Universal Music Latino</a> to release his Spanish albums and <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> to release English albums. In 2010, he parted with Interscope Records and signed with another Universal Music Group label <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a> to release bilingual albums. In 2015, Iglesias parted ways with Universal Music Group after a decade. He signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music</a>; his upcoming albums will be by <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Latin" title="Sony Music Latin">Sony Music Latin</a> in Spanish and <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> in English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iglesias has sold over 159 million records worldwide, making him one of the best-selling Spanish artists ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Iglesias has scored over 150 number-one hits across all of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts"><i>Billboard</i> charts</a>. He has had five <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> top five singles, including two number-ones, and holds the record for producing 27 number-one Spanish-language singles on the <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Tracks" title="Hot Latin Tracks">Hot Latin Tracks</a>. He also holds the record for most number-one hits and the longest-running number-one hit on that chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Iglesias also has 14 number-ones on <a href="/wiki/Dance_Club_Songs" title="Dance Club Songs"><i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Dance charts</a>, more than any other male artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Iglesias was born in Madrid, Spain, and is the third and youngest child of Spanish singer <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias" title="Julio Iglesias">Julio Iglesias</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipina</a> socialite and magazine journalist <a href="/wiki/Isabel_Preysler" title="Isabel Preysler">Isabel Preysler</a>. Isabel's aunt is actress <a href="/wiki/Neile_Adams" title="Neile Adams">Neile Adams</a>, the first wife of American actor <a href="/wiki/Steve_McQueen" title="Steve McQueen">Steve McQueen</a> and grandmother of actor <a href="/wiki/Steven_R._McQueen" title="Steven R. McQueen">Steven R. McQueen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was raised with two older siblings: <a href="/wiki/Chabeli_Iglesias" title="Chabeli Iglesias">Chabeli</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias,_Jr." title="Julio Iglesias, Jr.">Julio</a>. His mother is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipina</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and his father is Spanish, with family from <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>. His father has also claimed Puerto Rican<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> ancestry on his mother's side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> His parents divorced in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>At first, Iglesias and his two siblings stayed with their mother,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.standard.co.uk.2Fshowbiz.2Fenrique-iglesias-why-hes-different-to-his-lothario-father-julio-7299593.html_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.standard.co.uk.2Fshowbiz.2Fenrique-iglesias-why-hes-different-to-his-lothario-father-julio-7299593.html-16">[16]</a></sup> but in 1986, Iglesias' grandfather, Dr. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias,_Sr." title="Julio Iglesias, Sr.">Julio Iglesias Puga</a>, was kidnapped by the armed Basque terrorist group <a href="/wiki/ETA_(separatist_group)" title="ETA (separatist group)">ETA</a>. For their safety, Enrique and his brother Julio were sent to live with their father in Miami.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-refnov9_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-refnov9-17">[17]</a></sup> There, they were brought up mostly by the nanny, Elvira Olivares, to whom he later dedicated his first album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.standard.co.uk.2Fshowbiz.2Fenrique-iglesias-why-hes-different-to-his-lothario-father-julio-7299593.html_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.standard.co.uk.2Fshowbiz.2Fenrique-iglesias-why-hes-different-to-his-lothario-father-julio-7299593.html-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He also lived in <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, for one year with his mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> As his father's career kept him on the road, the young Iglesias was raised by the family nanny.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He attended the prestigious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulliver_Preparatory_School" title="Gulliver Preparatory School">Gulliver Preparatory School</a> and later went on to study business at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">University of Miami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iglesias did not want his father to know about his plans for a musical career and did not want his famous surname to help advance his career. He borrowed money from his family nanny and he recorded a demo cassette tape which consisted of a Spanish song and two English songs. Approaching his father's former publicist, Fernán Martínez, the two promoted the songs under the stage name 'Enrique Martínez' with the backstory of being an unknown singer from Guatemala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Iglesias was signed on to <a href="/wiki/Fonovisa_Records" title="Fonovisa Records">Fonovisa Records</a>. After dropping out of college, he traveled to Toronto to record his first album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref121_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref121-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1995.E2.80.9396:_Enrique_Iglesias">1995–96: <i>Enrique Iglesias</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995–96: Enrique Iglesias">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 July 1995, Iglesias released <i><a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias_(album)" title="Enrique Iglesias (album)">Enrique Iglesias</a></i>, a collection of light rock ballads, including hits such as "<a href="/wiki/Si_T%C3%BA_Te_Vas_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Si Tú Te Vas (Enrique Iglesias song)">Si Tú Te Vas</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Experiencia_Religiosa" title="Experiencia Religiosa">Experiencia Religiosa</a>", and others. This album <i>Vivir</i>, along with Iglesias next two albums, was released by the Mexican label Fonovisa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The record sold half a million copies in its first week, a rare accomplishment then for an album recorded in a language other than English, going Gold in Portugal within the first week of release, and sold over a million copies in the next three months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusicbio_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusicbio-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>His song "<a href="/wiki/Por_Amarte" title="Por Amarte">Por Amarte</a>" was included in <a href="/wiki/Televisa" title="Televisa">Televisa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Telenovela" title="Telenovela">telenovela</a> <i>Marisol</i>, but with a twist: instead of <i>Por amarte daría mi vida</i> (To love you, I'd give my life), the words were <i>Por amarte Marisol, moriría</i> (To love you, Marisol, I'd die). The CD also yielded Italian and Portuguese editions of the album, with most of the songs translated into those languages.</p>
<p>Five singles released from this album, such as "Por Amarte", "<a href="/wiki/No_Llores_Por_M%C3%AD" title="No Llores Por Mí">No Llores Por Mí</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Trapecista" title="Trapecista">Trapecista</a>" topped the Billboard's Latin charts. The album went on to win Iglesias a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Latin Pop Performance.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997.E2.80.9398:_Vivir_and_Cosas_del_Amor">1997–98: <i>Vivir</i> and <i>Cosas del Amor</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1997–98: Vivir and Cosas del Amor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1997, Iglesias' stardom continued to rise with the release of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vivir_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Vivir (Enrique Iglesias album)">Vivir</a></i> (<i>To Live</i>), which put him up with other English language music superstars in sales for that year. The album also included a cover version of the <a href="/wiki/Yazoo_(band)" title="Yazoo (band)">Yazoo</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Only_You_(Yazoo_song)" title="Only You (Yazoo song)">Only You</a>", translated into Spanish as "Solo en Tí".</p>
<p>Three singles released from <i>Vivir</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Enamorado_Por_Primera_Vez" title="Enamorado Por Primera Vez">Enamorado Por Primera Vez</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solo_en_ti" title="Solo en ti">Sólo en Ti</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Miente" title="Miente">Miente</a>") topped the Latin singles chart as well as those in several Spanish-speaking countries. Along with his father and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Miguel" title="Luis Miguel">Luis Miguel</a>, Iglesias was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award" title="American Music Award">American Music Award</a> in the first-ever awarded category of Favorite Latin Artist. It was said beforehand that the elder Iglesias would walk out if he did not win the award. Iglesias lost out to his father, but did perform the song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lluvia_Cae" title="Lluvia Cae">Lluvia Cae</a>" at the event.</p>
<p>Insisting on playing stadiums for his first concert tour, that summer, Iglesias, backed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sidemen" title="Sidemen">sidemen</a> for <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Joel" title="Billy Joel">Billy Joel</a>, played to sold-out audiences in sixteen countries. Beginning in <a href="/wiki/Odessa,_Texas" title="Odessa, Texas">Odessa, Texas</a> the tour went on to three consecutive nights in Mexico's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plaza_de_toros_M%C3%A9xico" title="Plaza de toros México">Plaza de Toros</a>, two consecutive nights at <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auditorio_Coca-Cola" title="Auditorio Coca-Cola">Auditorio Coca-Cola</a> and two at the Estadio River Plate in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, Argentina to over 130,000 people as well as 19 arenas in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998, Iglesias released his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cosas_del_Amor_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Cosas del Amor (Enrique Iglesias album)">Cosas del Amor</a></i> (<i>Things of Love</i>). Taking a more mature musical direction, the album, aided by the popular singles "<a href="/wiki/Esperanza_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Esperanza (Enrique Iglesias song)">Esperanza</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Nunca_Te_Olvidar%C3%A9" title="Nunca Te Olvidaré">Nunca Te Olvidaré</a>", both of which topped the Latin singles chart, helped cement his status in the <a href="/wiki/Latin_music_(genre)" title="Latin music (genre)">Latin music scene</a>.</p>
<p>Iglesias did a short tour of smaller venues to accompany the release of the album, with one show being televised from <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a>, Mexico. This was followed by a larger world tour of over eighty shows in even bigger venues. The <i>Cosas del Amor</i> Tour was the first ever concert tour sponsored by <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a>.</p>
<p>He won an American Music Award in the category of Favorite Latin Artist against <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Martin" title="Ricky Martin">Ricky Martin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chayanne" title="Chayanne">Chayanne</a>. The song "Nunca te Olvidaré" was also used as the theme music for a Spanish soap opera of the same name and he sang the song himself on the last episode of the series.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1999.E2.80.932001:_Enrique_and_Escape">1999–2001: <i>Enrique</i> and <i>Escape</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1999–2001: Enrique and Escape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By 1999, Iglesias had begun a successful crossover career into the English language music market. Thanks to other successful crossover acts, most notably that of <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Martin" title="Ricky Martin">Ricky Martin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Estefan" title="Gloria Estefan">Gloria Estefan</a>, Latino artists and music had a great surge in popularity in mainstream music. After attending one of his concerts in March 1999, <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> asked Iglesias to contribute to the soundtrack of his movie <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Wild_West" title="Wild Wild West">Wild Wild West</a></i>. His contribution "Bailamos" was released as a single and became a number one hit in the US.</p>
<p>After the success of "<a href="/wiki/Bailamos" title="Bailamos">Bailamos</a>", several mainstream record labels were eager to sign Iglesias. Signing a multi-album deal after weeks of negotiations with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interscope" title="Interscope">Interscope</a>, Iglesias recorded and released his first full CD in English, <i>Enrique</i>. The pop album, with some Latin influences, took two months to complete. It contained the song "<a href="/wiki/Rhythm_Divine" title="Rhythm Divine">Rhythm Divine</a>", a duet with <a href="/wiki/Whitney_Houston" title="Whitney Houston">Whitney Houston</a> entitled "<a href="/wiki/Could_I_Have_This_Kiss_Forever" title="Could I Have This Kiss Forever">Could I Have This Kiss Forever</a>", and a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Sad_Eyes_(Bruce_Springsteen_song)" title="Sad Eyes (Bruce Springsteen song)">Sad Eyes</a>".</p>
<p>In 2000, Iglesias performed at the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXIV" title="Super Bowl XXXIV">Super Bowl XXXIV</a> halftime show alongside <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collins" title="Phil Collins">Phil Collins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toni_Braxton" title="Toni Braxton">Toni Braxton</a>. Shock jock <a href="/wiki/Howard_Stern" title="Howard Stern">Howard Stern</a> repeatedly played a tape of a supposedly very off-key Iglesias on his radio show and accused the Iglesias of not being able to sing live. On 8 June 2000, Iglesias sang the song live on Stern's show with just a guitar accompanying him. After the performance, Stern remarked, "I respect you for coming in here; you really can sing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Iglesias noted that the off-key recording was also him, but that it was probably a recording made during a television taping where he was required to <a href="/wiki/Lip_sync" title="Lip sync">lip sync</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He would remark that the controversy was the best promotion he could have. The album's single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Be_With_You_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Be With You (Enrique Iglesias song)">Be With You</a>" became Iglesias's second number one single on <i>Billboard'</i>s Hot 100.</p>
<p>In 2001 Iglesias released his second English language album <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Escape (Enrique Iglesias album)">Escape</a></i>. Where most of the Latin crossover acts of the previous year experienced some difficulty matching the record sales of their first English language albums, Iglesias actually went on to sell even more. The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/Hero_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Hero (Enrique Iglesias song)">Hero</a>", became a number one hit in the United Kingdom, and in many other countries. The entire album was co-written by Iglesias.</p>
<p><i>Escape</i> is his biggest commercial success to date. The singles "<a href="/wiki/Escape_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Escape (Enrique Iglesias song)">Escape</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Turn_Off_The_Lights" title="Don't Turn Off The Lights">Don't Turn Off The Lights</a>" became radio staples, faring well and placing highly or topping various charts both in North America and elsewhere. A second edition of the album was released internationally and contained a new version of one of Iglesias's favorite tracks, "<a href="/wiki/Maybe_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Maybe (Enrique Iglesias song)">Maybe</a>", as well as a duet with <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Richie" title="Lionel Richie">Lionel Richie</a> called "<a href="/wiki/To_Love_a_Woman" title="To Love a Woman">To Love a Woman</a>".</p>
<p>Iglesias capitalized on the album's success with his "One-Night Stand World Tour" consisting of fifty sold-out shows in sixteen countries. Including <a href="/wiki/Radio_City_Music_Hall" title="Radio City Music Hall">Radio City Music Hall</a> and three consecutive nights in London's <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a>, the tour ended with a big show at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lia_Manoliu_Stadium" title="Lia Manoliu Stadium">Stadium National Lia Manoliu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>, Romania. The concert launched MTV Romania, with the video for "<a href="/wiki/Love_to_See_You_Cry" title="Love to See You Cry">Love to See You Cry</a>" being the first to be shown on the channel.</p>
<p>The second leg of the tour, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Turn_Off_The_Lights" title="Don't Turn Off The Lights">Don't Turn Off The Lights</a>", was completed in the summer of 2002, with two sold-out nights in <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> and another two in Mexico's <a href="/wiki/National_Auditorium" title="National Auditorium">National Auditorium</a>. The tour finished with a single show in the <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Clemente_Coliseum" title="Roberto Clemente Coliseum">Roberto Clemente Coliseum</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.9304:_Quiz.C3.A1s_and_7">2002–04: <i>Quizás</i> and <i>7</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2002–04: Quizás and 7">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 2002 Iglesias chose to release a fourth Spanish-language album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Quiz%C3%A1s_(album)" title="Quizás (album)">Quizás</a></i> (<i>Perhaps</i>). A more polished musical production than his previous Spanish albums and containing more introspective songs, the album's title track is a song about the strained relationship Iglesias has with his famous father.</p>
<p>The album debuted at number twelve on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> albums chart, the highest placement of a Spanish language album on the chart at the time. <i>Quizás</i> sold a million copies in a week, making it the fastest-selling album in Spanish in five years. The three singles released from the album all ended up topping the Latin chart, giving Iglesias a total of sixteen number ones on the chart. He currently holds the record for the most number one singles on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a>'s Latin Chart. His last single from the album, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Para_Qu%C3%A9_La_Vida" title="Para Qué La Vida">Para Qué La Vida</a>", reached a million spins on U.S. radio, the only Spanish language song to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The video to the song "<a href="/wiki/Quiz%C3%A1s_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Quizás (Enrique Iglesias song)">Quizás</a>" was the first Spanish language music video to be added to the selection on one of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s popular shows, <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i>. Iglesias performed the song on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i>, becoming the first to sing a Spanish song on the show, and opening doors for other artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Martin" title="Ricky Martin">Ricky Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juanes" title="Juanes">Juanes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Drexler" title="Jorge Drexler">Jorge Drexler</a> to perform their Spanish material. Iglesias included songs from <i>Quizás</i> in his "Don't Turn Off the Lights Tour", and the album went on to win a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy_Awards" title="Latin Grammy Awards">Latin Grammy Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy_Award_for_Best_Pop_Vocal_Album,_Male" title="Latin Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album, Male">Best Pop Vocal Album</a>.</p>
<p>By 2003 Iglesias released his seventh album, which he called <i><a href="/wiki/7_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="7 (Enrique Iglesias album)">7</a></i>, the second to be co-written by Iglesias. Among its more 1980s-inspired material, it features the song "Roamer", which he wrote with his friend and longtime guitarist, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Bruno" title="Tony Bruno">Tony Bruno</a>. The CD also contained the song "Be Yourself", a song about independence (the chorus talks about how Iglesias' own parents did not believe he'd ever succeed in his singing career). The first single was the song "<a href="/wiki/Addicted_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Addicted (Enrique Iglesias song)">Addicted</a>", and was followed closely by a remix of the song "<a href="/wiki/Not_in_Love_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Not in Love (Enrique Iglesias song)">Not In Love</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a>.</p>
<p>With this album, Iglesias went on his biggest world tour to date. The highly publicised tour started with twelve shows in the United States ending with Iglesias playing at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston_Rodeo" title="Houston Rodeo">Houston Rodeo</a> and continued on to several countries, most of which he'd never previously visited playing to sold-out arenas and stadiums in Australia, India, Egypt and Singapore before ending his tour in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9309:_Insomniac.2C_95.2F08_.C3.89xitos_and_Greatest_Hits">2007–09: <i>Insomniac</i>, <i>95/08 Éxitos</i> and <i>Greatest Hits</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2007–09: Insomniac, 95/08 Éxitos and Greatest Hits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a two-year hiatus Iglesias released his new album <i><a href="/wiki/Insomniac_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Insomniac (Enrique Iglesias album)">Insomniac</a></i> on 12 June 2007. The album was so named due to it being recorded mainly at night. The album contained a more contemporary pop style than that of previous albums. The album's highlights include "<a href="/wiki/Push_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Push (Enrique Iglesias song)">Push</a>", with rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, as well as "Ring My Bells", and a cover of <a href="/wiki/Ringside" title="Ringside">Ringside</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Tired_of_Being_Sorry" title="Tired of Being Sorry">Tired Of Being Sorry</a>".</p>
<p>The first single, "<a href="/wiki/Do_You_Know%3F_(The_Ping_Pong_Song)" title="Do You Know? (The Ping Pong Song)">Do You Know? (The Ping Pong Song)</a>", was released on 10 April 2007. It was Iglesias's highest charting song on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> since "<a href="/wiki/Escape_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Escape (Enrique Iglesias song)">Escape</a>". The song was also a hit throughout Europe, peaking in the top 10 in many countries. The Spanish version of the song, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Do_You_Know%3F_(The_Ping_Pong_Song)" title="Do You Know? (The Ping Pong Song)">Dímelo</a>", was number one on <a href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Songs" title="Hot Latin Songs"><i>Billboard'</i>s Hot Latin Songs</a> chart for eleven weeks, becoming his second best performing song on that chart at the time.</p>
<p>Iglesias followed up with the ballad "<a href="/wiki/Somebody%27s_Me" title="Somebody's Me">Somebody's Me</a>", which was released as a single in the American continent. The song was played extensively on AC radio and peaked high on Billboard's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_AC" title="Hot AC">Hot AC</a>. In Europe, the second single was "Tired Of Being Sorry", which performed well in many countries; he recorded a version of the song with French singer <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A2diya" title="Nâdiya">Nâdiya</a> which was number one in France for eleven weeks.</p>
<p>On 4 July 2007 Enrique Iglesias became the first Western artist to play a concert in Syria in three decades when he performed for a sold-out crowd of ten thousand in the capital <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and in the same week he performed on <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth" title="Live Earth">Live Earth</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>.</p>
<p>The Insomniac World Tour was launched at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coca-Cola_Dome" title="Coca-Cola Dome">Coca-Cola Dome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, South Africa, the same venue he ended his last world Tour and took him to sold out arenas throughout Europe. It was his first arena tour of the UK with him playing venues such as Manchester's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEN_Arena" title="MEN Arena">MEN Arena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Arena" title="Wembley Arena">Wembley Arena</a>. The tour ended with Iglesias performing at the newly opened <a href="/wiki/L.A._Live" title="L.A. Live">L.A. Live</a>. A second leg of the tour took him throughout Latin America, from Mexico to Argentina.</p>
<p>A solo version of "Push" was added to the soundtrack of the movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Step_Up_2_the_Streets" title="Step Up 2 the Streets">Step Up 2 the Streets</a></i>. The song was regarded as the third single from the album. A music video was shot which features the film's lead actors. Despite never being officially added to radio the song has charted in several countries and is one of his most highly rated songs amongst fans.</p>
<p>Iglesias's song "<a href="/wiki/Can_You_Hear_Me_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Can You Hear Me (Enrique Iglesias song)">Can You Hear Me</a>" has been chosen as the official song of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> football tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He performed the song live at the June 29, 2008 final in Vienna, Austria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The song featured on a re-issue of the Insomniac album being released in certain countries.</p>

<p>Iglesias released a Spanish greatest hits album named <i><a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias:_95/08_%C3%89xitos" title="Enrique Iglesias: 95/08 Éxitos">95/08 Éxitos</a></i> on 25 March 2008 which included his seventeen number one songs on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot Latin Songs chart plus two new songs. The first single was the song "<a href="/wiki/%C2%BFD%C3%B3nde_Est%C3%A1n_Coraz%C3%B3n%3F" title="¿Dónde Están Corazón?">¿Dónde Están Corazón?</a>", which was written by Argentine star <a href="/wiki/Coti" title="Coti">Coti</a> and became Iglesias's eighteenth number one single on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Hot Latin Songs. The album debuted at number one on <i>Billboard'</i>s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Latin_Albums" title="Top Latin Albums">Top Latin Albums</a> chart and number eighteen on the overall <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> albums chart. It was Iglesias's second Spanish album to debut in the top 20 of the <i>Billboard</i> 200 (<i><a href="/wiki/Quiz%C3%A1s_(album)" title="Quizás (album)">Quizás</a></i> debuted at number twelve in 2002). The album was certified double Platinum (Latin field) in the U.S. and in some Latin American countries.</p>
<p>The album's second single "<a href="/wiki/Lloro_Por_Ti" title="Lloro Por Ti">Lloro Por Ti</a>" also reached number one on the Hot Latin Songs chart and had an official remix featuring <a href="/wiki/Wisin_%26_Yandel" title="Wisin &amp; Yandel">Wisin &amp; Yandel</a>. Iglesias did a tour of the US to promote the compilation. Beginning in <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">Laredo, Texas</a>, Texas and ending at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Izod_Center" title="Izod Center">Izod Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, he was accompanied through most of the tour with <a href="/wiki/Bachata_(music)" title="Bachata (music)">bachata</a> band <a href="/wiki/Aventura_(band)" title="Aventura (band)">Aventura</a> who also performed "Lloro Por Ti" with him at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Premios_Juventud" title="2008 Premios Juventud">2008 Premios Juventud</a>.</p>
<p>Iglesias was a surprise performer at the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lo_Nuestro" title="Lo Nuestro">Lo Nuestro</a> Awards, opening the show with a medley of "¿Dónde Están Corazón?" and "Dímelo". He also performed at the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Latin_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Latin Music Awards"><i>Billboard</i> Latin Music Awards</a>, where he received a special award.</p>
<p>After the success of his Spanish greatest hits compilation, Iglesias released a <a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Enrique Iglesias album)">compilation of his English language hits</a> on 11 November. The album includes "<a href="/wiki/Can_You_Hear_Me_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Can You Hear Me (Enrique Iglesias song)">Can You Hear Me</a>" as well as two new songs. The first single is entitled "<a href="/wiki/Away_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Away (Enrique Iglesias song)">Away</a>", which featured <a href="/wiki/Sean_Garrett" title="Sean Garrett">Sean Garrett</a> and was followed by "<a href="/wiki/Takin%27_Back_My_Love" title="Takin' Back My Love">Takin' Back My Love</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ciara" title="Ciara">Ciara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The album debuted at number three on the official <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> and sold over 80,000 copies in its first two weeks of release alone.</p>
<p>Iglesias was the winner of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards_2008" title="World Music Awards 2008">World Music Awards</a> in the categories of "World's Best Selling Latin Performer" and "World's Best Selling Spanish Artist", in the ceremony held in Monaco on 9 November 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311:_Euphoria">2010–11: <i>Euphoria</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010–11: Euphoria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 July 2010, Iglesias released his ninth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Euphoria_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Euphoria (Enrique Iglesias album)">Euphoria</a></i>, his first work to be released under his new label, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic</a>. The album is Iglesias's first bilingual album with seven original English songs and six original Spanish songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The album won the <a href="/wiki/2011_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2011 Billboard Music Awards"><i>Billboard</i> Music Award for Top Latin Album</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2011_Latin_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2011 Latin Billboard Music Awards"><i>Billboard</i> Latin Awards for Latin Album of the Year</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_Latin_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2011 Latin Billboard Music Awards">Latin Pop Album of the Year</a> and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Latin Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Latin Grammy Award for Album of the Year</a>. Iglesias worked with three producers whom he had collaborated with before; <a href="/wiki/RedOne" title="RedOne">RedOne</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Taylor_(record_producer)" title="Mark Taylor (record producer)">Mark Taylor</a> and Carlos Paucer. The album features collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunidhi_Chauhan" title="Sunidhi Chauhan">Sunidhi Chauhan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Frank_E" title="DJ Frank E">DJ Frank E</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Luis_Guerra" title="Juan Luis Guerra">Juan Luis Guerra</a> and his third duet with <a href="/wiki/Wisin_%26_Yandel" title="Wisin &amp; Yandel">Wisin &amp; Yandel</a>. In a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Latin_Entertainment" title="Universal Music Latin Entertainment">Universal Latino</a>, Iglesias released different singles in both English and Spanish simultaneously to different formats.</p>
<p>The first English single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/I_Like_It_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="I Like It (Enrique Iglesias song)">I Like It</a>", which features the rapper Pitbull, was released on 3 May 2010 in the U.S. and became a success, reaching No. 4 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. The song was also featured in the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> reality series, <a href="/wiki/Jersey_Shore_(TV_series)" title="Jersey Shore (TV series)"><i>Jersey Shore</i></a>. "<a href="/wiki/Cuando_Me_Enamoro" title="Cuando Me Enamoro">Cuando Me Enamoro</a>" was released as the lead Spanish single from the album, and became the theme song of the Mexican <a href="/wiki/Telenovela" title="Telenovela">telenovela</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuando_me_enamoro_(Telenovela)" title="Cuando me enamoro (Telenovela)">same title</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Televisa" title="Televisa">Televisa</a>. The song debuted at number eight and number twenty-five on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">U.S. Latin Pop Songs</a> chart and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Tracks" title="Hot Latin Tracks">U.S. Hot Latin Songs</a> chart, respectively. The song became his twenty-fifth top ten single on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot Latin Songs chart and after four weeks of its release date it became his twenty-first No.1 song on this chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billhis_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-billhis-35">[35]</a></sup> In January 2011, the album's third English single "<a href="/wiki/Tonight_(I%27m_Lovin%27_You)" title="Tonight (I'm Lovin' You)">Tonight (I'm F**kin' You)</a>" broke into the top ten on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, also reaching No. 4 and became another big success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The song was released only for digital download in the United States but was featured on some editions of <i>Euphoria</i> in Europe and some Asian areas. The song became Iglesias' first number one on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40" title="Mainstream Top 40">Pop Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hot_100_Airplay_(Radio_Songs)" title="Hot 100 Airplay (Radio Songs)">Radio Songs</a> airplay charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> A remix version of the album track "<a href="/wiki/Dirty_Dancer" title="Dirty Dancer">Dirty Dancer</a>" was released as the fourth English single and became his ninth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Play</a> chart topper, tying with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_(singer)" title="Prince (singer)">Prince</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> as the male with the most #1 dance singles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Further, "<a href="/wiki/Ayer_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Ayer (Enrique Iglesias song)">Ayer</a>" served as the album's third Spanish single and seventh single overall. The <a href="/wiki/Euphoria_Tour_(Enrique_Iglesias)" title="Euphoria Tour (Enrique Iglesias)">Euphoria Tour</a> took Iglesias across the U.S., Canada, the U.K. and several European countries. One of the tour's legs took him to Australia while fellow artist Pitbull joined him as an opening act. <a href="/wiki/Prince_Royce" title="Prince Royce">Prince Royce</a> also served as opening act during the tour's second leg across North America.</p>
<p>In August 2011, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/I_Like_How_It_Feels" title="I Like How It Feels">I Like How It Feels</a>" to radio. This was planned to serve as the lead single from the <i>Euphoria</i> album's proposed re-issue that never came to fruition, <i>Euphoria Reloaded</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9314:_Sex_and_Love">2012–14: <i>Sex and Love</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2012–14: Sex and Love">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 August 2012, Iglesias unveiled his brand new single, "<a href="/wiki/Finally_Found_You" title="Finally Found You">Finally Found You</a>", a collaboration with American rapper <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Adams" title="Sammy Adams">Sammy Adams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> It was released to the US iTunes Store on 25 September 2012. The song was released in UK on 9 December 2012. On 8 December 2012, Iglesias performed at Z100 Jingle Ball in Miami and the <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio" title="IHeartRadio">iHeartRadio Festival</a> interview session before the shows Iglesias stated he's working on some new music and — when asked about his time in the studio — Iglesias said, "It's kind of like going fishing, you never know when you’re going to catch a big one." Continuing on to tell what fans can expect to hear, he said he's ready to try something new saying, "I come out with so many albums and I want to make sure that if I come out with an album it sounds new. At least to me." It has been confirmed that Iglesias will be working with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Taylor_(producer)" title="Mark Taylor (producer)">Mark Taylor</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>, and Carlos Paucar for his new album.</p>
<p>On 31 May 2013, Iglesias performed at the Mawazine Festival in Rabat, Morocco. The show broke the highest attendance record as more than one hundred twenty thousand fans gathered to watch the concert which is considered as the highest as of yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 June 2013, <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a> confirmed that Enrique's first single from his upcoming album is titled "<a href="/wiki/Turn_the_Night_Up" title="Turn the Night Up">Turn the Night Up</a>". The single was released in the middle of July. Iglesias released a special video which featured photos and videos that fans had sent in; fans could also make their own version using media from their personal Facebook albums. The song was released to US Top 40 radio on 22 July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On 18 July 2013 Iglesias uploaded the single's cover art on his official website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 August 2013, "<a href="/wiki/Loco_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Loco (Enrique Iglesias song)">Loco</a>" was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> as the lead Spanish single from his upcoming album. The smooth bachata track features vocals from urban bachata superstar <a href="/wiki/Romeo_Santos" title="Romeo Santos">Romeo Santos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The single released worldwide on 26 August 2013. It became Iglesias' 24th No. 1 on the Billboard's Hot Latin Songs chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 September 2013, Z100 Radio played Enrique's third single from his upcoming bilingual studio album. The single titled "<a href="/wiki/Heart_Attack_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Heart Attack (Enrique Iglesias song)">Heart Attack</a>" was released in October 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> It officially impacted Top 40/Mainstream radio in the U.S. on 1 October 2013.</p>
<p>Iglesias confirmed "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Freak" title="I'm a Freak">I'm a Freak</a>" as the third English single from his tenth studio album. The single features <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a> and was released in January 2014. Iglesias recorded a song with <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a> called "Beautiful", which appears on her twelfth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_Me_Once" title="Kiss Me Once">Kiss Me Once</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On 29 January 2014, Iglesias announced the title of his tenth studio album would be <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_Love" title="Sex and Love">Sex and Love</a></i>.The album was released on 14 March 2014. The album features guest vocals from <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romeo_Santos" title="Romeo Santos">Romeo Santos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marco_Antonio_Sol%C3%ADs" title="Marco Antonio Solís">Marco Antonio Solís</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yandel" title="Yandel">Yandel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gente_de_Zona" title="Gente de Zona">Gente de Zona</a> and <a href="/wiki/India_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="India Martínez">India Martínez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<p>His latest hit is "<a href="/wiki/Bailando_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Bailando (Enrique Iglesias song)">Bailando</a>" ft. Sean Paul, Descemer Bueno, and Gente De Zona. "Bailando" got him 3 Latin Grammys ten years after his album, <i><a href="/wiki/Euphoria_(Enrique_Iglesias_album)" title="Euphoria (Enrique Iglesias album)">Euphoria</a></i> received a nomination. "Bailando" helped him match <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>'s previous record on Billboard's <a href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Songs" title="Hot Latin Songs">Hot Latin Songs</a> chart. "Bailando" spent 41 weeks at #1 on that chart. "Bailando" was also <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>'s second most watched music video of 2014, behind <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>'s hit single, "<a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_(Katy_Perry_song)" title="Dark Horse (Katy Perry song)">Dark Horse</a>". "Bailando" has currently over 1.7 billion views and holds the record for the most watched Latin music video and the second-most-watched non-English music video ever, as of 20 December 2016. "Bailando" was included on the <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Dance_2015" title="Just Dance 2015">Just Dance 2015</a></i> soundtrack.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_Love" title="Sex and Love">Sex and Love</a></i> was Spotify's 7th most-streamed album worldwide in 2014, and "Bailando" was the most-streamed song in both Mexico and Spain. Iglesias was also called the King of 2014, due to his tenth album, Sex And Love, and his hit single, Bailando. Billboard called him "The Crowd Pleaser" of 2014. After more than a decade with Universal Music, Iglesias left the record label in 2015 and signed on with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>

<p>Iglesias has collaborated with songwriter <a href="/wiki/Guy_Chambers" title="Guy Chambers">Guy Chambers</a> to write "Un Nuovo Giorno", the lead single from <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Bocelli" title="Andrea Bocelli">Andrea Bocelli</a>'s first pop album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The song was later translated into English as "<a href="/wiki/First_Day_of_My_Life_(Melanie_C_song)" title="First Day of My Life (Melanie C song)">First Day of My Life</a>" and recorded by Spice Girl <a href="/wiki/Melanie_C" title="Melanie C">Melanie C</a>. The song has since gone to become a huge hit throughout Europe, and peaked in the number one spot in numerous countries. Iglesias also co-wrote the single "<a href="/wiki/The_Way/Solitaire" title="The Way/Solitaire">The Way</a>" for <a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a> runner up <a href="/wiki/Clay_Aiken" title="Clay Aiken">Clay Aiken</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Four songs co-written by Iglesias appear on the UK band <a href="/wiki/The_Hollies" title="The Hollies">The Hollies</a>' current album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In 2010 Idol Allstars 2010 (Swedish Idol Series) released a song "All I Need Is You" co-written by Iglesias with <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Carlsson" title="Andreas Carlsson">Andreas Carlsson</a>, Kalle Engström and <a href="/wiki/Kristian_Lundin" title="Kristian Lundin">Kristian Lundin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> He also co-wrote <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>'s song <a href="/wiki/Dance_Again" title="Dance Again">Dance Again</a> released in 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> which reached number-one position in the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Songs</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000, Iglesias co-produced an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Off_Broadway" title="Off Broadway">off Broadway</a> musical called <i>Four Guys Named Jose and Una Mujer Named Maria</i>. In the musical, four Americans of Latin heritage possess a common interest in music and meet and decide to put on a show. The show contained many references and allusions to many classic and contemporary Latin and pop songs by the likes of <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Miranda" title="Carmen Miranda">Carmen Miranda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selena" title="Selena">Selena</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richie_Valens" title="Richie Valens">Richie Valens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chayanne" title="Chayanne">Chayanne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Martin" title="Ricky Martin">Ricky Martin</a> and Iglesias himself.</p>
<p>Iglesias starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Banderas" title="Antonio Banderas">Antonio Banderas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salma_Hayek" title="Salma Hayek">Salma Hayek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rodriguez" title="Robert Rodriguez">Robert Rodriguez</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_Mexico" title="Once Upon a Time in Mexico">Once Upon a Time in Mexico</a></i>, in which he played the well-spoken gun-wielding Lorenzo. In 2007, he had a guest appearance in the TV comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Two_and_a_Half_Men" title="Two and a Half Men">Two and a Half Men</a></i> as a carpenter/handyman.</p>
<p>He also guest starred as Gael, an Argentinean guitar playing/surfer/massage therapist love interest of Robin in season 3 of the CBS hit TV show <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i>.</p>
<p>Iglesias also played the part of an evil Roman emperor in a Pepsi advertising in 2004. He's also starred in commercials for Tommy Hilfiger, Doritos and for Viceroy watches.</p>

<p>Iglesias received surgery in 2003 to remove a circular mole from the right side of his face, citing possible concerns that over time it could become cancerous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2001, he started dating tennis player <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kournikova" title="Anna Kournikova">Anna Kournikova</a>. They reportedly split in October 2013 but have since reconciled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Enrique Iglesias built a new mansion for himself on the beaches of Miami, estimated to be worth approximately $26 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Humanitarian_cause">Humanitarian cause</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Humanitarian cause">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, Iglesias was included in the project <a href="/wiki/Download_to_Donate" title="Download to Donate">Download to Donate</a> started by an organization <i><a href="/wiki/Music_for_Relief" title="Music for Relief">Music for Relief</a></i> started by American rock band <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a>. He was co-producer of the album <i><a href="/wiki/Download_to_Donate_for_Haiti" title="Download to Donate for Haiti">Download to Donate for Haiti</a></i>, a charity album for the <i><a href="/wiki/2010_Haiti_earthquake" title="2010 Haiti earthquake">2010 Haiti earthquake</a></i>, with the co-vocalist of the band <a href="/wiki/Mike_Shinoda" title="Mike Shinoda">Mike Shinoda</a>. Both of them promoted the album at various venues, one of them is <i><a href="/wiki/Larry_King_Live" title="Larry King Live">Larry King Live</a></i>, where he and Shinoda explained the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Iglesias has urged his followers to donate money through the <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> to help the victims of the deadly <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Typhoon Haiyan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mother-homeland_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-mother-homeland-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.straitstimes.com.2Fbreaking-news.2Flifestyle.2Fstory.2Fenrique-iglesias-appeals-aid-philippines-20131113_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.straitstimes.com.2Fbreaking-news.2Flifestyle.2Fstory.2Fenrique-iglesias-appeals-aid-philippines-20131113-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.hollywood.com.2Fnews.2Fbrief.2F55042262.2Fenrique-iglesias-appeals-to-fans-to-continue-donating-to-philippines-typhoon-victims_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.hollywood.com.2Fnews.2Fbrief.2F55042262.2Fenrique-iglesias-appeals-to-fans-to-continue-donating-to-philippines-typhoon-victims-61">[61]</a></sup> The typhoon struck one month after the Philippines was hit by a 7.2-magnitude earthquake which destroyed the homes and livelihoods of around 350,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.gmanetwork.com.2Fnews.2Fstory.2F335178.2Fshowbiz.2Fshowbizabroad.2Fsinger-enrique-iglesias-appeals-for-aid-for-yolanda-victims_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.gmanetwork.com.2Fnews.2Fstory.2F335178.2Fshowbiz.2Fshowbizabroad.2Fsinger-enrique-iglesias-appeals-for-aid-for-yolanda-victims-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.abs-cbnnews.com.2Fnode.2F340053_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.abs-cbnnews.com.2Fnode.2F340053-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iglesias has supported City of Hope, Habitat For Humanity, Help for Heroes, Live Earth, Muscular Dystrophy Association, Music for Relief, Special Olympics, The Salvation Army, and charitable causes like <a href="/wiki/Alex%27s_Lemonade_Stand_Foundation" title="Alex's Lemonade Stand Foundation">Alex's Lemonade Stand Foundation</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Farchive.indianexpress.com.2Fnews.2Fenrique-iglesias-appeals-fans-to-donate-for-typhoon-haiyan-relief.2F1196819_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Farchive.indianexpress.com.2Fnews.2Fenrique-iglesias-appeals-fans-to-donate-for-typhoon-haiyan-relief.2F1196819-64">[64]</a></sup> and hunger relief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Enrique_Iglesias" title="List of awards and nominations received by Enrique Iglesias">List of awards and nominations received by Enrique Iglesias</a></div>
<p>He has won more than 200 awards from various ceremony including 23 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Music Awards">Billboard Music Awards</a> (with 9 times nomination) and 36 <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Latin_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Latin Music Awards">Billboard Latin Music Awards</a> (with 45 times nomination), as well as 8 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards" title="American Music Awards">American Music Awards</a> (with 6 times nomination), 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy</a> (with 3 times nomination), 5 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy_Awards" title="Latin Grammy Awards">Latin Grammy</a> (with 4 times nomination), 10 <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Awards</a> (with 2 times nomination), 6 MTV awards, 19 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lo_Nuestro" title="Lo Nuestro">Premios Lo Nuestro</a> Awards (with 24 times nomination) and 15 <a href="/wiki/Premios_Juventud" title="Premios Juventud">Premios Juventud</a> Awards (with 21 times nomination) etc. He has been nominated over 465 times from various Awards. He also won an award for Best International Pop Act at the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Immies" title="MTV Immies">MTV India Awards</a>. As well as being named "King of Latin Pop". In 2000, he was also awarded Most Fashionable Artist at the VH1/<i>Vogue</i> Fashion Awards. In 2001, for the release of his second English studio album <i>Escape</i>, he received awards for Best-Selling Pop Male Artist and European Male Artist at the <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Enrique_Iglesias_Profile_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-Enrique_Iglesias_Profile-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
