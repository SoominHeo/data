<p><b>Henrique Capriles Radonski</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[enˈrike kaˈpɾiles raˈðonski]</a></span>), more commonly known as <b>Henrique Capriles</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venezuelan" title="Venezuelan">Venezuelan</a> politician and lawyer, currently serving as 36th Governor of <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(state)" title="Miranda (state)">Miranda</a>. Born in Caracas on 11 July 1972, he received a degree on law from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_Cat%C3%B3lica_Andr%C3%A9s_Bello" title="Universidad Católica Andrés Bello">Universidad Católica Andrés Bello</a>, and later tax law from the <a href="/wiki/Central_University_of_Venezuela" title="Central University of Venezuela">Central University of Venezuela</a>. Capriles first ventured into politics at age 26, when he became the youngest member ever elected of the Venezuelan parliament. He secured a seat into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Venezuela" title="Chamber of Deputies of Venezuela">Chamber of Deputies</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_parliamentary_election,_1998" title="Venezuelan parliamentary election, 1998">1998 parliamentary elections</a>, under the Christian democratic party <a href="/wiki/Copei" title="Copei">Copei</a>. He served as Vice President of the Congress and President of the Chamber of Deputies until their dissolution by the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Constitutional_Assembly_election,_1999" title="Venezuelan Constitutional Assembly election, 1999">Constitutional Assembly in August 1999</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000, he co-founded the political party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primero_Justicia" title="Primero Justicia">Primero Justicia</a>, alongside politicians <a href="/wiki/Julio_Borges" title="Julio Borges">Julio Borges</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leopoldo_Lopez" title="Leopoldo Lopez">Leopoldo Lopez</a>, and ran successfully for the mayorship of the <a href="/wiki/Baruta" title="Baruta">Baruta municipality</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_regional_elections,_2000" title="Venezuelan regional elections, 2000">regional elections held in July 2000</a>, and later for the governorship of the Miranda state <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_regional_elections,_2008" title="Venezuelan regional elections, 2008">in 2008</a>. Capriles became the opposition candidate at the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_presidential_election,_2012" title="Venezuelan presidential election, 2012">2012</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_presidential_election,_2013" title="Venezuelan presidential election, 2013">2013 presidential elections</a>, and faced then-President <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a> and Vice President <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Maduro" title="Nicolás Maduro">Nicolás Maduro</a> respectively. His defeat in 2012 marked the first loss of his political career. Maduro narrowly defeated Capriles in the 2013 elections, a result that sparked controversy and debate admist the opposition's claims of electoral fraud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-irregularities_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-irregularities-2">[2]</a></sup> Between both presidential campaigns, Capriles successfully secured his re-election as Governor of Miranda during the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_regional_elections,_2012" title="Venezuelan regional elections, 2012">2012 regional elections</a>.</p>
<p>Capriles Radonski is of <a href="/wiki/Sephardi_Jews" title="Sephardi Jews">Sephardi Jewish</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jewish" title="Ashkenazi Jewish">Ashkenazi Jewish</a> descent; his grandparents immigrated from Russia and Poland during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. However, he considers himself a devout Catholic, revealing that his greatest hero in history was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" title="Jesus Christ">Jesus Christ</a>. Capriles dated Venezuelan actress Erika de la Vega between his first and second tenure as Mayor. Prior to his political career, he worked in the public and private sectors at several tax and law firms of Venezuela. Capriles is a member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Fiscal_Association" title="International Fiscal Association">International Fiscal Association</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Henrique Capriles was born in Caracas on 11 July 1972. His parents are Mónica Cristina Radonski-Bochenek and Henrique Capriles García.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genref1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-genref1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaprilesWins_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CaprilesWins-4">[4]</a></sup> His maternal grandparents were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jew" title="Ashkenazi Jew">Ashkenazi Jews</a> who immigrated from Russia and Poland following <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. His great-grand parents were murdered by the Germans in the <a href="/wiki/Treblinka_extermination_camp" title="Treblinka extermination camp">Treblinka extermination camp</a> during World War II. His maternal grandmother, Lili Bochenek, lived for 20 months in the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Ghetto" title="Warsaw Ghetto">Warsaw Ghetto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genref1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-genref1-3">[3]</a></sup> Capriles’s paternal grandfather, Armando Capriles-Myerston, was a <a href="/wiki/Sephardi_Jews" title="Sephardi Jews">Sephardi Jew</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genref1_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-genref1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Capriles’s father was a successful businessman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genref1_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-genref1-3">[3]</a></sup> In the 1950s, he helped launch <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft Foods</a>' entry into Venezuela by inviting the vice-president of its <a href="/wiki/Nabisco" title="Nabisco">Nabisco</a> subsidiary and persuading him to invest in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nabisco_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nabisco-5">[5]</a></sup> His maternal grandparents, Andrés Radonski and Lili Bochenek, migrated to Venezuela in 1947. Andrés was an engineer active in the cinema business in Poland; he opened his first cinema several years later, in the eastern city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_La_Cruz" title="Puerto La Cruz">Puerto La Cruz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rival1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rival1-6">[6]</a></sup> The company operated under the name Circuito Radonski. It was merged in 1998 alongside Venefilms and Grupo Blanco to create the country's largest cinema chain, <a href="/wiki/Cinex" title="Cinex">Cinex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cinex_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-cinex-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Capriles was raised a Catholic. His parents agreed to educate their children in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_faith" title="Catholic faith">Catholic faith</a> "until they were old enough to decide for themselves" as a "compromise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Giusti_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Giusti-8">[8]</a></sup> Capriles has continued as a "fervent Catholic" through his adulthood, commenting that his greatest hero in history was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" title="Jesus Christ">Jesus Christ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25preg_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-25preg-9">[9]</a></sup> Capriles enrolled at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_Cat%C3%B3lica_Andr%C3%A9s_Bello" title="Universidad Católica Andrés Bello">Andrés Bello Catholic University</a> to study commerce law. He received his degree in 1994, and then continued studies at the <a href="/wiki/Central_University_of_Venezuela" title="Central University of Venezuela">Central University of Venezuela</a>. He received a degree in tax law several years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baruta_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baruta-10">[10]</a></sup> He took additional studies at the IBFD International Tax Academy in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, the Pan American Center of Tax Managers in <a href="/wiki/Viterbo" title="Viterbo">Viterbo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baruta_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Baruta-10">[10]</a></sup> He is a sitting member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Fiscal_Association" title="International Fiscal Association">International Fiscal Association</a> and the World Association of Young Jurors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baruta_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Baruta-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_and_Chamber_of_Deputies_.281995-1999.29">Early years and Chamber of Deputies (1995-1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years and Chamber of Deputies (1995-1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Capriles first ventured into politics between 1995 and 1998, when he aided his cousin, deputee Armando Capriles, in the writing of laws for the Bicameral Commission of Energy of the then-existing Congress of the Republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup> Armando, who was a partisan of the Christian democratic party <a href="/wiki/Copei" title="Copei">Copei</a>, later offered Henrique the possibility of being a candidate for a seat at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Venezuela" title="Chamber of Deputies of Venezuela">Chamber of Deputies</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_parliamentary_election,_1998" title="Venezuelan parliamentary election, 1998">1998 parliamentary elections</a>, affiliating with <a href="/wiki/Copei" title="Copei">Copei</a> (Social Christian Party). Capriles accepted, and successfully secured a seat at the Congress in representation of the <a href="/wiki/Zulia" title="Zulia">Zulia</a> state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup> He became the youngest member ever elected to the Venezuelan parliament,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNNwire_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNNwire-12">[12]</a></sup> and later held the positions of Vice President of the Congress and President of the Chamber of Deputies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup> In August 1999, the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Constitutional_Assembly_election,_1999" title="Venezuelan Constitutional Assembly election, 1999">Constitutional Assembly</a> abolished Capriles' seat, and dissolved the Congress.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayor_of_Baruta_.282000.E2.80.932008.29">Mayor of Baruta (2000–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mayor of Baruta (2000–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Congress was replaced by the National Assembly, and new deputees were elected in a <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_general_election,_2000" title="Venezuelan general election, 2000">general election held, in 2000</a>. That year, Capriles founded the political party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primero_Justicia" title="Primero Justicia">Primero Justicia</a>, alongside politicians <a href="/wiki/Julio_Borges" title="Julio Borges">Julio Borges</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leopoldo_Lopez" title="Leopoldo Lopez">Leopoldo Lopez</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup> although the entity had already existed as a civil association since 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-primeroj_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-primeroj-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Munoz_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Munoz-14">[14]</a></sup> He did not run for a seat at the newly created Assembly; however, he and Leopoldo Lopez pursued the <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayoralties</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Baruta" title="Baruta">Baruta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chacao,_Venezuela" title="Chacao, Venezuela">Chacao</a> municipalities, respectively, at the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_regional_elections,_2000" title="Venezuelan regional elections, 2000">regional elections held in July 2000</a>. Lopez won with 51,5% of the vote, whereas Capriles received 62,9%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup> Borges <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venezuelan_parliamentary_election,_2000" title="Venezuelan parliamentary election, 2000">secured a seat</a> in the National Assembly. As mayor, Capriles focused on the reduction of crime within his municipality, as well as on improving the overall infrastructure of the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, President <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a> was the target of a <a href="/wiki/2002_Venezuelan_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt" title="2002 Venezuelan coup d'état attempt">failed coup d'état</a> that removed him from office on 11 April 2002, after several days of violent protests in Caracas. The coup, triggered by major political discontent in a sector of the Venezuelan population, was initially staged by members of the military and the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Federation_of_Chambers_of_Commerce" title="Venezuelan Federation of Chambers of Commerce">Venezuelan Federation of Chambers of Commerce</a> (<i>Fedecámaras</i>), represented by its president <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Carmona" title="Pedro Carmona">Pedro Carmona</a>, who was declared the interim president. The following day, the Cuban embassy, located in Baruta, received severe damage, caused by opposition protesters who cut off the water and electricity supply, smashed windows, damaged six staff vehicles, and blocked the Cuban ambassador, German Sanchez Otero, from leaving. The same day, Interior Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ramon_Rodriguez_Chacin" title="Ramon Rodriguez Chacin">Ramon Rodriguez Chacin</a> was detained by the municipal police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBA1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-CUBA1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBA2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-CUBA2-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Chávez returned to the presidency on 13 April 2002. Capriles then faced charges as a result of the attacks on the embassy. In March 2004, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danilo_Anderson" title="Danilo Anderson">Danilo Anderson</a> publicly declared that Capriles would be arrested; the warrant was annulled in early April. In May 2004, Capriles was arrested on the orders of Anderson, on the grounds that Capriles might flee the country; he was released on probation in September, pending his trial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBA1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-CUBA1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBA2_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-CUBA2-16">[16]</a></sup> In December 2006, Capriles was acquitted of the charge of fomenting violence in the siege of the Cuban embassy during the coup attempt, but five months later, his acquittal was annulled by the court of appeal, and the case was re-opened in October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBA1_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-CUBA1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBA2_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-CUBA2-16">[16]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_State" title="U.S. Department of State">U.S. Department of State</a> mentioned Capriles' case in its 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States%27_Country_Reports_on_Human_Rights_Practices" title="United States' Country Reports on Human Rights Practices">Human Rights report</a> as a denial of a fair public trial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-US2008HR_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-US2008HR-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governor_of_Miranda_.282008.E2.80.93present.29">Governor of Miranda (2008–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governor of Miranda (2008–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_regional_elections,_2008" title="Venezuelan regional elections, 2008">Venezuelan regional elections, 2008</a>, Capriles was elected Governor of <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(state)" title="Miranda (state)">Miranda state</a>, defeating <a href="/wiki/Diosdado_Cabello" title="Diosdado Cabello">Diosdado Cabello</a>. During his tenure, Capriles invested in education, opening 39 schools by 2011, compared to 7 under the previous governor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talcual_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-talcual-11">[11]</a></sup> Capriles passed on the governorship of the state of Miranda to the Secretary General of Miranda, Adriana D'Elia, on 6 June 2012, in compliance with Venezuelan law which states an incumbent governor cannot run for the presidency of the nation. He was elected again on 16 December 2012, beating former Vice President <a href="/wiki/El%C3%ADas_Jaua" title="Elías Jaua">Elías Jaua</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20-23_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-20-23-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_presidential_election">2012 presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012 presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_presidential_election,_2012" title="Venezuelan presidential election, 2012">Venezuelan presidential election, 2012</a></div>
<p>Capriles was selected in primaries held in February 2012 as the opposition candidate against Hugo Chávez in the presidential elections to be held in October 2012; he won the opposition primaries with 1,900,528 (64.2%) votes of the 3,059,024 votes cast (votes abroad not included).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Capriles named former Brazilian president <a href="/wiki/Luiz_In%C3%A1cio_Lula_da_Silva" title="Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva">Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva</a> as his political inspiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VenPoll_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-VenPoll-20">[20]</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Luiz_In%C3%A1cio_Lula_da_Silva" title="Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva">Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva</a> endorsed Chávez in the 2012 election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2012, Capriles was subjected to what some foreign journalists characterized as attacks by state-run media sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChavezMedia_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChavezMedia-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheMan_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheMan-23">[23]</a></sup> The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i> said that Capriles "was exposed in a campaign in Venezuela's state-run media, which insinuated he was, among other things, a homosexual and a <a href="/wiki/Zionism" title="Zionism">Zionist</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChavezRivalWSJ_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChavezRivalWSJ-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChavezRival_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChavezRival-25">[25]</a></sup> A 13 February 2012 opinion article published on the web site of the state-owned <a href="/wiki/Radio_Nacional_de_Venezuela" title="Radio Nacional de Venezuela">Radio Nacional de Venezuela</a>, titled "The Enemy is Zionism"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADL17022012_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ADL17022012-26">[26]</a></sup> mentioned Capriles's Jewish ancestry and a meeting he had held with local Jewish leaders,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChavezMedia_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-ChavezMedia-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChavezRival_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-ChavezRival-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JewishGay_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-JewishGay-27">[27]</a></sup> saying, "This is our enemy, the Zionism that Capriles today represents ... Zionism, along with capitalism, are responsible for 90% of world poverty and imperialist wars." Chavez himself had repeatedly denied allegations of tolerating or promoting anti-Semitism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChavezRival_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-ChavezRival-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 October 2012, Capriles lost the election to then-President <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_presidential_election">2013 presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henrique_Capriles_Radonski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013 presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_presidential_election,_2013" title="Venezuelan presidential election, 2013">Venezuelan presidential election, 2013</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Capriles faced interim president <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Maduro" title="Nicolás Maduro">Nicolás Maduro</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_presidential_election,_2013" title="Venezuelan presidential election, 2013">presidential election</a> on 14 April, 2013. Voters gave Maduro—who had assumed the role of <a href="/wiki/Acting_president" title="Acting president">acting president</a> since Chávez's death—a narrow victory over Capriles. Capriles rejected the results of the election, claiming election irregularities and calling for a full audit of the election results. Maduro said he would accept an audit of the election results; the election board did not agree to opposition demands for a total recount.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Russiatoday_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Russiatoday-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
