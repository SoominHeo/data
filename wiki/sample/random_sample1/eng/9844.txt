<p><b>Enterbrain</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">エンターブレイン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, formerly <b>Enterbrain, Inc.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社エンターブレイン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushiki Gaisha Entāburein</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Publisher" title="Publisher">publisher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> company of <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Corporation" title="Kadokawa Corporation">Kadokawa Corporation</a> founded on January 30, 1987 as <b>ASCII Film Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">アスキー映画株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Asukī Eiga Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Magazines published by Enterbrain are generally focused on <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a> and computer entertainment as well as video game and strategy guides. In addition, the company publishes a small selection of <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> artbooks. Enterbrain is based in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, Japan, with a paid-in capital of 410 million <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">yen</a>. Enterbrain's current president is <a href="/wiki/Hirokazu_Hamamura" title="Hirokazu Hamamura">Hirokazu Hamamura</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Enterbrain_publications">Enterbrain publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enterbrain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Enterbrain publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_magazines_published_by_ASCII_Media_Works" title="List of magazines published by ASCII Media Works">List of magazines published by ASCII Media Works</a></div>
<ul>
<li><i>B's LOG</i>: Magazine focused on female gamers.</li>
<li><i>Weekly <a href="/wiki/Famitsu" title="Famitsu">Famitsu</a></i>: Up to the minute video game and video game industry news and reviews.</li>
<li><i>Famitsu PS2</i>: Biweekly magazine focused on the <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> hardware and software.</li>
<li><i>Famitsu Xbox</i>: Monthly magazine focused on <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a> hardware and software.</li>
<li><i>Famitsu DS + Cube &amp; Advance</i>: Monthly magazine focused on <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_GameCube" title="Nintendo GameCube">Nintendo GameCube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy_Advance" title="Game Boy Advance">Game Boy Advance</a> hardware and software.</li>
<li><i>TECH Win DVD</i>: A magazine aimed specifically to PC users. It comes with two CD-ROMs worth of goodies and information.</li>
<li><i>Tech Gian</i>: A <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a> magazine focused on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adult_video_games" title="Adult video games">adult video games</a>.</li>
<li><i>MAGI-CU</i>: A visual entertainment magazine based on female game characters.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Comic_Beam" title="Comic Beam">Comic Beam</a></i>: Comic Beam was formerly known as ASCII Comic. It is a <a href="/wiki/Comic_magazine" title="Comic magazine">comic magazine</a> filled with original <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>.</li>
<li><i>Harta</i> (formerly <i>Fellows!</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>): A periodical comic magazine consisting of original manga stories.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Arcadia" title="Monthly Arcadia">Monthly Arcadia</a></i> (月刊アルカディア): a 2000 monthly magazine focused on <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> machines. It was started by former staff of (bi)monthly <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Gamest" title="Gamest">Gamest</a></i> (ゲーメスト) from 1986 which was published by <i><a href="/wiki/Shinseisha" title="Shinseisha">Shinseisha</a></i>, Ltd. Arcadia also has game hints and advice for the latest arcade games as well as high score reports from Japanese arcades.</li>
<li><i>Sarabure</i>: A <a href="/wiki/Horse_racing" title="Horse racing">horse racing</a> magazine.</li>
<li><i>Famitsu Connect!On</i>: Magazine focused on online video games.</li>
<li><i>Logout Tabletalk RPG Series</i>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_role-playing_games" title="Japanese role-playing games">Tabletop role-playing games</a>.</li>
</ul>
