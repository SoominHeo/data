<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in May 1903 by workers at the <a href="/wiki/Carl_Zeiss_AG" title="Carl Zeiss AG">Carl Zeiss AG</a> optics factory as the company-sponsored <i>Fussball-Club der Firma Carl Zeiss</i>. The club underwent name changes in 1911 to <i>Fussball Club Carl Zeiss Jena e.V.</i> and in March 1917 to <i>1. Sportverein Jena e.V.</i></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1930s_and_World_War_II">The 1930s and World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The 1930s and World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1933, <i>1. SV Jena</i> joined the <a href="/wiki/Gauliga_Mitte" title="Gauliga Mitte">Gauliga Mitte</a>, one of 16 top-flight divisions formed in the reorganization of German football under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Reich" title="Third Reich">Third Reich</a>. The team captured division titles in 1935, 1936, 1940, and 1941. This earned <i>Jena</i> entry to the national finals, but they performed poorly and were never able to advance out of preliminary-round group play. After the 1943–44 season, the Gauliga Mitte broke up into a collection of city-based leagues as World War II overtook the area.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Postwar_play_in_East_Germany"><span id="Team_trivia"></span>Postwar play in East Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Postwar play in East Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the immediate aftermath of the war, associations of all types (including sports and football clubs) were banned in Germany by the occupying Allied authorities. Jena was reconstituted in June 1946 as <i>SG Ernst Abbe Jena</i> and, like many other clubs in <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a>, would undergo a number of name changes: <i>SG Stadion Jena</i> (October 1948), <i>SG Carl Zeiss Jena</i> (March 1949), <i>BSG Mechanik Jena</i> (January 1951), <i>BSG Motor Jena</i> (May 1951) and <i>SC Motor Jena</i> (November 1954).</p>
<p>In the aftermath of World War II, East German authorities tagged sports teams with the names of socialist heroes: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ernst_Karl_Abbe" title="Ernst Karl Abbe">Ernst Abbe</a> was a local son and physicist associated with the Zeiss optical factory. He made an early contribution to easing the plight of workers by introducing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eight_hour_day" title="Eight hour day">8-hour work day</a> at the Zeiss plant (a milestone for labour during the late 19th century).</p>
<p>In 1950 the club became a founding member of the DDR Liga (II), and in their second season captured a divisional title to win promotion to the top-flight DDR Oberliga for a single-season appearance. Renamed <i>SC Motor Jena</i> in 1954, they played their way back to the upper league by 1957. <i>Jena</i> won its first honours with the capture of the <a href="/wiki/FDGB-Pokal" title="FDGB-Pokal">FDGB-Pokal</a> in 1960 and followed up with the East German national title in 1963. The club was "re-founded" as <i>FC Carl Zeiss Jena</i> in January 1966, and became one of East Germany's <a href="/wiki/Football_club_(GDR)" title="Football club (GDR)">football clubs</a>, "focus centres" for the development of talented players for the national side. Jena would go on to become a dominant side in the DDR-Oberliga between then and 1975. They took two more national titles in 1968 and 1970, but finished in second place another half-dozen times to sides like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Viktoria_Frankfurt" title="FC Viktoria Frankfurt">Vorwärts Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dynamo_Dresden" title="Dynamo Dresden">Dynamo Dresden</a> and <a href="/wiki/1._FC_Magdeburg" title="1. FC Magdeburg">1. FC Magdeburg</a>. In addition to their national titles, <i>FCC</i> captured East German Cups in 1972, 1974 and 1980. The club also appeared in the 1981 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="Cup Winners' Cup">European Cup Winners' Cup</a> final, losing 1–2 to <a href="/wiki/FC_Dinamo_Tbilisi" title="FC Dinamo Tbilisi">Dinamo Tbilisi</a>. This was arguably the club's greatest achievement.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="German_reunification">German reunification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: German reunification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> in 1990, Jena was seeded into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2._Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="2. Fußball-Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a>. Their second-place finish in 1992 deteriorated into a 17th-place finish in 1994 and relegation to <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_Nordost" title="Regionalliga Nordost">Regionalliga Nordost</a> (III). They won immediate re-promotion, and played for three more years at the tier-II level. Since 1999 the team has primarily played tier III and IV football, but a second place-finish in the Regionalliga Nord secured Jena promotion to the 2. Bundesliga for the 2006–07 season. Jena remained in the 2. Bundesliga in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_2._Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="2007–08 2. Fußball-Bundesliga">2007–08</a>, avoiding relegation by winning 2–1 away against <i><a href="/wiki/FC_Augsburg" title="FC Augsburg">FC Augsburg</a></i> in their final match of the season. They would finish last in the 2. Bundesliga in 2007–08, returning to the third tier. However, this would not be one of the Regionalligen; the <a href="/wiki/German_Football_Association" title="German Football Association">German Football Association</a> (DFB) launched the new <a href="/wiki/3._Liga" title="3. Liga">3. Liga</a> for 2008–09, of which Jena was a charter member. On 9 November 2009 Chairman Peter Schreiber announced his retirement;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> on 13 November the executive board accepted his resignation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and on 25 November Hartmut Bayer became the new chairman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The second team was involved in the <a href="/wiki/2009_European_football_betting_scandal" title="2009 European football betting scandal">2009 European football betting scandal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> accused of match-fixing in the game against <a href="/wiki/ZFC_Meuselwitz" title="ZFC Meuselwitz">ZFC Meuselwitz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 10 December 2009 the club announced that it was in financial distress, owing over €1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In January 2010 the players agreed to accept a lower salary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Carl Zeiss Jena have a friendship with Welsh side Newport County, after the two sides played against each other in the European Cup Winners Cup in the early 1980s. As with Carl Zeiss Jena, Newport County have seen similar struggles off and on the pitch, and both teams regularly play each other during pre-season.</p>
<p>The club was relegated from the 3. Liga in 2012 at has been playing in the tier four Regionalliga Nordost as an upper table side, finishing second in 2013 and third in 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_players">Notable players</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notable players">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Category:FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena_players" title="Category:FC Carl Zeiss Jena players">Category:FC Carl Zeiss Jena players</a></div>
<p>FCC sent 33 players to the <a href="/wiki/East_Germany_national_football_team" title="East Germany national football team">DDR (East German) national side</a>.</p>
<p>Before the end of World War II, Jena sent three players to the German national side: <a href="/wiki/Willy_Krau%C3%9F" title="Willy Krauß">Willy Krauß</a> (1911–12), Heinz Werner (1935) and <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_G%C3%A4rtner" title="Ludwig Gärtner">Ludwig Gärtner</a> (1939–41).</p>
<p>American defender, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Bliss" title="Brian Bliss">Brian Bliss</a>, played at the club from 1992–1996 and received regular calls to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States men's national soccer team</a>. He went on to play for <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">MLS</a> side <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Crew_SC" title="Columbus Crew SC">Columbus Crew SC</a> and would later serve as technical director in the club's front office.</p>
<p>Another notable player is former Germany goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Robert_Enke" title="Robert Enke">Robert Enke</a>, who started his career at the club and then went on to play for clubs such as <a href="/wiki/Hannover_96" title="Hannover 96">Hannover 96</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reserve_team">Reserve team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FC_Carl_Zeiss_Jena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Reserve team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/German_reserve_football_teams" title="German reserve football teams">club's reserve team</a>, <b>FC Carl Zeiss Jena II</b>, currently plays in the tier five <a href="/wiki/NOFV-Oberliga_S%C3%BCd" title="NOFV-Oberliga Süd">NOFV-Oberliga Süd</a>. It first played at this level from 1994 to 1999, and again since 2006 with a third place in 1996 and 2010 as its best results.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Archiv_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Archiv-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fussballreserve_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fussballreserve-20">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team also won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thuringia_Cup" title="Thuringia Cup">Thuringia Cup</a> in 1993. The latter allowed the club qualification to the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_DFB-Pokal" title="1993–94 DFB-Pokal">1993–94 DFB-Pokal</a> where it lost 2–0 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>.</p>
