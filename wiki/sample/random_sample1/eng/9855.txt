<p><b>Ákos Elek</b> (born 21 July 1988) is a <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Di%C3%B3sgy%C5%91ri_VTK" title="Diósgyőri VTK">Diósgyőr</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_League" title="Hungarian League">Hungarian League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81kos_Elek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kazincbarcika">Kazincbarcika</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81kos_Elek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kazincbarcika">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elek started his career in the <a href="/wiki/Kazincbarcikai_SC" title="Kazincbarcikai SC">Kazincbarcikai SC</a> in 2005. The team played in the <a href="/wiki/Nemzeti_Bajnoks%C3%A1g_II" title="Nemzeti Bajnokság II">Hungarian Second Division</a>. He scored his only goal against Makó FC.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Videoton_FC">Videoton FC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81kos_Elek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Videoton FC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008 Elek signed a contract with the <a href="/wiki/Videoton_FC" title="Videoton FC">Videoton FC</a>. He played his first match against <a href="/wiki/BFC_Si%C3%B3fok" title="BFC Siófok">Siófok</a> on 26 July 2006. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010-11_Nemzeti_Bajnoks%C3%A1g_I" title="2010-11 Nemzeti Bajnokság I">2010-11 Nemzeti Bajnokság I</a> Elek won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_League" title="Hungarian League">Hungarian League</a> with his club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Di.C3.B3sgy.C5.91r">Diósgyőr</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81kos_Elek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diósgyőr">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 July 2012, Elek was signed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_League" title="Hungarian League">Hungarian League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Di%C3%B3sgy%C5%91ri_VTK" title="Diósgyőri VTK">Diósgyőr</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Changchun_Yatai">Changchun Yatai</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81kos_Elek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Changchun Yatai">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 January 2015, Elek signed a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Changchun_Yatai_F.C." title="Changchun Yatai F.C.">Changchun Yatai F.C.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Being a permanent member of his club in Videoton Elek was invited to join the <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungarian</a> national team by Hungarian coach <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A1ndor_Egerv%C3%A1ri" title="Sándor Egervári">Sándor Egervári</a>. Elek scored his first international goal against <a href="/wiki/Iceland_national_football_team" title="Iceland national football team">Iceland</a> at the Puskás Ferenc Stadium. The match finished 4–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Elek was selected for Hungary's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a> squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played in the last group match in a 3–3 draw against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Parc_Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Parc Olympique Lyonnais">Parc Olympique Lyonnais</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a> on 22 June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
