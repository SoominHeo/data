<p><b>Élodie Bouchez-Bangalter</b> (born 5 April 1973) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_actress" title="French actress">French actress</a>. She is known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9e_Rienne" title="Renée Rienne">Renée Rienne</a> on the fifth and final season of the television show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alias_(TV_Series)" title="Alias (TV Series)">Alias</a></i> and for playing Maïté Alvarez in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Reeds" title="Wild Reeds">Wild Reeds</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89lodie_Bouchez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bouchez is best known for her <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Award" title="César Award">César Award</a>'s Most Promising Actress winning film <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Reeds" title="Wild Reeds">Wild Reeds</a></i> (1994) by <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_T%C3%A9chin%C3%A9" title="André Téchiné">André Téchiné</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Actress_Award_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Best Actress Award (Cannes Film Festival)">Best Actress</a> Award for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dreamlife_of_Angels" title="The Dreamlife of Angels">La Vie rêvée des anges</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/1998_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="1998 Cannes Film Festival">1998 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-1">[1]</a></sup> She also won the Best Actress award for <i><a href="/wiki/Poetical_Refugee" title="Poetical Refugee">Poetical Refugee</a></i> (original French title - <i>La faute à Voltaire</i>) at the Cologne Mediterranean Film Festival-2001.</p>
<p>In the fall of 2005, she joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Alias_(TV_series)" title="Alias (TV series)">Alias</a></i> for its fifth and final season. She played <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9e_Rienne" title="Renée Rienne">Renée Rienne</a>, an assassin who works unofficially for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_ops" title="Black ops">black ops</a> division of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a>. Although considered a main cast member, she only appeared in select episodes, her character acting as something of a "secret weapon". Bouchez has also guest starred on Showtime's lesbian drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_L_Word" title="The L Word">The L Word</a></i>, where she portrays Claude, a French writer who meets Jenny on a trip to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89lodie_Bouchez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bouchez lives in <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California" title="Beverly Hills, California">Beverly Hills, California</a> with her husband <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Bangalter" title="Thomas Bangalter">Thomas Bangalter</a> (of the electronic music duo <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a>) and their two children, Tara-Jay and Roxan. Songwriter <a href="/wiki/David_Mead_(musician)" title="David Mead (musician)">David Mead</a> wrote his song "Élodie" about her.</p>
