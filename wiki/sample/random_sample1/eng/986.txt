<p><b>Futebol Clube Paços de Ferreira</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[ˈpasuʃ dɨ fɨˈʁɐjɾɐ]</a></span>) is a Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Pa%C3%A7os_de_Ferreira" title="Paços de Ferreira">Paços de Ferreira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porto_District" title="Porto District">Porto district</a>. Founded in 1950, it currently plays in the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Portuguese first division</a>, holding home games at the 7,000-seat capacity <i><a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_da_Mata_Real" title="Estádio da Mata Real">Estádio da Mata Real</a></i>, where the team has been based since 1973. The club's colours are yellow and green.</p>
<p>Paços de Ferreira have won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segunda_Liga" title="Segunda Liga">Segunda Liga</a> three times, and in 2007 qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> for the first time. In the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Primeira_Liga" title="2012–13 Primeira Liga">2012–13 season</a> the team surprisingly finished third and qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Champions League">2013–14 UEFA Champions League</a> play-offs for the first time in their history.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F.C._Pa%C3%A7os_de_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F.C._Pa%C3%A7os_de_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The origin of the club dates back to the 1930s, when it was named <i>Sport Club Pacense</i>. They played for two decades without any official recognition until they entered the lower divisions in 1950, under the name <i>Futebol Clube Vasco da Gama</i>. The club then changed their kit colours to the current ones and renamed themselves <i>Futebol Clube Paços de Ferreira</i>.</p>
<p>Their first match under the current name came on 19 November 1950, beating <a href="/wiki/A.D._Lousada" title="A.D. Lousada">Lousada</a> 2–1. Agostinho Alves was the first goal scorer in the history of the <i>Pacenses</i>. The club then played in Portugal's third regional division until the 1956–57 season, where they were crowned champions. The club crest was created in 1961–62, and was used ever since.</p>
<p>The club was relegated and then promoted again and supporters hit the streets of the city on 17 June 1973 when they defeated Perosinho 3–0. One year later, they won the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Third_Division" title="Portuguese Third Division">third division championship</a> on 14 June 1974, after defeating Estrela de Portalegre. The hero of the game was the goalscorer Mascarenhas.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F.C._Pa%C3%A7os_de_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After establishing themselves in the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">first division</a> during the 1990s, and suffering a relegation in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Primeira_Liga" title="2003–04 Primeira Liga">2003–04</a>, the club finished sixth in the first division in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Primeira_Liga" title="2006–07 Primeira Liga">2006–07</a>, thus <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_UEFA_Cup" title="2007–08 UEFA Cup">qualifying</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>, their first ever European competition, under manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mota_(football_manager)" title="José Mota (football manager)">José Mota</a>. They lost 1–0 on aggregate to <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ Alkmaar</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in the first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Having finished last in the league in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Primeira_Liga" title="2007–08 Primeira Liga">2007–08</a>, Paços would have normally been relegated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liga_de_Honra" title="Liga de Honra">second level</a>, but was readmitted after <a href="/wiki/Boavista_F.C." title="Boavista F.C.">Boavista</a>'s confirmed irregularities. In <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Primeira_Liga" title="2008–09 Primeira Liga">the following year</a>, already without manager Mota, the team had a reasonably successful season: a comfortable 10th place in the league and a second <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2009–10 UEFA Europa League">Europa League qualification spot</a> after losing the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="Taça de Portugal">Cup of Portugal</a> <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="2008–09 Taça de Portugal">final</a> 1–0 to eventual league champions Porto on 31 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The two clubs met again on 9 August in the Super Cup, which Porto won 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Paços entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009-10_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2009-10 UEFA Europa League">2009-10 UEFA Europa League</a> in the second qualifying round, where they defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Zimbru_Chi%C5%9Fin%C4%83u" title="FC Zimbru Chişinău">Zimbru Chişinău</a> of Moldova before being eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Bnei_Yehuda_Tel_Aviv_F.C." title="Bnei Yehuda Tel Aviv F.C.">Bnei Yehuda</a> of Israel in the third qualifying round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Primeira_Liga" title="2012–13 Primeira Liga">2012–13 season</a>, they surprisingly qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Champions League">2013–14 UEFA Champions League</a> play-offs for the first time in their history after achieving 3rd place in the league by passing favourites Braga and <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting CP</a>, making it their highest finish ever. The club were managed that season by <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Fonseca" title="Paulo Fonseca">Paulo Fonseca</a>, who left at the end to join <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Porto" title="F.C. Porto">FC Porto</a>, and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Costinha" title="Costinha">Costinha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Costinha's side were drawn against Russians <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit St. Petersburg</a> in their play-off, and lost the two games. Due to bad results and the last place in the <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a>, in October 2013 <a href="/wiki/Henrique_Calisto" title="Henrique Calisto">Henrique Calisto</a> returned to <a href="/wiki/Pa%C3%A7os_de_Ferreira" title="Paços de Ferreira">Paços de Ferreira</a> and replaced <a href="/wiki/Costinha" title="Costinha">Costinha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=F.C._Pa%C3%A7os_de_Ferreira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The supporters' club, "Ultras Yellow Boys," was founded in 1996, returning afterwards in 2001. Two previous groups, however extinct, existed: <i>"Febre Amarela"</i> and "Yellowmania".</p>
