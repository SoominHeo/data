<p><i><b>Ellobiopsis</b></i> is a genus of alveolate parasitic <a href="/wiki/Eukaryote" title="Eukaryote">eukaryotes</a>.</p>
<p>This genus was created in 1910 by Caullery. Species in this genus infect crustaceans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Timofeev2002_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Timofeev2002-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The type species is <i>Ellobiopsis chattoni</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ellobiopsis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This genus has been difficult to classify, being placed in several different taxa in the past (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Chytridiomycota" title="Chytridiomycota">Chytridiomycota</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinoflagellata" title="Dinoflagellata">Dinoflagellata</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but it is now known to be an alveolate. Its closest relations appear to be the genus <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thalassomyces&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thalassomyces (page does not exist)">Thalassomyces</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G.C3.B3mez2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-G.C3.B3mez2009-3">[3]</a></sup> These genera have been grouped together to form the family <i>Ellobiopsidae</i>.</p>
