<p><i><b>Elementary</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Procedural_drama" title="Procedural drama">procedural drama</a> series that presents a contemporary update of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Sir Arthur Conan Doyle">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle</a>'s character <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a>. The series was created by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Doherty_(writer)" title="Robert Doherty (writer)">Robert Doherty</a> and stars <a href="/wiki/Jonny_Lee_Miller" title="Jonny Lee Miller">Jonny Lee Miller</a> as Sherlock Holmes and <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Liu" title="Lucy Liu">Lucy Liu</a> as Dr. Joan Watson. The series premiered on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> on September 27, 2012. The series is set and filmed primarily in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vulture_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-vulture-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The show follows Holmes, a recovering drug addict and former <a href="/wiki/Consultant" title="Consultant">consultant</a> to <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Yard" title="Scotland Yard">Scotland Yard</a>, as he assists the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">New York City Police Department</a> in solving crimes. His indifference to police procedure often leads to conflict with <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters#Inspector_Gregson" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Captain Thomas Gregson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Aidan_Quinn" title="Aidan Quinn">Aidan Quinn</a>), although the two still remain mutually respectful of one another. He is accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. Joan Watson</a>, who initially acts as his <a href="/wiki/Sober_companion" title="Sober companion">sober companion</a>. She is a former surgeon and was hired by Sherlock's father to help him in his <a href="/wiki/Drug_rehabilitation" title="Drug rehabilitation">rehabilitation</a>. They eventually begin to work together on his cases, and she becomes Holmes' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apprentice" title="Apprentice">apprentice</a>. The series also features Holmes' ongoing conflict with his <a href="/wiki/Archenemy" title="Archenemy">nemesis</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor_Moriarty" title="Professor Moriarty">Jamie Moriarty</a> (<a href="/wiki/Natalie_Dormer" title="Natalie Dormer">Natalie Dormer</a>). Other supporting roles include <a href="/wiki/Jon_Michael_Hill" title="Jon Michael Hill">Jon Michael Hill</a> as Detective Marcus Bell, <a href="/wiki/Rhys_Ifans" title="Rhys Ifans">Rhys Ifans</a> as Sherlock's brother, <a href="/wiki/Mycroft_Holmes" title="Mycroft Holmes">Mycroft Holmes</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Noble" title="John Noble">John Noble</a> as Sherlock's father, Morland Holmes.</p>
<p>Before the series premiered, it was met with some criticism given it followed closely on the heels of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s modern adaptation <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_(TV_series)" title="Sherlock (TV series)">Sherlock</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Elementary_CBS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Elementary_CBS-2">[2]</a></sup> After the premiere, it was picked up for a full season and later an extra two episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The season two premiere was partly filmed on location in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The series has since been well received by critics, who have praised the performances, writing, and novel approach to the source material. On March 25, 2016, CBS renewed the series for a fifth season, which premiered on October 2, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBS_Fall_Premiere_Dates_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBS_Fall_Premiere_Dates-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following his <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Fall_from_grace_(disambiguation)" title="Fall from grace (disambiguation)">fall from grace</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and a stint in <a href="/wiki/Drug_rehabilitation" title="Drug rehabilitation">rehab</a>, an eccentric modern <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a> relocates to <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, where his <a href="/wiki/Wealth" title="Wealth">wealthy</a> father forces him to live with a <a href="/wiki/Sober_companion" title="Sober companion">sober companion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. Joan Watson</a>. Formerly a successful <a href="/wiki/Surgeon" title="Surgeon">surgeon</a> until she lost a patient, Watson views her current job as another opportunity to help people. However, Sherlock is nothing like her previous clients. He informs her that none of her expertise as an addiction specialist applies to him and that he has devised his own post-rehab regimen – resuming his work as a police consultant in New York City. Watson has no choice but to accompany her grouchy new charge on his jobs.</p>
<p>Over time, Sherlock finds her medical background helpful, and Watson realizes she has a talent for investigation. Sherlock's police contact, Captain <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters#Inspector_Gregson" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Thomas Gregson</a>, knows from previous experience working with <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Yard" title="Scotland Yard">Scotland Yard</a> that Sherlock is brilliant at solving cases, and welcomes him as part of the team. The investigative group also includes Detective Marcus Bell, an investigator with sharp intuition and intimidating <a href="/wiki/Interrogation" title="Interrogation">interrogation</a> skills. Although initially skeptical of Holmes and his unorthodox methods, Bell begins to recognize Sherlock as an asset to their investigations.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_characters">Cast and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cast and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Minor Sherlock Holmes characters</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main">Main</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jonny_Lee_Miller" title="Jonny Lee Miller">Jonny Lee Miller</a> as <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a>: A former <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Yard" title="Scotland Yard">Scotland Yard</a> consultant who now lives in New York City after completing <a href="/wiki/Drug_rehabilitation" title="Drug rehabilitation">drug rehabilitation</a> there for addiction-related problems in the United Kingdom. Holmes is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deductive" title="Deductive">deductive</a> genius with a variety of unusual interests and enthusiasms that assist him in his investigations. Feeling that the more interesting criminal cases are in America, he stays in New York. He contacts an old associate, Captain Thomas Gregson of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">NYPD</a> to resume his previous work as a consulting detective. He is forced by his father to live with Dr. Joan Watson, his "<a href="/wiki/Sober_companion" title="Sober companion">sober companion</a>" who provides him with aftercare. Miller's Holmes displays many <a href="/wiki/Canon_(fiction)" title="Canon (fiction)">canonical</a> aspects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Sir Arthur Conan Doyle">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle</a>'s character, while his familial relations, especially his resentment for his father, have been added into his narrative. In between seasons two and three, Sherlock spent eight months in Britain working for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MI-6" title="MI-6">MI-6</a>. He returned to New York in "Enough Nemesis to Go Around" with a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prot%C3%A9g%C3%A9" title="Protégé">protégé</a>, Kitty Winter. At the conclusion of season three, Holmes suffers a relapse, but his father's connections allow him to resume working for the NYPD. In Season 4, it is revealed in mid-season that his mother, May Holmes, was also an opiate addict like him.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lucy_Liu" title="Lucy Liu">Lucy Liu</a> as <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. Joan Watson</a> (née Yun): Holmes' sober companion. Watson was a successful surgeon, which adds to her complement of skills. She comes to Holmes when she is hired by his father as his sober companion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBSAbout_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBSAbout-9">[9]</a></sup> to help him remain abstinent after his release from <a href="/wiki/Drug_rehabilitation" title="Drug rehabilitation">rehabilitation</a>. After her contracted time is finished, she remains on after lying to Holmes, telling him that his father had retained her services. He comes to rely on her input and grows to trust her as she helps him come to terms with his life after addiction. After a while, Holmes reveals that he found out that she was no longer being paid to stay as a companion. He offers her a position as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apprentice" title="Apprentice">apprentice</a>, telling her how much she means to him and how she helps him to focus. Watson accepts and starts her training as a detective with Holmes. After Sherlock left for London, Joan became the go-to consulting detective for the 11th Precinct, while also taking on more traditional <a href="/wiki/Private_investigator" title="Private investigator">private investigator</a>-type cases that Sherlock eschews. Despite this, the two resumed working together after Holmes returned to New York, albeit with Joan taking on the occasional independent case away from Holmes. Joan has a brother Oren and a half-sister Lin Wen (née Yun). Joan and her sister have the same father, different mothers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aidan_Quinn" title="Aidan Quinn">Aidan Quinn</a> as <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters#Inspector_Gregson" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Captain Thomas "Tommy" Gregson</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[note 1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Police_captain" title="Police captain">captain</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">New York City Police Department</a>'s 11th Precinct. He was previously assigned to <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Yard" title="Scotland Yard">Scotland Yard</a> to observe their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counter-Terrorism" title="Counter-Terrorism">Counter-Terrorism</a> Bureau, where he crossed paths with Sherlock and was impressed with his work. He genuinely likes Holmes, and the two have a mutual respect for each other, though he admits that Sherlock is a "pain in the ass". In season 2, Gregson separated from his wife of over twenty years, Cheryl, and they are divorced by season 3. In "Rip Off" (season 3, episode 5), it is revealed that his daughter, Hannah Gregson (Liza J. Bennett), is an ambitious patrol officer with the 15th Precinct. In "Absconded" (season 3, episode 23), Gregson is offered a promotion to Deputy Chief due to the good work of his unit, but despite hints that some higher-ups wanted him to accept the offer, he decided to remain as he valued his current role and ability to interact with people more than the possibilities offered by the promotion. It is also mentioned in that episode that he served at the 14th Precinct as a newly promoted Detective and was made head of the Major Case Squad at age 40. In Season Four, it is revealed mid-season that Gregson is now dating Paige Cowen, a former detective who quit after her unit was accused of taking bribes; they briefly break up after Joan runs into them at a restaurant, as Paige claims she doesn't want people to think ill of Gregson even if she wasn't involved in her unit's actions, but Joan soon learns that Paige actually has <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">Multiple sclerosis</a>, and convinces Gregson to give the relationship another chance. He is a recipient of the U.S. Flag Bar, World Trade Center Bar, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department_Medal_of_Honor" title="New York City Police Department Medal of Honor">NYPD Medal of Honor</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department_Medal_for_Valor" title="New York City Police Department Medal for Valor">NYPD Medal for Valor</a>, and the NYPD 170th Commemorative Breast Bar.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jon_Michael_Hill" title="Jon Michael Hill">Jon Michael Hill</a> as Detective First Grade Marcus Bell:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> A junior officer with the 11th Precinct with whom Holmes and Watson often work. While initially against the idea of getting help from Sherlock, he comes to realize Sherlock's talent as a detective and readily takes advice from him. He was briefly reassigned to an observational role in Season Two after sustaining a potentially serious shoulder injury due to a hostile witness Holmes had questioned earlier, but a confrontation with Holmes helped Bell get over the psychological issues that were hindering his recovery and he has since returned to his old role. He is a recipient of the U.S. Flag Bar, NYPD Excellent Police Duty, and the NYPD 170th Commemorative Breast Bar.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Noble" title="John Noble">John Noble</a> as Morland Holmes: Sherlock's father who arrives in New York after Sherlock has a relapse. He works as an eminent business consultant, making arrangements for various companies to achieve their goals regardless of what they might be with Sherlock describing him as a 'neutral' party in that he has no concern about the consequences of his clients' goals so long as they are achieved. He has decided to stay in New York for unknown long-term reasons involving Sherlock, with Joan speculating from independent research that he suffered serious stomach damage in a failed attempt to murder him two years ago, and may believe that he is being targeted again. With a view to resolving the threat, at the end of Season 4, he takes over the leadership of Moriarty's organization which will help him to dismantle the group and guarantee safety of his son from further harm.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nelsan_Ellis" title="Nelsan Ellis">Nelsan Ellis</a> as <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters#Shinwell_Johnson" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Shinwell Johnson</a>: A previous patient of Dr. Watson and an ex-convict and gang member for the SBK (South Bronx Killas). He and Watson became acquainted once more when he was released from prison and was placed on probation. During his time on probation, Watson helped him settle back into his life outside of prison while also assisting him in his attempt to build a relationship with his daughter. He was briefly an unofficial informant for an FBI agent and is now an official informant for the Bronx Gang Squad (episodes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep105" title="List of Elementary episodes">105</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep106" title="List of Elementary episodes">106</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recurring">Recurring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recurring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ophelia_Lovibond" title="Ophelia Lovibond">Ophelia Lovibond</a> as Kitty Winter: Sherlock's newest protégée whom he brought with him from <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> after leaving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MI6" title="MI6">MI6</a>. She was initially tasked with spying on Watson until she was discovered. Sherlock tends to be strict with her, but admires her detective skills. Kitty's real name is unknown as she was kidnapped and raped in London prior to meeting Sherlock and she had changed her name in an effort to forget it. Her character is based on Kitty Winter in Doyle's "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Illustrious_Client" title="The Adventure of the Illustrious Client">The Adventure of the Illustrious Client</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup> After confronting and disfiguring her rapist, she decided she needed to leave the United States to avoid possible arrest, and to go somewhere she could use the skills that Sherlock and Watson had taught her.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ato_Essandoh" title="Ato Essandoh">Ato Essandoh</a> as Alfredo Llamosa: Sherlock's <a href="/wiki/Narcotics_Anonymous" title="Narcotics Anonymous">NA</a> sponsor who is a recovering addict himself. Alfredo is also reformed from a life of crime stealing cars. He is now paid by various car companies to test their cars' security systems, and he occasionally lets Sherlock try out his own skills on them. Alfredo is one of Sherlock's few real friends, but is not hesitant to criticize him and pushes him to continue his rehab regimen, including becoming a sponsor himself. He also teaches Joan how to bypass automotive security. Holmes 'fired' Alfredo as his sponsor so that he could help Alfredo as a friend.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhys_Ifans" title="Rhys Ifans">Rhys Ifans</a> as <a href="/wiki/Mycroft_Holmes" title="Mycroft Holmes">Mycroft Holmes</a>: Sherlock's older brother who still lives in London. He and Sherlock had a very bitter relationship in the past, but Mycroft is taking steps to reconcile with his brother, and becomes good friends with Joan. He owns a chain of restaurants and is an excellent cook. It is later revealed that Mycroft is in the employ of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MI6" title="MI6">MI6</a>, and it becomes necessary for Mycroft to fake his death in "The Grand Experiment", an act that Holmes felt represented a lack of faith in Holmes to find another solution to the current dilemma.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Natalie_Dormer" title="Natalie Dormer">Natalie Dormer</a> as <a href="/wiki/Irene_Adler" title="Irene Adler">Irene Adler</a>/<a href="/wiki/Professor_Moriarty" title="Professor Moriarty">Jamie Moriarty</a>: As Irene, she is Sherlock's former lover, while in her true identity as Moriarty she is a criminal mastermind who romanced Sherlock—and then faked Irene's death—to draw his investigations away from her criminal activities. It was her supposed death as Irene that caused Sherlock's already established drug use to escalate. Despite Sherlock discovering her true identity, and her subsequent imprisonment, the two continue to have conflicting feelings for each other—Holmes noting during a conversation with Bell that "the love of [his] life is an unrepentant homicidal maniac"—and great mutual respect for each other's intellectual powers. She has also gained an amount of respect for Joan, as the latter's ability to fool her is what got her arrested; when Joan's life is threatened by drug kingpin Elana March, she arranges the criminal's death in her cell.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sean_Pertwee" title="Sean Pertwee">Sean Pertwee</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lestrade" title="Lestrade">Gareth Lestrade</a>: Sherlock's British colleague and rival. While Sherlock was based in London, he worked with Lestrade, who was then a member of the Metropolitan Police. Lestrade took credit for solving cases that were actually solved by Sherlock. Lestrade is clearly not in Sherlock's league, but he is a skilled— if overzealous and impulsive— detective.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Candis_Cayne" title="Candis Cayne">Candis Cayne</a> as <a href="/wiki/Minor_Sherlock_Holmes_characters#Mrs_Hudson" title="Minor Sherlock Holmes characters">Ms. Hudson</a>: An expert in Ancient Greek who essentially makes a living as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kept_woman" title="Kept woman">kept woman</a> and muse for various wealthy men; Sherlock allows her to stay at the brownstone after a breakup, and she subsequently agrees to clean for them once a week as a source of income. Sherlock initially attempts to make Joan pay for the work as she complained about his messiness but she refuses and they settle on sharing the expense. Seen in single episodes in each of first three seasons (episodes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep19" title="List of Elementary episodes">19</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep45" title="List of Elementary episodes">45</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep55" title="List of Elementary episodes">55</a>), but mentioned in numerous others through season four.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Betty_Gilpin" title="Betty Gilpin">Betty Gilpin</a> as Fiona 'Mittens' Helbron: a brilliant software engineer for a technology company called Pentillion, who also is known as 'Mittens' in the hacker community. She is on the <a href="/wiki/List_of_fictional_characters_on_the_autism_spectrum" title="List of fictional characters on the autism spectrum">autism spectrum</a>, and is a cat lover (hence her hacker moniker). She is briefly considered a suspect in one case (episode <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep81" title="List of Elementary episodes">81</a>) but later assists Holmes and Watson in their investigation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup> She later contacts Watson for assistance in another matter, and begins a romantic relationship with Holmes (episodes <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep84" title="List of Elementary episodes">84</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep90" title="List of Elementary episodes">90</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jordan_Gelber" title="Jordan Gelber">Jordan Gelber</a> as Dr. Eugene Hawes, M.E.: <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> <a href="/wiki/Medical_examiner" title="Medical examiner">medical examiner</a> that provides Sherlock and Watson with details relating to murders that cross paths with their investigations. He and Sherlock are regular chess partners ("the first Thursday of the month" is mentioned in the episode <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep88" title="List of Elementary episodes">"Hounded"</a>). After he is almost killed when a bomb is detonated in the city morgue (<a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep83" title="List of Elementary episodes">"Down Where the Dead Delight"</a>), he develops an addiction to drugs. After noticing the indicators, Sherlock implores him to get him some help (<a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep88" title="List of Elementary episodes">"Hounded"</a>). He takes a leave of absence to recover, but returns to active duty as the M.E. in the Season 5 episode <a href="/wiki/List_of_Elementary_episodes#ep104" title="List of Elementary episodes">"How the Sausage Is Made"</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Writer and producer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Doherty_(writer)" title="Robert Doherty (writer)">Robert Doherty</a> created the show. Doherty has commented that it was Carl Beverly<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> who "initially was the one who brought up the possibility of developing a Sherlock show."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roberts_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts-19">[18]</a></sup> Beverly spoke about the relationship between Sherlock and Watson in the show in July 2012:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Rob [Doherty] often calls it a bromance, but one of the bros just happens to be a woman. He said that from the very beginning and I think it's really an apt description. There's this idea that a man and a woman can't be together on a show especially without needing to be together sexually or in love or whatever, and this is really about the evolution of a friendship and how that happens. Watching that should be as much the story of this show as the mysteries that you see week in and week out about who killed who.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roberts_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts-19">[18]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Liu was cast by February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goldberg022712_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-goldberg022712-20">[19]</a></sup> That July, she said that Watson is not "someone who's on the sideline; she's his <a href="/wiki/Sober_companion" title="Sober companion">sober companion</a>, she's engaged in <i>him</i>, not the mystery, [...] From that point on you get to see how that blossoms out. The foot-in-the-bucket and that kind of Watson happens because in entertainment, there's got to be a sidekick. In this case, that's not the direction we're going in. Ask me in six episodes and if I have a foot in a bucket then we'll have a discussion."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goldberg072912_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-goldberg072912-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_to_BBC.27s_Sherlock">Relationship to BBC's <i>Sherlock</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Relationship to BBC's Sherlock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_(TV_series)" title="Sherlock (TV series)">Sherlock</a></i>, a contemporary reworking of the Sherlock Holmes story, premiered in the UK in July 2010 and in the U.S. in October 2010. The British show has since sold to more than 200 territories. In January 2012, shortly after CBS's announcement they had ordered the pilot for <i>Elementary</i>, <i>Sherlock</i> producer <a href="/wiki/Sue_Vertue" title="Sue Vertue">Sue Vertue</a> told newspaper <i>The Independent</i> "we understand that CBS are doing their own version of an updated Sherlock Holmes. It's interesting, as they approached us a while back about remaking our show. At the time, they made great assurances about their integrity, so we have to assume that their modernised Sherlock Holmes doesn't resemble ours in any way, as that would be extremely worrying."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> The following month Vertue said that "We have been in touch with CBS and informed them that we will be looking at their finished pilot very closely for any infringement of our rights."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sherwin021512_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-sherwin021512-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>CBS made a statement on the issue: "Our project is a contemporary take on Sherlock Holmes that will be based on Holmes, Watson and other characters in the public domain, as well as original characters. We are, of course, respectful of all copyright laws and will not infringe on any stories or works that may still be protected."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sherwin021512_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-sherwin021512-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Creator Robert Doherty discussed comparisons between <i>Sherlock</i> and <i>Elementary</i> the following July, pointing out that a tradition of updated Holmes stories dates back to the <a href="/wiki/Basil_Rathbone" title="Basil Rathbone">Basil Rathbone</a> films of the 1940s, and that he did not think it was the case that <i>Elementary</i> took anything from <i>Sherlock</i>, which he described as a "brilliant show" having watched its first series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roberts_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts-19">[18]</a></sup> Several months later, Lucy Liu confirmed the producers of the UK <i>Sherlock</i> were shown the pilot, "saw how different it was from theirs," and were "okay with it now."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vulture_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-vulture-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some interior scenes are <a href="/wiki/Shot_(filmmaking)" title="Shot (filmmaking)">shot</a> at <a href="/wiki/Silvercup_Studios" title="Silvercup Studios">Silvercup Studios</a> in Long Island City. Some exterior shots of Sherlock's <a href="/wiki/Brownstone" title="Brownstone">brownstone</a> are filmed in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> which is a stand-in for <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Heights" title="Brooklyn Heights">Brooklyn Heights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first season was met with positive reviews from critics, who highlighted the show's novel approach to the source material, the writing quality, and the performances and chemistry found between its two leads and supporting cast. Season one holds an 83% approval rating on aggregate review site <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on 42 collected reviews, with an average score of 7.3 out of 10. The site's consensus reads: "It may not appeal to purists, but Elementary provides a fresh new spin on Sherlock Holmes, and Jonny Lee Miller shines in the title role."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> It also holds a <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> score of 73 out of 100 based on 29 sampled reviews, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian's</a> Phelim O'Neill felt that "Jonny Lee Miller and Lucy Liu make it a double act to rival <i>Sherlock</i>" and noted that "the pacing feels perfect and the details are light: viewers can keep up with the investigation and feel involved, not something every investigative show achieves".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup> Lori Rackl of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Chicago_Sun-Times" title="The Chicago Sun-Times">the Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> gave the pilot episode 3 stars out of 4, and said "While the latest interpretation doesn't live up to the British import, it's still more entertaining than your typical CBS procedural."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[27]</a></sup> Hank Stuever of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> gave it a B+ and felt that the show "exhibits enough stylish wit in its mood and look to quickly distinguish itself from the latest British <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_(TV_series)" title="Sherlock (TV series)">Sherlock</a></i> series (seen on PBS)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Season two was met with equally positive reviews. It holds a 100% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on eight reviews, with an average score of 8.5 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup> Several critics praised <a href="/wiki/Rhys_Ifans" title="Rhys Ifans">Rhys Ifans</a> for his portrayal of Mycroft Holmes, with Myles McNutt of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> calling his casting choice "inspired" and praising him for being able to match with Miller's "bitterness" and praising the premiere episode overall <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup> - he later went on to offer positive words on Ifans' performance in the finale episodes pertaining to Mycroft's story, despite finding flaws in the overall arc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup> Noel Kirkpatrick of <a href="/wiki/TV.com" title="TV.com">TV.com</a> also praised Ifans, saying he "very finely" played the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[32]</a></sup> The episode "The Diabolical Kind" also attracted wide acclaim, with many singling out the emotional depth and <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Dormer" title="Natalie Dormer">Natalie Dormer</a>'s performance as Moriarty. McNutt called Moriarty's presence in both the episode and the series as a whole "refreshingly dominant" and also praised the storytelling and dialogue, singling out several bits of witty humor in the episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[33]</a></sup> The episode has a 9.0 rating on TV.com with Kirkpatrick claiming Dormer was "having a ball" playing the role of Moriarty and saying there was "good stuff" to be had in her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[34]</a></sup> Kirkpatrick also appreciated the season as a whole for its development of Holmes' character, as well as the performance of the cast.</p>
<p>Season three continues the <i>Elementary</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s trend of a positive critical response. It holds a 100% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on six reviews, with an average score of 8.9 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[35]</a></sup> IGN praised the evolution of Watson as a character in the show, saying "While other Holmes/Watson incarnations focus Watson being a friend, medic, and put-upon backup, Elementary has elevated the character into someone with loftier aspirations."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[36]</a></sup> Particular praise was given to <a href="/wiki/Ophelia_Lovibond" title="Ophelia Lovibond">Ophelia Lovibond</a> for her performance as Sherlock's protege Kitty Winter, with critics feeling she was a welcome addition to the cast. The episode "The One That Got Away" garnered critical acclaim for its resolution of Kitty's story, as well as the performances of Miller and Lovibond. The Season 3 finale was met with positive reviews. IGN's Matt Fowler gave the Season finale: "A Controlled Descent" an 8.3/10 saying that "The one-two punch of Sherlock both giving into his anger and his heroin lust was a scorching way to send us out of Season 3".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Season four, like previous seasons, was met with a positive critical response. It holds a 100% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on eight reviews, with an average score of 7.4 out of 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[38]</a></sup> IGN's Matt Fowler gave the season 4 premiere episode "The Past is Parent" a 7.3/10. He praised Joan and Sherlock's deepening friendship and <a href="/wiki/John_Noble" title="John Noble">John Noble</a>'s performance as Sherlock's father, but criticized the fact that the episode didn't capitalize off the crisis from the Season 3 finale, saying that "while there wasn't anything necessarily bad about "The Past is Parent," it just failed to capitalize off the momentum from last season".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[39]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast">Broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elementary_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Australia, <i>Elementary</i> premiered on <a href="/wiki/Network_Ten" title="Network Ten">Network Ten</a> on February 3, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[56]</a></sup> The second season started airing on March 23, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[57]</a></sup> The third season started airing on March 2, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Canada, it airs simultaneously on <a href="/wiki/Global_Television_Network" title="Global Television Network">Global</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[61]</a></sup> In New Zealand, it premiered on <a href="/wiki/Prime_(New_Zealand)" title="Prime (New Zealand)">Prime</a> on February 27, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the United Kingdom and Ireland, the series was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living" title="Sky Living">Sky Living</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[63]</a></sup> It debuted on October 23, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[64]</a></sup> The second season premiered on October 22, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[65]</a></sup> The third season began airing on November 11, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 3, 2013, <i>Elementary</i> was broadcast after <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVII" title="Super Bowl XLVII">Super Bowl XLVII</a>. The episode drew 20.8 million viewers despite running out of prime time in the Eastern time zone as a result of a <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVII#Power_outage" title="Super Bowl XLVII">game delay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[68]</a></sup></p>
