<p><b>Elissar Zakaria Khoury</b>, commonly known as <b>Elissa</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">إليسا</span>‎‎  <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Arabic" title="Help:IPA for Arabic">[ʔeˈliːsa, ʔeˈlis.sa]</a></span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_people" title="Lebanese people">Lebanese</a> recording artist.</p>
<p>Elissa has sold over 30 million albums and was the highest-selling female Middle Eastern artist for 2005, 2006, 2009, and 2010. In 2005, 2006, and 2010 she received the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Music_Award" title="World Music Award">World Music Award</a> for Best Selling Middle Eastern Artist.</p>
<p>Since 2013, Elissa has also been a judge on the television show <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Arabic_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (Arabic TV series)">MBC The X Factor</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elissa_(Lebanese_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1992.E2.80.9399:_Studio_El_Fan.2C_and_debut_album_Baddi_Doub">1992–99: Studio El Fan, and debut album <i>Baddi Doub</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elissa_(Lebanese_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1992–99: Studio El Fan, and debut album Baddi Doub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elissa's musical career began in 1992, when she won the silver medal in <a href="/wiki/Studio_El_Fan" title="Studio El Fan">Studio El Fan</a>, a Lebanese music competition. Her first album, <i>Baddi Doub</i>, was released by <a href="/wiki/EMI_Records" title="EMI Records">EMI Records</a> in 1998.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.9303:_W_Akhertha_Maak_and_Ayshalak">2000–03: <i>W Akhertha Maak</i> and <i>Ayshalak</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elissa_(Lebanese_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–03: W Akhertha Maak and Ayshalak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elissa released her second album, <i>We Akhertha Maak</i>, which included the duet, "Betghib Betrouh", sung with Lebanese <a href="/wiki/Ragheb_Alama" title="Ragheb Alama">Ragheb Alama</a>. She was the only Arabic artist to perform in front of former United States President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> at the Stars Charity show in Dubai. That year she won the <a href="/wiki/Murex_d%27Or" title="Murex d'Or">Murex d'Or</a> award for Best Female Artist. Her third studio album, <i>Ayshalak</i>, was released in 2002. In 2002 Elissa inaugurated Kuwait's Virgin Megastore with Sir <a href="/wiki/Richard_Branson" title="Richard Branson">Richard Branson</a>. Her clip, "Ajmal Ihsas" won an Arabic Music Award for Best Video Clip in Dubai and in 2003 she signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>. Subsequently, she shot two advertisements with American pop star <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a> for Pepsi for the World Football Cup. Both advertisements were shot in Los Angeles. She collaborated with Irish singer <a href="/wiki/Chris_de_Burgh" title="Chris de Burgh">Chris De Burgh</a> in the duet, "Lebanese Nights."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.9306:_Ahla_Donia_and_Bastanak">2004–06: <i>Ahla Donia</i> and <i>Bastanak</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elissa_(Lebanese_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004–06: Ahla Donia and Bastanak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004 she signed with the Saudi-owned <a href="/wiki/Rotana_Group" title="Rotana Group">Rotana</a>, under which she released her fourth studio album, <i>Ahla Donya</i>, in May 2004. In 2005, Elissa received the <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Awards</a> for Best Selling Artist from the Middle East. The ceremony took place in Hollywood's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kodak_Theater" title="Kodak Theater">Kodak Theater</a>. In December 2005, Elissa received the Murex d'Or for Best Lebanese Artist and Best Video Clip for "Hobak Wajaa". In 2006, she released her fifth studio album <i>Bastanak</i>. On November 15, 2006, Elissa received the World Music Awards for the second year in a row for the sales of <i>Bastanak</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9309:_Ayami_Beek.2C_Tesadaa_Bemeen">2007–09: <i>Ayami Beek</i>, <i>Tesadaa Bemeen</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elissa_(Lebanese_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2007–09: Ayami Beek, Tesadaa Bemeen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elissa's sixth and seventh albums were <i><a href="/wiki/Ayami_Beek" title="Ayami Beek">Ayami Beek</a></i> (December 20, 2007) and <i><a href="/wiki/Tesadaa_Bemeen" title="Tesadaa Bemeen">Tesadaa Bemeen</a></i> (December 26, 2009). Elissa received her third World Music Award for <i>Tesadaa Bemeen</i>. In November 2010, she was awarded the prize for Best Arab Female Artist in the Jordan Music Awards, and a few days later she received the Big Apple Music Award for Best Female Artist in the Middle East.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.932015:_As.27ad_Wahda.2C_Halet_Hob.2C_and_MBC_The_X_Factor">2012–2015: <i>As'ad Wahda</i>, <i>Halet Hob</i>, and MBC The X Factor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elissa_(Lebanese_singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012–2015: As'ad Wahda, Halet Hob, and MBC The X Factor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her eighth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Asaad_Wahda" title="Asaad Wahda">As'ad Wahda</a></i>, was released in June 2012. In December 2012, Elissa was chosen to be one of the judges of <i>The X Factor Arabia</i>; It was on the air the first four months of 2013. On June 23, 2013 Elissa received two Murex D’or awards, for the Best Lebanese Singer and Best Arabic Song "Asaad Wahda".</p>
<p>Elissa released her ninth studio album <i>Halet Hob</i> in July 2014. She released the first music video of "Hob Kol Hayati" on November 21, 2014. Elissa returned as a judge on <i>The X Factor</i> for its second season on March 14, 2015. Elissa released her cover version of "<a href="/wiki/Mawtini" title="Mawtini">Mawtini</a>" on April 29, 2015, along with its music video. The second video from her ninth studio album was released on May 14, 2015, for "Ya Merayti".</p>
