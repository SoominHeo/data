<p><b>Elliot Robert Lee</b> (born 16 December 1994) is an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a>. Lee has also had loan spells with <a href="/wiki/Colchester_United_F.C." title="Colchester United F.C.">Colchester United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a>. He is the son of former footballer <a href="/wiki/Rob_Lee" title="Rob Lee">Rob Lee</a> and the brother of footballer <a href="/wiki/Olly_Lee" title="Olly Lee">Olly Lee</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Ham_United">West Ham United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: West Ham United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Durham,_England" title="Durham, England">Durham</a>, Lee was a youth team player with <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>, making his debut for the <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="West Ham United F.C. Reserves and Academy">reserve team</a> in 2011 before signing his first professional contract in August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reserves_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-reserves-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> His first appearance in a first-team squad was on 1 January 2013 when he was an unused <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> game against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Lee then made his professional debut for West Ham, as a 78th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Vaz_T%C3%AA" title="Ricardo Vaz Tê">Ricardo Vaz Tê</a>, on 16 January 2013 in a 1–0 defeat in the FA Cup 3rd Round replay at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 August 2013, Lee scored two first half goals for the <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="West Ham United F.C. Reserves and Academy">West Ham development squad</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_Under-21s_and_Academy" title="Aston Villa Under-21s and Academy">Aston Villa Under-21s</a>. On 31 August he made his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut for West Ham, coming on as an 88th-minute substitute for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohammed_Diam%C3%A9" title="Mohammed Diamé">Mohammed Diamé</a> in a 1–0 home defeat by <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Three weeks later, Lee scored a hat-trick against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Newcastle United F.C. Reserves and Academy">Newcastle Reserves</a> in a 5–4 away win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He went on to score 11 games that season for the development squad.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Colchester_United">Loan to Colchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loan to Colchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 22 October 2013 he signed for <a href="/wiki/Colchester_United_F.C." title="Colchester United F.C.">Colchester United</a> on a one-month youth loan. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His only Colchester goal was scored on 2 November in an away draw against <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a>: in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stoppage_time" title="Stoppage time">stoppage time</a> in the second half, with Rotherham winning 2–1, Lee scored with a header which hit the bar and bounced over the line to make the final score 2–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He returned to West Ham on 21 November after four appearances and one goal for Colchester United.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Southend_United">Loan to Southend United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Loan to Southend United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 31 October 2014 Lee joined <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a> on a three-month loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Within hours of signing for Southend, Lee suffered a <a href="/wiki/Hamstring" title="Hamstring">hamstring</a> injury and returned to West Ham for treatment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 5 November Lee was ruled out of playing for at least two months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> His loan was cancelled on 6 November and he returned to West Ham United, his parent club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Luton_Town">Loan to Luton Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Loan to Luton Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 19 February 2015, Lee joined <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a> on loan for one month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Lee chose to wear the number 38 shirt in memory of his former team-mate at West Ham, <a href="/wiki/Dylan_Tombides" title="Dylan Tombides">Dylan Tombides</a> who also wore number 38.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dt38_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-dt38-19">[19]</a></sup> He scored on his debut for Luton two days later, four minutes after coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Andy_Drury" title="Andy Drury">Andy Drury</a> in a 3–2 defeat away to <a href="/wiki/AFC_Wimbledon" title="AFC Wimbledon">AFC Wimbledon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 24 March 2015, Lee scored twice in a 2–3 home defeat by <a href="/wiki/Wycombe_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wycombe Wanderers F.C.">Wycombe Wanderers</a>. On scoring he revealed a T-shirt showing a picture of Tombides, to whom he dedicated his goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The following day his loan contract with Luton was extended until the end of the 2014–15 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-all201415_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-all201415-22">[22]</a></sup> Luton manager <a href="/wiki/John_Still_(footballer)" title="John Still (footballer)">John Still</a> said of Lee "Elliot's been fantastic for us and we're thrilled to have him for the rest of this season."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-all201415_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-all201415-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_West_Ham">Return to West Ham</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to West Ham">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Lee scored another hat-trick for the development squad when they faced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_Premier" title="Conference Premier">Conference Premier</a> side <a href="/wiki/Woking_F.C." title="Woking F.C.">Woking</a> in a pre-season friendly on 5 August 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Lee was an unused substitute in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shoot-out</a> defeat by <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> in the second round on 26 August 2014. He was given the number 36 for the first team the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On his first start for West Ham, on 9 July 2015, Lee scored his first goal for the club. In a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Europa_League" title="Europa League">Europa League</a> first qualifying round game away against <a href="/wiki/FC_Lusitanos" title="FC Lusitanos">FC Lusitanos</a> of Andorra, he scored the only goal from a cross from <a href="/wiki/Matt_Jarvis" title="Matt Jarvis">Matt Jarvis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Blackpool">Loan to Blackpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Loan to Blackpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 26 November 2015, Lee was loaned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> club <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> until 3 January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He made his debut two days later, as a half-time substitute for <a href="/wiki/Bright_Osayi-Samuel" title="Bright Osayi-Samuel">Bright Osayi-Samuel</a> in a 1–0 win over local rivals <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Town" title="Fleetwood Town">Fleetwood Town</a> at <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield_Road" title="Bloomfield Road">Bloomfield Road</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In January 2016, having picked-up an injury, Lee refused the offer of an extension to his loan and returned to West Ham. His refusal angered Blackpool manager, <a href="/wiki/Neil_McDonald_(footballer)" title="Neil McDonald (footballer)">Neil McDonald</a>, who was quoted as saying that Lee "would rather be at home with his mum and dad” than playing for Blackpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mum_and_Dad_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mum_and_Dad-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_loan_to_Colchester_United">Second loan to Colchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Second loan to Colchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 18 January 2016, Lee signed on loan for Colchester United until the end of the 2015–16 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He scored his first goal of his second spell on 12 March in a 3–3 draw with <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barnsley">Barnsley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Barnsley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 June 2016, Lee signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is the son of former <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> midfielder <a href="/wiki/Rob_Lee" title="Rob Lee">Rob Lee</a>. His older brother, <a href="/wiki/Olly_Lee" title="Olly Lee">Olly</a>, formerly with West Ham, now plays for Luton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Brentwood_School,_Essex" title="Brentwood School, Essex">Brentwood School</a> in Essex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
