<p><b>Elizabeth Egan</b> "<b>Liz</b>" <b>Gillies</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brantley_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-brantley-1">[1]</a></sup> (born July 26, 1993<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crushable.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-crushable.com-2">[2]</a></sup>) is an American actress and singer. She appeared in several television series and commercials, before acquiring her first significant role prior to her 17th birthday, playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jade_West" title="Jade West">Jade West</a> on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Victorious" title="Victorious">Victorious</a></i>. She made her <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut at age 15 in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/13_(musical)" title="13 (musical)">13</a></i>, playing the character of Lucy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13_Original_Cast_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-13_Original_Cast-3">[3]</a></sup> She had a main role in FX's <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock 'n' roll</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Sex%26Drugs%26Rock%26Roll" title="Sex&amp;Drugs&amp;Rock&amp;Roll">Sex&amp;Drugs&amp;Rock&amp;Roll</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Gillies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_beginnings_.281993.E2.80.932007.29">Early life and career beginnings (1993–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Gillies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career beginnings (1993–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Liz was born in <a href="/wiki/Haworth,_New_Jersey" title="Haworth, New Jersey">Haworth, New Jersey</a> to Dave and Lorrie Gillies. She has one younger brother born 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She is of Irish and Italian descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crushable.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crushable.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gillies' acting career began at age twelve, when she started going to local open casting calls. She quickly began appearing in commercials for companies such as <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Mobile" title="Virgin Mobile">Virgin Mobile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile2011_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Profile2011-6">[6]</a></sup> Her first television role was as a recurring character in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Donnellys" title="The Black Donnellys">The Black Donnellys</a></i>. She has stated that she was not allowed to watch the finished show in its entirety because her parents felt it was not appropriate for her at the time. She appeared in three episodes. In 2007, she played small roles in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Clique_(film)" title="The Clique (film)">The Clique</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harold_(film)" title="Harold (film)">Harold</a></i>, and <i>Locker 514</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2008, Gillies was cast as Lucy in a Godspeed production of <a href="/wiki/Jason_Robert_Brown" title="Jason Robert Brown">Jason Robert Brown</a>'s new musical <i><a href="/wiki/13_(musical)" title="13 (musical)">13</a></i>, alongside her future <i>Victorious</i> co-star, <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a>. Later that year, <i>13</i> moved to <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a>, which made it the first Broadway production to have a cast and band entirely made up of teenagers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Gillies remained with the production until its closure on January 4, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_with_Nickelodeon_.282010.E2.80.932013.29">Involvement with Nickelodeon (2010–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Gillies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Involvement with Nickelodeon (2010–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Gillies was cast as bad girl and occasional antagonist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jade_West" title="Jade West">Jade West</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> television show <i><a href="/wiki/Victorious" title="Victorious">Victorious</a></i>, a sitcom revolving around teenagers at a performing arts-based high school in Hollywood. This marked her second time working alongside <i>13</i> co-star Ariana Grande. Regarding her <i>Victorious</i> character, Gillies said "It’s wonderful. I love playing Jade. I always say she’s not so much the “mean” girl, but the “bad” girl in so many ways. She has a lot of human qualities to her — she’s not just completely sociopathic. She’s sweet with her boyfriend. It’s nice to play a character with some depth."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The show premiered on March 27, 2010, and ended on February 2, 2013 after four seasons. Gillies reprised her role as Jade in a special episode of the <i><a href="/wiki/ICarly" title="ICarly">iCarly</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Victorious" title="Victorious">Victorious</a></i> spinoff series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_and_Cat" title="Sam and Cat">Sam and Cat</a></i>. The episode aired in 2014.</p>
<p>During her time on <i>Victorious</i>, Gillies lent her talents to other Nickelodeon shows, voicing the character of Daphne on the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Winx_Club" title="Winx Club">Winx Club</a></i> and recording Winx Club's official song, "We Are Believix." She also appeared in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Time_Rush" title="Big Time Rush">Big Time Rush</a></i> and as a contestant on game shows <i><a href="/wiki/BrainSurge" title="BrainSurge">BrainSurge</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Figure_It_Out" title="Figure It Out">Figure It Out</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_appearances_and_movie_roles_.282012.E2.80.932015.29">Other appearances and movie roles (2012–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Gillies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other appearances and movie roles (2012–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning in 2012, Gillies began playing small guest roles outside of Nickelodeon. Notable appearances include <i><a href="/wiki/White_Collar_(TV_series)" title="White Collar (TV series)">White Collar</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Exes" title="The Exes">The Exes</a></i>.</p>
<p>Following the cancellation of <i>Victorious</i>, Gillies took some time off from acting. In 2013, she was cast as Courtney in the musical adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Jawbreaker_(film)" title="Jawbreaker (film)"><i>Jawbreaker</i></a> and participated in a reading of the show in Manhattan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The musical did not end up being taken to Broadway.</p>
<p>In 2014, Gillies appeared in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_(2014_film)" title="Animal (2014 film)">Animal</a></i> as Mandy and the Lifetime movie <i>Killing Daddy</i>, playing Callie Ross. <i>Animal</i> was filmed in the summer of 2013 in <a href="/wiki/Manchester,_Connecticut" title="Manchester, Connecticut">Manchester, Connecticut</a> and was produced by <a href="/wiki/Drew_Barrymore" title="Drew Barrymore">Drew Barrymore</a>. The film also features Gillies' former <i>Winx Club</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Keke_Palmer" title="Keke Palmer">Keke Palmer</a> and was released on iTunes June 17, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Killing Daddy</i>, a Lifetime original movie,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> stars Gillies as Calista "Callie" Ross, a troubled young woman who returns to her estranged family with a plan for her recently disabled father and her inheritance. The movie aired on Lifetime on July 5, 2014.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vacation_and_Sex.26Drugs.26Rock.26Roll_.282015.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Vacation</i> and <i>Sex&amp;Drugs&amp;Rock&amp;Roll</i> (2015–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Gillies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vacation and Sex&amp;Drugs&amp;Rock&amp;Roll (2015–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, Gillies was cast as Heather in the remake of <i><a href="/wiki/Vacation_(2015_film)" title="Vacation (2015 film)">Vacation</a></i>. The film received a wide release on July 29, 2015.</p>
<p>Also in 2015, Gillies was cast in the comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Sex%26Drugs%26Rock%26Roll" title="Sex&amp;Drugs&amp;Rock&amp;Roll">Sex&amp;Drugs&amp;Rock&amp;Roll</a></i> as Gigi, an aspiring singer and daughter of a former rock star named Johnny Rock (portrayed by the show's creator <a href="/wiki/Denis_Leary" title="Denis Leary">Denis Leary</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The show premiered July 16, 2015 to mixed reviews, but Gillies' performance as Gigi was well-received. The show ran for two seasons, with the second season finale airing on September 1, 2016. On September 9, 2016, FX declined to renew the show for a third season, effectively canceling the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Gillies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gillies was featured in several songs on the soundtracks <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victorious_(soundtrack)" title="Victorious (soundtrack)">Victorious (soundtrack)</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victorious_2.0" title="Victorious 2.0">Victorious 2.0</a>,</i> including "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Give_It_Up_(Victorious_song)" title="Give It Up (Victorious song)">Give It Up</a>" (duet with Ariana Grande), and "Take a Hint" (duet with Victoria Justice). She also wrote and recorded the song "You Don't Know Me" for an episode of <i>Victorious</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and it was later featured in <i>Victorious 3.0</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Victorious_3.0_Track_List_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Victorious_3.0_Track_List-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liz_Gillies_Tweet_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Liz_Gillies_Tweet-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gillies has a YouTube account called LizGilliesOfficial, where she uploads covers of popular songs including: "<a href="/wiki/Wild_Horses_(The_Rolling_Stones_song)" title="Wild Horses (The Rolling Stones song)">Wild Horses</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, "<a href="/wiki/You_and_I_(Lady_Gaga_song)" title="You and I (Lady Gaga song)">You and I</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>, "<a href="/wiki/For_No_One" title="For No One">For No One</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Jealous_Guy" title="Jealous Guy">Jealous Guy</a>" by <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Father_and_Son_(song)" title="Father and Son (song)">Father and Son</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Cat_Stevens" title="Cat Stevens">Cat Stevens</a> and "One and Only" by <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> She has also covered songs with <a href="/wiki/Max_Schneider" title="Max Schneider">Max Schneider</a> ("<a href="/wiki/Somewhere_Only_We_Know" title="Somewhere Only We Know">Somewhere Only We Know</a>") and <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a> ("<a href="/wiki/The_Christmas_Song" title="The Christmas Song">The Christmas Song</a>").</p>
<p>In July 2012, it was reported that Gillies was working on an alternative rock album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 10, 2013, Gillies recorded a duet with <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a> called "Santa Baby" for Grande's Christmas EP <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_Kisses_EP" title="Christmas Kisses EP">Christmas Kisses</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
