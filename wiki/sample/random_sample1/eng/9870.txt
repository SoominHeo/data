<p><b>Élisabeth Depardieu</b> (née <b>Élisabeth Dominique Lucie Guignot</b>; born 5 August 1941)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jorf20080323_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jorf20080323-1">[1]</a></sup> is a French actress, writer, co-producer, ex-wife of actor <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Depardieu" title="Gérard Depardieu">Gérard Depardieu</a> and mother of actors <a href="/wiki/Guillaume_Depardieu" title="Guillaume Depardieu">Guillaume</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julie_Depardieu" title="Julie Depardieu">Julie</a>.</p>

<p><b>Élisabeth Dominique Lucie Guignot</b> was born to a well-off Parisian family and married <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Depardieu" title="Gérard Depardieu">Gérard Depardieu</a> on 19 February 1970. They had two children together, actors Guillaume Depardieu and Julie Depardieu. The couple divorced in 1996.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89lisabeth_Depardieu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Élisabeth appeared in two movies with then-husband Gérard Depardieu: <i><a href="/wiki/Jean_de_Florette" title="Jean de Florette">Jean de Florette</a></i> (1986), in which they played husband and wife, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Tartuffe" title="Le Tartuffe">Le Tartuffe</a></i> (1984). She participated at the 19th <a href="/wiki/Brest_European_Short_Film_Festival" title="Brest European Short Film Festival">Brest European Short Film Festival</a> in 2004 as a member of the jury.</p>
<p>She starred in the film <i>Chevalier</i> (Knight of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%A9gion_d%27honneur" title="Légion d'honneur">Légion d'honneur</a>) in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jorf20080323_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jorf20080323-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
