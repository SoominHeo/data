<p><b>Elizabeth Lee McGovern</b> (born July 18, 1961) is an American film, television, and theater actor, and musician. She received an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Award</a> nomination for her role as <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Nesbit" title="Evelyn Nesbit">Evelyn Nesbit</a> in the 1981 film <i><a href="/wiki/Ragtime_(film)" title="Ragtime (film)">Ragtime</a></i>. She is also known for her performance as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Downton_Abbey_characters#Cora_Crawley.2C_Countess_of_Grantham" title="List of Downton Abbey characters">Cora Crawley, Countess of Grantham</a> in the British drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Downton_Abbey" title="Downton Abbey">Downton Abbey</a></i>, for which she has been nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a>. Her other films include <i><a href="/wiki/Ordinary_People" title="Ordinary People">Ordinary People</a></i> (1980), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Once_Upon_A_Time_In_America" title="Once Upon A Time In America">Once Upon A Time In America</a></i> (1984), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Handmaid%27s_Tale_(film)" title="The Handmaid's Tale (film)">The Handmaid's Tale</a></i> (1990) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wings_of_the_Dove_(1997_film)" title="The Wings of the Dove (1997 film)">The Wings of the Dove</a></i> (1997).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_McGovern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McGovern was born in <a href="/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois" title="Evanston, Illinois">Evanston, Illinois</a>, the daughter of Katharine Wolcott (née Watts), a high school teacher, and William Montgomery McGovern, Jr., a university professor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her paternal grandfather was adventurer <a href="/wiki/William_Montgomery_McGovern" title="William Montgomery McGovern">William Montgomery McGovern</a>, her maternal great-grandfathers were U.S. diplomat <a href="/wiki/Ethelbert_Watts" title="Ethelbert Watts">Ethelbert Watts</a> and Admiral <a href="/wiki/Charles_P._Snyder_(admiral)" title="Charles P. Snyder (admiral)">Charles P. Snyder</a>, and her maternal great-great-grandfather was Congressman <a href="/wiki/Charles_P._Snyder" title="Charles P. Snyder">Charles P. Snyder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The McGovern family moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles</a>, where her father accepted a teaching position with the law school at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCLA" title="UCLA">UCLA</a>. Her younger sister is the novelist <a href="/wiki/Cammie_McGovern" title="Cammie McGovern">Cammie McGovern</a>.</p>
<p>Agent Joan Scott saw her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Skin_of_Our_Teeth" title="The Skin of Our Teeth">The Skin of Our Teeth</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Thornton_Wilder" title="Thornton Wilder">Thornton Wilder</a>, was impressed by her talent, and recommended she take acting lessons.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> McGovern studied at the <a href="/wiki/American_Conservatory_Theater" title="American Conservatory Theater">American Conservatory Theater</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, then at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Juilliard_School" title="The Juilliard School">The Juilliard School</a> in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_McGovern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1980, while studying at Juilliard, McGovern was offered a part in what became her first film, <i><a href="/wiki/Ordinary_People" title="Ordinary People">Ordinary People</a></i>, in which she played the girlfriend of troubled teenager Conrad Jarrett (<a href="/wiki/Timothy_Hutton" title="Timothy Hutton">Timothy Hutton</a>). The following year she completed her acting education at the American Conservatory Theatre and Juilliard, and began to appear in plays, first <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> and later in famous theaters.</p>
<p>In 1981 she earned an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Best Supporting Actress</a> for her role as <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Nesbit" title="Evelyn Nesbit">Evelyn Nesbit</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Ragtime_(film)" title="Ragtime (film)">Ragtime</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heawood_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heawood-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1984, she starred in <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Leone" title="Sergio Leone">Sergio Leone</a>'s gangster epic <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_America" title="Once Upon a Time in America">Once Upon a Time in America</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Robert_De_Niro" title="Robert De Niro">Robert De Niro</a>'s romantic interest Deborah Gelly. She had leading roles in two other films that year, <i><a href="/wiki/Racing_with_the_Moon" title="Racing with the Moon">Racing with the Moon</a></i>, a coming-of-age story also starring <a href="/wiki/Sean_Penn" title="Sean Penn">Sean Penn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Cage" title="Nicolas Cage">Nicolas Cage</a>, and the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Lovesick_(1983_film)" title="Lovesick (1983 film)">Lovesick</a></i>, as a patient whose psychiatrist (<a href="/wiki/Dudley_Moore" title="Dudley Moore">Dudley Moore</a>) falls in love with her, risking his practice.</p>
<p>In 1989, she played <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Rourke" title="Mickey Rourke">Mickey Rourke</a>'s girlfriend in <i><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Handsome" title="Johnny Handsome">Johnny Handsome</a></i>, directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walter_Hill_(filmmaker)" title="Walter Hill (filmmaker)">Walter Hill</a>, and the same year she appeared as a rebellious <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a> in <a href="/wiki/Volker_Schl%C3%B6ndorff" title="Volker Schlöndorff">Volker Schlöndorff</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Handmaid%27s_Tale_(film)" title="The Handmaid's Tale (film)">The Handmaid's Tale</a></i>.</p>
<p>McGovern co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Bacon" title="Kevin Bacon">Kevin Bacon</a> in a romantic comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/She%27s_Having_a_Baby" title="She's Having a Baby">She's Having a Baby</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/John_Hughes_(filmmaker)" title="John Hughes (filmmaker)">John Hughes</a>, and starred in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bedroom_Window" title="The Bedroom Window">The Bedroom Window</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Hanson" title="Curtis Hanson">Curtis Hanson</a>. She teamed with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a> in 1990's <i><a href="/wiki/A_Shock_to_the_System" title="A Shock to the System">A Shock to the System</a></i>, a comic mystery about a man who plots the murder of his wife.</p>
<p>In a 1994 comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Favor" title="The Favor">The Favor</a></i>, McGovern played a woman who cheats on her boyfriend (played by <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad Pitt</a>) by becoming her married best friend's proxy in a tryst with a man the friend has fantasized about.</p>
<p>McGovern appeared in a number of films in the 2000s, including <i><a href="/wiki/Woman_in_Gold_(film)" title="Woman in Gold (film)">Woman in Gold</a></i>, a drama starring <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_McGovern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McGovern has appeared in several television productions, mostly in the UK. In 1999 and 2000 McGovern played Marguerite St. Just in a <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> television series loosely based on the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scarlet_Pimpernel" title="The Scarlet Pimpernel">The Scarlet Pimpernel</a></i>.</p>
<p>On American TV, she appeared in a 2006 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> titled "Harm," in which her character of Dr. Faith Sutton was a psychiatrist accused of complicity in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detainee_abuse" title="Detainee abuse">detainee abuse</a>. Her other television work includes <i>Broken Glass</i> (<a href="/wiki/Arthur_Miller" title="Arthur Miller">Arthur Miller</a>, 1996); <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_from_the_Crypt_(TV_series)" title="Tales from the Crypt (TV series)">Tales from the Crypt</a></i>; <i><a href="/wiki/The_Changeling_(play)" title="The Changeling (play)">The Changeling</a></i>; <i>Tales from Hollywood</i>; the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i>Men and Women</i>; <i>The Man in the Brooks Brothers Shirt</i>; <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shelley_Duvall%27s_Faerie_Tale_Theatre" title="Shelley Duvall's Faerie Tale Theatre">Shelley Duvall's Faerie Tale Theatre</a></i> ("Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs"); and <i><a href="/wiki/If_Not_for_You_(TV_series)" title="If Not for You (TV series)">If Not for You</a></i> (CBS 1995, own series).</p>
<p>In May 2007, she played Ellen Doubleday, <a href="/wiki/Daphne_du_Maurier" title="Daphne du Maurier">Daphne du Maurier</a>'s paramour, in <i>Daphne</i>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC2" title="BBC2">BBC2</a> television drama by <a href="/wiki/Amy_Jenkins" title="Amy Jenkins">Amy Jenkins</a> based on <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Forster" title="Margaret Forster">Margaret Forster</a>'s biography of the author.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2008, McGovern appeared as Dame Celia Westholme in "Appointment with Death", an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Agatha_Christie%27s_Poirot" title="Agatha Christie's Poirot">Agatha Christie's Poirot</a></i>. In the same year, she appeared in the three-part BBC comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Freezing_(TV_series)" title="Freezing (TV series)">Freezing</a></i>, written by James Wood and directed and co-produced by her husband Simon Curtis. First broadcast on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a>, it was also shown on BBC2 in February 2008. McGovern played an American expatriate actress named Elizabeth, living in <a href="/wiki/Chiswick" title="Chiswick">Chiswick</a> with her publisher husband, played by <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Bonneville" title="Hugh Bonneville">Hugh Bonneville</a>, and co-starring <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hollander" title="Tom Hollander">Tom Hollander</a> as her theatrical agent.</p>
<p>From 2010 to 2015, she portrayed <a href="/wiki/List_of_Downton_Abbey_characters#Cora_Crawley.2C_Countess_of_Grantham" title="List of Downton Abbey characters">Cora, Countess of Grantham</a>, wife of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Downton_Abbey_characters#Robert_Crawley.2C_Earl_of_Grantham" title="List of Downton Abbey characters">Robert, the Earl of Grantham</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Bonneville" title="Hugh Bonneville">Hugh Bonneville</a>) in the British TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Downton_Abbey" title="Downton Abbey">Downton Abbey</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_McGovern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McGovern is also a singer-songwriter. In 2008 she began fronting the band Sadie and the Hotheads at The Castle pub venue in <a href="/wiki/Portobello_Road" title="Portobello Road">Portobello Road</a>, London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The band released an album of songs she developed with The Nelson Brothers, who are now part of the band. The album also includes Ron Knights on bass and Rowan Oliver, borrowed from <a href="/wiki/Goldfrapp" title="Goldfrapp">Goldfrapp</a>, as drummer for the recording sessions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heawood_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Heawood-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Dockery" title="Michelle Dockery">Michelle Dockery</a>, who plays McGovern's eldest daughter in <i>Downton Abbey</i>, has occasionally sung with the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_McGovern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When she was 23, McGovern was engaged to actor <a href="/wiki/Sean_Penn" title="Sean Penn">Sean Penn</a> whom she met on the set of <i><a href="/wiki/Racing_with_the_Moon" title="Racing with the Moon">Racing with the Moon</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1992, she married British film director and producer <a href="/wiki/Simon_Curtis_(filmmaker)" title="Simon Curtis (filmmaker)">Simon Curtis</a>; the couple have two daughters, Matilda and Grace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
