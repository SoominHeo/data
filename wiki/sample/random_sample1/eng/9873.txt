<p><b>Elizabeth Banks</b> (born <b>Elizabeth Irene Mitchell</b>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> February 10, 1974)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> is an American actress, director, and producer. Banks made her film debut in the low-budget independent film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surrender_Dorothy_(1998_film)" title="Surrender Dorothy (1998 film)">Surrender Dorothy</a></i> (1998), and is known for her roles in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/Wet_Hot_American_Summer" title="Wet Hot American Summer">Wet Hot American Summer</a></i> (2001), Sam Raimi's <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_in_film" title="Spider-Man in film"><i>Spider-Man</i> trilogy</a> (2002–2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Seabiscuit_(film)" title="Seabiscuit (film)">Seabiscuit</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/The_40-Year-Old_Virgin" title="The 40-Year-Old Virgin">The 40-Year-Old Virgin</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Zack_and_Miri_Make_a_Porno" title="Zack and Miri Make a Porno">Zack and Miri Make a Porno</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Role_Models" title="Role Models">Role Models</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Next_Three_Days" title="The Next Three Days">The Next Three Days</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect" title="Pitch Perfect">Pitch Perfect</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lego_Movie" title="The Lego Movie">The Lego Movie</a></i> (2014), and <a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film_series)" title="The Hunger Games (film series)"><i>The Hunger Games</i> film series</a> (2012–2015). In 2014, she portrayed <a href="/wiki/Melinda_Ledbetter" title="Melinda Ledbetter">Melinda Ledbetter</a>, the girlfriend and later wife of <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_Boys" title="The Beach Boys">the Beach Boys</a> co-founder <a href="/wiki/Brian_Wilson" title="Brian Wilson">Brian Wilson</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Bill_Pohlad" title="Bill Pohlad">Bill Pohlad</a>'s film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_%26_Mercy" title="Love &amp; Mercy">Love &amp; Mercy</a></i> and made her directorial debut with <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_2" title="Pitch Perfect 2">Pitch Perfect 2</a></i>, whose $69M opening weekend gross set a record for a first-time director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On television, Banks had a <a href="/wiki/Recurring_character" title="Recurring character">recurring role</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avery_Jessup" title="Avery Jessup">Avery Jessup</a> on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/30_Rock" title="30 Rock">30 Rock</a></i>, which garnered her two <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations. She also had recurring roles on the comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(TV_series)" title="Scrubs (TV series)">Scrubs</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Family" title="Modern Family">Modern Family</a></i>, the latter of which earned her a Primetime Emmy Award nomination. In 2015, she reprised her role as Lindsay in <i>Wet Hot American Summer</i> for the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Wet_Hot_American_Summer:_First_Day_of_Camp" title="Wet Hot American Summer: First Day of Camp">Wet Hot American Summer: First Day of Camp</a></i>.</p>
<p>As of November 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Banks&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Box_Office_Mojo" title="Box Office Mojo">Box Office Mojo</a> ranked Banks as the 32nd highest-grossing actor of all time, and the sixth highest-grossing female actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Banks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banks was born in <a href="/wiki/Pittsfield,_Massachusetts" title="Pittsfield, Massachusetts">Pittsfield, Massachusetts</a> and grew up on Brown Street, the eldest of four children of Ann (née Wallace) and Mark P. Mitchell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Her father, a Vietnam veteran, was a factory worker for <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> and her mother worked in a bank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She has said that she grew up "Irish + WASP + Catholic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Growing up, Banks played baseball and rode horses. She was in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little_League" title="Little League">Little League</a> when she broke her leg sliding into third. She then tried out for the school play, which was her start in acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She graduated from <a href="/wiki/Pittsfield_High_School_(Massachusetts)" title="Pittsfield High School (Massachusetts)">Pittsfield High School</a> in 1992, and is a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Junior_Classical_League" title="Massachusetts Junior Classical League">Massachusetts Junior Classical League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a>, where she was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Delta_Delta_Delta" title="Delta Delta Delta">Delta Delta Delta</a> Sorority. She graduated <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magna_cum_laude" title="Magna cum laude">magna cum laude</a></i> in 1996 with a major in communications and a minor in theater arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1998, she completed schooling at the <a href="/wiki/American_Conservatory_Theater" title="American Conservatory Theater">American Conservatory Theater</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, where she earned an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">MFA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Banks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banks changed her name to avoid confusion with actress <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Mitchell" title="Elizabeth Mitchell">Elizabeth Mitchell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> After auditioning in New York, she was offered a role on the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara_(TV_series)" title="Santa Barbara (TV series)">Santa Barbara</a></i>. Taking the role would have required her to quit her education at the American Conservatory Theatre, and Banks ultimately decided to forgo the offer due to having taken out <a href="/wiki/Student_loan" title="Student loan">student loans</a> to complete her degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> She made her acting debut in the 1998 independent film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surrender_Dorothy_(1998_film)" title="Surrender Dorothy (1998 film)">Surrender Dorothy</a></i>, as Elizabeth Casey, and appeared in various films over the next seven years including <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Swept_Away_(2002_film)" title="Swept Away (2002 film)">Swept Away</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> before gaining more prominent widespread exposure through the 2005 comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/The_40-Year-Old_Virgin" title="The 40-Year-Old Virgin">The 40-Year-Old Virgin</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2005, at the <a href="/wiki/Williamstown_Theatre_Festival" title="Williamstown Theatre Festival">Williamstown Theatre Festival</a>, Banks starred in <a href="/wiki/William_Inge" title="William Inge">William Inge</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bus_Stop_(play)" title="Bus Stop (play)">Bus Stop</a></i> as Cherie, the sexy, blonde, aspiring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nightclub_singer" title="Nightclub singer">nightclub singer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Jeffrey Borak wrote that Banks' portrayal was acted "with poise, clarity and a shrewd feel for Cherie's complexities. Her performance is all of a piece and in harmony, stylistically, with the performances around her..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2005, she appeared on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Stella_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Stella (U.S. TV series)">Stella</a></i>, and in May 2006, she had a role in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Urologist" title="My Urologist">season five finale</a> of the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(TV_series)" title="Scrubs (TV series)">Scrubs</a></i> as Dr. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Briggs" title="Kim Briggs">Kim Briggs</a>, the love interest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Dorian" title="John Dorian">J.D.</a> (<a href="/wiki/Zach_Braff" title="Zach Braff">Zach Braff</a>). The character appeared throughout seasons six, seven, and eight as a recurring guest star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, Banks appeared in the American football drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Invincible_(2006_film)" title="Invincible (2006 film)">Invincible</a></i>, in which she played <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wahlberg" title="Mark Wahlberg">Mark Wahlberg</a>'s love interest. Later, she and co-star Wahlberg were nominated for the "Best Kiss" award at the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Movie_Awards" title="MTV Movie Awards">MTV Movie Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-banks_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-banks-23">[23]</a></sup> That same year, she landed the starring role in the comedy-horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Slither_(2006_film)" title="Slither (2006 film)">Slither</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2007, Banks played the female lead in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Meet_Bill" title="Meet Bill">Meet Bill</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Eckhart" title="Aaron Eckhart">Aaron Eckhart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Alba" title="Jessica Alba">Jessica Alba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio-22">[22]</a></sup> That same year, she had a small role in the Christmas comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Fred_Claus" title="Fred Claus">Fred Claus</a></i>, co-starring <a href="/wiki/Vince_Vaughn" title="Vince Vaughn">Vince Vaughn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Giamatti" title="Paul Giamatti">Paul Giamatti</a> as Santa's little helper, Charlyne. In 2008, she played the ex-wife and mother of the daughter of Ryan Reynolds lead in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Definitely,_Maybe" title="Definitely, Maybe">Definitely, Maybe</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Isla_Fisher" title="Isla Fisher">Isla Fisher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Reynolds" title="Ryan Reynolds">Ryan Reynolds</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> starred with <a href="/wiki/Seth_Rogen" title="Seth Rogen">Seth Rogen</a> as the eponymous female lead in the <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Smith" title="Kevin Smith">Kevin Smith</a> comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Zack_and_Miri_Make_a_Porno" title="Zack and Miri Make a Porno">Zack and Miri Make a Porno</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-bio-22">[22]</a></sup> and played United States First Lady <a href="/wiki/Laura_Bush" title="Laura Bush">Laura Bush</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/W._(film)" title="W. (film)">W.</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biopic" title="Biopic">biopic</a> of <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Banks appeared in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Uninvited_(2009_film)" title="The Uninvited (2009 film)">The Uninvited</a></i>, a remake of the <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Tale_of_Two_Sisters" title="A Tale of Two Sisters">A Tale of Two Sisters</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The film was about an intrusive stepmother who makes life miserable for the teen daughters of her new husband. Banks based her character, Rachel, on <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_De_Mornay" title="Rebecca De Mornay">Rebecca De Mornay</a>'s character in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hand_That_Rocks_the_Cradle_(film)" title="The Hand That Rocks the Cradle (film)">The Hand That Rocks the Cradle</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sg-eb_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-sg-eb-28">[28]</a></sup> "It was very important to me that every line reading I gave could be interpreted two ways," says Banks of her role, "So that when you go back through the movie you can see that".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sg-eb_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-sg-eb-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banks is a frequent co-star of actor <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rudd" title="Paul Rudd">Paul Rudd</a>, the two having appeared in five films together to date (<i><a href="/wiki/Wet_Hot_American_Summer" title="Wet Hot American Summer">Wet Hot American Summer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Baxter" title="The Baxter">The Baxter</a></i>, <i>The 40-Year-Old Virgin</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Role_Models" title="Role Models">Role Models</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Idiot_Brother" title="Our Idiot Brother">Our Idiot Brother</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She is also a frequent co-star of actor <a href="/wiki/Tobey_Maguire" title="Tobey Maguire">Tobey Maguire</a>, the two having also appeared in five films together (<i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_(2002_film)" title="Spider-Man (2002 film)">Spider-Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Seabiscuit_(film)" title="Seabiscuit (film)">Seabiscuit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2" title="Spider-Man 2">Spider-Man 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3">Spider-Man 3</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Details_(film)" title="The Details (film)">The Details</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banks was cast as a love interest for <a href="/wiki/Jack_Donaghy" title="Jack Donaghy">Jack Donaghy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alec_Baldwin" title="Alec Baldwin">Alec Baldwin</a>) in the fourth season of the <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>-winning sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/30_Rock" title="30 Rock">30 Rock</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Intended to appear in four episodes in 2010, Banks went on to become a recurring character with 13 appearances by the end of the fifth season, including her marriage in the episode <i><a href="/wiki/Mrs._Donaghy" title="Mrs. Donaghy">Mrs. Donaghy</a></i>. Her performance in season five earned her a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Guest_Actress_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series</a> for the <a href="/wiki/63rd_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="63rd Primetime Emmy Awards">63rd Primetime Emmy Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emmys_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-emmys-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banks appeared in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film)" title="The Hunger Games (film)">The Hunger Games</a></i> (2012), playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Characters_in_the_Hunger_Games_trilogy#Effie_Trinket" title="List of Characters in the Hunger Games trilogy">Effie Trinket</a>, a woman from "The Capitol" who escorts the District 12 tributes to the Hunger Games. She reprised the role in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Catching_Fire" title="The Hunger Games: Catching Fire">The Hunger Games: Catching Fire</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_%E2%80%93_Part_1" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1</a></i> (2014), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Mockingjay_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 2">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 2</a></i> (2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-bio-22">[22]</a></sup> Banks co-starred in the 2014 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Every_Secret_Thing_(film)" title="Every Secret Thing (film)">Every Secret Thing</a></i>, playing Detective Nancy Porter investigating the disappearance of a young child with similarities to a case she had previously been involved with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Banks was recognized by <i>Elle</i> Magazine during The Women in Hollywood Awards, honoring women for their outstanding achievements in film, spanning all aspects of the motion picture industry, including acting, directing, and producing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banks portrayed <a href="/wiki/Brian_Wilson#Personal_life" title="Brian Wilson">Melinda Ledbetter Wilson</a>, the wife of <a href="/wiki/Brian_Wilson" title="Brian Wilson">Brian Wilson</a>, in the 2015 biopic <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_and_Mercy_(film)" title="Love and Mercy (film)">Love and Mercy</a></i>, which is based on the life of the legendary musician and founding member of <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_Boys" title="The Beach Boys">The Beach Boys</a>, as portrayed by <a href="/wiki/John_Cusack" title="John Cusack">John Cusack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> After producing and appearing, as Gail, in the first <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect" title="Pitch Perfect">Pitch Perfect</a></i> film, Banks directed its sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_2" title="Pitch Perfect 2">Pitch Perfect 2</a></i> (2015), making her feature directorial debut. She also produced the film, and again played Gail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2015, she was named as a member of the Jury for the Main Competition at the <a href="/wiki/72nd_Venice_International_Film_Festival" title="72nd Venice International Film Festival">2015 Venice Film Festival</a>. The festival is chaired by <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Cuar%C3%B3n" title="Alfonso Cuarón">Alfonso Cuarón</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Also in 2015, Banks became a spokeswoman for <a href="/wiki/Realtor.com" title="Realtor.com">Realtor.com</a> in their series of television commercials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In February 2016, Banks was cast as <a href="/wiki/Rita_Repulsa" title="Rita Repulsa">Rita Repulsa</a> in the 2017 <a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers#Reboot_film" title="Power Rangers"><i>Power Rangers</i> reboot film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> As of mid-February 2016, Banks became the commercial face of <a href="/wiki/Old_Navy" title="Old Navy">Old Navy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Banks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banks met her husband, sportswriter and producer <a href="/wiki/Max_Handelman" title="Max Handelman">Max Handelman</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> on her first day at college on September 6, 1992. They were married in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> She and her husband had their first child, Felix, who was born through a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surrogate_mother" title="Surrogate mother">surrogate</a> in March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In November 2012, Banks announced the birth of another son, Magnus Mitchell, also by surrogate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banks went through parts of the process of <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">conversion to Judaism</a>, her husband's faith, and studied with rabbis. In 2013, speaking of her religion, she stated that she practices Judaism, though "I did not have my <i><a href="/wiki/Mikveh" title="Mikveh">mikveh</a></i>, so technically I'm not converted", but that she has "been essentially a Jew for like 15 years", adding "Frankly, because I'm already doing everything [practicing religious rituals], I feel like I'm as Jewish as I'm ever going to be".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Butnick_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Butnick-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banks was a vocal supporter of Hillary Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> involved in a rendition of <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Platten" title="Rachel Platten">Rachel Platten's</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Fight_Song_(Rachel_Platten_song)" title="Fight Song (Rachel Platten song)">Fight Song</a>" with a host of other celebrities at the Democratic National Convention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
