<p><b>Elizabeth Ann Perkins</b> (born November 18, 1960) is an American actress. Her film roles have included <i><a href="/wiki/Big_(film)" title="Big (film)">Big</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flintstones_(film)" title="The Flintstones (film)">The Flintstones</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Miracle_on_34th_Street_(1994_film)" title="Miracle on 34th Street (1994 film)">Miracle on 34th Street</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/About_Last_Night..." title="About Last Night...">About Last Night...</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Avalon_(1990_film)" title="Avalon (1990 film)">Avalon</a></i>. She is known for her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celia_Hodes" title="Celia Hodes">Celia Hodes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Weeds_(TV_series)" title="Weeds (TV series)">Weeds</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Perkins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perkins was born in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, New York, the daughter of Jo Williams, a drug treatment counselor and concert pianist, and James Perkins, a farmer, writer, and businessman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her paternal grandparents were Greek emigrants from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salonika" title="Salonika">Salonika</a> who anglicized their surname from "Pisperikos" to "Perkins" when they moved to the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Perkins was raised in <a href="/wiki/Colrain,_Massachusetts" title="Colrain, Massachusetts">Colrain</a>, Massachusetts; her parents divorced in 1963.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPW_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPW-5">[5]</a></sup> She began working in theatre with Arena Civic Theatre, a non-profit community theatre group based in <a href="/wiki/Greenfield,_Massachusetts" title="Greenfield, Massachusetts">Greenfield, Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BG_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BG-6">[6]</a></sup> Perkins attended <a href="/wiki/Northfield_Mount_Hermon_School" title="Northfield Mount Hermon School">Northfield Mount Hermon School</a>, an elite preparatory school, and then spent 1978 to 1981 in Chicago attaining her BFA in Acting from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goodman_School_of_Drama" title="Goodman School of Drama">Goodman School of Drama</a> at <a href="/wiki/DePaul_University" title="DePaul University">DePaul University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPW_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-EPW-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1984, she made her theatrical debut on Broadway in <a href="/wiki/Neil_Simon" title="Neil Simon">Neil Simon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Brighton_Beach_Memoirs" title="Brighton Beach Memoirs">Brighton Beach Memoirs</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and afterward, worked in a number of ensemble companies, including The New York Shakespeare Festival and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steppenwolf_Theater" title="Steppenwolf Theater">Steppenwolf Theater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Perkins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was listed as one of the 12 "Promising New Actors of 1986" in John Willis' Screen World, and has since landed numerous film roles. Perkins made her film debut in 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Edward_Zwick" title="Edward Zwick">Edward Zwick</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/About_Last_Night..." title="About Last Night...">About Last Night...</a></i> and had a career breakthrough co-starring with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Big_(film)" title="Big (film)">Big</a></i>. She received critical acclaim for her performance in <a href="/wiki/Barry_Levinson" title="Barry Levinson">Barry Levinson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Avalon_(1990_film)" title="Avalon (1990 film)">Avalon</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DW_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-DW-9">[9]</a></sup> and was a standout opposite <a href="/wiki/William_Hurt" title="William Hurt">William Hurt</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Doctor_(1991_film)" title="The Doctor (1991 film)">The Doctor</a></i> (1991), receiving critical acclaim for her performance as a <a href="/wiki/Terminal_illness" title="Terminal illness">terminal</a> cancer patient.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPW_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-EPW-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1993, Perkins appeared in the television project <i>For Their Own Good</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WB_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-WB-10">[10]</a></sup> She later starred in the comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Battery_Park_(TV_series)" title="Battery Park (TV series)">Battery Park</a></i> and has appeared in television and films including the <a href="/wiki/Miracle_on_34th_Street_(1994_film)" title="Miracle on 34th Street (1994 film)">1994 remake</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Miracle_on_34th_Street" title="Miracle on 34th Street">Miracle on 34th Street</a></i> and 2000's <i><a href="/wiki/28_Days_(film)" title="28 Days (film)">28 Days</a></i> starring as <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Bullock" title="Sandra Bullock">Sandra Bullock</a>'s sister. Perkins also had a small role in the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo" title="Finding Nemo">Finding Nemo</a></i>, voicing the clownfish who was the wife of Marlin and mother of Nemo, and who was killed by a <a href="/wiki/Barracuda" title="Barracuda">barracuda</a> at the beginning of the film. Perkins also appeared as a psychiatrist in the 2005 suspense thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ring_Two" title="The Ring Two">The Ring Two</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Watts" title="Naomi Watts">Naomi Watts</a>.</p>
<p>She played <a href="/wiki/Wilma_Flintstone" title="Wilma Flintstone">Wilma Flintstone</a> in the 1994 live-action comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flintstones_(film)" title="The Flintstones (film)">The Flintstones</a></i>.</p>
<p>From 2005 to 2009, Perkins played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celia_Hodes" title="Celia Hodes">Celia Hodes</a>, an alcoholic and image-obsessed <a href="/wiki/Parent-Teacher_Association" title="Parent-Teacher Association">PTA</a> mother, alongside <a href="/wiki/Mary-Louise_Parker" title="Mary-Louise Parker">Mary-Louise Parker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Kirk" title="Justin Kirk">Justin Kirk</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Weeds_(TV_series)" title="Weeds (TV series)">Weeds</a></i>. For her work on <i>Weeds</i>, Perkins received two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> nominations for Best Supporting Actress in a TV Series, Miniseries or Made for TV Motion Picture (in 2006 and 2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPW_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-EPW-5">[5]</a></sup> She was also nominated three times for an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> for Best Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series for her work on <i>Weeds</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPW_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-EPW-5">[5]</a></sup> At a screening of the season 2 finale of <i>Weeds</i>, at the Museum of TV and Radio on October 25, 2006, Perkins said that she considers Celia Hodes her favorite role in her career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPW_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-EPW-5">[5]</a></sup> On May 6, 2010, she announced that the fifth season of <i>Weeds</i> was her last despite the cliffhanger her character had in the season finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>She starred in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> comedy series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/How_to_Live_with_Your_Parents_(for_the_Rest_of_Your_Life)" title="How to Live with Your Parents (for the Rest of Your Life)">How to Live with Your Parents (for the Rest of Your Life)</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She portrayed the uncredited character Lizzie in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lorax_(film)" title="The Lorax (film)">The Lorax</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elizabeth_Perkins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perkins married <a href="/wiki/Terry_Kinney" title="Terry Kinney">Terry Kinney</a> in 1984, and divorced in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DIV_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-DIV-13">[13]</a></sup> She has one daughter. In 2000, she married Argentinian-born cinematographer <a href="/wiki/Julio_Macat" title="Julio Macat">Julio Macat</a>, gaining three stepsons: Maximillian, Alexander and Andreas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2005, at the age of 44, she learned that she had <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latent_autoimmune_diabetes" title="Latent autoimmune diabetes">latent autoimmune diabetes</a>, a form of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juvenile_diabetes" title="Juvenile diabetes">juvenile diabetes</a> that is most often diagnosed in middle age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
