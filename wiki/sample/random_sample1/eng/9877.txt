<p><b>Elisabeth Schumann</b> (13 June 1888 – 23 April 1952) was a German <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">soprano</a> who sang in <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Operetta" title="Operetta">operetta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oratorio" title="Oratorio">oratorio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lied" title="Lied">lieder</a>. She left a substantial legacy of recordings.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elisabeth_Schumann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="537" data-file-width="698" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg/220px-Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg/330px-Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg/440px-Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Elisabeth_Schumann_%26_Richard_Strauss.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Elisabeth Schumann and Richard Strauss</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Merseburg" title="Merseburg">Merseburg</a>, Schumann trained for a singing career in Berlin and Dresden. She made her stage debut in Hamburg in 1909. Her initial career started in the lighter <a href="/wiki/Soubrette" title="Soubrette">soubrette</a> roles that expanded into mostly <a href="/wiki/Lyric_soprano" title="Lyric soprano">lyrical</a> roles, some <a href="/wiki/Coloratura_soprano" title="Coloratura soprano">coloratura</a> roles, and even a few <a href="/wiki/Dramatic_soprano" title="Dramatic soprano">dramatic</a> roles. She remained at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamburg_Opera" title="Hamburg Opera">Hamburg Opera</a> until 1919, also singing during the 1914/15 season at the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a>, New York. From 1919 to 1938, she was a star of the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_State_Opera" title="Vienna State Opera">Vienna State Opera</a>. Her most famous role was that of Sophie in <a href="/wiki/Richard_Strauss" title="Richard Strauss">Richard Strauss</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Rosenkavalier" title="Der Rosenkavalier">Der Rosenkavalier</a></i>; but she also excelled in Mozart, taking the roles of Pamina in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Die_Zauberfl%C3%B6te" title="Die Zauberflöte">Die Zauberflöte</a></i>, Zerlina in <i><a href="/wiki/Don_Giovanni" title="Don Giovanni">Don Giovanni</a></i>, Blonde in <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Entf%C3%BChrung_aus_dem_Serail" title="Die Entführung aus dem Serail">Die Entführung aus dem Serail</a></i> and Susanna in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_nozze_di_Figaro" title="Le nozze di Figaro">Le nozze di Figaro</a></i>.</p>
<p>The conductor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karl_Alwin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karl Alwin (page does not exist)">Karl Alwin</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Alwin" title="de:Karl Alwin">de</a>)</span> was her second husband from 1920 until 1938. In 1938 she emigrated to New York City where she lived until her death on 23 April 1952. During World War II she gave recitals but mainly taught singing, privately and at the <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Institute_of_Music" title="Curtis Institute of Music">Curtis Institute of Music</a> in Philadelphia. After the war she gave many recitals in Europe, making a particularly successful comeback in England.</p>
<p>She was a much-loved artist, admired for her vivacity, elegance, and beauty. She was closely connected with Richard Strauss, <a href="/wiki/Otto_Klemperer" title="Otto Klemperer">Otto Klemperer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lotte_Lehmann" title="Lotte Lehmann">Lotte Lehmann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Walter" title="Bruno Walter">Bruno Walter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Furtw%C3%A4ngler" title="Wilhelm Furtwängler">Wilhelm Furtwängler</a>, and other leading musicians of the first half of the 20th century.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors">Honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elisabeth_Schumann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Honorary Member of the Vienna State Opera and the first female Honorary Member of the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Philharmonic" title="Vienna Philharmonic">Vienna Philharmonic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Opera_roles">Opera roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elisabeth_Schumann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Opera roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a career spanning 28 years, Schumann sang 91 roles. Her debut role was the Shepherd Boy in <i><a href="/wiki/Tannh%C3%A4user_(opera)" title="Tannhäuser (opera)">Tannhäuser</a></i> on 2 September 1909 in Hamburg; on 1 November 1937 her last role was the First Flower maiden in <i><a href="/wiki/Parsifal" title="Parsifal">Parsifal</a></i> in Vienna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
