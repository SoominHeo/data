<p><b>El Djem</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tunisian_Arabic" title="Tunisian Arabic">Tunisian Arabic</a>: <b>الجمّ</b> <i><span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:El_jam.wav" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/cb/El_jam.wav" title="El jam.wav">il jām</a></span></span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <b>Thysdrus</b>) is a town in <a href="/wiki/Mahdia_Governorate" title="Mahdia Governorate">Mahdia Governorate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>, population 48,611 (2014 census). It is home to some of the most impressive <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> remains in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, like the world-famous "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_amphitheater" title="Roman amphitheater">Roman amphitheater</a> of Thysdrus".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Djem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eljem.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="227" data-file-width="300" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Eljem.jpg/200px-Eljem.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/53/Eljem.jpg 1.5x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Eljem.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
El Djem in the morning</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Roman city of <a href="/wiki/Thysdrus" title="Thysdrus">Thysdrus</a> was built, like almost all <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> settlements in ancient Tunisia, on former <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Punic</a> settlements. In a less arid climate than today's, Thysdrus, which became part of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_province" title="Roman province">Roman province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Byzacena" title="Byzacena">Byzacena</a>, prospered especially in the 2nd century, when it became an important center of <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a> manufacturing for export. It was the seat of a Christian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishopric" title="Bishopric">bishopric</a>, which is included in the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>'s list of <a href="/wiki/Titular_see" title="Titular see">titular sees</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the early 3rd century AD, when the amphitheater was built, Thysdrus rivaled <a href="/wiki/Hadrumetum" title="Hadrumetum">Hadrumetum</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Sousse" title="Sousse">Sousse</a>) as the second city of Roman <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthage</a>. However, following the abortive revolt that began there in 238 AD, and <a href="/wiki/Gordian_I" title="Gordian I">Gordian I</a>'s suicide in his <a href="/wiki/Roman_villa" title="Roman villa">villa</a> near Carthage, Roman troops loyal to the Emperor <a href="/wiki/Maximinus_Thrax" title="Maximinus Thrax">Maximinus Thrax</a> destroyed the city.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Djem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amphitheatre">Amphitheatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Djem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Amphitheatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Amphitheatre_of_El_Jem" title="Amphitheatre of El Jem">Amphitheatre of El Jem</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>El Djem is famous for its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphitheater" title="Amphitheater">amphitheater</a>, often incorrectly called a Colosseum (roughly translated from Latin as 'that thing by the Colossus'), which is capable of seating 35,000 spectators. Only the <a href="/wiki/Colosseum" title="Colosseum">Flavian Amphitheater</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> (about 50,000 spectators) and the ruined theatre of <a href="/wiki/Capua" title="Capua">Capua</a> are larger.</p>
<p>The amphitheatre at El Djem was built by the Romans under <a href="/wiki/Proconsul" title="Proconsul">proconsul</a> <a href="/wiki/Gordian_I" title="Gordian I">Gordian</a>, who was acclaimed Emperor at Thysdrus, around 238 and was mainly used for <a href="/wiki/Gladiator" title="Gladiator">gladiator</a> shows and small <a href="/wiki/Chariot_racing" title="Chariot racing">chariot races</a> (like in <i><a href="/wiki/Ben-Hur_(1959_film)" title="Ben-Hur (1959 film)">Ben-Hur</a></i>).</p>
<p>Many tourists come here to see what it was like to be inside what was once a place where lions and people met their fate. Much of it is crumbled but the essence of it still remains. It is also possible that construction of the amphitheatre was never finished.</p>
<p>Until the 17th century it remained more or less whole. From then on its stones were used for building the nearby village of El Djem and transported to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mosque_of_Uqba" title="Mosque of Uqba">Great Mosque</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kairouan" title="Kairouan">Kairouan</a>, and at a tense moment during struggles with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottomans" title="Ottomans">Ottomans</a>, the Turks used cannons to flush rebels out of the amphitheatre.</p>
<p>The ruins of the amphitheatre were declared a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> in 1979. It hosts the annual <a href="/wiki/Festival_international_de_musique_symphonique_d%27El_Jem" title="Festival international de musique symphonique d'El Jem">Festival international de musique symphonique d'El Jem</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Djem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Drifting sand is preserving the market city of Thysdrus and the refined suburban <a href="/wiki/Villa" title="Villa">villas</a> that once surrounded it. Some floor <a href="/wiki/Mosaic" title="Mosaic">mosaics</a> have been found and published, but field archaeology has scarcely been attempted. Recently with aerial photos, a huge racetrack stadium has been discovered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the world of writing materials, Thysdrus lay in the Empire of <a href="/wiki/Papyrus" title="Papyrus">Papyrus</a>, which preserves ruins remarkably well if kept as dry as at El Djem.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Djem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> a major military airfield was located near El Djem, used first by the German <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a>. It was attacked on numerous occasions and later used by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Air_Force" title="Twelfth Air Force">Twelfth Air Force</a> as a transport field. There are few, if any, remains of the airfield today with the land being returned to agricultural uses outside of the city.</p>
