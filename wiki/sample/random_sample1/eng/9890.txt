<p><b>Elton Tyron Brand</b> (born March 11, 1979) is an American retired professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player. After playing college basketball for <a href="/wiki/Duke_Blue_Devils_men%27s_basketball" title="Duke Blue Devils men's basketball">Duke</a>, he was selected with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_first_overall_pick" title="NBA first overall pick">first overall pick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1999_NBA_draft" title="1999 NBA draft">1999 NBA draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">Chicago Bulls</a>, and later played for the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_career">High school career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: High school career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brand attended <a href="/wiki/Peekskill_High_School" title="Peekskill High School">Peekskill High School</a>, where he was immediately added to the <a href="/wiki/Varsity_team" title="Varsity team">varsity</a> basketball roster. He averaged 40 points and 20 rebounds per game, played AAU basketball with future NBA players <a href="/wiki/Lamar_Odom" title="Lamar Odom">Lamar Odom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metta_World_Peace" title="Metta World Peace">Ron Artest</a>, and by his senior year he was consistently ranked among the top high school basketball players in the country and was selected as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Mr._Basketball_Award" title="New York Mr. Basketball Award">New York State Mr. Basketball</a>. At the same time, he became something of a cult hero in Peekskill, helping his team win two state championships. Recruited heavily after his successful high school career, Brand decided to enroll at <a href="/wiki/Duke_University" title="Duke University">Duke University</a> alongside a cluster of other high school stars, including <a href="/wiki/Shane_Battier" title="Shane Battier">Shane Battier</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a sophomore, Brand was the dominant inside presence for a <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Duke_Blue_Devils_men%27s_basketball_team" title="1998–99 Duke Blue Devils men's basketball team">Duke team</a> that is widely regarded as one of the most talented teams in recent NCAA history. After leading the <a href="/wiki/Duke_Blue_Devils" title="Duke Blue Devils">Blue Devils</a> to the championship game of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Final_Four" title="Final Four">Final Four</a>—where they were upset by the <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Connecticut_Huskies_men%27s_basketball_team" title="1998–99 Connecticut Huskies men's basketball team">Connecticut Huskies</a>—Brand was named the consensus National Player of the Year. He subsequently decided to leave Duke after his sophomore season and declared for the <a href="/wiki/1999_NBA_draft" title="1999 NBA draft">1999 NBA draft</a>. Brand, along with sophomore <a href="/wiki/William_Avery_(basketball)" title="William Avery (basketball)">William Avery</a> and freshman <a href="/wiki/Corey_Maggette" title="Corey Maggette">Corey Maggette</a>, were the first three players under <a href="/wiki/Mike_Krzyzewski" title="Mike Krzyzewski">Mike Krzyzewski</a> to leave early for the draft and not play the full four years at Duke.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_Bulls_.281999.E2.80.932001.29">Chicago Bulls (1999–2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chicago Bulls (1999–2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 30, 1999, Brand was selected by the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Chicago_Bulls_season" title="1999–2000 Chicago Bulls season">Chicago Bulls</a> with the <a href="/wiki/List_of_first_overall_NBA_draft_picks" title="List of first overall NBA draft picks">first overall pick</a> of the <a href="/wiki/1999_NBA_draft" title="1999 NBA draft">1999 NBA draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In his rookie season, he averaged 20.1 points and 10 rebounds per game and in May 2000, he shared <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_Rookie_of_the_Year" title="NBA Rookie of the Year">NBA Rookie of the Year</a> honors with <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a> guard <a href="/wiki/Steve_Francis" title="Steve Francis">Steve Francis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Through much of the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_NBA_season" title="2000–01 NBA season">2000–01 season</a>, Brand was hailed as the linchpin of a possible new Chicago dynasty. Brand averaged 20.1 points and 10.1 rebounds per game; his 3.9 offensive rebounds per game were the second-best in the NBA.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Los_Angeles_Clippers_.282001.E2.80.932008.29">Los Angeles Clippers (2001–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Los Angeles Clippers (2001–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Elton_Brand_Clippers_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="269" data-file-width="190" height="269" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f6/Elton_Brand_Clippers_cropped.jpg" width="190"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Elton_Brand_Clippers_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Brand played with the Clippers for six seasons</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After two successful seasons with the Bulls, Brand was traded to the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Los_Angeles_Clippers_season" title="2001–02 Los Angeles Clippers season">Los Angeles Clippers</a> in June 2001 for <a href="/wiki/Brian_Skinner" title="Brian Skinner">Brian Skinner</a> and the draft rights to <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Chandler" title="Tyson Chandler">Tyson Chandler</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2002, Brand became the first Clipper since <a href="/wiki/Danny_Manning" title="Danny Manning">Danny Manning</a> (in 1994) to be selected to the All-Star team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Brand became a restricted free agent in 2003, the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Miami_Heat_season" title="2003–04 Miami Heat season">Miami Heat</a> made an offer worth $82 million over six years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In what was an unprecedented move by team owner <a href="/wiki/Donald_Sterling" title="Donald Sterling">Donald Sterling</a>, the Clippers matched Miami's offer and managed to keep Brand a Clipper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Prior to this, the biggest contract Sterling had approved to that point was a five-year, $15 million deal for <a href="/wiki/Eric_Piatkowski" title="Eric Piatkowski">Eric Piatkowski</a> in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Sterling had also refused to offer Brand a contract extension one year prior, when he was willing to accept less than the maximum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_NBA_season" title="2005–06 NBA season">2006 NBA season</a>, Brand experienced a personal renaissance. He posted career-highs in points per game (24.7) and field-goal percentage (52.7), while leading the Clippers to a 47–35 record, their then-best record in team history, and good enough for the sixth seed in the Western Conference. Brand was selected to the <a href="/wiki/2006_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2006 NBA All-Star Game">2006 NBA All-Star Game</a> and considered as a strong candidate for the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Most_Valuable_Player" title="Most Valuable Player">NBA MVP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Brand led the franchise to its first playoff series win since 1976, when the team was known as the Buffalo Braves. Although the Clippers eventually lost in game 7 of the second round (Western Conference Semifinals) against the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Phoenix_Suns_season" title="2005–06 Phoenix Suns season">Phoenix Suns</a>, the Clippers had the best season that their franchise had ever seen at that time. Brand received the <a href="/wiki/Joe_Dumars" title="Joe Dumars">Joe Dumars</a> Trophy after being named the 2005–06 <a href="/wiki/NBA_Sportsmanship_Award" title="NBA Sportsmanship Award">NBA Sportsmanship Award</a> winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his stellar <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_NBA_season" title="2005–06 NBA season">2005–06 season</a>, Brand regressed somewhat in the following season. His scoring average in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_NBA_season" title="2006–07 NBA season">2006–07</a> dropped and the Clippers missed the playoffs.</p>
<p>He missed most of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_NBA_season" title="2007–08 NBA season">2007–08 season</a> due to a ruptured left Achilles' tendon. However, Brand made his return to the Clippers' lineup April 2, 2008 after being out since the end of the previous season. He contributed 19 points in his return. Brand played in only eight games that season. Brand later opted out of the final year of his contract and became a <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a>. Sources indicated that Brand chose to opt out from his contract in order to provide Clippers with more payroll flexibility in hopes of strengthening their roster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This came to fruition when Clippers landed <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> star <a href="/wiki/Baron_Davis" title="Baron Davis">Baron Davis</a>. Briefly, this gave Clippers' management and the media the reassurance that Brand would indeed re-sign with the team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philadelphia_76ers_.282008.E2.80.932012.29">Philadelphia 76ers (2008–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Philadelphia 76ers (2008–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1204" data-file-width="673" height="394" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg/220px-Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg/330px-Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg/440px-Elton_Brand_vs_Warriors_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>On July 9, 2008, Brand signed with the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Philadelphia_76ers_season" title="2008–09 Philadelphia 76ers season">Philadelphia 76ers</a> to a reported five-year, $82 million contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sixers-brand_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-sixers-brand-12">[12]</a></sup> Brand longed to return to the East Coast, and chose Philadelphia as it was the closest suitor to his hometown of <a href="/wiki/Peekskill,_New_York" title="Peekskill, New York">Peekskill, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on February 5, 2009 that Brand would have season-ending shoulder surgery for the injury he sustained on December 17, 2008. During the game that night against the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Milwaukee_Bucks_season" title="2008–09 Milwaukee Bucks season">Milwaukee Bucks</a>, Brand dislocated his shoulder when he was knocked to the floor while going for a rebound. Brand had been coming off of the bench so that he could be slowly worked back into the lineup, but after over a month on the bench and only six games back during which his production was severely limited, the decision was made to go forward with the surgery. The procedure was successfully performed on February 9.</p>
<p>On February 4, 2013 Brand scored a Sixers career high 33 points in a 100–98 win over the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a>. He finished his first tenure with the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a> averaging 11 points per game and just over 8 rebounds per game during the 2011–12 season. He averaged 8.6 points and 4.8 rebounds in the <a href="/wiki/2012_NBA_Playoffs" title="2012 NBA Playoffs">2012 NBA Playoffs</a> in a total of 13 games when the Sixers lost in Game 7 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals to the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> 85–75.</p>
<p>On July 11, 2012, Brand was released from the 76ers via the <a href="/wiki/NBA_salary_cap#Amnesty_clause" title="NBA salary cap">amnesty clause</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amnesty_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-amnesty-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dallas_Mavericks_.282012.E2.80.932013.29">Dallas Mavericks (2012–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Dallas Mavericks (2012–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On July 13, 2012, Brand was claimed off waivers by the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Dallas_Mavericks_season" title="2012–13 Dallas Mavericks season">Dallas Mavericks</a> with a winning bid of $2.1 million. The 76ers still had to pay out the remaining balance of the $18.2 million owed to Brand this season in the final year of his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012–13, he averaged 7.2 points, 6.0 rebounds, 1.3 blocks and 1.0 assists in 21.2 minutes per game. He played 72 games and started 18 of them. The Mavericks finished 41-41 and missed the playoffs for the first time since the 1999–2000 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlanta_Hawks_and_first_retirement_.282013.E2.80.932015.29">Atlanta Hawks and first retirement (2013–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Atlanta Hawks and first retirement (2013–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 15, 2013, Brand signed with the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hawkssign_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-hawkssign-16">[16]</a></sup> On September 23, 2014, he re-signed with the Hawks<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hawkssign2_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-hawkssign2-17">[17]</a></sup> and changed his long-time jersey number of 42, to 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On August 11, 2015, Brand announced his retirement from professional basketball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_the_76ers_and_second_retirement_.282016.29">Return to the 76ers and second retirement (2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Return to the 76ers and second retirement (2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 4, 2016, Brand came out of retirement to re-enter the NBA, citing that <a href="/wiki/Duke_Blue_Devils_men%27s_basketball" title="Duke Blue Devils men's basketball">Duke</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_Krzyzewski" title="Mike Krzyzewski">Mike Krzyzewski</a> helped convince him to return to play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Later that day, he signed with the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>, returning to the franchise for a second stint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On January 26, he was activated for the first time, but did not play for the 76ers against the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Phoenix_Suns_season" title="2015–16 Phoenix Suns season">Phoenix Suns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On March 4, he made his season debut for the 76ers, playing in an NBA game for the first time since May 20, 2015. In 13 minutes off the bench, he recorded eight points and four rebounds in a 112–102 loss to the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Miami_Heat_season" title="2015–16 Miami Heat season">Miami Heat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On March 12, he recorded a 10-point game against the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Detroit_Pistons_season" title="2015–16 Detroit Pistons season">Detroit Pistons</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> scoring in double digits for the first time since April 14, 2014. On March 27, he recorded a second 10-point game in a loss to the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Golden_State_Warriors_season" title="2015–16 Golden State Warriors season">Golden State Warriors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Two days later, he scored seven points and grabbed a season-high nine rebounds in a loss to the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Charlotte_Hornets_season" title="2015–16 Charlotte Hornets season">Charlotte Hornets</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> as he became the 51st player in NBA history to reach 9,000 career rebounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 7, 2016, Brand re-signed with the 76ers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> However, on October 20, 2016, he announced his second retirement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-playing_career">Post-playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Post-playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 6, 2016, Brand was named as the player development consultant of the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Player_profile">Player profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Player profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2450" data-file-width="2083" height="259" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg/220px-Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg/330px-Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg/440px-Elton_Brand_in_March_2013.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Early in his career, Brand established himself as one of the top power forwards of the NBA, and now holds career averages of 16.6 points, 8.8 rebounds (3.3 being offensive rebounds) and 1.8 blocks per game in 1,005 career games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brand uses his wide body and surprising athleticism for a man his weight in order to out-muscle his opponents. A large wingspan also allows him to be a proficient shot-blocker.</p>
<p>In his first years in the league, he had a few, but effective and powerful moves underneath the basket which already made him an effective post player.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> During the off-season prior to the 2005–06 season, he trimmed his weight to increase his quickness and added more finesse moves underneath the basket so that he could score without having to utilize power all the time. But most importantly, he worked on his shooting range and developed a dependable 18 ft. jumper. As a result, he raised his scoring by 4.7 points in that season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elton_Brand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brand started the <i>Elton Brand Foundation</i> in the spring of 2000. His foundation is an organization that provides support to worthy causes in Chicago, Illinois; Peekskill, New York; and Durham, North Carolina.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2006, Elton married his longtime girlfriend (and fellow Duke student) Shahara Simmons in North Carolina. Brand also played for Team USA in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIBA_World_Championship" title="FIBA World Championship">2006 FIBA World Basketball Championships</a>, averaging 8.9 points and 3.3 rebounds per game.</p>
<p>Brand is a founding member and president of Gibraltar Films,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> a company engaged in film investment, acquisition, production, and distribution of motion pictures. The first project of Gibraltar Films was the production of a Vietnam-era prisoner-of-war film <i><a href="/wiki/Rescue_Dawn" title="Rescue Dawn">Rescue Dawn</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Werner_Herzog" title="Werner Herzog">Werner Herzog</a>. Brand attended the film's premiere at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
