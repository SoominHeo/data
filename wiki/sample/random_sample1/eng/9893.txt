<p>The <b>Battle of Emsdorf</b> was fought on 14 July 1760 during the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kirchhain#Town_divisions" title="Kirchhain">Emsdorf</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>, Germany, between forces of British, Hanoverian and Hessian troops under the Prince of Hesse-Kassel (or Hesse-Cassel) against German troops in French service<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> under <i>Marechal de Camp</i> von Glaubitz. It was part of the campaign to disrupt the French line of communications by capturing <a href="/wiki/Marburg" title="Marburg">Marburg</a>, a French supply depot.</p>
<p>The Anglo-Hanoverian force consisted of six Hanoverian and Hessian infantry battalions, some Hanoverian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C3%A4gers" title="Jägers">jägers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Luckner" title="Nicolas Luckner">Luckner</a>'s light cavalry <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hussars" title="Hussars">hussars</a> and the British 15th Light Dragoons. The French force consisted of five infantry battalions from the German regiments of Royal-Bavarière and Anhalt, a regiment of hussars and some light troops.</p>
<p>The British forces initially surprised the French force in camp as they sat down to lunch. The French hussars fled at the onset while two of the infantry battalions managed to form up before receiving the concentrated fire of the Hessian battalions. Glaubitz sent one battalion back to Marburg while he tried to withdraw to Kirchhain with the rest of his troops. Most of the escape routes were cut by Luckner and the light troops. The Anglo-Hanoverians ultimately captured over 1650 prisoners, mostly due to several charges by the British 15th Light Dragoons into the retreating French force.</p>
<p>The victory was largely won by the well-handled British <a href="/wiki/15th_The_King%27s_Hussars" title="15th The King's Hussars">15th Light Dragoons</a> who suffered heavily with 125 of the 186 Allied casualties. Lieutenant Colonel <a href="/wiki/Sir_William_Erskine,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir William Erskine, 1st Baronet">William Erskine</a> of the 15th Dragoons presented King George III with 16 colours captured by his regiment after the battle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, the objective of capturing Marburg was not achieved.</p>
<p>The Battle of Emsdorf was also the first ever <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_Honour" title="Battle Honour">Battle Honour</a> awarded. Earlier battles were then given the status of a Battle Honour.</p>
