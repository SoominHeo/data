<p><i><b>Ecchi</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エッチ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>etchi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Japanese" title="Help:IPA for Japanese">[et.tɕi]</a></span>)</span> is an often used <a href="/wiki/Slang" title="Slang">slang</a> term in the Japanese language for playfully sexual actions. As an <a href="/wiki/Adjective" title="Adjective">adjective</a>, it is used with the meaning of "sexy", "dirty" or "naughty"; as a <a href="/wiki/Verb" title="Verb">verb</a>, <i>ecchi suru</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エッチする</span>)</span>, with the meaning to have sex; or as a <a href="/wiki/Noun" title="Noun">noun</a>, to describe someone of <a href="/wiki/Lascivious_behavior" title="Lascivious behavior">lascivious behavior</a>. It is perhaps softer than the Japanese word <i>ero</i> (エロ　from <a href="/wiki/Eros" title="Eros">Eros</a>), and does not imply perversion in the way <a href="/wiki/Hentai" title="Hentai">hentai</a> does.</p>
<p>The word <i>ecchi</i> has been adopted by fans of Japanese media to describe works with sexual overtones. In Japanese, the word <i>ecchi</i> is often used to describe a person's conduct, but in fandom, it has come to be used to refer to <a href="/wiki/Softcore_pornography" title="Softcore pornography">softcore</a> or playful sexuality, as distinct from the word <i>hentai</i>, which connotes perversion or fetishism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s.keller-2008_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-s.keller-2008-2">[2]</a></sup> Works described as ecchi do not show <a href="/wiki/Sexual_intercourse" title="Sexual intercourse">sexual intercourse</a> or genitalia, but sexual themes are referenced. <i>Ecchi</i> themes are a type of <a href="/wiki/Fan_service" title="Fan service">fan service</a>, and can be found in most comedy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen" title="Shōnen">shōnen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seinen_manga" title="Seinen manga">seinen manga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harem_(genre)" title="Harem (genre)">harem</a> <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r.brenner_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-r.brenner-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-askjohn_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-askjohn-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology_and_use_in_Japan">Etymology and use in Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology and use in Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The correct transcription of the word <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エッチ</span> in <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a> notation is "etchi."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, it is typically written as "ecchi".</p>
<p>In the word 'hentai,'(変態) the first kanji 'hen' refers to strangeness, and the second kanji 'tai' refers to a condition or state. "Hentai" was introduced in the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> as a term for <i>change of form</i> or <i>transformation</i> in <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a>. In this context, it was used to refer to disorders such as <a href="/wiki/Hysteria" title="Hysteria">hysteria</a> or to describe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paranormal_phenomena" title="Paranormal phenomena">paranormal phenomena</a> like <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">hypnosis</a> or <a href="/wiki/Telepathy" title="Telepathy">telepathy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hikaru-2004_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-hikaru-2004-6">[6]</a></sup> Slowly the meaning expanded until it had the meaning of <i>non-standard</i>. In the 1910s, it was used in <a href="/wiki/Sexology" title="Sexology">sexology</a> in the compound expression "hentai seiyoku" (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">変態性欲</span>, <i>abnormal sexual desire</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and became popular within the theory of sexual deviance (<i>Hentai seiyoku ron</i>), published by Eiji Habuto and Jun′ichirō Sawada in 1915.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the 1920s, many publications dealt with deviant sexual desires and the <a href="/wiki/Ero_guro" title="Ero guro">Ero Guro Nansensu</a> movement. Matsuzawa calls it a period characterized by a "<i>hentai boom</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the 1930s, censorship became more common leading to fewer books being published on this theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p><span id="H"></span>After the Second World War, in the 1950s, interest in hentai was renewed, and people would sometimes refer to it just by the first English letter, <i>H</i> (pronounced as <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エッチ</span>, <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="/tʃ/ 'ch' in 'china'">tʃ</span></span>/</a></span></span>). In 1952, the magazine <i>Shukan Asahi</i> reported that a woman who was groped by a stranger in a movie theater reacted with <i>"ara etchi yo"</i> (<i>"hey, that's perverse"</i>). In this context, "etchi" must be understood as <i>sexually forward</i> and is synonymous to <i>iyarashii</i> (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">嫌らしい</span>, dirty or disgusting) or <i>sukebe</i> (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">すけべ</span>, a person with sex on the brain). From this, the word "etchi" started to branch off, and assume new connotations. In the 1960s, etchi started to be used by youth to refer to <a href="/wiki/Sex" title="Sex">sex</a> in general. By the 1980s, it was used to mean <i>sex</i> as in the phrase <i>etchi suru</i> (to have sex).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hikaru-2004_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-hikaru-2004-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other neologisms such as <i>sekkusu</i> are often used to refer to sex, in addition to the term "ecchi." Ecchi is now used as a qualifier for anything related to erotic or pornographic content. Its exact meaning varies with context, but in general, it is most similar to the English word "naughty" (when used as an adjective). The Japanese media tend to use other words, e.g. <i>ero-manga</i> (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">エロ</span>), <i>adult manga</i> (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">アダルト</span>), or anime / manga for persons over 18 years (<span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">18禁アニメ</span>, <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">18禁</span>). The prefix "H-" is also sometimes used to refer to pornographic genres, e.g. H-anime, H-manga, etc.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_usage">Western usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Western usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Japan, oiroke manga (お色気漫画) is used to describe manga with very light or playful erotic content such as is found in shonen manga. In western nations though, <i>ecchi</i> has become the preferred term. The more explicit seijin manga (成人向け漫画, <i>seijinmukemanga</i>) are more likely to be referred to as hentai in the west. This does correlate to a similar distinction in Japanese. For instance, if a young woman were to call a young man <i>e(t/c)chi</i>, that might be construed as flirting, whereas <i>hentai</i> sounds more like condemnation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>[...] Bezeichnet erotische Darstellungen. Im Vergleich zu Hentai weniger explizit.<br/>
[...] [Ecchi] refers to erotic depictions. In comparison to hentai, it is less explicit.</p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— Sebastian Keller, Der Manga und seine Szene in Deutschland von den Anfängen in den 1980er Jahren bis zur Gegenwart: Manga- mehr als nur große Augen<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s.keller-2008_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-s.keller-2008-2">[2]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Works aimed at a female audience can contain scenes which are seen as ecchi. Examples are <i>R-18 Love Report!</i> from Emiko Sugi and <i><a href="/wiki/Oruchuban_Ebichu" title="Oruchuban Ebichu">Oruchuban Ebichu</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Risa_It%C5%8D" title="Risa Itō">Risa Itō</a>, which are aimed at the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Shōjo">shōjo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Josei" title="Josei">josei</a> audience, but contain rather explicit content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r.brenner_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-r.brenner-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-askjohn_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-askjohn-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Common elements of ecchi include conversations with sexual references or misunderstandings (e.g. double entendre or innuendo), misunderstandings in visual depictions (e.g. suggestive posing), revealing or sexualized clothing (e.g. underwear or <a href="/wiki/Cosplay" title="Cosplay">cosplay</a>), nudity (e.g. ripped apart clothing, wet clothing, clothing malfunctions) and the portrayal of certain actions (e.g. groping). This kind of sexuality is often used for comical effect. A typical example scene would contain a male protagonist that trips over a female character, giving the impression of sexual harassment.</p>
<p>The concept of ecchi is very closely related to <a href="/wiki/Fan_service" title="Fan service">fan service</a>. While fan service describes every aspect to please the fans, ecchi relates to sexual themes. A special kind of fan service, that is usually bound or justified by the narrative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Typical_examples">Typical examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Typical examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>There are many elements that may classify a work as ecchi, but these elements have to occur quite often (for example, in all episodes of a show). Graphically speaking, different techniques are used to show sexy pictures, usually by revealing parts of the female body such as the back or breasts. Some of these patterns are recurrent, such as scenes in a shower, <a href="/wiki/Onsen" title="Onsen">onsen</a>, or fighting scenes in which clothes are torn apart. The imagination of characters is also a common device for showing their sexual fantasies, as well as transformation scenes of <a href="/wiki/Magical_girl" title="Magical girl">magical girls</a>. In the end, any excuse is valid to show a character partially or completely nude.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steiff-2010_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-steiff-2010-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nudity">Nudity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nudity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">Censorship</a> with artificial light rays is one common method to hide some elements in <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series. The degree of censorship can vary widely across television stations, even among those broadcasting the series at the same time.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Levels of nudity vary strongly between works depending on the intended audience and the preferences of the authors. For example, in some cases, though the breasts are shown on the screen, nipples and genitals are obscured by props, clothing, or effects. This kind of censorship was typical for Lala in <i><a href="/wiki/To_Love-Ru" title="To Love-Ru">To Love-Ru</a></i>, Blair in <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Eater_(manga)" title="Soul Eater (manga)">Soul Eater</a></i> or even <a href="/wiki/Asuka_Langley_Soryu" title="Asuka Langley Soryu">Asuka Langley Soryu</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a></i>. Meanwhile, in <i><a href="/wiki/Ladies_versus_Butlers!" title="Ladies versus Butlers!">Ladies versus Butlers!</a></i> and other anime, the nipples are clearly visible through clothing, no matter how thick it is. Nosebleeds are a typical reaction to nudity, as they represent sexual arousal.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Panties">Panties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Panties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The visibility of the underwear (<a href="/wiki/Panties" title="Panties">panties</a>) is one common motif. Typically the male will react in an exaggerated manner and be castigated. The color and style of the panties are seen as an indication of the female's character, e.g. white for innocent characters and red for sexually aggressive characters. Panties are a popular main theme in ecchi (for instance, <i><a href="/wiki/Chobits" title="Chobits">Chobits</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Panty_%26_Stocking_with_Garterbelt" title="Panty &amp; Stocking with Garterbelt">Panty &amp; Stocking with Garterbelt</a></i> feature them heavily), but they are also featured in other shows just for sexual appeal.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sexual_intercourse">Sexual intercourse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sexual intercourse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Normally, there is no explicit <a href="/wiki/Sex" title="Sex">sex</a> in work which is considered ecchi in the West - such works are classified as <a href="/wiki/Hentai" title="Hentai">hentai</a>. However, in an ecchi work, it may appear as if a couple are having sex. For instance, the two may be seen in silhouette from outside a tent, appearing to be having sex, although they are doing something nonsexual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steiff-2010_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-steiff-2010-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
