<p><b>Engel Manuel Beltré</b> (born November 1, 1989) is a <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Piratas_de_Campeche" title="Piratas de Campeche">Piratas de Campeche</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Baseball_League" title="Mexican Baseball League">Mexican Baseball League</a>. Beltré briefly lived in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, attending <a href="/wiki/DeWitt_Clinton_High_School" title="DeWitt Clinton High School">DeWitt Clinton High School</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sosh_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sosh-1">[1]</a></sup> As a child, he was a devoted Yankees fan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Beltré was signed by the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> as an international free agent out of the Dominican in 2006 for $575k or $600k.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sosh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sosh-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 31, 2007, Beltré along with <a href="/wiki/Kason_Gabbard" title="Kason Gabbard">Kason Gabbard</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Murphy_(baseball)" title="David Murphy (baseball)">David Murphy</a> were traded from the Red Sox to the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> for <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Gagn%C3%A9" title="Éric Gagné">Éric Gagné</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beltré started the 2013 season with the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock_Express" title="Round Rock Express">Round Rock Express</a>. He was recalled by the Rangers on June 23, 2013 when <a href="/wiki/Craig_Gentry" title="Craig Gentry">Craig Gentry</a> was added to the disabled list. He made his MLB debut on June 26 against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> as a pinch runner in the ninth inning and was caught stealing second base. He got his first two major league hits the next day against <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hughes" title="Phil Hughes">Phil Hughes</a>. Beltré was optioned back to Round Rock on August 2, and recalled on August 5. He elected free agency on November 6, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beltré played in the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2013 World Baseball Classic">2013 World Baseball Classic</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_baseball_team" title="Spain national baseball team">Spain national baseball team</a>.</p>
<p>On May 3, 2016, Beltre signed with the <a href="/wiki/Piratas_de_Campeche" title="Piratas de Campeche">Piratas de Campeche</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Baseball_League" title="Mexican Baseball League">Mexican Baseball League</a>.</p>
