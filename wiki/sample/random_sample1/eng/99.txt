<p>Year <b>165</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CLXV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Orfitus and Pudens</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 918 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 165 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=165&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=165&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=165&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>A Roman military operation under <a href="/wiki/Avidius_Cassius" title="Avidius Cassius">Avidius Cassius</a> is successful against <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthia</a>, capturing <a href="/wiki/Artaxata" title="Artaxata">Artaxata</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seleucia_on_the_Tigris" title="Seleucia on the Tigris">Seleucia on the Tigris</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ctesiphon" title="Ctesiphon">Ctesiphon</a>. The Parthians sue for peace.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Pandemic" title="Pandemic">pandemic</a> known as the <a href="/wiki/Antonine_Plague" title="Antonine Plague">Antonine Plague</a> breaks out in Rome after the <a href="/wiki/Roman_army" title="Roman army">Roman army</a> returns from <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthia</a>. The plague significantly depopulates the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Legio_II_Italica" title="Legio II Italica">Legio II <i>Italica</i></a> is levied by emperor <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Aurelius" title="Marcus Aurelius">Marcus Aurelius</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dura-Europos" title="Dura-Europos">Dura-Europos</a> is taken by the Romans.</li>
<li>The Romans establish a <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> at Doura Europos on the <a href="/wiki/Euphrates" title="Euphrates">Euphrates</a>, a control point for the commercial route to the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>.</li>
<li>Avidius Cassius takes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nisibis" title="Nisibis">Nisibis</a> and conquers the north of <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>.</li>
<li>Marcus Aurelius creates four legal districts (iuridici) in Italy (5 if <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a> is included).</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The philosopher <a href="/wiki/Justin_Martyr" title="Justin Martyr">Justin</a> of Nablus is executed in Rome as a <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>.</li>

</ul>
