<p><b>Mother Lü</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">呂母</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin"><b>Lǚ Mǔ</b></span></i>; died 18 AD) was a rebel leader against the <a href="/wiki/Xin_dynasty" title="Xin dynasty">Xin dynasty</a> in ancient China. She started a peasant uprising after her son Lü Yu was executed by the government for a minor offence, and became the first female rebel leader in Chinese history. After she died of an illness, her followers became a major force of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Eyebrows_Rebellions" title="Red Eyebrows Rebellions">Red Eyebrows Rebellions</a> which played a significant role in the downfall of the Xin dynasty and the restoration of the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a> by Liu Xiu, enthroned as <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Guangwu_of_Han" title="Emperor Guangwu of Han">Emperor Guangwu of Han</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mother_L%C3%BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mother Lü was born during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Han" title="Western Han">Western Han</a> dynasty of ancient China. In 9 AD, the chief minister <a href="/wiki/Wang_Mang" title="Wang Mang">Wang Mang</a> usurped the imperial throne and proclaimed himself Emperor of the <a href="/wiki/Xin_dynasty" title="Xin dynasty">Xin dynasty</a>. Wang implemented a number of policies which were opposed by the wealthy landowning class. Economic stress caused by the flooding of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a> further weakened the legitimacy of his rule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rodriguez2007_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez2007-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mother Lü lived in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haiqu_County" title="Haiqu County">Haiqu County</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%B5%B7%E6%9B%B2%E5%8E%BF" title="zh:海曲县">海曲县</a>), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Langya_Commandery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Langya Commandery (page does not exist)">Langya Commandery</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%90%85%E9%82%AA%E9%83%A1" title="zh:琅邪郡">琅邪郡</a>), in present-day <a href="/wiki/Rizhao" title="Rizhao">Rizhao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a> province. Her family was very wealthy, worth millions of coins according to the <i><a href="/wiki/Book_of_the_Later_Han" title="Book of the Later Han">Book of the Later Han</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rebellion">Rebellion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mother_L%C3%BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rebellion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 14 AD, her son Lü Yu (呂育), who had served in the government of Haiqu County, was executed by the county magistrate for a minor offence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup> To avenge his death, Mother Lü plotted a rebellion, using her wealth to recruit poor peasants and purchase weapons and supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup> She soon raised an army of several thousand people from a populace that had already been dissatisfied with the government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hinsch2010_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hinsch2010-4">[4]</a></sup> Mother Lü assumed the title of General and led her rebel force to storm the capital of Haiqu County. After capturing the county magistrate, she beheaded him<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hinsch2010_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hinsch2010-4">[4]</a></sup> and sacrificed his head on her son's tomb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fan_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fan-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_legacy">Death and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mother_L%C3%BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mother Lü's success inspired numerous people all over the country to rebel against Wang Mang's rule, and her own force grew rapidly to tens of thousands of soldiers, but she soon died of an illness in 18 AD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After her death, most of Lü's followers joined forces with the rebels led by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fan_Chong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fan Chong (page does not exist)">Fan Chong</a>, another native of Langya who had rebelled in <a href="/wiki/Ju_County" title="Ju County">Ju County</a> in 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup> The joint rebel army became known as the <a href="/wiki/Red_Eyebrows" title="Red Eyebrows">Red Eyebrows</a>, which was one of the two leading rebel forces that would overthrow Wang Mang's regime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hinsch2010_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hinsch2010-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Historians credit Mother Lü with starting the wave of uprisings that led to the downfall of the Xin dynasty and the restoration of the Han dynasty by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liu_Xiu" title="Liu Xiu">Liu Xiu</a> (Emperor Guangwu), the first emperor of Eastern Han.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hinsch2010_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hinsch2010-4">[4]</a></sup> She is the first female rebel leader recorded in Chinese history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-Lin-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
