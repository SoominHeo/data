<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Women's shot put world record progression</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The first <b><a href="/wiki/World_record" title="World record">world record</a></b> in the <b>women's <a href="/wiki/Shot_put" title="Shot put">shot put</a></b> was recognised by the Fédération Sportive Féminine Internationale (FSFI) in 1924. The FSFI was absorbed by the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Athletics_Federations" title="International Association of Athletics Federations">International Association of Athletics Federations</a> in 1936. These women's distances were achieved with an 4 kilograms (8.8 lb) shot put.</p>
<p>As of June 21, 2009, the IAAF (and the FSFI before it) have ratified 50 world records in the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iaaf_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-iaaf-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
