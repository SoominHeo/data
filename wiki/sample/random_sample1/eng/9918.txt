<p><b>Joshibi University of Art and Design</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">女子美術大学</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Joshibijutsu Daigaku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span><br/>
abbreviated "<b>女子美</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">Joshibi</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>", is a private <a href="/wiki/Art_school" title="Art school">art university</a> located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suginami,_Tokyo" title="Suginami, Tokyo">Suginami</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sagamihara,_Kanagawa" title="Sagamihara, Kanagawa">Sagamihara</a> in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>The mission and aims of Joshibi, developing creative minds, encourages students to contribute to local, national and international societies, female independence and evaluation of social status for female through fine art and design, as well as train and educate qualified art tutors and creative artists.</p>
<p>Joshibi is the first fine art institution for female students in Japan and is the oldest private art school.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joshibi_University_of_Art_and_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joshibi was founded on October 30, 1900, as Private Women's School of Fine Arts (PWSFA) located in Hongo-ku (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bunkyo-ku,_Tokyo" title="Bunkyo-ku, Tokyo">Bunkyo-ku</a>), while female students were not admitted to membership of the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_University_of_the_Arts" title="Tokyo University of the Arts">Tokyo University of the Arts</a> until 1946.</p>
<p>Joshibi founding members include Tamako Yokoi (former tutor of Joshi-Gakuin School) and Bunzo Fujita (former Professor of Tokyo University of Arts). Fujita served the first principal, and created school's logo "<b>美</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">Bi</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>". PWSFA was officially oped in April 1901 with 60 students at Yumi-cho. PWSFA suffered from financial difficulties six months after the school opened. Yokoi and Fujita petitioned Shidu Sato (wife of Susumu Sato (Military Physician)). Sato's family owned PWSFA in January 1902, Shidu served the second principal. Yokoi died on January 4, 1903, from stomach cancer. Yumi-cho campus was burned down in October 1908. Kikuzaka campus was opened at Kikuzaka-cho　in July 1909. <a href="/wiki/Joshibi_High_School_of_Art_and_Design" title="Joshibi High School of Art and Design">Joshibi High School of Art and Design</a> was opened in April 1915, Fujita serve the first school principal.</p>
<p>PWSFA renamed Women's School of Fine Arts (WSFA) in 1919. WSFA was accredited by the Ministry of Education as a vocational college in 1929, renamed Women's Academy of Fine Arts (WAFA). WAFA moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suginami,_Tokyo" title="Suginami, Tokyo">Suginami</a> in January 1935 due to the increasing number of students. The Ministry of Education has introduced a new university education law in 1949, WAFA renamed Women's College of Fine Arts (WCFA).</p>
<p>The College of Art and Design relocated to Sagamihara in 1990 due to campus expansion. <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master's degree</a> programs started to be offered in 1994, and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> programs started to be offered in 1996.</p>
<p>WCFA renamed Joshibi University of Art and Design in 2001. Joshibi Art Museum (JAM) was opened at Sagamihara campus in 2002.</p>
<p>The College of Art and Design underwent restructuring into 3 departments and 13 majors in April 2010.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics_and_structures">Academics and structures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joshibi_University_of_Art_and_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academics and structures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The College of Art and Design has been offered a Bachelor of Fine Arts(BFA 4-Year) in 3 departments 13 majors:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Department of Fine Arts</i>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_Painting" title="Oil Painting">Oil Painting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Printing" title="Printing">Printing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nihonga" title="Nihonga">Nihonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">Sculpture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Art#Theory" title="Art">Art Theory</a> and Practice, Art Education.</li>
<li><i>Department of Design and Crafts</i>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crafts" title="Crafts">Crafts</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphic_Design" title="Graphic Design">Visual Design</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Product_Design" title="Product Design">Product Design</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Design" title="Environmental Design">Environmental Design</a>.</li>
<li><i>Department of Cross Disciplinary art and design</i>: <a href="/wiki/New_media_art" title="New media art">Media Art</a>, Art and Design for <a href="/wiki/Healing" title="Healing">Healing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">Fashion design</a>, Art Produce and <a href="/wiki/Museology" title="Museology">Museum Study</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Junior College of Art Design has been offered an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associate_Degree" title="Associate Degree">Associate Degree</a> of Fine Arts(AFA 2-Year) in three majors:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fine Arts</li>
<li>Information Design</li>
<li>Creative Design</li>
</ul>
<p>Master and Doctor of Philosophy(Ph.D.) degree programs have been offered for both female and male students in 3 areas: <a href="/wiki/Fine_art" title="Fine art">Fine art</a>, Design, Art and <a href="/wiki/Cultural_studies" title="Cultural studies">Cultural studies</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses_and_Museum">Campuses and Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joshibi_University_of_Art_and_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campuses and Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i>Suginami Campus</i></li>
</ul>
<p>Established in 1935, is located in Wada, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suginami,_Tokyo" title="Suginami, Tokyo">Suginami</a>. The Department of Cross-Disciplinary Art and Design, Graduate School of Art and Design, Junior College of Art and Design, <a href="/wiki/Joshibi_High_School_of_Art_and_Design" title="Joshibi High School of Art and Design">Joshibi High School of Art and Design</a>, University Headquarters, Alumnae Association Head Office are located at Suginami Campus.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Sagamihara Campus</i></li>
</ul>
<p>Established in 1990, is located in Asamizo-dai, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sagamihara,_Kanagawa" title="Sagamihara, Kanagawa">Sagamihara</a>. The College of Art and Design(Department of Fine arts, Department of Design and Crafts), Graduate School of Art and Design are located at Sagamihara Campus.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Joshibi Art Museum (JAM)</i> is located in Sagamihara Campus opened in 2001. The museum's collection more than 15,000 works of art include OKUBO Fukuko, KATAOKA Tamako, GOKURA Kazuko, TADA Minami, MIGISHI Setsuko. The museum has strong relationship with female artists and focusing on female art works. The museum had acquired a part of the former Kanebo Collection in 2009. It's one of the largest textile collections in Japan which covers from the ancient to the contemporary textiles from around the world.</li>
<li><i>Joshibi Galleria nike</i> is an art gallery re-opened in July 2012, located at Suginami Campus. Galleria nike is a working art gallery where public exhibition, research, teaching and learning intersect. The historical hall is the affiliated hall to Galleria nike introducing history of Joshibi and art works. All the collections are open to the public.</li>
<li><i>Joshibi Art Gallery in Shanghai</i> is an art gallery opened in November 2012 in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> as an art project related to the 40th anniversary of the normalization of diplomatic relations between Japan and China. The gallery is affiliated to JAM and the first art gallery established by Japanese art university outside Japan. The gallery introduces art works of Joshibi graduates and lecturers, new artists, teaching and learning intersect.</li>
</ul>
