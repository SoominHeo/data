<p><i><b>Ekiden</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">駅伝</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a term referring to a long-distance relay running race, typically on roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Otake_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Otake-2">[2]</a></sup> The Japanese term originally referred to a post-horse or <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach" title="Stagecoach">stagecoach</a> which transmitted communication by stages.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term originated in Japan, although the concept of a long distance relay race is not unique to any country. The first <i>ekiden</i> race was sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Shimbun" title="Yomiuri Shimbun">Yomiuri Shimbun</a> in 1917, and was run over three days between the old Japanese capital of <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> and the modern capital of Tokyo, a distance of 508 km, to celebrate the anniversary of the moving of the capital to Tokyo.</p>
<p>As written in Japanese, <i>Ekiden</i> combines the characters for "station" (駅) and "transmit" (伝). This name was coined by the poet <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toki_Zemmaro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toki Zemmaro (page does not exist)">Toki Zemmaro</a> (1885–1980), who was head of the Yomiuri Shimbun's Social Affairs Department at the time. The original concept of the race hearkens back to Japan's old <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_(road)" title="Tōkaidō (road)">Tōkaidō</a> communication and transportation system in which stations were posted at intervals along the road. In the race, each runner on a team runs the distance from one "station" to the next, and then hands off a cloth sash, or <i>tasuki</i>, to the next runner.</p>
<p>The lengths of <i>ekiden</i> in Japan can vary greatly, as can the number of runners on a team. For example, in the national junior high ekiden championship, 5 girls cover 12 kilometers and 6 boys cover 18 kilometers. The national high school championship involves 5 girls in a 21 kilometer race and 7 boys in 42.195 kilometer race. In the national inter-prefecture championships, 9 women run 42.195 kilometers and 7 men run 48 kilometers. For the collegiate <a href="/wiki/Hakone_Ekiden" title="Hakone Ekiden">Hakone Ekiden</a>, a 2-day event, 10 male athletes for each team run 219 kilometers. Each runner covers just over a half marathon. The mid-December National Corporate Women's Ekiden Championships, 6 runners cover 42.195 kilometers. In the January 1 National Corporate Men's Ekiden Championships, 7 runners cover 100.0 kilometers. For the mid January national open championships (teams composed of selected junior high, high school and open athletes), the distances are 42.195 for the women and 48.0 for the men. Notably in distance, in the <a href="/wiki/Round-Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB_Ekiden" title="Round-Kyūshū Ekiden">Round-Kyūshū Ekiden</a>, a 10 days event, 72 segments cover 1064 kilometers around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kyushu_Island" title="Kyushu Island">Kyushu Island</a> and it is the longest relay race in the world. Read more detail at <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2011/12/quick-guide-to-championship-ekiden.html" rel="nofollow">Japan Running News</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Japan">In Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>One of the most popular modern ekiden in Japan is the <a href="/wiki/Hakone_Ekiden" title="Hakone Ekiden">Hakone Ekiden</a>, which features teams of 10 male students from various Japanese universities of the Tokyo (Kanto) region. This race from central Tokyo along Tokyo Bay, past Yokohama to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakone,_Kanagawa" title="Hakone, Kanagawa">Hakone</a> and back is held over two days at the New Year, covering 219 kilometers. It is a popular spectator sport that draws large crowds (a million or more) along the whole route and receives <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2011/12/watch-new-year-ekiden-and-hakone-ekiden.html" rel="nofollow">full network television coverage nationwide</a> over the two days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Otake_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Otake-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Runners in the race compete to set individual records (e.g., stage times, number of schools passed) as well as to support their teams, and the race is considered to display many aspects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_culture" title="Japanese culture">Japanese culture</a> and spirit,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Otake_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Otake-2">[2]</a></sup> including individual perseverance, identity within a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Group_(sociology)" title="Group (sociology)">group</a>, and the importance within the Japanese hierarchy of allegiance to a major university. In the latter matter, there is a similarity to American school sports allegiance. The competitive level <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2012/01/2012-hakone-ekiden-and-2011-ncaa.html" rel="nofollow">compares well</a> with the highest levels of American universities. This comparison is based on participants' (Hakone Ekiden and NCAA Division 1 championships) personal best marks at 5000 meters, although in the Hakone Ekiden each runner covers just over a half marathon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secondary_schools">Secondary schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Secondary schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_to_cross_country">Comparison to cross country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Comparison to cross country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the athletes, ekiden provides an experience quite different from for track and field. In Japanese secondary and tertiary education, ekiden racing gives the school's long distance runners a team racing experience that <a class="external text" href="http://espn.go.com/blog/high-school/track-and-xc/post/_/id/731/ekiden-a-closer-look-at-the-japanese-way" rel="nofollow">parallels but is yet different from</a> <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_running" title="Cross country running">cross country running</a> as practiced in North American secondary and tertiary schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn.go.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-espn.go.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>School-based teams and competition in the name of one's school are highly prized concepts common to both Japan and North America. It has been pointed out that such fervor over school sports is not shared by all, such as Korea where after-school academic clubs are more the norm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> a point of worry for some track and field enthusiasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other similarities between high school cross country as conducted in North America and high school ekiden in Japan include the season (autumn), approximate team size (5 to 7 competitors), and competition taking place on non-track venues. The differences are significant.</p>
<p>Unlike the American school sports clear distinction of "track season" and "cross country season", the Japanese autumn ekiden races are intermingled with standard track competitions, although many autumn track meets have a narrow focus, calling themselves "long distance" meets. Japan has a very highly structured format leading to a national high school championship for track in early August and for ekiden in late December,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn.go.com_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-espn.go.com-5">[5]</a></sup> whereas the USA school format ends at state championships in both sports. Japanese school ekiden teams compete infrequently, some only once or twice between September and December, unless they qualify to go on to higher levels; nationals are held in late December. American school cross country teams typically race almost weekly during September and October, with a narrowed set of school teams competing for state championships in late October or November. December post season American races like the <a href="/wiki/Nike_Cross_Nationals" title="Nike Cross Nationals">Nike Cross Nationals</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker_Cross_Country_Championships" title="Foot Locker Cross Country Championships">Foot Locker Cross Country Championships</a> are not officially school based.</p>
<p>Ekidens are usually road races, and as such require intensive route management and police traffic control. Cross country races can include grass, mud, sand, and steep slopes. Each ekiden competitor runs a different distance, whereas all cross country runners run the same course and start at the same time . Some Japanese high school ekiden stages are longer and some stages are shorter than the "standard" 5000 meters for USA high school cross country (see below). Ekiden competition does not highlight the single individual winner of the race as in cross country, but ekiden analysis does highlight individual fastest runners in each stage; these runners may be in the middle of the field. Each ekiden runner has to run without reference to his or her own teammates for help in judging pace,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn.go.com_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-espn.go.com-5">[5]</a></sup> whereas cross country team races can include strategies like "pack" running. This same contrast can be made between track and ekiden; only the runners in the first ekiden stage run as a group; later stage runners must judge for themselves the appropriate pace and the decisions to challenge and pass other runners, whose abilities might be significantly different and whose position was determined by the efforts of preceding teammates.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-Japan_High_School_Ekiden_Championships">All-Japan High School Ekiden Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: All-Japan High School Ekiden Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/All-Japan_High_School_Ekiden_Championship" title="All-Japan High School Ekiden Championship">All-Japan High School Ekiden Championship</a></div>
<p><b>National championships</b> are held for junior high and high school teams in late December. A series of city <a class="external text" href="http://www.flotrack.org/video/363400-Tokyo-High-School-Ekiden-Championships-2010" rel="nofollow">(2010 Tokyo city championships video)</a>, prefecture and regional championships in November (<a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2008/11/national-roundup-of-all-japan-high.html" rel="nofollow">Japanrunningnews</a>) select the representative school teams for the national championship in late December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_format">Race format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Although there is no single definition of ekiden format, for purposes of the Japanese national championships for junior high school and high school, the format has been set. High school girls cover a half marathon; 5 runners follow the 6 – 4.1 – 3 – 3 – 5 kilometer pattern (total 21.1 kilometers).　High school boys cover a full marathon; 7 runners follow the 10 – 3 – 8.1075 – 8.0875 – 3 – 5 – 5 kilometer pattern (total 42.195 kilometers).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> See the Kyoto <a class="external text" href="http://www9.nhk.or.jp/rr-live/race01/index4.html" rel="nofollow">2011 girls course map</a>; <a class="external text" href="http://www9.nhk.or.jp/rr-live/race02/index4.html" rel="nofollow">2011 boys course map</a>; an interactive map for 2011 <a class="external text" href="http://www.koukouekiden.jp/course/map.html" rel="nofollow">showing elevation change</a> too.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-Japan_junior_high_school_ekiden_championship">All-Japan junior high school ekiden championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: All-Japan junior high school ekiden championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In mid December, 48 teams representing all the regions and prefectures of Japan gather in Yamaguchi City of Yamaguchi Prefecture for the annual All-Japan Junior High School Ekiden Championships. For junior high school girls, a team of 5 girls run stages that follow the 3 – 2 – 2 – 2 – 3 kilometer pattern (total 12 kilometers). The boys team consists of 6 runners following the 3 – 3 – 3 – 3 – 3 – 3 kilometer pattern (total 18 kilometers).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The 2011 race was the 19th annual competition. The junior high championship ekiden is not a road race but rather each runner runs a loop within the confines of a park. The boys team (6 boys) complete 18 kilometers, in a 3k-3k-3k-3k-3k-3k pattern. The girls team (5 runners) complete 12 kilometers, in a 3k-2k-2k-2k-3k pattern. In 2008 the winning boys team had an average pace of 5:01.9/mile or 9:26/3000 meters; the girls team had an average pace of 5:21.6/mile or just over 10:00/3000 meters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Further articles in Japanese: <a class="external text" href="http://www.diciotto.com/ekiden16/link.html" rel="nofollow">2008 Meet Site</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://rrn.at.webry.info/200812/article_4.html" rel="nofollow">2008 Team Results</a>; <a class="external text" href="http://www.diciotto.com/ekiden17/link.html" rel="nofollow">2009 Meet Site</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://rrn.at.webry.info/200912/article_4.html" rel="nofollow">2009 Team Results</a>; <a class="external text" href="http://www.diciotto.com/ekiden18/link.html" rel="nofollow">2010 Meet Site</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://rrn.at.webry.info/201012/article_5.html" rel="nofollow">2010 Team Results</a>; <a class="external text" href="http://www.diciotto.com/ekiden19/link.html" rel="nofollow">2011 Meet Site</a>.</p>
<p>Many of the elite high school and junior high school runners from these two races will be selected to represent their prefectures in the <a href="/wiki/All-Japan_Interprefectural_Ekiden_Championships" title="All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championships">All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championships</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_and_regional">Local and regional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Local and regional">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nihonkai_Boys_Ekiden_.E2.80.93_Nihonkai_Kurayoshi_Girls_Ekiden">Nihonkai Boys Ekiden – Nihonkai Kurayoshi Girls Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Nihonkai Boys Ekiden – Nihonkai Kurayoshi Girls Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A 5-stage 21.0975 km high school girls ekiden is held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurayoshi" title="Kurayoshi">Kurayoshi, Tottori</a>. Ninety high school teams participated in the 26th annual meet on October 2, 2011. <a class="external text" href="http://www.nnn.co.jp/event/kurayosi/youkou.html" rel="nofollow">meet website</a>. In the boys race, the 31st annual <b>Nihonkai Ekiden</b>, 128 teams participated in the 7 stage race. Both the boys and girls races are exactly the same configuration as the national high school championships.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Japan_Women.27s_Ekiden">East Japan Women's Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: East Japan Women's Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>The annual <b>East Japan Women's Ekiden</b> is held in <a href="/wiki/Fukushima,_Fukushima" title="Fukushima, Fukushima">Fukushima, Fukushima</a>. Nine stages cover 42.195 kilometers. It is a regional version of the Empress Cup National Women's Ekiden in January, with 18 prefectural teams made up of runners ranging from junior high to professional. The winning team in the 24th annual running in 2008 was from Tokyo, with a time of 2:19:15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>In addition to the above mention <a href="/wiki/Hakone_Ekiden" title="Hakone Ekiden">Hakone Ekiden</a> <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9D%B1%E4%BA%AC%E7%AE%B1%E6%A0%B9%E9%96%93%E5%BE%80%E5%BE%A9%E5%A4%A7%E5%AD%A6%E9%A7%85%E4%BC%9D%E7%AB%B6%E8%B5%B0" title="ja:東京箱根間往復大学駅伝競走">Japanese Wiki page</a> at the beginning of the new year, there are many other collegiate ekiden races.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Izumo_Ekiden">Izumo Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Izumo Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%87%BA%E9%9B%B2%E5%85%A8%E6%97%A5%E6%9C%AC%E5%A4%A7%E5%AD%A6%E9%81%B8%E6%8A%9C%E9%A7%85%E4%BC%9D%E7%AB%B6%E8%B5%B0" title="ja:出雲全日本大学選抜駅伝競走">w:ja:出雲全日本大学選抜駅伝競走</a></div>
<p>The Izumo Ekiden in <a href="/wiki/Izumo,_Shimane" title="Izumo, Shimane">Izumo City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shimane_Prefecture" title="Shimane Prefecture">Shimane Prefecture</a> of western Japan marks the beginning of the collegiate ekiden season and is for male university athletes. Teams come from all over Japan. The race is 6 stages (8k, 5.8k, 8.5k, 6.5k, 5k, 10.2k.) covering 43.1 kilometers. The 20th annual race was held on October 13, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Since 1998, a select team of Ivy league alumni runners from the United States has competed and found the competition quite stiff. The Ivy league representatives have finished in the last half of the field each time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In the October 10, 2011 race, the Ivy League Team of <a href="/wiki/Christo_Landry" title="Christo Landry">Christopher Landry</a>, Scott Smith, Landon Peacock, Andrew Benford, Thomas Robbins and Jonathan Grey finished 8th out of 21 teams <a class="external text" href="http://www.izumo-ekiden.jp/record/record.html" rel="nofollow">official results website</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-Japan_Collegiate_Women.27s_Ekiden_Championship_.28Morinomiyako_Ekiden.29">All-Japan Collegiate Women's Ekiden Championship (Morinomiyako Ekiden)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: All-Japan Collegiate Women's Ekiden Championship (Morinomiyako Ekiden)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Japan_Collegiate_Ekiden_Championships" title="Japan Collegiate Ekiden Championships">Japan_Collegiate_Ekiden_Championships</a></div>
<p>The annual All-Japan Collegiate Women's Ekiden Championship, begun in 1981 and also called Morinomiyako Ekiden, is held in <a href="/wiki/Sendai" title="Sendai">Sendai</a> <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Prefecture" title="Miyagi Prefecture">Miyage Prefecture</a> at the end of October. It is a 6-stage, 38.6 kilometer race from Miyagi Track and Field Grounds to Sendai City Hall. <a class="external text" href="http://www.ntv.co.jp/morinomiyako/course/index.html" rel="nofollow">Video showing Sendai course and terrain</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-Japan_Collegiate_.28Men.27s.29_Ekiden_Championship">All-Japan Collegiate (Men's) Ekiden Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: All-Japan Collegiate (Men's) Ekiden Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Japan_Collegiate_Ekiden_Championships" title="Japan Collegiate Ekiden Championships">Japan_Collegiate_Ekiden_Championships</a></div>
<p>On the first Sunday of November, the men compete in the 8 stage, 106.8 kilometer National Collegiate Ekiden Championship in Aichi and Mie Prefectures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This race is the second of the season's big three, the first being the Izumo Ekiden and the last being the Hakone Ekiden. Here, 27 teams battle for the national title. The race starts in front of <a href="/wiki/Atsuta_Shrine" title="Atsuta Shrine">Atsuta Shrine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya City</a> and then proceeds out of the city, along the coast and 106.8 kilometers later to <a href="/wiki/Ise_Grand_Shrine" title="Ise Grand Shrine">Ise Grand Shrine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ise,_Mie" title="Ise, Mie">Ise, Mie Prefecture</a>. Two versions of the course map are available: <a class="external text" href="http://daigaku-ekiden.com/coursemap/" rel="nofollow">the meet website's own map</a>, and <a class="external text" href="http://www.tv-asahi.co.jp/ekiden/courseMap/index.html" rel="nofollow">TV Asahi's google map version</a>. TV Asahi provides <a class="external text" href="http://www.tv-asahi.co.jp/ekiden/highlight/index.html" rel="nofollow">2011 video highlights</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biwako_University_Ekiden">Biwako University Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Biwako University Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>More than 20 Kansai area universities gather for the eight-stage, 83.6 km Biwako University Ekiden. It has been likened to the Kanto area's Hakone Ekiden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shikoku_Ekiden">Shikoku Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Shikoku Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Shikoku Ekiden is five-stage, 21.0975 km, run November 20, 2011.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All-Japan_Collegiate_Women.27s_Invitational_Ekiden">All-Japan Collegiate Women's Invitational Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: All-Japan Collegiate Women's Invitational Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%85%A8%E6%97%A5%E6%9C%AC%E5%A4%A7%E5%AD%A6%E5%A5%B3%E5%AD%90%E9%81%B8%E6%8A%9C%E9%A7%85%E4%BC%9D%E7%AB%B6%E8%B5%B0%E5%A4%A7%E4%BC%9A" title="ja:全日本大学女子選抜駅伝競走大会">w:ja:全日本大学女子選抜駅伝競走大会</a></div>
<p>Tsukuba, Ibaraki Prefecture, is host to the All-Japan Collegiate Women's Invitational Ekiden, the 6th annual having been held on December 23‚ 2008. The race is 6 stages and a distance of 30.67 kilometers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%85%A8%E6%97%A5%E6%9C%AC%E5%A4%A7%E5%AD%A6%E5%A5%B3%E5%AD%90%E9%81%B8%E6%8A%9C%E9%A7%85%E4%BC%9D%E7%AB%B6%E8%B5%B0%E5%A4%A7%E4%BC%9A" title="ja:全日本大学女子選抜駅伝競走大会">(Japanese wiki page)</a>. <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2009/12/bukkyo-ends-ritsumeikans-national-reign.html" rel="nofollow">2009 results in English</a>. The competition was recessed for 2010 and 2011, with hopes of securing sponsorships in 2012 or later, says the Inter-University Athletic Union of Japan <a class="external text" href="http://www.iuau.jp/ev2011/8jyosi-senbatsu/8js_annai.pdf" rel="nofollow">PDF</a>.</p>

The <b>Empress Cup – Interprefectural Women's Ekiden</b> is held in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> (<a class="external text" href="http://www.womens-ekiden.jp/course/map.html" rel="nofollow">course map</a>), on the third Sunday in January. <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_All-Japan_Interprefectural_Ekiden_Championship">Men's All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Men's All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/All-Japan_Interprefectural_Ekiden_Championships" title="All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championships">All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championships</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/All-Japan_Interprefectural_Ekiden_Championships" title="All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championships">Men's All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championship (<b>RESULTS</b>)</a>, 7 runners run 48 kilometers in stages of 7k 3k 8.5k 5k 8.5k 3k and 13k<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> (<a class="external text" href="http://www.hiroshima-ekiden.com/information/course.html" rel="nofollow">Hiroshima course map</a>). In both races, each team is composed of runners selected to represent one prefecture, a total of 47 teams in each race. The majority of the team members are high school and junior high school elite runners. Junior high runners are assigned to the two 3k stages and high school runners are assigned to the 7k, the 5k and 8.5k stages. Open runners are placed in the remaining 8.5k stage and the 13k stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2009 the team from Nagano took its 5th title in 6 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Results and discussion <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2009/01/nagano-breaks-national-interprefectural.html" rel="nofollow">2009</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2010/01/shades-of-seko-kensuke-takezawa.html" rel="nofollow">2010</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2011/01/ugachi-anchors-tochigis-first-national.html" rel="nofollow">2011</a>, <a class="external autonumber" href="http://2012" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> in English. <a class="external text" href="http://www9.nhk.or.jp/rr-live/" rel="nofollow">Live 2012 internet and television coverage</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.hiroshima-ekiden.com/" rel="nofollow">official meet website</a> in Japanese.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_All-Japan_Interprefectural_Ekiden_Championship_.E2.80.93_The_Empress_Cup">Women's All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championship – The Empress Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Women's All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championship – The Empress Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Inter-Prefectural_Women%27s_Ekiden" title="Inter-Prefectural Women's Ekiden">Inter-Prefectural Women's Ekiden</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Inter-Prefectural_Women%27s_Ekiden" title="Inter-Prefectural Women's Ekiden">Women's All-Japan Interprefectural Ekiden Championships</a> – Empress Cup – is held in Kyoto on the third Sunday in January. The 2012 running marks the 30th annual event. Forty-seven teams from the 47 <a href="/wiki/Prefectures_of_Japan" title="Prefectures of Japan">prefectures</a> of Japan compete. The course is the same as the course for the high school boys national ekiden race held on the third Sunday of December (see above) (<a class="external text" href="http://www.womens-ekiden.jp/course/map.html" rel="nofollow">map in Japanese</a>), except that the women's relay has 9 stages (rather than 7) over the 42.195 kilometers. The stages are 6k, 4k, 3k, 4k, 4.0175k, 4.0875k, 4k, 3k, and 10k. The two 3k stages are reserved for junior high athletes and generally high school athletes run the five stages that are 4k or just over 4k. Collegiate and open runners take the 6k and 10k stages. The mixed-age squads maintain a rapid pace.</p>
<p>In 2009, the team from Kyoto took its 13th championship and, as of that year, in the ekiden's 20 years Kyoto has only twice placed outside the top 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Okayama took its first ever title in 2010, Kyoto reclaimed it in 2011, and in 2012 Kyoto finished second, a minute back from the winning team from neighboring Osaka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="School-Age_participants">School-Age participants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: School-Age participants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Of the 94 junior high school boys running the 3k stages in 2009, 62 were between 8:30 and 9:10. Of the 94 junior high school girls running 3k stages, 39 were between 9:21 and 10:00. Of the 188 high school girls running the 4 legs of 4000m to 4175m, 68 were under 10:00/3k pace. The 141 high school boys ran 3 distances: 7k (34 were between 20:10 and 21:00) or 5k (25 were between 14:28 and 15:00) or 8.5k (28 were between 23:55 and 26:00).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Chiba_Ekiden.7CChiba_International_Ekiden">International_Chiba_Ekiden|Chiba International Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: International Chiba Ekiden|Chiba International Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/International_Chiba_Ekiden" title="International Chiba Ekiden">International Chiba Ekiden</a></div>
<p>Inaugurated in 1988, the November <a href="/wiki/International_Chiba_Ekiden" title="International Chiba Ekiden">Chiba International Ekiden (<b>2008–2011 results posted</b>)</a> was two separate races – one for men and one for women. From 2007, it has changed format into a combine male/female race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 2008, 13 teams from 11 countries participated; in 2011 it was 12 teams from 10 countries. The race is contested in six stages over 42.195 kilometers, exactly a marathon. <a class="external text" href="http://www.inter-chibaekiden.jp/map.html" rel="nofollow">Course MAP</a>. The race begins and ends at the Chiba Sports Center Track and Field Complex (on the monorail). The first 5k stage, third 10k stage, and fifth 10k stage are reserved as men's stages, while 5k second stage, 5k fourth stage and 7.195k sixth stage are reserved for women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Race commentaries in English: <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2007/11/japan-wins-2007-international-chiba.html" rel="nofollow">2007</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2008/11/teenaged-ethiopian-squad-breaks.html" rel="nofollow">2008</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2009/11/japanese-juggernaut-takes-back-intl.html" rel="nofollow">2009</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2010/11/japanese-university-select-team-takes.html" rel="nofollow">2010</a> <a class="external text" href="http://japanrunningnews.blogspot.com/2011/11/kenya-over-japan-as-both-break.html" rel="nofollow">2011</a>.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_International_Women%27s_Ekiden" title="Yokohama International Women's Ekiden">Yokohama International Women`s Ekiden</a>, from 2000 to 2008, was won 7 times by foreign teams. <b><a href="/wiki/Yokohama_International_Women%27s_Ekiden" title="Yokohama International Women's Ekiden">Results 1983–2009</a></b>. The 27th annual running of the race on February 22, 2009, was the final time. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asahi_Ekiden">Asahi Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Asahi Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%9D%E6%97%A5%E9%A7%85%E4%BC%9D" title="ja:朝日駅伝">w:ja:朝日駅伝</a></div>

The 60th annual version was held on January 12, 2009. A mixture of corporate and university teams competed, 21 teams taking part in the 7-stage, 99.9 kilometer ekiden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <a class="external text" href="http://www2.kbc.co.jp/sports/asahi-ekiden/" rel="nofollow">race web site (in Japanese)</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www2.kbc.co.jp/sports/asahi-ekiden/coursemap/" rel="nofollow">map</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_Kita-Kyushu_Ekiden">Women's Kita-Kyushu Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Women's Kita-Kyushu Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The corporate women's teams take their turn, racing in the <b>Women's Kita-Kyushu Ekiden</b>. The 20th annual race was held on January 18, 2009, from Fukuoka to Kokura on Japan's southern island of Kyushu. <a class="external text" href="http://www.rkb.ne.jp/sports_top/ekiden/kitakyu/09juni_open.html" rel="nofollow">results (in Japanese)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The distance is 32.8 kilometers. The open division women run it in 5 stages and the high school women's teams run it in 6 stages. First place in 2009 was 1:43:56 for the open and 1:47:22 for the high school. Eighth place for both was just over 1:50. 1:50 is about a 10:03 3k pace. The 2012 race was the 23rd competition; <a class="external text" href="http://rkb.jp/sports_top/movie/ekiden/11start.php" rel="nofollow">official video of 2012 race</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kanto_Region">Kanto Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: Kanto Region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kanto Region</a> is a region of Japan, and the south and western portions includes Tokyo, Saitama, Chiba and Kanagawa prefectures. The winter months provide a full schedule of community races. The races below are listed in calendar order, starting in October. The listing is not exhaustive for the region and does not include the exclusively secondary and tertiary school and professional ekidens which are linked to the prefecture and national championships (see above). And the many other regions of Japan would have similar calendars.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tamagawa_Ekiden_Carnival">Tamagawa Ekiden Carnival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: Tamagawa Ekiden Carnival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Saturday in October (October 5, 2011) is the date set for the <a href="/wiki/Tama_River" title="Tama River">Tamagawa</a> Ekiden Carnival. The race is an out-and-back relay along the <a href="/wiki/Tama_River" title="Tama River">Tama River</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://ekiden-carnival.jp/tamagawa/course.html" rel="nofollow">map</a>. Each runner goes out and returns to the central meet site. The long course version is 23 kilometers (10 km～5 km～3 km～5 km), and the short course version is 12 kilometers (5 km～3 km～1 km～3 km). The race is held at Furuichi track and field facility (古市場陸上競技場), and a good map can be found at the facility's Facebook page: <a class="external text" href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/古市場陸上競技場/140232956047376" rel="nofollow">race location map</a>; clicking brings up a <a href="/wiki/Bing_Maps" title="Bing Maps">Bing</a> map. This ekiden strives to be inclusive of all ages and abilities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Japan_International_.28Sagamihara.29_Ekiden">East Japan International (Sagamihara) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: East Japan International (Sagamihara) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Sunday in October is when the East Japan International Ekiden (東日本国際駅伝) is held in (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sagamihara,_Kanagawa" title="Sagamihara, Kanagawa">Sagamihara, Kanagawa</a>). The meet proclaims itself for teams from "my pace" to "elite." It is held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sagamihara,_Kanagawa" title="Sagamihara, Kanagawa">Sagamihara</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sagami_General_Depot" title="Sagami General Depot">Sagami General Depot</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%9B%B8%E6%A8%A1%E7%B7%8F%E5%90%88%E8%A3%9C%E7%B5%A6%E5%BB%A0" title="ja:相模総合補給廠">ja:相模総合補給廠</a>); entrance via Gate 5. The proximity of American and Japanese culture lends the international flavor to the event. A post-race American lunch picnic is a special feature. The race event is sponsored by <a class="external text" href="http://www.runningkanagawa.com/eji_ekiden.html" rel="nofollow">Running Kanagawa</a> and various local governments.</p>
<p>There are entry categories for all ages and speeds. Relay distances are 25k, 20k, and 15k, all divided into 4 stages. Registration is usually closed by mid August. (Contact information 〒231-0004　横浜市中区元浜町3–21–2　ヘリオス関内ビル４Ｆ East Japan International Ekiden, <a class="external free" href="TEL:045-222-8412" rel="nofollow">TEL:045-222-8412</a>). The meet website proclaims that 2,645 teams were entered in 2011.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shounan_.28Hiratsuka_Kanagawa.29_Ekiden">Shounan (Hiratsuka Kanagawa) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Shounan (Hiratsuka Kanagawa) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The third Sunday in November (November 20, 2011) is the date for the Shounan Hiratsuka Ekiden (湘南ひらつか駅伝) in <a href="/wiki/Hiratsuka,_Kanagawa" title="Hiratsuka, Kanagawa">Hiratsuka (Kanagawa)</a> (<a class="external text" href="http://www.solius.jp/marathon/race/hiratsuka_ekiden.html" rel="nofollow">map and information</a>). The format is 4 stage race, but the distance per stage varies from 1 km (lower elementary) to 5 km (open). Male, female and mixed teams are welcomed, limited to 70 teams; individuals who come can be linked up with a team on day of meet.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Okutama_.28Tokyo.29_Ekiden">Okutama (Tokyo) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Okutama (Tokyo) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the first Sunday of December, the <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.ome.tokyo.jp/taiiku/okutama_taikai.html" rel="nofollow">Okutama Ekiden</a> has been run in the mountain valleys of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cme,_Tokyo" title="Ōme, Tokyo">Ōme</a> of Western Tokyo annually since 1936. It is a mixture of local university teams, serious running clubs and high school teams. In the December 4, 2011 race, 111 open teams, 29 college teams, 4 high school teams, and 50 women's teams participated.</p>
<p>One of the hallmarks of this ekiden is that it literally parallels a train line and races from station to station to station. <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.ome.tokyo.jp/taiiku/documents/kiseizu.pdf" rel="nofollow">Course map pdf</a>. Based on the 2011 race, men's teams run 6 stages totaling 47 kilometers (7.4 km – 8.4 km – 6.5 km – 6.3 km – 8.4 km – 7.4 km). This race begins 10:00 at the Ome City Hall. The women's ekiden is 3 stages over 11.1 kilometers (3.6 km – 3.6 km – 3.9 km). It begins at <a class="external text" href="http://postmap.org/map/16586" rel="nofollow">Mitake Station (御嶽駅前)</a> at 11:30. Slow teams are subject to a time limit. <a class="external text" href="http://www.geocities.jp/teamgamba/_gl_images_/123.jpg" rel="nofollow">Start picture.</a> <a class="external text" href="http://www.namban.org/index.php/reports/more/70th_okutama_ekiden_report" rel="nofollow">A foreign runner`s report</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saitama_Elementary_Ekiden">Saitama Elementary Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: Saitama Elementary Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Saturday of December marks the <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama</a> Elementary Ekiden, held at <a href="/wiki/Kumagaya_Athletic_Stadium" title="Kumagaya Athletic Stadium">Kumagaya Sports Park</a>. In 2011, 44 mixed club teams competed in the following pattern: girl boy girl boy girl boy. Each runner ran 1.5 kilometers. <a class="external text" href="http://sairiku.net/newinfo/kekka/2011/H231203er.pdf" rel="nofollow">Results</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saitama_Ekiden">Saitama Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: Saitama Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second Sunday of January (January 15, 2012) is the annual <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama</a> Ekiden. The 2012 race was the 79th annual. The open men's race is 6 stages covering 50.9 kilometers. High school boys cover 41.7 km in 6 stages. Open women and high school girls cover 22.8 km in 5 stages. Application for participation closed November 11, 2011. <a class="external text" href="http://www.pref.saitama.lg.jp/site/ekiden/" rel="nofollow">Meet website</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://wakaba-green-mate.cocolog-nifty.com/blog/saitamaekiden.html" rel="nofollow">secondary site</a>. The race begins in <a href="/wiki/Fukaya,_Saitama" title="Fukaya, Saitama">Fukaya City</a> and ends in <a href="/wiki/Ageo,_Saitama" title="Ageo, Saitama">Ageo</a>, with the shorter races starting at way points in between. Forty open boys and men's teams, 40 high school boys teams, and almost 20 high school girls teams typify recent participation levels. <a class="external text" href="http://sairiku.net/newinfo/kekka/2012/H240115r.pdf" rel="nofollow">2012 results in Japanese</a>.</p>
<p>A junior high race is conducted at the same time, the recent meets involving 60 junior high girls teams and 60 junior high boys. Girls run (3.2 km～2 km～2 km～2 km～3 km), and boys run (3.2 km～3 km～3 ～3 ～3 ～3 ). The junior high race is conducted within <a href="/wiki/Kumagaya_Athletic_Stadium" title="Kumagaya Athletic Stadium">Kumagaya Sports Park</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shibuya_.28Tokyo.29_New_Ekiden">Shibuya (Tokyo) New Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=45" title="Edit section: Shibuya (Tokyo) New Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the third Sunday of January, a four-stage 11.6 km ekiden is held annually in <a href="/wiki/Yoyogi_Park" title="Yoyogi Park">Yoyogi Park</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shibuya,_Tokyo" title="Shibuya, Tokyo">Shibuya (Tokyo)</a>.　The race is called Shibuya New Ekiden (しぶやニュー駅伝確定結果). The 58th annual running was January 20, 2008 and was attended by over 204 teams. See <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.shibuya.tokyo.jp/eng/current-topics.html" rel="nofollow">Mayor`s report in English</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.namban.org/index.php/news/more/2009_shibuya_ekiden/" rel="nofollow">a 2009 Namban Running Club report</a>.</p>
<p>The January 15, 2012 race was the 62nd annual race. According to the <a class="external text" href="http://www.shibutai.com/ekiden2012/2012kekka.html" rel="nofollow">2012 results page (a series of pdfs)</a>, 35 girls junior high teams, 72 boys junior high teams, 27 open women's teams, 152 open men's teams, and 26 over-40 teams participated, a significant growth in recent years. Additionally 25 other 7-member teams of mixed age (which must have male and female from upper elementary and from junior high, plus an over 40 contestant and an open male and female contest over high school) competed, by adding on two 1,350 meter stages for the elementary runners and an additional 2,900 meter stage for a total of 7 stages.</p>
<p>The ekiden is run in heats, so that each category has its own start time. Preregistration for this annual event is open during the second and third week of November, at the Shibuya Sports Center and the Ward Central Office.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sagamihara_.28Kanagawa.29_Ekiden">Sagamihara (Kanagawa) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=46" title="Edit section: Sagamihara (Kanagawa) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The third Sunday in January is the traditional date for the <a href="/wiki/Sagamihara" title="Sagamihara">Sagamihara (Kanagawa)</a> Ekiden, sponsored by the <a class="external text" href="http://www.jade.dti.ne.jp/taikyo/" rel="nofollow">Sagamihara Amateur Sports Association</a>. The race is held at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sagamihara_Asamizo_Park_Stadium" title="Sagamihara Asamizo Park Stadium">Asamizo-Stadium</a> park (park website in <a class="external text" href="http://www.asamizo-stadium.jpn.org/" rel="nofollow">Japanese</a>). The 2012 competition was the 72nd annual event.</p>
<p>Competition is categorized into junior high, high school, collegiate and open. Collegiate and open teams (10 in 2012) start the 6 stage race at 9:00 and run a 6-stage course around the outside of the park. The 2012 winning time was 1:53:12. The high school teams (20 in 2012, gender unspecified), starting at 9:10, do the same. Winning time in 2012 was 1:56:30. Open teams (69 teams in 2012, gender unspecified) start a 5-stage race at 9:20, using the same inside-the-park course that the junior high teams run later. The junior high 5-stage race begins at 10:30. In 2012, nearly 20 teams for each gender participated, the boys winner was 44:24 (1 minute slower than the open winner) and the girls winner was 51:06.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tokyo_German_Village_.28Chiba.29_Ekiden">Tokyo German Village (Chiba) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=47" title="Edit section: Tokyo German Village (Chiba) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The fourth Saturday in January has been designated for the <a class="external text" href="http://doitsu-ekiden.wizspo.jp/" rel="nofollow">Tokyo German Village Ekiden</a>, which began in 2011. January 28, 2012 was the second. The race is conducted in the park of <a class="external text" href="http://www.t-doitsumura.co.jp/access/index.php" rel="nofollow">Tokyo German Village, Chiba</a>. The 4-member teams complete 20 km, with each stage running two 2.5 km laps within the park. Pair runs and "chibiko" children's runs are also held. The event is limited to 600 teams. There is a post-race BBQ and beer garden. <a class="external text" href="http://doitsu-ekiden.wizspo.jp/page1/" rel="nofollow">Information and video</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="OkuMusashi_.28Saitama.29_Ekiden">OkuMusashi (Saitama) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=48" title="Edit section: OkuMusashi (Saitama) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the last Sunday of January, the OkuMusashi Ekiden (see <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A5%A5%E3%82%80%E3%81%95%E3%81%97%E9%A7%85%E4%BC%9D%E7%AB%B6%E8%B5%B0%E5%A4%A7%E4%BC%9A" title="ja:奥むさし駅伝競走大会">奥むさし駅伝競走大会</a>) has been held in <a href="/wiki/Hann%C5%8D,_Saitama" title="Hannō, Saitama">Hannō</a> City, <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, since 2002. A similar competition had been held for 48 years, but was ended in 1999. The current race is an ekiden, with participating teams being a mixture of local university teams, serious running clubs and high school teams; the vast majority are male teams. In 2012 (the tenth annual), 59 high school teams representing 52 high schools entered, along with 161 teams from universities and running clubs. Most university squads use this race to give younger runners a chance for competition, giving the high school teams a chance to finish near the top. <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.hanno.saitama.jp/taiikuka/ekiden/1index.html" rel="nofollow">Hanno city meet page</a> in Japanese.</p>

The course winds through the city of Hanno, then up the long, narrow valley toward Chichibu, with a turn-around at <a href="/wiki/Nishi-Agano_Station" title="Nishi-Agano Station">Nishi Agano station</a>. There are 6 legs or stages, the first three being up hill to Nishi Agano and the returning 3 descending back to Hanno City. The first and last leg are just over 9 kilometers, and the middle stages are between 4.2 and 5.4 k and the exchange points are situated near rail stations along the valley. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hachioji_.28Tokyo.29_All-Kanto_Yume_Kaido_Ekiden">Hachioji (Tokyo) All-Kanto Yume Kaido Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=50" title="Edit section: Hachioji (Tokyo) All-Kanto Yume Kaido Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the first Sunday of February, from in front of <a href="/wiki/Hachi%C5%8Dji_Station" title="Hachiōji Station">Hachioji JR Station</a>, the city of <a href="/wiki/Hachi%C5%8Dji,_Tokyo" title="Hachiōji, Tokyo">Hachiōji</a>-sponsored All-Kanto Hachioji Yume Kaido (Dream Street) Ekiden (全関東八王子夢街道駅伝競走大会) begins. <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.hachioji.tokyo.jp/event/sports/008875.html" rel="nofollow">Meet website</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.hachioji.tokyo.jp/dbps_data/_material_/localhost/soshiki/supotsushinkoka/yume_ekiden/ed62top02.jpg" rel="nofollow">2011 start picture</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.hachioji.tokyo.jp/dbps_data/_material_/localhost/soshiki/supotsushinkoka/yume_ekiden/58course.jpg" rel="nofollow">course map</a>. The course proceeds past <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nishi-Hachioji_Station" title="Nishi-Hachioji Station">Nishi Hachioji</a> Station and loops around Keio <a href="/wiki/Hazama_Station_(Tokyo)" title="Hazama Station (Tokyo)">Hazama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mejirodai_Station" title="Mejirodai Station">Meijirodai</a> stations.</p>
<p>The 2012 edition of the race is the 62nd annual. Since 2001 the meet has expanded its categories, with increased emphasis on junior high and on separating men's open and collegiate. For the February 5, 2012 meet, 203 men's open teams, 78 men's collegiate teams, 68 women's open (including a few collegeiate) teams, 72 boys' high school teams, 23 girls' high school teams, 25 boys' junior high teams and 16 girls' junior high teams were entered, nearly 500 relay teams. (Registration for the meet is open from first of November to first of December.) The printed meet program names approximately 1000 volunteers, not including innumerable police and traffic regulators along the 10 kilometers of city streets.</p>
<p>Hachioji is home to a large number of colleges. What is notable is that so few colleges and universities fielded a women's team for this race.</p>
<p>The race starts in two waves, at 10:00 (high school boys and collegiate and open men) and 10:10 (junior high boys and all women's teams). Teams in the first wave run 20.7 kilometers, divided into 4 stages (4.7k – 6.2k – 5.4k – 4.4k). The teams in the second wave run 14.9 kilometers; the high school and open women divide the distance into 4 stages (4.7k – 2.3k – 3.4k – 4.4k) and the junior high teams divide the distance into 5 stages. <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.hachioji.tokyo.jp/kyoiku/sports/ekiden/031260.html" rel="nofollow">Details in Japanese</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tokyo_International_Exchange_Ekiden">Tokyo International Exchange Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=51" title="Edit section: Tokyo International Exchange Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Tokyo International Exchange Ekiden (国際交流駅伝) is held on the second Saturday of February (February 11, 2012 – registration closes mid January). Information can be found at the non profit <a class="external text" href="http://jsie.org/" rel="nofollow">Japan Society for International Exchange in Sports</a>, with a <a class="external text" href="http://jsie.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/2012_ekiden_inTokyo.pdf" rel="nofollow">2012 informational pdf</a>. The race is limited to contestants 18 and older. Teams of three members link to relay the tasuki in three 5 km stages. The course begins and ends at <a href="/wiki/Takebashi_Station" title="Takebashi Station">Takebashi</a>, encompassing one loop around the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Imperial_Palace" title="Tokyo Imperial Palace">Tokyo Imperial Palace</a> for each stage.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kanagawa_Ekiden">Kanagawa Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=52" title="Edit section: Kanagawa Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa_Prefecture" title="Kanagawa Prefecture">Kanagawa</a> Ekiden, held on the second Sunday of February, is an inter-city competition. Teams represent cites or towns, and are composed of a mixture of junior high, high school and open/collegiate runners. Junior high The 2012 competition is the 66th annual meet. Since 2003, the course has been a 51.5 kilometer race (in seven stages) from <a href="/wiki/Hadano,_Kanagawa" title="Hadano, Kanagawa">Hadano</a> City's Chuo Sports Park (秦野市中央運動公園) to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Sagami" title="Lake Sagami">Lake Sagami</a> <a class="external text" href="http://www.kanagawa-park.or.jp/cityparkguidance/sagamiko/" rel="nofollow">State Park</a> (県立相模湖公園). Hadano city is not far from the US <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Facility_Atsugi" title="Naval Air Facility Atsugi">Atsugi Naval Air Facility</a>. Lake Sagami is west of Hachioji and <a href="/wiki/Mount_Takao" title="Mount Takao">Takao</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sagamiko_Station" title="Sagamiko Station">Sagamiko Station</a> is one stop beyond Takao, with 2 or 3 local trains per hour connecting. The Sagamiko State Park is a ten-minute walk from the station.</p>
<p>The race starts at 9:00 and runners will start appearing at Sagamiko State Park shortly after 11:30.</p>
<p>The teams for this race are mostly, but not entirely, students. Though it is a competition between communities, participation is not open to the general running public. <a class="external text" href="http://www.jade.dti.ne.jp/taikyo/kana-ekiden/kana-ekiden.htm" rel="nofollow">Results</a> of present and past competitions, in Japanese. The race format places junior high boys in the first leg (3.0 km) and junior high or high school girls in the 4th leg (2.7 km). The other 5 legs are longer, usually run by males, and with no age restrictions.</p>

A 22.2 km 4 stage relay (8.2～4.7～4.7～4.6) for junior high age and up, and an 14.3 km 4 stage "Enjoy" relay (5.1～3.1～3.1～3.0) for elementary and up. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tamagawa_Riverside_Ekiden">Tamagawa Riverside Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=54" title="Edit section: Tamagawa Riverside Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kawasaki_City" title="Kawasaki City">Kawasaki City</a> hosts the Tamagawa Riverside Ekiden in Kawasaki on the third Sunday of March (March 18, 2012). It runs along the Tamagawa, or the <a href="/wiki/Tama_River" title="Tama River">Tama River</a>. The competition is limited to 1350 teams, and registration is closed by the first of February.</p>
<p>Competition is divided into three categories: family/kids (2 km～1 km～1 km～2 km), short for junior high and above (3 km～3 km～2 km～1 km), and long for high school and above (10 km～5 km～3 km～3 km～5 km). Mixed teams are permitted and masters (40+) teams can run the long course. <a class="external text" href="http://www.solius.jp/marathon/race/tamagawa-riverekiden.html" rel="nofollow">Website includes map</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.kawasaki.jp/25/25sports/home/ekiden/service.html" rel="nofollow">another map</a>; <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.kawasaki.jp/25/25sports/home/ekiden/index.html" rel="nofollow">host city meet website</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tama-ko_.28Tokyo.29_Ekiden">Tama-ko (Tokyo) Ekiden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=55" title="Edit section: Tama-ko (Tokyo) Ekiden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1182" data-file-width="2780" height="94" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg/220px-LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg/330px-LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg/440px-LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LakeTama_and_SayamaMap.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<b>Tama-ko</b> is the lower of two man-made lakes west of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tokorozawa-city&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tokorozawa-city (page does not exist)">Tokorozawa</a> and partially within <a href="/wiki/Higashiyamato,_Tokyo" title="Higashiyamato, Tokyo">Higashi Yamato City</a>. Another <a class="external text" href="http://www.u.arizona.edu/~sandiway/bike/tamako/map.jpg" rel="nofollow">map (reverse angle)</a>.</div>

<p>The <b>Tama-ko Ekiden</b> near Lake Tama on the <a class="external text" href="http://www.u.arizona.edu/~sandiway/bike/tamako/index.html" rel="nofollow">Lake Tama Bicycle Trail</a> is an example of a citizen's ekiden, hosted by the city of <a href="/wiki/Higashiyamato,_Tokyo" title="Higashiyamato, Tokyo">Higashi Yamato</a> in western Tokyo, south of the lake.</p>
<p>The Tama-ko Ekiden has two versions. The men's open and high school boys' (may include girl members) teams run a combined 4-stage relay of 28.968 km around Lake Tama. The remaining contestants of a wide assortment of categories run a 4-stage race of 9.628 km within the confines of <a class="external text" href="http://www.sayamaparks.com/sayama/" rel="nofollow">Sayama Koen</a> park; <a class="external text" href="http://www.sayamaparks.com/sayama/images/Illustmap3-2.gif" rel="nofollow">(Park map)</a>.</p>
<p>The race is held on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shunbun_no_hi" title="Shunbun no hi">Vernal Equinox – 春分の日</a>, which in 2012 is on Tuesday, March 20. It is a national holiday in Japan. Registration typically ends January 31; there is a limit of 300 teams. In 2009, over 240 4-person teams competed, representing all ages. <a class="external text" href="http://www.city.higashiyamato.lg.jp/events/detail.1.35376.html" rel="nofollow">Meet information in Japanese</a>; Local contact information: 電話：042-563-2111, 〒207–8585　 東京都東大和市中央3–930.</p>
<p>The race of 2012 is the 22nd annual event. Interestingly, for many of the first 20 years, the reconstruction of the dam forming Lake Tama forced the event organizers to eliminate the round-the-lake format and use local streets instead of the park.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hawaii">Hawaii</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=61" title="Edit section: Hawaii">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Honolulu Rainbow EKIDEN:</b> The Honolulu Rainbow EKIDEN began in March 3, 2013 as Hawaii's first "Ekiden" relay race for amateur runners. The event features teams consisting of 3 to 5 runners who will compete in a long distance relay race. Each runner will run from Kapiolani Park to Diamond Head and return to Kapiolani Park where they will hand over a sash (ribbon rei) to the next runner who will run the same course. Each lap is approx 5 km (3.1 miles).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Honolulu Ekiden &amp; Music Festival:</b> The first annual Honolulu Ekiden &amp; Music Festival was held on May 12, 2013. Certified by the USATF the course consists of six different sections; the course totals 42.195 km/26 miles . Starting at the base of Diamond Head at Kapiolani Park, the course progresses eastward along the coast and winds past Hanauma Bay wiith a turnaround point at Sandy Beach Park. A maximum number of six (6) runners may participate on a team. For teams with less than six members, a runner or runners may run more than one section consecutively. Individual runners are also welcome to participate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
