<p>The <b>2006 FIFA Club World Cup Final</b> took place at the <a href="/wiki/International_Stadium_Yokohama" title="International Stadium Yokohama">International Stadium Yokohama</a>, Japan on 17 December 2006.</p>
<p>The match pitted <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Internacional" title="Sport Club Internacional">Internacional</a> of Brazil, the <a href="/wiki/CONMEBOL" title="CONMEBOL">CONMEBOL</a> club champions, against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> of Spain, the <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> club champions. Internacional won 1–0, after a counter-attack led by <a href="/wiki/Iarley" title="Iarley">Iarley</a> and the goal scored by <a href="/wiki/Adriano_Gabiru" title="Adriano Gabiru">Adriano Gabiru</a> at the 82nd minute, in a match watched by 67,128 people. In doing so, Internacional won their first <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="FIFA Club World Cup">FIFA Club World Cup</a>/<a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_Cup_(football)" title="Intercontinental Cup (football)">Intercontinental Cup</a> and Barcelona remained without any world club title. <a href="/wiki/Deco" title="Deco">Deco</a> was named as <a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" title="Man of the match">man of the match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoM_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-MoM-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
