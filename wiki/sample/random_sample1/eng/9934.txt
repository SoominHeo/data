<p><i><b>Yeon Gaesomun</b></i> (<span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">연개소문</span>; <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">淵蓋蘇文</span>) is the title of a television historical drama.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2006, the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> television station <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> began a 100-episode drama on the life of <a href="/wiki/Yeon_Gaesomun" title="Yeon Gaesomun">Yeon Gaesomun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its production cost was very high, about 40 billion <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_won" title="South Korean won">South Korean won</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeon_Gaesomun_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yeon Gae Somun is the general who killed the penultimate King of Goguryeo, Yeongnyu, in 642. It was a period of tremendous instability, both because of the pressure by Shilla, but also because the political situation in Goguryeo was very confusing. After General Yeon put Yeongnyu’s nephew Bojang as his puppet on the throne following his coup d’etat, he tried with little success to calm down Yeon’s two sons in trying to gain power, and started repressing Buddhism (the Kingdom’s official religion) in favour of Taoism.</p>
