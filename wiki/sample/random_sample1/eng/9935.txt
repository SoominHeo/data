For a fibre bundle <i>F</i> with structure group <i>G</i>, the transition functions of the fibre (i.e., the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cocycle_(algebraic_topology)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cocycle (algebraic topology) (page does not exist)">cocycle</a>) in an overlap of two coordinate systems <i>U</i><sub>α</sub> and <i>U</i><sub>β</sub> are given as a <i>G</i>-valued function <i>g</i><sub>αβ</sub> on <i>U</i><sub>α</sub>∩<i>U</i><sub>β</sub>. One may then construct a fibre bundle <i>F</i>′ as a new fibre bundle having the same transition functions, but possibly a different fibre.</p>

This then specializes to the case when the specified fibre is a <a href="/wiki/Principal_homogeneous_space" title="Principal homogeneous space">principal homogeneous space</a> for the left action of the group on itself, yielding the associated principal bundle. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Associated_bundles_in_general">Associated bundles in general</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Associated_bundle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Associated bundles in general">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Let π : <i>E</i> → <i>X</i> be a fiber bundle over a <a href="/wiki/Topological_space" title="Topological space">topological space</a> <i>X</i> with structure group <i>G</i> and typical fibre <i>F</i>. By definition, there is a <a href="/wiki/Group_action" title="Group action">left action</a> of <i>G</i> (as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transformation_group" title="Transformation group">transformation group</a>) on the fibre <i>F</i>. Suppose furthermore that this action is <a href="/wiki/Group_action#Types_of_actions" title="Group action">effective</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> There is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Locally_trivial" title="Locally trivial">local trivialization</a> of the bundle <i>E</i> consisting of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_cover" title="Open cover">open cover</a> <i>U</i><sub>i</sub> of <i>X</i>, and a collection of <a href="/wiki/Bundle_map" title="Bundle map">fibre maps</a></p>
<dl>
<dd>φ<sub>i</sub> : π<sup>−1</sup>(<i>U</i><sub>i</sub>) → <i>U</i><sub>i</sub> × <i>F</i></dd>
</dl>
<p>such that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transition_map" title="Transition map">transition maps</a> are given by elements of <i>G</i>. More precisely, there are continuous functions <i>g</i><sub>ij</sub> : (<i>U</i><sub>i</sub> ∩ <i>U</i><sub>j</sub>) → <i>G</i> such that</p>
<dl>
<dd>ψ<sub>ij</sub>(<i>u</i>,<i>f</i>) := φ<sub>i</sub> o φ<sub>j</sub><sup>−1</sup>(<i>u</i>,<i>f</i>) = (<i>u</i>,<i>g</i><sub>ij</sub>(<i>u</i>)<i>f</i>) for each (<i>u</i>,<i>f</i>) ∈ (<i>U</i><sub>i</sub> ∩ <i>U</i><sub>j</sub>) × <i>F</i>.</dd>
</dl>
<p>Now let <i>F</i>′ be a specified topological space, equipped with a continuous left action of <i>G</i>. Then the bundle <b>associated</b> to <i>E</i> with fibre <i>F</i>′ is a bundle <i>E</i>′ with a local trivialization subordinate to the cover <i>U</i><sub>i</sub> whose transition functions are given by</p>
<dl>
<dd>ψ′<sub>ij</sub>(<i>u</i>,<i>f</i>′) = (<i>u</i>, <i>g</i><sub>ij</sub>(<i>u</i>) <i>f</i>′) for (<i>u</i>,f′) ∈(<i>U</i><sub>i</sub> ∩ <i>U</i><sub>j</sub>) × <i>F</i>′</dd>
</dl>
<p>where the <i>G</i>-valued functions <i>g</i><sub>ij</sub>(<i>u</i>) are the same as those obtained from the local trivialization of the original bundle <i>E</i>.</p>
<p>This definition clearly respects the cocycle condition on the transition functions, since in each case they are given by the same system of <i>G</i>-valued functions. (Using another local trivialization, and passing to a common refinement if necessary, the <i>g</i><sub>ij</sub> transform via the same coboundary.) Hence, by the <a href="/wiki/Fiber_bundle_construction_theorem" title="Fiber bundle construction theorem">fiber bundle construction theorem</a>, this produces a fibre bundle <i>E</i>′ with fibre <i>F</i>′ as claimed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Principal_bundle_associated_to_a_fibre_bundle">Principal bundle associated to a fibre bundle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Associated_bundle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Principal bundle associated to a fibre bundle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As before, suppose that <i>E</i> is a fibre bundle with structure group <i>G</i>. In the special case when <i>G</i> has a <a href="/wiki/Group_action#Types_of_actions" title="Group action">free and transitive</a> left action on <i>F</i>′, so that <i>F</i>′ is a principal homogeneous space for the left action of <i>G</i> on itself, then the associated bundle <i>E</i>′ is called the principal <i>G</i>-bundle associated to the fibre bundle <i>E</i>. If, moreover, the new fibre <i>F</i>′ is identified with <i>G</i> (so that <i>F</i>′ inherits a right action of <i>G</i> as well as a left action), then the right action of <i>G</i> on <i>F</i>′ induces a right action of <i>G</i> on <i>E</i>′. With this choice of identification, <i>E</i>′ becomes a principal bundle in the usual sense. Note that, although there is no canonical way to specify a right action on a principal homogeneous space for <i>G</i>, any two such actions will yield principal bundles which have the same underlying fibre bundle with structure group <i>G</i> (since this comes from the left action of <i>G</i>), and isomorphic as <i>G</i>-spaces in the sense that there is a globally defined <i>G</i>-valued function relating the two.</p>
<p>In this way, a principal <i>G</i>-bundle equipped with a right action is often thought of as part of the data specifying a fibre bundle with structure group <i>G</i>, since to a fibre bundle one may construct the principal bundle via the associated bundle construction. One may then, as in the next section, go the other way around and derive any fibre bundle by using a fibre product.</p>

</math></span><img alt="B" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline mw-math-element" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/47136aad860d145f75f3eed3022df827cee94d7a" style="vertical-align: -0.338ex; width:1.774ex; height:2.176ex;"/></span>, up to <a href="/wiki/Isomorphism" title="Isomorphism">isomorphism</a>. More concretely, this asks whether the transition data for <span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y mw-math-element" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

</mstyle>
</mrow>

</semantics>
</math></span><img alt="B" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline mw-math-element" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/47136aad860d145f75f3eed3022df827cee94d7a" style="vertical-align: -0.338ex; width:1.774ex; height:2.176ex;"/></span> can consistently be written with values in <span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y mw-math-element" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

In other words, we ask to identify the image of the associated bundle mapping (which is actually a <a href="/wiki/Functor" title="Functor">functor</a>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples_of_reduction">Examples of reduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Associated_bundle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Examples of reduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Examples for <a href="/wiki/Vector_bundle" title="Vector bundle">vector bundles</a> include: the introduction of a <i>metric</i> resulting in reduction of the structure group from a <a href="/wiki/General_linear_group" title="General linear group">general linear group</a> GL(<i>n</i>) to an <a href="/wiki/Orthogonal_group" title="Orthogonal group">orthogonal group</a> O(<i>n</i>); and the existence of complex structure on a real bundle resulting in reduction of the structure group from real general linear group GL(2<i>n</i>,<b>R</b>) to complex general linear group GL(<i>n</i>,<b>C</b>).</p>
<p>Another important case is finding a decomposition of a vector bundle <i>V</i> of rank <i>n</i> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whitney_sum" title="Whitney sum">Whitney sum</a> (direct sum) of sub-bundles of rank <i>k</i> and <i>n-k</i>, resulting in reduction of the structure group from GL(<i>n</i>,<b>R</b>) to GL(<i>k</i>,<b>R</b>) × GL(<i>n-k</i>,<b>R</b>).</p>
<p>One can also express the condition for a <a href="/wiki/Foliation" title="Foliation">foliation</a> to be defined as a reduction of the <a href="/wiki/Tangent_bundle" title="Tangent bundle">tangent bundle</a> to a block matrix subgroup - but here the reduction is only a necessary condition, there being an <i>integrability condition</i> so that the <a href="/wiki/Frobenius_theorem_(differential_topology)" title="Frobenius theorem (differential topology)">Frobenius theorem</a> applies.</p>
