<p>The <b>Inspector General of the Bundeswehr</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Generalinspekteur der Bundeswehr, GenInspBw</i></span>), also translated as <b>Chief of Staff of the Federal Armed Forces</b>, is the highest-ranking military post held by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commissioned_officer" title="Commissioned officer">commissioned officer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bundeswehr" title="Bundeswehr">Bundeswehr</a>, the present-day armed forces of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>
<p>All Inspectors General have been of the rank of a (<a href="/wiki/Four-star_rank" title="Four-star rank">four-star</a>) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General" title="General">general</a> or <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a>, and they head the <i>Führungsstab der Streitkräfte</i>, the German Defence Staff within the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_of_Defence_(Germany)" title="Federal Ministry of Defence (Germany)">Federal Ministry of Defence</a>, and is the direct military advisor to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Minister_of_Defence_(Germany)" title="Federal Minister of Defence (Germany)">Federal Minister of Defence</a> who is the peacetime <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_in_chief#Germany" title="Commander in chief">holder of the supreme command authority</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Inhaber der Befehls- und Kommandogewalt</i></span>).</p>
<p>Subordinate to the Inspector General are the commanders of the branches of the Bundeswehr, the <a href="/wiki/Inspector_of_the_Army" title="Inspector of the Army">Inspector of the Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inspector_of_the_Air_Force" title="Inspector of the Air Force">Inspector of the Air Force</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Inspector_of_the_Navy" title="Inspector of the Navy">Inspector of the Navy</a>, and the commanders of the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Support_Service_(Germany)" title="Joint Support Service (Germany)">Joint Support Service</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joint_Medical_Service_(Germany)" title="Joint Medical Service (Germany)">Joint Medical Service</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Title_and_translations">Title and translations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspector_General_of_the_Bundeswehr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Title and translations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While official translations of the position include "Chief of Staff" and variations of this term, the German term "<i>Generalinspekteur</i>" – "Inspector General" – was specifically created to avoid the term of "<i>Generalstabschef</i>" ("Chief of <a href="/wiki/German_General_Staff" title="German General Staff">General Staff</a>") deemed historically compromised. When the Bundeswehr was created in 1955, many traditional military terms were considered inappropriate after the German <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a>'s conduct in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Therefore, Federal German Bundeswehr has no "General Staff", but the "<a href="/wiki/Army_Command_(Germany)" title="Army Command (Germany)">Army Command</a>" ("<i>Kommando Heer</i>").</p>
