<p><i><b>Five Treasure Box</b></i> is the fourth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by South Korean <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/F.T._Island" title="F.T. Island">F.T. Island</a>. It was released in South Korea on September 10, 2012, under FNC Entertainment and distributed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mnet_Media" title="Mnet Media">Mnet Media</a>. Being the band's first studio album in three years, the title of the album reflects the "treasure" of the songs on the album, where they contributed to seven of the ten tracks on the album by songwriting or composition. The album sold over 30,000 copies in its first week and debuted at number one on the weekly Gaon albums chart. The single "I Wish" has peaked at number eight on the weekly Gaon singles chart and earned F.T. Island one K-Chart win on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s music television program <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Treasure_Box&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album is titled <i>Five Treasure Box</i> due to the members contributing to the lyrics and composition of seven of the ten tracks on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SC_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osen_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Osen-2">[2]</a></sup> Guitarist <a href="/wiki/Choi_Jong-hoon" title="Choi Jong-hoon">Choi Jong-hoon</a> stated that the band "worked on everything from guitar rift, sound and song genre".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-IS-3">[3]</a></sup> The album includes five Korean-language tracks previously recorded in Japanese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hankooki_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hankooki-4">[4]</a></sup> Speaking of these tracks, bassist Lee Jae-jin explained that "it was too much of a waste to lock up our songs [in Japan]". The band rewrote the lyrics in Korean and suggested to FNC Entertainment to include them on the album. Drummer Choi Min-hwan explained that the band chose "the best" songs to rewrite, while taking the "album’s mood" into consideration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Osen_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Osen-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the album jacket photos, each member was pictured with their most "treasured" possessions. Guitarist <a href="/wiki/Choi_Jong-hoon" title="Choi Jong-hoon">Choi Jong-hoon</a> was pictured with exercise equipment, lead vocalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Hongki" title="Lee Hongki">Lee Hongki</a> was pictured with his three dogs, bassist Lee Jae-jin was pictured with magazines, comic books, and <a href="/wiki/Potato_chip" title="Potato chip">potato chips</a>, drummer Choi Min-hwan was pictured with a television and a teddy bear, and guitarist Song Seung-hyun was pictured with <a href="/wiki/Sheet_music" title="Sheet music">sheet music</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Headset_(audio)" title="Headset (audio)">headset</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Treasure_Box&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Five Treasure Box</i> contains elements of <a href="/wiki/Modern_rock" title="Modern rock">modern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hankooki_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hankooki-4">[4]</a></sup> The promotional track "I Wish" was written by Han Seong-ho and composed by Kim Do-hun and Han.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Classified as a <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IS_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-IS-3">[3]</a></sup> "I Wish" begins with an <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_guitar" title="Acoustic guitar">acoustic guitar</a>, and the song gradually builds up into the <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hankooki_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hankooki-4">[4]</a></sup> The band members spoke of disappointment that the song was being used as the promotional single. They expressed understanding in the choice of FNC Entertainment wanting to promote a song with wider appeal, but the band showed preference over the tracks they composed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IS_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-IS-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Treasure_Box&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>F.T. Island first performed a snippet of "I Wish" on September 1, 2012, at their Take Island concert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nate_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nate-7">[7]</a></sup> A music video teaser was revealed on September 6, which featured label mate Hye-jeong from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AOA_(band)" title="AOA (band)">AOA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> That same day, a promotional truck playing the music video teaser drove throughout <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> in order to promote the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The full music video and the album was released on September 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 13, F.T. Island began promoting "I Wish" by performing the song, as well as "<a href="/wiki/Let_It_Go!" title="Let It Go!">Let It Go!</a>", on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The band also made their comeback performances in the following days with "I Wish" and "Paper Plane" on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">Korean Broadcasting System</a>'s (KBS) <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> performing "I Wish" and "Compass (The Way)" on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation</a>'s (MBC) <i><a href="/wiki/Show!_Music_Core" title="Show! Music Core">Show! Music Core</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">Seoul Broadcasting System</a>'s (SBS) <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">The Music Trend</a></i> with "I Wish" and "Life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On September 20, "I Wish" earned its first and only K-Chart win on <i>M! Countdown</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 25, F.T. Island performed "Compass", "I Wish", and "Life" on Choi Hwa-jeong's SBS radio show <i>Power Time</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Treasure_Box&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the first week of its release, <i>Five Treasure Box</i> sold over 30,000 copies in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SC_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-SC-1">[1]</a></sup> It went on to debut at number one on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart" title="Gaon Chart">Gaon</a> albums chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaon_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gaon-17">[17]</a></sup> The album charted at number three on the Gaon monthly albums chart for September, selling 46,982 copies that month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On the issue dated September 13, 2012, "I Wish" debuted at number 23 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Korea_K-Pop_Hot_100" title="Korea K-Pop Hot 100">Korea K-Pop Hot 100</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The following week, it rose to number 13 on said chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and it peaked at number 12 the week after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On the Gaon singles chart, the song debuted at number eight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaon_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gaon-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
