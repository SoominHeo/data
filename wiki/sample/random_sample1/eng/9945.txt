<p>A <b>training ship</b> is a ship used to train students as <a href="/wiki/Sailor" title="Sailor">sailors</a>. The term is especially used for ships employed by <a href="/wiki/Navy" title="Navy">navies</a> to train future officers. Essentially there are two types: those used for training at sea and old hulks used to house classrooms.</p>
<p>The hands-on aspect provided by <a href="/wiki/Sail_training" title="Sail training">sail training</a> has also been used as a platform for everything from semesters-at-sea for undergraduate oceanography and biology students, marine science and physical science for high school students, and character building for at-risk youths.</p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_Cadet_Corps" title="Sea Cadet Corps">Sea Cadet Corps</a> all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_unit" title="Military unit">Units</a> use a <a href="/wiki/Ship_prefix" title="Ship prefix">ship prefix</a> "T.S.", followed by the ship's proper name. For example, the Fishguard Sea Cadets' ship's name is T.S. <i>Skirmisher</i>. The T.S. prefix is used as the <a href="/wiki/Sea_Cadets" title="Sea Cadets">Sea Cadets</a> is not part of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>, and cannot be prefixed "<a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Ship" title="Her Majesty's Ship">HMS</a>".</p>
