<p><b>Show business</b>, sometimes shortened to <b>show biz</b> or <b>showbiz</b> (since <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 1945),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oed_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-oed-4">[4]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Vernacular" title="Vernacular">vernacular</a> term for all aspects of the <b>entertainment industry</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oed_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-oed-4">[4]</a></sup> From the <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> side (including managers, agents, producers, and distributors), the term applies to the creative element (including artists, performers, writers, musicians, and technicians) and was in common usage throughout the 20th century, although the first known use in print dates from 1850.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> At that time and for several decades, it typically included an initial <i>the</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oed_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-oed-4">[4]</a></sup> By the latter part of the century, it had acquired a slightly arcane quality associated with the era of <a href="/wiki/Variety_show" title="Variety show">variety</a>, but the term is still in active use. In modern entertainment industry, it is often associated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fashion_industry" title="Fashion industry">fashion industry</a> (creating trend and fashion) and acquiring <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual property</a> rights from the invested research in the entertainment business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
