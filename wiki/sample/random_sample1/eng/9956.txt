<p>The <b>Peninsula Shield Force</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globsec_GCC_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-globsec_GCC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GulfDN_replacement_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GulfDN_replacement-2">[2]</a></sup> (or <i>Peninsula Shield</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globsec_GCC_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-globsec_GCC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GulfDN_replacement_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GulfDN_replacement-2">[2]</a></sup>) (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">دِرْعُ الجَزيرَة</span>‎‎) is the military side of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cooperation_Council_for_the_Arab_States_of_the_Gulf" title="Cooperation Council for the Arab States of the Gulf">Cooperation Council for the Arab States of the Gulf</a> (a.k.a. the Gulf Cooperation Council or GCC). It is intended to deter, and respond to, military aggression against any of the GCC member countries: <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation">Creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1984, <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Cooperation_Council" title="Gulf Cooperation Council">Gulf Cooperation Council</a> (GCC) decided to create a joint military force of 10,000 soldiers divided into two <a href="/wiki/Brigade" title="Brigade">brigades</a>, called the Peninsula Shield Force, based in Saudi Arabia near the Kuwaiti and Iraqi borders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globsec_GCC_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-globsec_GCC-1">[1]</a></sup> The PSF is composed of infantry, armor, artillery, and combat support elements from each of the GCC countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1992, the Peninsula Shield Force was headed by a Saudi Arabian, based near <a href="/wiki/King_Khalid_Military_City" title="King Khalid Military City">King Khalid Military City</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hafar_al_Batin" title="Hafar al Batin">Hafar al Batin</a>, and had one infantry brigade of 5,000 men from all the GCC member states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globsec_GCC_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-globsec_GCC-1">[1]</a></sup> As of late 2006, the Peninsula Shield Force had 7,000 personnel and functioned as a joint intervention force to defend the joint border of Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In November 2006, the GCC Joint Defense Council considered a Saudi proposal to expand the capabilities of the Shield and to establish a joint command and control system.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In December 2007, Kuwait's National Security Council chief Shaikh Ahmed Fahad Al Ahmed Al Sabah announced that the GCC plans to create a replacement for the Peninsula Shield Force. He stated that "the GCC options would always be unified just as they were when leaders declared the establishment of a common market at the Doha Summit."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GulfDN_replacement_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-GulfDN_replacement-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_and_structure">Leadership and structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leadership and structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of March 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the Peninsula Shield Force was commanded by Major General <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mutlaq_bin_Salem_Al_Azima&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mutlaq bin Salem Al Azima (page does not exist)">Mutlaq bin Salem Al Azima</a> and has about 40,000 troops<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awsat_commander_alAzima_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-awsat_commander_alAzima-3">[3]</a></sup> and continues to have its permanent base at <a href="/wiki/King_Khalid_Military_City" title="King Khalid Military City">King Khalid Military City</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hafar_al_Batin" title="Hafar al Batin">Hafar al Batin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awsat_commander_alAzima_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-awsat_commander_alAzima-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Peninsula Shield Force commander Al Azima, any intervention by the force must include participation from all GCC member states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awsat_commander_alAzima_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-awsat_commander_alAzima-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_the_Shield">Use of the Shield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Use of the Shield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990.E2.80.9391">1990–91</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990–91">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Peninsula Shield Force was not sufficiently developed to be deployed in defence of <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Kuwait" title="Invasion of Kuwait">invasion of Kuwait</a> by <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> in August 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A force of about 3,000 men from the PSF, in addition to forces of its member states, took part in the liberation of Kuwait in March 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Singh_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Singh-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003">2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>10,000 troops and two ships of PSF were deployed to Kuwait in February 2003 ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Iraq_War" title="Iraq War">Iraq War</a> to protect Kuwait from potential Iraqi attacks. It did not participate in operations against Iraq.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Singh_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Singh-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Role_in_Bahraini_uprising">Role in Bahraini uprising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Role in Bahraini uprising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahraini_uprising_(2011%E2%80%93present)" title="Bahraini uprising (2011–present)">Bahraini uprising (2011–present)</a></div>
<p>On 14 March 2011, Peninsula Shield forces, requested by the Bahraini government, entered Bahrain via the causeway from Saudi Arabia. Forces were from Saudi Arabia and the UAE. Kuwait and Oman refrained from sending troops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kw_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-kw-7">[7]</a></sup> The Bahraini uprising was the first GCC deployment in relation to an internal threat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PenForce_NYT14March_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-PenForce_NYT14March-8">[8]</a></sup> In late March, Peninsula Shield Force commander al-Azima stated that the role of the force in Bahrain is to "secure Bahrain's vital and strategically important military infrastructure from any foreign interference" and to protect Bahraini borders while Bahrain security forces are "preoccupied with [Bahraini] internal security".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awsat_commander_alAzima_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-awsat_commander_alAzima-3">[3]</a></sup> He denied that the force caused any Bahraini citizen to "suffer so much as a scratch", and said that the force entered Bahrain "to bring goodness, peace, and love".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awsat_commander_alAzima_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-awsat_commander_alAzima-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2011 Bahraini intervention, involves about 10 percent of the Peninsula Shield Forces. Every military unit present in Bahrain includes soldiers from all six GCC member states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-awsat_commander_alAzima_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-awsat_commander_alAzima-3">[3]</a></sup> In October 2011 the Peninsula Shield announced its intention to sue “a number of satellite TV channels for propagating lies and allegations about the Peninsula Shield forces that entered Bahrain”. This followed persistent claims by these channels about the Gulf forces strafing demonstrators with warplanes and destroying mosques.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Frequent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Saudi_Arabian_protests" title="2011 Saudi Arabian protests">Saudi Arabian street protests</a> in and near <a href="/wiki/Qatif" title="Qatif">Qatif</a> in mid to late March, originally calling for political prisoners to be released, extended to opposition to the Peninsula Shield Force's presence in Bahrain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pressTV_17March_4kQatif_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-pressTV_17March_4kQatif-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arabtimes_4kQatifetc_18March_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-arabtimes_4kQatifetc_18March-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThReut_20Mar_Qatif_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThReut_20Mar_Qatif-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThReut_Qatif25Mar_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ThReut_Qatif25Mar-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_urging_of_closer_defense_ties">American urging of closer defense ties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peninsula_Shield_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: American urging of closer defense ties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chuck_Hagel" title="Chuck Hagel">Chuck Hagel</a> called for greater defense cooperation in the GCC, including block sales of American arms to the organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 December 2013, the GCC announced the formation of a joint military command.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016 Saudi Arabia held a military drill named "Northern Thunder", a consortium of 21 countries to display its military cooperation with Arab countries. Pakistan, Turkey and other nations also contributed to the drill.</p>
