<p><b>Futbolo Klubas Ekranas</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuanian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club, playing in the city of <a href="/wiki/Panev%C4%97%C5%BEys" title="Panevėžys">Panevėžys</a>.</p>
<p>The club was founded in 1964. But folded in 2014 due to financial problems.</p>
<p>It won Lithuanian championships in 1985 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_Lithuania" title="Soviet Lithuania">Soviet Lithuania</a>), 1993, 2005, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011 and 2012, the Lithuanian Cup in 1985 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_Lithuania" title="Soviet Lithuania">Soviet Lithuania</a>), 1998, 2000, 2010 and 2011 and the Lithuanian Super Cup in 1998, 2006, 2010, 2011 and 2012.</p>
<p>On 5 November 2004, before the <a href="/wiki/A_Lyga" title="A Lyga">A Lyga</a> champion-deciding match against <a href="/wiki/FBK_Kaunas" title="FBK Kaunas">FBK Kaunas</a>, the club was controversially expelled from the first division by the National Football Club Association (NFKA) for alleged match fixing, but reinstated a day later.</p>
<p>In August 2006 FK Ekranas won the first <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Champions_Cup" title="Baltic Champions Cup">Baltic Champion's Cup</a> tournament in <a href="/wiki/Liep%C4%81ja" title="Liepāja">Liepāja</a> after beating Estonian champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TVMK_Tallinn" title="TVMK Tallinn">TVMK Tallinn</a> 6–4 and drawing with the home side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FHK_Liep%C4%81jas_Metalurgs" title="FHK Liepājas Metalurgs">Liepājas Metalurgs</a> 1–1.</p>
