<p><i><b>Cruel Winter Blues</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">열혈남아</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Yeol-hyeol-nam-a</span></i>; lit. "A Hot-blooded Man") is a 2006 South Korean film written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jeong-beom" title="Lee Jeong-beom">Lee Jeong-beom</a>.</p>
<p>Mis-marketed as a gangster action movie, reviewers called it an intense family drama about motherhood, as a strong, self-sacrificing mother has no choice but to endure the hardships of having a hoodlum son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cruel_Winter_Blues&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A mob hitter, Jae-mun (<a href="/wiki/Sol_Kyung-gu" title="Sol Kyung-gu">Sol Kyung-gu</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> is the seasoned veteran of a mob family. He decides to go after rival mobster Dae-sik, the man who killed his boss and childhood friend a few years earlier. He takes Chi-guk with him as his partner in the assassination. Although a physically imposing black belt in <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">Taekwondo</a>, Chi-guk (<a href="/wiki/Jo_Han-sun" title="Jo Han-sun">Jo Han-sun</a>) is a soft-spoken newcomer to gang life, who is quietly offended by Jae-mun's arbitrary and random acts of cruelty and rudeness against him and others. Together, they travel to Dae-sik's hometown, a remote rural village called Bulgyo in <a href="/wiki/South_Jeolla_Province" title="South Jeolla Province">South Jeolla Province</a>.</p>
<p>While waiting for an opportunity to perform the hit, Jae-mun coincidentally befriends Dae-sik's mother Jeon-sim (<a href="/wiki/Na_Moon-hee" title="Na Moon-hee">Na Moon-hee</a>) and spends some time with her. She comes to lovingly treat him as another son, and Jae-mun begins to feel conflicted. A tragic plot develops, culminating in a bloody showdown between Jae-mun and Dae-sik.</p>
