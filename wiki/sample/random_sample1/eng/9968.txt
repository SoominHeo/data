<p><b>Yeom Ki-Hun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">염기훈</span>; born 30 March 1983) is a Korean <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player. He currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Suwon_Samsung_Bluewings" title="Suwon Samsung Bluewings">Suwon Samsung Bluewings</a>. Yeom is a talented player who has made his way onto the national team following his continental performances. Yeom, who recovered from a serious automobile accident during the 2006 season, is a tricky left winger with a penchant for scoring crucial goals, including an important goal in the first leg of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League_2006" title="AFC Champions League 2006">AFC Champions League</a> final.</p>
<p>On 16 December 2008, Yeom Ki-Hun joined <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> on a trial basis without receiving Ulsan's approval.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He incurred blame for this incident.</p>
