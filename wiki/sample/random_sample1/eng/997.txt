<p><b>FK Inter Bratislava</b> (<small>Slovak pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovak" title="Help:IPA for Slovak">[ˈinter ˈbracislaʋa]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovak</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a>, playing its home matches at the <a href="/wiki/Stadium_%C5%A0KP_Inter_D%C3%BAbravka" title="Stadium ŠKP Inter Dúbravka">Štadión ŠKP Inter Dúbravka</a>. The club competes in III. liga Bratislava, the third tier in the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_football_league_system" title="Slovak football league system">Slovak football league system</a>, after winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Majstrovstv%C3%A1_regi%C3%B3nu" title="Majstrovstvá regiónu">Majstrovstvá regiónu</a> in the 2013–14 season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FK_Inter_Bratislava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Inter Bratislava</b> was founded in 1940 by the Apollo <a href="/wiki/Refinery" title="Refinery">refinery</a> (later renamed <a href="/wiki/Slovnaft" title="Slovnaft">Slovnaft</a>). Following the end of World War II and the re-establishment of <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, the club developed into an important force in Czechoslovak football. While it remains unclear, whether it is Inter Bratislava or <a href="/wiki/FK_%C5%A0KP_Inter_D%C3%BAbravka_Bratislava" title="FK ŠKP Inter Dúbravka Bratislava">FK ŠKP Inter Dúbravka Bratislava</a>, who can claim the successful run of Červená Hviezda Bratislava in the 1950s and early 1960s as its own, club's achievements in the subsequent decades (as TJ Internacionál Slovnaft Bratislava) can be hardly disputed. Between 1962 and 1993 the club spent 29 out of 31 seasons in the Czechoslovak top flight, finishing twice as runner-up in the 1970s and winning the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Cup" title="Slovak Cup">Slovak Cup</a> in the seasons 1983–84, 1987–88, and 1989–90. Over these years, a number of Inter players represented <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia_national_football_team" title="Czechoslovakia national football team">senior level</a>. In 1976, <a href="/wiki/Jozef_Barmo%C5%A1" title="Jozef Barmoš">Jozef Barmoš</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ladislav_Jurkemik" title="Ladislav Jurkemik">Ladislav Jurkemik</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ladislav_Petr%C3%A1%C5%A1" title="Ladislav Petráš">Ladislav Petráš</a> were in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1976_squads" title="UEFA Euro 1976 squads">squad</a> that won the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1976" title="UEFA Euro 1976">UEFA Euro 1976</a>. Four years later, Barmoš and Jurkemik were also a part of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1980_squads" title="UEFA Euro 1980 squads">side</a> that finished third at the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1980" title="UEFA Euro 1980">1980 European Championship</a>. In a decade that followed the dissolution of Czechoslovakia, Inter went on to flourish in the newly established <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Super_Liga" title="Slovak Super Liga">top tier of Slovak football</a> as well as in the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Cup" title="Slovak Cup">Slovak Cup</a>, winning the <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)" title="Double (association football)">Slovak double</a> in the 1999–2000 and 2000–2001 seasons.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inter.27s_fall_and_re-establishment">Inter's fall and re-establishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FK_Inter_Bratislava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Inter's fall and re-establishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Inter Bratislava won the <a href="/wiki/2._Liga_(Slovakia)" title="2. Liga (Slovakia)">1. liga</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Slovak_First_League" title="2008–09 Slovak First League">2008–2009 season</a> and was supposed to be promoted to the Slovak top flight. However, financial problems of the club led its owner Ľubomír Chrenko to sell Inter's licence to <a href="/wiki/FK_Senica" title="FK Senica">FK Senica</a> in June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As a result, players of the senior squad of Inter Bratislava joined Senica, whilst youth teams of Inter were preserved by the Inter Bratislava Civic Association, which had been formed from the Inter Fan Club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The senior side was re-established in the 2010–2011 season, playing in the V. liga, i.e. the sixth tier of Slovak football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Major changes in the structure of the club were accompanied by Inter's move from the <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0tadi%C3%B3n_Pasienky" title="Štadión Pasienky">Štadión Pasienky</a>, which had been used by the team since 1967, to the considerably smaller Štadión Drieňová ulica. After playing at the Štadión Drieňová ulica for four seasons, the senior team moved to the <a href="/wiki/Stadium_%C5%A0KP_Inter_D%C3%BAbravka" title="Stadium ŠKP Inter Dúbravka">Štadión ŠKP Inter Dúbravka</a> in the summer of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The grounds have a capacity of 10,200. Since the season 2015/2016 due to unknown issues the Men team returned to stadium Drieňová ulica and the youth teams remained on Stadium ŠKP Inter Dúbravka</p>
