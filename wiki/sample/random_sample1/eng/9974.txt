<p><b>Andrew Yeom Soo-Jung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">염수정</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">廉洙政</span>; born 5 December 1943) is the <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_South_Korea" title="Roman Catholicism in South Korea">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Seoul" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Seoul">Archbishop of Seoul</a>, Cardinal and <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a> <a href="/wiki/Primate_(bishop)" title="Primate (bishop)">Primate</a> of <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> since his appointment was announced on 10 May 2012 by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Yeom succeeded the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">Archbishop Emeritus</a> <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Cheong_Jin-suk" title="Nicolas Cheong Jin-suk">Cardinal Nicolas Cheong Jin-suk</a> and the fifth native Korean Archbishop of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. In January 2014 it was announced that he will be elevated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinalate" title="Cardinalate">cardinalate</a> at the next <a href="/wiki/Papal_consistory" title="Papal consistory">consistory</a> on 22 February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The Metropolitan Archbishop of Seoul also serves as Apostolic Administrator of the Diocese of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Pyongyang" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. Aside from Archbishop, he was also the <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">Chairman</a> of Peace Broadcasting Corporation (Korean: 평화방송 or PBC). Founded in 1990, this corporation included a Catholic television channel and a radio station, both of which became highly valued Christian voices and tireless champions of values such as peace, reconciliation, the defence of life, dignity and inalienable human rights.</p>
<p>Yeom was made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinalate" title="Cardinalate">Cardinal</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> in a papal consistory on 22 February 2014 at <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Basilica" title="St. Peter's Basilica">Saint Peter's Basilica</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>. He will be eligible to participate in any future <a href="/wiki/Papal_conclave" title="Papal conclave">Papal conclaves</a> until he reaches the age of eighty on 5 December 2023.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yeom Soo-jung was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ansong" title="Ansong">Ansong</a>, Gyeonggi Province, to a devout Catholic parents, Yeom Han-jin (Calixto) and Baek Geum-wol (Susanna) descendants of Joseph Yeom Deok-sun (who were among the first Koreans to convert to Christianity) and Peter Yeom Seok-tae and his wife Kim Maria who were arrested and executed in 1850 for their Catholic faith. His ancestors were among the lay people who brought Christianity to the Korean peninsula in the 19th century, and his great-great grandfather and his wife were executed as part of the Joseon Dynasty's persecution of Christians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Yeom family has kept their religious belief for generations through persecution, leading Archbishop Yeom, the fifth generation Catholic, to enter the priesthood. The third of six children, Yeom loves explaining how he discovered and cultivated his priestly vocation thanks to family prayer: his grandmother Magdalena Park and his mother, went to mass every day for 30 years to pray for their children to become priests. His two brothers Yeom Soo-wan and Yeom Soo-eui have also followed him, currently leading two dioceses in Seoul.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_Studies">Further Studies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Further Studies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of 15, Yeom decided to become a priest and entered the seminary. He graduated from the Catholic University of Korea in 1970<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreaherald_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-koreaherald-5">[5]</a></sup> before being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Orders" title="Holy Orders">ordained</a> a priest by Cardinal Kim Sou-hwan on 8 December 1970 for the diocese of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. Yeom Soo-jung went on to obtain a Master of Education in Counseling Psychology from Korea University. He has also studied at the East Asian Pastoral Institute in the Philippines, while serving in various parishes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>After his ordination, he served as a curate from 1971–1973, then as President of the Minor <a href="/wiki/Seminary" title="Seminary">Seminary</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Songshin_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Songshin High School (page does not exist)">Songshin High School</a>, from 1973–1977, and then served as pastor from 1977 until 1978. <p>After he left his post as chancellor, he was appointed as one of the Seoul Archdiocese's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vicar_Forane" title="Vicar Forane">Vicar Foranes</a>, and at the same time, as a pastor, serving in these two roles until 2001. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Episcopacy">Episcopacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Episcopacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 1 December 2001 it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> had appointed him, at the age of 58, as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auxiliary_Bishop" title="Auxiliary Bishop">Auxiliary Bishop</a> of Seoul and at the same time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titular_Bishop" title="Titular Bishop">Titular Bishop</a> of <i>Thibiuca</i>. He was consecrated on 25 January 2002 in the Changcheon-dong Stadium, Seoul, by Archbishop Nicholas Cheong Jin-suk of Seoul, assisted by Andrew Choi Chang-mou, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Gwangju" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Gwangju">Archbishop of Gwangju</a>, and by John Chang-yik, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Chunchon" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Chunchon">Bishop of Chunchon</a>. After his episcopal consecration, he became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vicar_General" title="Vicar General">Vicar General</a> of the Archdiocese of Seoul and episcopal vicar for the pastoral and the apostolate of the mass media. Member of the Permanent Council and of the Commission for Missions, and for the Commission for the Pastoral of Health of the Korean Episcopal Conference; as well as president of the Committee for the Apostolate of the Lay. He retained these roles until 10 May 2012, when <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a> appointed him as the next Metropolitan Archbishop of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Seoul. He succeeded his former superior, Cardinal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicholas_Cheong_Jin-suk" title="Nicholas Cheong Jin-suk">Nicholas Cheong Jin-suk</a>, 80, who had surpassed the canonical retirement age after offering his resignation back in 2006. Because the Archdiocese of Seoul is the most important diocese in the Koreas and has been headed by a Cardinal in the past, it is likely that, since his predecessor is over the voting age for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papal_Conclave" title="Papal Conclave">Papal Conclave</a>, he will be named a Cardinal himself in a <a href="/wiki/Papal_consistory" title="Papal consistory">Consistory</a> in the near future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreaherald_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-koreaherald-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Archbishop_of_Seoul">Archbishop of Seoul</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Archbishop of Seoul">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a> appointed Yeom as the 5th Archbishop of Seoul on 10 May 2012, succeeding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicholas_Cheong_Jin-suk" title="Nicholas Cheong Jin-suk">Cardinal Nicholas Cheong Jin-suk</a> and thus making him <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/De_Facto" title="De Facto">de facto</a> <a href="/wiki/Primate_(bishop)" title="Primate (bishop)">Primate</a> of <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. At the installation ceremony he said "We need to keep the dignity of human life in a society that takes life lightly. The church will fight for that," said Archbishop Yeom during the inaugural mass. The Seoul Archdiocese held the inaugural ceremony for the new archbishop on the 62nd anniversary of the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, as a symbolic gesture, praying especially for the reconciliation and reunification of the two Koreas. Since then, martyrdom has left an indelible mark on his Episcopal ministry. His ministry has also been marked by constants references to the precious faith of martyrs which is seen an example of authentic testimony all Christians should follow no matter what their life condition or state. He also expressed a strong eagerness for dialogue, reconciliation and peace between the two Koreas to calm the tensions that are circling the peninsula, introducing the risk of a new conflict.. The inaugural Mass was attended by Cardinal Cheong, Culture Minister Choe Kwang-shik, Vatican Nuncio Archbishop <a href="/wiki/Filipino_Apostolic_Nuncios" title="Filipino Apostolic Nuncios">Osvaldo Padilla</a>, political leaders including former opposition leader Sohn Hak-kyu, Rep. Kang Ki-gap and Gyeonggi Governor Kim Moon-soo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As Archbishop of Seoul, he is head of the largest local Church in the Koreas, and the officeholder is traditionally also apostolic administrator of Pyongyang, the North Korean capital. The Pyongyang diocese has been vacant since the death of its last ordinary, Bishop <a href="/wiki/Francis_Hong_Yong-ho" title="Francis Hong Yong-ho">Francis Hong Yong-ho</a>, who was imprisoned by the North Korean government in 1949, and was later disappeared.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_growing_church_in_Korea">A growing church in Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: A growing church in Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to statistics, as of 31 December 2011, of the 15 dioceses and 1 military ordinariate in Korea, the Archdiocese of Seoul is the most populous with 27% of the total <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_Korea" title="Roman Catholicism in Korea">Catholics in Korea</a>. The number of Catholics in Korea is 5,309,964, an increase of 2% (104,375) from 2011. Catholics account for 10.3% of the total population. The total number of Catholics in Korea has slightly and consistently increased at a yearly average of 2–3% during the past 10 years. It has passed the 10% mark of the total population since 2009. According to the Statistics, the number of newly baptised in 2011 was 134,562, a decrease of 4.3% from the previous year. By gender, newly baptised men represented 73,228 and women 61,334. The number of infants baptised amounted to 25,717, an increase of 7.5% over the previous year.</p>
<p>In 2012, the Church grew by 1.6%, as nearly 85,000 Koreans became Catholic. There has also been an increase in vocations to the priesthood and religious life in recent years. Earlier this month, two auxiliary bishops and 38 new priests were ordained for Seoul. Government surveys have shown that more than 45% of South Koreans practice no religion and that about 22% are Buddhists. Yet when Catholics (11%) and Protestants (18%) are combined, Christianity as a whole claims the largest number of religious adherents. By contrast, Christianity is officially suppressed in North Korea under the communist regime, and unofficial estimates by South Korean Church officials place the number of Catholics there at only 5,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Archbishop Yeom received the <a href="/wiki/Pallium" title="Pallium">Pallium</a> from <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a> on 29 June 2012 (the <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_Saints_Peter_and_Paul" title="Feast of Saints Peter and Paul">Feast of Saints Peter and Paul</a>), along with several archbishops from various countries. Yeom was one of several Asian archbishops whom the Pontiff granted the pallium, one of whom being the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Manila" title="Archbishop of Manila">Archbishop of Manila</a> <a href="/wiki/Luis_Antonio_Tagle" title="Luis Antonio Tagle">Luis Antonio Tagle</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardinal-Priest">Cardinal-Priest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cardinal-Priest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Pope named Archbishop Yeom to the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Cardinals" title="College of Cardinals">College of Cardinals</a> on Saturday, 22 February 2014. As Cardinal-Priest, he was assigned the <a href="/wiki/Titular_church" title="Titular church">titular church</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Crisogono" title="San Crisogono">San Crisogono</a>. Yeom is the Third <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a> national to be made Cardinal in the Catholic Church, following the two preceding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">Cardinal Emeritus</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicholas_Cheong_Jin-Suk" title="Nicholas Cheong Jin-Suk">Nicholas Cheong Jin-Suk</a>, and the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">Cardinal</a> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Kim_Sou-hwan" title="Stephen Kim Sou-hwan">Stephen Kim Sou-hwan</a>. On 23 February, the day after the announcement was made, a celebratory ceremony was held at Seoul’s <a href="/wiki/Myeongdong_Cathedral" title="Myeongdong Cathedral">Myeongdong Cathedral</a>, where he said, "I will make efforts to realize Pope Francis' vision of a Church toiling for the poor and those on the margins of society and to make it a Church serving the community. ... I respect efforts made by late Cardinal Kim and Cardinal Cheong, and will add mine to them." His appointment as cardinal was welcomed by many Koreans, though only 11 percent of the country is Catholic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 22 May 2014, he was named member of the Congregations for the Evangelization of Peoples and for the Clergy. On Saturday, 4 October 2014, he took possession of the title of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Crisogono" title="San Crisogono">San Crisogono</a>. He participated in <a href="/wiki/Third_Extraordinary_General_Assembly_of_the_Synod_of_Bishops" title="Third Extraordinary General Assembly of the Synod of Bishops">Third Extraordinary General Assembly of the Synod of Bishops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>, 5 to 19 October 2014, on the theme "The Pastoral Challenges of the Family in the Context of Evangelization", by papal appointment.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_Arms">Coat of Arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Coat of Arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1226" data-file-width="1150" height="192" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg/180px-Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg/270px-Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg/360px-Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg.png 2x" width="180"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_Andrew_Yeom_Soo_Jung.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Arms of Archbishop Yeom Soo Jung as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Seoul" title="Archbishop of Seoul">Archbishop of Seoul</a>. The motto below is <i>Amen. Veni, Domine Jesu!</i> ("Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!")</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Coat of Arms of Archbishop Yeom features the Traditional Red Hat, or the <a href="/wiki/Galero" title="Galero">galero</a>, replaces the Green one with its 15 tassels dangling in two five-tiered formations from either side. Below the Red Hat is the Crucifix symbolizes the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Martyrs" title="Korean Martyrs">Holy Martyrs of Korea</a> who were the victims of religious persecution against the Catholic Church during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> in the 19th Century.</p>
<p>The shield is a symbol of God's salvation, and the three Rainbow colors: Purple (Love), Blue (Hope) and Green (Faith). The Herald of a new life, Dove, symbol of peace as a messenger came in the past, and will come in the present and future, and the spirit of the Lord brings into the presence among us even today and revealed that symbolizes the Holy Spirit. The Big Star in the center represents the Virgin Mary as she protects the two shining stars below symbolizing the peaceful reunification between the <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">Democratic People's Republic of Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">Republic of Korea</a>. An anchor cross and the two Greek letters "<i>A</i>" (Alpha) and "<i>Ω</i>" (Omega) that all the hopes and aspirations of the Korean People will be in the God's plan. The background's color Blue, Yellow, and Red symbolize Peace, Sharing, and Sacrifice.</p>
<p>Archbishop Yeom's Latin motto is taken from the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Revelation" title="Book of Revelation">Book of Revelation</a> 22:20, <i>Amen. Veni, Domine Jesu!</i> meaning "Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!"</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Views">Views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His pastoral approach is also characterised by a respect for life and the mission. Respect for life is an aspect that is especially important to the Korean Church. Indeed, over the course of the years, it has doubled its efforts to increase public awareness as research centres using human embryos for scientific experiments continue to grow in number. The Church has focused its missionary work primarily on Asia, a sign of how this unique Church has matured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reconciliation_Between_North_and_South_Korea">Reconciliation Between North and South Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Yeom_Soo-jung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reconciliation Between North and South Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cardinal Yeom made history on Wednesday morning, 21 May 2014 becoming the first Korean Roman Catholic leader to cross the inter-Korean border into the <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North</a>. He and his South Korean priests traveled to a joint North-South industrial park in <a href="/wiki/Kaesong" title="Kaesong">Kaesong</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> to tour the complex and meet South Koreans working there. Yeom told reporters after his return from the one-day visit that seeing South and North Koreans working in harmony gave him hope that the two countries can "overcome their pain and sorrow."</p>
<p>The joint industrial park, located just north of the heavily armed border, is the last remaining cross-border rapprochement project between the rival Koreas. It combines South Korean initiative, capital and technology with cheap North Korean labor. Operations at the decade-old complex were suspended for months last year when tensions sharply rose over repeated North Korean threats of nuclear war. South Korean Catholic officials denied media speculation that Yeom's trip might be aimed at preparing for a possible visit by Pope Francis to North Korea when he visits South Korea in August. Fr. Heo Young-yup, who went to Kaesong with the cardinal, said Yeom didn't meet any North Korean officials there. <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> plans to visit South Korea on 14-18 August to participate in the 6th Asian Youth Day, preside over a beatification ceremony for <a href="/wiki/Korean_Martyrs" title="Korean Martyrs">Paul Yun Ji-Chung and 123 companions</a> and bring a message of peace to the war-divided peninsula. His visit will be the first in 25 years by a pope to the Korean Peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> after <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a></p>
