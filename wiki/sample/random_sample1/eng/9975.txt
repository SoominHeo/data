<p><b>Yŏmju County</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_North_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of North Korea"><i>kun</i></a>, or county, in northwestern <a href="/wiki/North_Pyongan_Province" title="North Pyongan Province">North P'yŏngan</a> province, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>, on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a>. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Ryongchon_County" title="Ryongchon County">Ryongch'ŏn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pihyon_County" title="Pihyon County">P'ihyŏn</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Tongrim_County" title="Tongrim County">Tongrim</a> to the east, and <a href="/wiki/Cholsan_County" title="Cholsan County">Ch'ŏlsan</a> to the south; to the west lies the ocean. The county was created in 1952 from portions of Ch'ŏlsan and Ryongch'ŏn.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yomju_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The terrain of Yŏmju is mostly level or rolling, sloping upward from the southwest to the northeast. In the north and east are small mountains like <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mangilsan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mangilsan (page does not exist)">Mangilsan</a> (613 m). The islands of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pansong_archipelago" title="Pansong archipelago">Pansŏng archipelago</a> lie offshore and are a noted regional attraction. Other islands include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Taedasado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Taedasado (page does not exist)">Taedasado</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Changdo_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Changdo Island (page does not exist)">Changdo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yomju_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although warmed somewhat by the sea, the local climate remains basically continental, with an average annual temperature of 8.4 °C reflecting the fluctuation between January's -8.7 °C and August's 24 °C. Annual rainfall is relatively low, 800-1000 mm.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yomju_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the level terrain, 54.4% of the land in Yŏmju is cultivated, with only 15% remaining as forestland (mostly pine). The county is North P'yŏngan's second-largest producer of rice; other local crops include soybeans, maize, tobacco, and fruit. Fishing and livestock raising are also important local industries, as is salt harvesting. Yŏmju produces enough salt to satisfy 10% of national demand. A large <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">aquaculture</a> installation also operates in the county, dating from the early 1980s; it is reported to cover more than 1 km², with more than 80 separate ponds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yomju_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yŏmju county is served by the <a href="/wiki/Pyongui_Line" title="Pyongui Line">P'yŏngŭi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tasado_Line" title="Tasado Line">Tasado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paengma_Line" title="Paengma Line">Paengma</a> lines of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_State_Railway" title="Korean State Railway">Korean State Railway</a>.</p>
