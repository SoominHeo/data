<p><b>Jasiel Amon Robinson</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Yung Joc</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recording_artist" title="Recording artist">recording artist</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>. He is best known for his <a href="/wiki/Hit_single" title="Hit single">hit singles</a> "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Goin%27_Down_(Yung_Joc_song)" title="It's Goin' Down (Yung Joc song)">It's Goin' Down</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Know_You_See_It" title="I Know You See It">I Know You See It</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robinson's father, Stanley Tucker, owned a hair-care products company and helped him get an opportunity to write a jingle for <a href="/wiki/Revlon" title="Revlon">Revlon</a>. Robinson followed in his father's entrepreneurial footsteps and founded his own label to release his music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Joc_City_.282005.E2.80.932006.29"><i>New Joc City</i> (2005–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New Joc City (2005–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yung Joc met Atlanta producer Nitti Beatz and the two recorded the hit "It's Goin Down" in the spring of 2005. After teaming with Russell "Block" Spencer, founder of the Block Enterprises label, Spencer signed Joc to <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean Combs</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy South</a> for a multimillion-dollar deal,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and Bad Boy released Joc's debut <i><a href="/wiki/New_Joc_City" title="New Joc City">New Joc City</a></i> that year.</p>
<p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup> Yung Joc was also featured on the songs "<a href="/wiki/Show_Stopper" title="Show Stopper">Show Stopper</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Danity_Kane" title="Danity Kane">Danity Kane</a> (from Diddy's <i><a href="/wiki/Making_the_Band" title="Making the Band">Making The Band 4</a></i>) &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassie_(singer)" title="Cassie (singer)">Cassie</a>'s album track <i>Call U Out</i>. Yung Joc was on 2006 "<a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a>' Richest Rappers List", ranking at #20, having grossed approximately $10 million that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> "It's Goin' Down" reached #3 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and #1 on the Hot Rap Tracks chart. The next single, "I Know U See It", reached #17 on the Hot 100 and #2 on the Hot Rap Tracks.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hustlenomics_.282007.E2.80.932009.29"><i>Hustlenomics</i> (2007–2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hustlenomics (2007–2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Joc's next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hustlenomics" title="Hustlenomics">Hustlenomics</a></i>, was released in 2007, with the singles "Coffee Shop" and "Bottle Poppin'". <i>Hustlenomics</i> sold 69,000 copies in its first week of release, debuting at number three on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sales-3">[3]</a></sup> As of April 2009, it has sold approximately 197,000 copies according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nielsen_Soundscan" title="Nielsen Soundscan">Nielsen Soundscan</a>. In 2007 Yung Joc was featured on his new artist <a href="/wiki/Hotstylz" title="Hotstylz">Hotstylz</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Lookin_Boy" title="Lookin Boy">Lookin Boy</a>". During 2008 Yung Joc was featured in the hit singles on "<i><a href="/wiki/Get_Like_Me_(David_Banner_song)" title="Get Like Me (David Banner song)">Get Like Me</a></i>, "<i><a href="/wiki/So_Fly" title="So Fly">So Fly</a></i>" &amp; "<i><a href="/wiki/Beep_(Bobby_Valentino_song)" title="Beep (Bobby Valentino song)">Beep</a></i>". Then in 2009 he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Day26" title="Day26">Day26</a> single <a href="/wiki/Imma_Put_It_on_Her" title="Imma Put It on Her">Imma Put It on Her</a>. Yung Joc released a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> entitled <i>Grind Flu</i> for free on his label website Swagg Team Entertainment on August 11, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BET_2009_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BET_2009-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hhdxmrn_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hhdxmrn-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mr._Robinson.27s_Neighborhood_.282010-present.29"><i>Mr. Robinson's Neighborhood</i> (2010-present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mr. Robinson's Neighborhood (2010-present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yung Joc recently released his first single from the album <i>Mr. Robinson's Neighborhood</i> entitled "Yeah Boy". They have also recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> shot &amp; released the music video for the single. Yung Joc has also stated in a December 26 interview<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> that he would love to work with New York-based producer <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> for a couple of tracks. The second single has been confirmed as "I Know What She Like" that features <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>.</p>
<p>On October 7, 2012, <a href="/wiki/RCA_Music_Group" title="RCA Music Group">RCA Music Group</a> announced it was disbanding <a href="/wiki/J_Records" title="J Records">J Records</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>. With the shutdown, Joc (and all other artists previously signed to these three labels) were to release his future material (including <i>Mr. Robinson's Neighborhood</i>) on the <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 18, 2014 Joc released his first official single entitled "I Got Bitches" from his upcoming third album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On September 11, 2014 Joc released his second single from his upcoming album entitled "Features" featuring former collaborator &amp; singer <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2010, Joc formed a new record label through <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>, called Swagg Team Entertainment, after a widely publicized lawsuit with <a href="/wiki/Block_Entertainment" title="Block Entertainment">Block Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_South" title="Bad Boy South">Bad Boy South</a>. Joc spoke about the lawsuit and his relationship with <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a> in a recent<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> interview.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Swagg Team Entertainment saw their debut from Chicago rap group <a href="/wiki/Hotstylz" title="Hotstylz">Hotstylz</a>; with their first single "<a href="/wiki/Lookin_Boy" title="Lookin Boy">Lookin Boy</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It also saw the debut from Dallas rap group <a href="/wiki/GS_Boyz" title="GS Boyz">GS Boyz</a>; their first single being "<a href="/wiki/Stanky_Legg" title="Stanky Legg">Stanky Legg</a>."</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reality_Television">Reality Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reality Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2014, Joc joined the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> reality series <a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: Atlanta">Love &amp; Hip Hop: Atlanta</a> as a supporting cast member.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In December 2011, Joc's recording studio for his label Swagg Team was robbed, and the criminals took about $70,000 worth of studio equipment along with a hard drive containing Joc's unreleased music for his third studio album. It later was announced that one of the robbers identity was revealed by Joc. Joc knew one of the robbers named "Honcho" of <a href="/wiki/Thomaston,_Georgia" title="Thomaston, Georgia">Thomaston, Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2015, Joc had eight children by four women: Amoni, Ja’Kori, Amir, Chase, twins Cadence and Camora, and twins Eden and Allon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 18, 2009, it was announced that Joc was suing his starter labels <a href="/wiki/Block_Entertainment" title="Block Entertainment">Block Entertainment</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Entertainment</a> due to unpaid royalties of his music. Eventually, however, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diddy_(entertainer)" title="Diddy (entertainer)">Diddy</a> &amp; Joc settled their differences. Little is known as to whether Joc settled his differences with Block entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 18, 2012, it was announced that Joc along with <a href="/wiki/Block_Entertainment" title="Block Entertainment">Block Entertainment</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Entertainment</a> were being sued by local <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> record label Master Mind Music, due to copyright infringement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gucci_Mane">Gucci Mane</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yung_Joc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Gucci Mane">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>'s song entitled "Fuck The World" there was a controversial line when he rapped "I got all eyes on me like Pac did, but I ain't tryin' to go broke like Joc did." During a radio conversation Joc responded to the song by stating "Gucci is a master at getting his name in other people's mouths, I ain't trippin', I know what my financial situation is".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
