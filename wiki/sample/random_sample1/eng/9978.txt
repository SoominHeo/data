<p><b>Yŏnggwang County</b> is a county in <a href="/wiki/South_Hamgyong_Province" title="South Hamgyong Province">South Hamgyŏng</a> province, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yonggwang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county is primarily mountainous, and is traversed by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pujollyong_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pujollyong Mountains (page does not exist)">Pujŏllyŏng Mountains</a> (부전령산맥). However, there are areas of rolling and level ground, especially along the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Songchon_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Songchon River (page does not exist)">Sŏngch'ŏn River</a> (성천강). Other significant streams include the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hungrim_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hungrim River (page does not exist)">Hŭngrim River</a> (흑림강), Chadongch'ŏn (자동천), Ch'ŏnbulsanch'ŏn (천불산천), and Kigokch'ŏn (기곡천). Roughly 80% of the county is occupied by forestland. The highest point is <a href="/wiki/Mataesan" title="Mataesan">Mataesan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_Divisions">Administrative Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yonggwang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yonggwang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was originally part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamju" title="Hamju">Hamju</a> County, from which it was separated in a general reorganization of local government in 1952. Formerly known as <b>Oro</b>, it was named in 1981 after Yŏnggwang, <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/glory" title="wiktionary:glory">glory</a>. It is the site of one of the steles erected by <a href="/wiki/Jinheung_of_Silla" title="Jinheung of Silla">Chinhŭng of Silla</a> in the 6th century.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yonggwang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite the rugged terrain, there is agriculture in Yŏnggwang. Local crops include maize, rice, soybeans, wheat, and barley. Small-scale orchards and livestock operations also exist. There are deposits of coal and gold. In addition, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power" title="Hydroelectric power">hydroelectric power</a> is produced in the region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yonggwang_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sinhung_Line" title="Sinhung Line">Sinhŭng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Changjin_Line" title="Changjin Line">Changjin</a> lines of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_State_Railway" title="Korean State Railway">Korean State Railway</a> pass through the county, which is also served by roads.</p>
