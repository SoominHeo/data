<p>The <b>Communist Party of Britain</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Marxism%E2%80%93Leninism" title="Marxism–Leninism">Marxist-Leninist</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of political parties in the United Kingdom">political party</a> organised in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and since 2012 has been the sole British representative at the <a href="/wiki/International_Meeting_of_Communist_and_Workers%27_Parties" title="International Meeting of Communist and Workers' Parties">International Meeting of Communist and Workers' Parties</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16_IMCWP_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-16_IMCWP-12">[12]</a></sup> The party emerged from a dispute between <a href="/wiki/Eurocommunism" title="Eurocommunism">Eurocommunists</a> and Marxist-Leninists in the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Great_Britain" title="Communist Party of Great Britain">Communist Party of Great Britain</a> in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emergence_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-emergence-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Former members of the party include <a href="/wiki/Bob_Crow" title="Bob Crow">Bob Crow</a> (former general secretary of the <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Rail,_Maritime_and_Transport_Workers" title="National Union of Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers">RMT</a> union), <a href="/wiki/Ken_Gill" title="Ken Gill">Ken Gill</a> (former general secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing,_Science_and_Finance" title="Manufacturing, Science and Finance">MSF</a> union, and member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TUC_General_Council" title="TUC General Council">TUC General Council</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Hudson_(activist)" title="Kate Hudson (activist)">Kate Hudson</a> (general secretary of <a href="/wiki/Campaign_for_Nuclear_Disarmament" title="Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament">CND</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Communist Party was re-established in April 1988<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emergence_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-emergence-13">[13]</a></sup> by a disaffected section of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Great_Britain" title="Communist Party of Great Britain">Communist Party of Great Britain</a> (CPGB), which had largely embraced <a href="/wiki/Eurocommunism" title="Eurocommunism">Eurocommunism</a>. This section included the editorship of the <i><a href="/wiki/Morning_Star_(British_newspaper)" title="Morning Star (British newspaper)">Morning Star</a></i> newspaper; largely supporters of the Communist Campaign Group, formed to oppose the party's new direction. The founders of the new party attacked the leadership of the CPGB for allegedly abandoning 'class politics' and the leading role of the working class in the revolutionary process in Britain. The youth wing of the CPGB, the <a href="/wiki/Young_Communist_League_(Great_Britain)" title="Young Communist League (Great Britain)">Young Communist League</a>, had collapsed, and the <i>Morning Star</i> was losing circulation.</p>
<p>The following year, the leaders of CPGB formally declared that they had abandoned the party's programme, <i>The British Road to Socialism</i>. Members of the party perceived this as the party turning its back on <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a>. The CPGB dissolved itself in 1991 and reformed as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Left_(United_Kingdom)" title="Democratic Left (United Kingdom)">Democratic Left</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Many members of the <a href="/wiki/Straight_Left" title="Straight Left">Straight Left</a> faction who had stayed in the CPGB formed a group called "Communist Liaison" which later opted to join the new Communist Party. Others remained in the Democratic Left or joined the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a>.</p>
<p>The party still has members who were active in the CPGB (which dissolved itself in 1991), some of whom were active in the <a href="/wiki/Anti-Apartheid_Movement" title="Anti-Apartheid Movement">Anti-Apartheid Movement</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and trade union disputes such as the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Clyde_Shipbuilders#Union_strategy" title="Upper Clyde Shipbuilders">Upper Clyde Work-in</a> or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_miners%27_strike_(1984%E2%80%931985)" title="UK miners' strike (1984–1985)">Miners' Strike of 1984–1985</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The new Communist Party was largely the creation of the "Communist Campaign Group" and one of its prominent leaders, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hicks_(trade_unionist)" title="Mike Hicks (trade unionist)">Mike Hicks</a>, was elected to the post of general secretary when the party was founded in 1988. In January 1998 Hicks was ousted as general secretary in a 17 - 13 vote moved by <a href="/wiki/John_Haylett" title="John Haylett">John Haylett</a> (who was also editor of the <i>Morning Star</i>) at a meeting of the party's Executive Committee. Hicks' supporters on the Management Committee of the <i>Morning Star</i> responded by suspending and then sacking Haylett, which led to a prolonged strike at the <i>Morning Star</i>, ending in victory for Haylett and his reinstatement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Some of Hicks' supporters were expelled and others resigned in protest. They formed a discussion group called <a href="/wiki/Marxist_Forum" title="Marxist Forum">Marxist Forum</a>.</p>
<p>The party traces its roots back to 1920 and claims figures such as <a href="/wiki/Willie_Gallacher_(politician)" title="Willie Gallacher (politician)">Willie Gallacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Pollitt" title="Harry Pollitt">Harry Pollitt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Piratin" title="Phil Piratin">Phil Piratin</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Gollan" title="John Gollan">John Gollan</a> as part of its legacy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party is part of the <a href="/wiki/Stop_the_War_Coalition" title="Stop the War Coalition">Stop the War Coalition</a>; the movement's chair, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Murray_(campaigner_and_journalist)" title="Andrew Murray (campaigner and journalist)">Andrew Murray</a> was a Communist Party member until late 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Prior to the formation of the <a href="/wiki/Respect_Party" title="Respect Party">Respect - The Unity Coalition</a>, with the support of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Workers_Party_(UK)" title="Socialist Workers Party (UK)">Socialist Workers Party</a>, the party engaged in a debate about whether to join an electoral alliance with Respect and <a href="/wiki/George_Galloway" title="George Galloway">George Galloway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Those in favour, including general secretary <a href="/wiki/Robert_Griffiths_(politician)" title="Robert Griffiths (politician)">Rob Griffiths</a>, Andrew Murray and <i>Morning Star</i> editor John Haylett, were however defeated at a Special Congress in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009 the party was one of the founder organisations of the <a href="/wiki/No2EU" title="No2EU">No2EU</a> electoral alliance alongside the <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Rail,_Maritime_and_Transport_Workers" title="National Union of Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers">RMT</a> and a number of other left parties. The aim of the alliance is to stand in European Parliament elections on a platform of opposition to the European Union which it considers undemocratic and neo-liberal.</p>
<p>In 2013 the party was a founder of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Assembly_Against_Austerity" title="People's Assembly Against Austerity">People's Assembly Against Austerity</a> along with a number of other political and campaign groups to create a broad organisation in opposition to Austerity policies of the major political parties of Britain and of the European Union. The <a href="/wiki/The_People%27s_Charter_(21st_century)" title="The People's Charter (21st century)">People's Charter</a>, that the Communist Party had helped create several years earlier, subsequently was voted to be incorporated into the People's Assembly.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology_and_summary_of_main_policies">Ideology and summary of main policies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ideology and summary of main policies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>“The aim of the Communist Party is to achieve a socialist Britain in which the means of production, distribution and exchange will be socially owned and utilised in a planned way for the benefit of all. This necessitates a revolutionary transformation of society, ending the existing capitalist system of exploitation and replacing it with a socialist society in which each will contribute according to ability and receive according to work done. Socialist society creates the conditions for advance to a fully communist form of society in which each will receive according to need.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-communist-party.org.uk_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-communist-party.org.uk-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party's main policies are set out in the <i>Alternative Economic and Political Strategy</i>, contained within this is the <i>Left Wing Programme</i> which comprises the following fourteen key policies:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Redistribution_of_income_and_wealth" title="Redistribution of income and wealth">Redistribute wealth</a> from the rich and big business to working people and their families with a <a href="/wiki/Wealth_tax" title="Wealth tax">wealth tax</a>, higher <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporation_tax" title="Corporation tax">corporation tax</a>, cuts in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Value_Added_Tax_(United_Kingdom)" title="Value Added Tax (United Kingdom)">VAT</a> and higher <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pensions" title="Pensions">pensions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welfare" title="Welfare">social benefits</a>.</li>
<li>Set the <a href="/wiki/National_Minimum_Wage_Act_1998" title="National Minimum Wage Act 1998">national minimum wage</a> at half <a href="/wiki/Median" title="Median">median</a> male earnings rising to two-thirds, with no exemptions, and enforce equal pay through compulsory pay audits.</li>
<li>Immediately restore the link between the state retirement pension and earnings at its original value and introduce a second state pension which includes contributions from employers and the state.</li>
<li>Halt all forms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privatisation" title="Privatisation">privatisation</a> and invest in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_services" title="Public services">public services</a> and their staff, with a massive programme in particular to build more <a href="/wiki/Council_house" title="Council house">council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheltered_housing" title="Sheltered housing">sheltered housing</a>.</li>
<li>Integrate all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faith_schools" title="Faith schools">religious</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Private_school_(UK)" title="Private school (UK)">private</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_and_Inspections_Act_2006" title="Education and Inspections Act 2006">trust</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academy_(English_school)" title="Academy (English school)">city academy</a> schools into a unified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secular" title="Secular">secular</a> education system under <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_Education_Authority" title="Local Education Authority">local control</a>.</li>
<li>Protect and develop <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing</a> industry through public investment, measures against the export of jobs and <a href="/wiki/Capital_(economics)" title="Capital (economics)">capital</a>, and expanding <a href="/wiki/Fair_trade" title="Fair trade">fair trade</a> with People's <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_countries" title="Developing countries">developing countries</a>.</li>
<li>Restore <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_ownership" title="Public ownership">public ownership</a> in the energy and transport sectors – including the <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gas" title="Gas">gas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_industry" title="Water industry">water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">railway industries</a> – so that we can plan to meet future needs by developing <a href="/wiki/Clean_coal" title="Clean coal">clean coal</a> technology and <a href="/wiki/Tidal_power" title="Tidal power">tidal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_power" title="Offshore wind power">offshore wind power</a>.</li>
<li>Match public <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subsidies" title="Subsidies">subsidies</a> to failing private companies with a public shareholding and take <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banking" title="Banking">banking</a> and key industries such as <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_industry" title="Pharmaceutical industry">pharmaceuticals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arms_industry" title="Arms industry">armaments</a> into democratic public ownership.</li>
<li>Cut military spending to average European levels, switch military <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26D" title="R&amp;D">R&amp;D</a> and production to meeting social needs and scrap plans to replace <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Trident_programme" title="UK Trident programme">Trident</a> with a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a> system.</li>
<li>Oppose all steps towards a military, monopoly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capitalist" title="Capitalist">capitalist</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Europe" title="United States of Europe">United States of Europe</a> including an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EU" title="EU">EU</a> constitution, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Directive_on_services_in_the_internal_market" title="Directive on services in the internal market">EU services directive</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_labour_law" title="European labour law">EU labour law</a> reform.</li>
<li>Repeal all <a href="/wiki/Trade_Union_and_Labour_Relations_(Consolidation)_Act_1992" title="Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992">anti-trade union</a>, anti-democratic and supposedly racist <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_immigration_law" title="United Kingdom immigration law">immigration laws</a> with full employment rights and <a href="/wiki/Trade_union" title="Trade union">trade union</a> participation for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Migrant_workers" title="Migrant workers">migrant workers</a>.</li>
<li>Strengthen the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">Scottish Parliament</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Welsh_Assembly" title="Welsh Assembly">Welsh Assembly</a> and restore powers and resources to <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a>.</li>
<li>Abolish the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a>, break up the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">media</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monopolies" title="Monopolies">monopolies</a>, scrap plans for <a href="/wiki/Identity_Cards_Act_2006" title="Identity Cards Act 2006">ID cards</a> and bring the <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Security_Service_(United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Northern_Ireland)" title="Security Service (United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland)">intelligence services</a> under democratic control.</li>
<li>Cancel all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_World_debt" title="Third World debt">Third World debt</a>, promote genuine trade-and-<a href="/wiki/Aid" title="Aid">aid</a> policies and oppose the <a href="/wiki/General_Agreement_on_Trade_in_Services" title="General Agreement on Trade in Services">GATS</a> plan to privatise public services across the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-communist-party.org.uk_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-communist-party.org.uk-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stance_on_socialist_countries">Stance on socialist countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stance on socialist countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party supports what it regards as existing socialist states and has fraternal relationships with the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Cuba" title="Communist Party of Cuba">Cuban</a>, <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lao_People%27s_Revolutionary_Party" title="Lao People's Revolutionary Party">Lao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Vietnam" title="Communist Party of Vietnam">Vietnamese</a> Communist Parties, as well as with <a href="/wiki/Communist_state#Communist_parties_as_part_of_a_ruling_coalition" title="Communist state">other ruling Communist Parties</a> around the world. It is affiliated nationally to the <a href="/wiki/Cuba_Solidarity_Campaign" title="Cuba Solidarity Campaign">Cuba Solidarity Campaign</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_Solidarity_Campaign" title="Venezuela Solidarity Campaign">Venezuela Solidarity Campaign</a>.</p>

<p>The party's stance on the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> is summed up in <i>Britain's Road to Socialism</i>;</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Russia and the other countries of the Soviet Union were transformed from semi-feudal, semi-capitalist monarchist dictatorships into modern societies with near-full employment, universally free education and healthcare, affordable housing for all, extensive and cheap public transport, impressive scientific and cultural facilities, rights for women and degrees of self-government for formerly oppressed nationalities. This was achieved through a world historic break with capitalist ownership and social relations, on the basis of social ownership of industry and centralised economic planning.</p>
<p>But the struggle to survive and to build socialism in the face of powerful external as well as internal enemies also led to distortions in society that might otherwise have been avoided. In particular, a bureaucratic-command system of economic and political rule became entrenched. The Communist Party of the Soviet Union and the trade unions became integrated into the apparatus of the state, eroding working class and popular democracy. Marxism-Leninism was used dogmatically to justify the status quo rather than make objective assessments of it.</p>
At times, and in the late 1930s in particular, severe violations of socialist democracy and law occurred. Large numbers of people innocent of subversion or sabotage were persecuted, imprisoned and executed. This aided the world-wide campaign of lies and distortions aimed at the Soviet Union, the international communist movement and the concept of socialism.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accusations_of_Revisionism">Accusations of Revisionism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accusations of Revisionism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Within the Marxist-Leninist movement in Britain, rival organisation such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Great_Britain_(Marxist-Leninist)" title="Communist Party of Great Britain (Marxist-Leninist)">Communist Party of Great Britain (Marxist-Leninist)</a> and groups aligned with it such as the <a href="/wiki/Stalin_Society" title="Stalin Society">Stalin Society</a>, have accused the Communist Party of Britain under Griffiths of being <a href="/wiki/Revisionism_(Marxism)" title="Revisionism (Marxism)">revisionists</a>. For instance in 2016, <i><a href="/wiki/Lalkar" title="Lalkar">Lalkar</a></i>, the political journal edited by <a href="/wiki/Harpal_Brar" title="Harpal Brar">Harpal Brar</a>, chairman of the CPGB-ML criticised the organisation and it's aligned newspaper the <i><a href="/wiki/Morning_Star_(British_newspaper)" title="Morning Star (British newspaper)">Morning Star</a></i> as being "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khrushchev" title="Khrushchev">Khrushchevite</a> revisionist" for distancing itself from the legacy of <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In the same year, Red Youth, the youth wing of the CPGB-ML, criticised Griffiths and his party in an article entitled <i>"Britain's Road to Revisionism"</i> as "rank opportunist" and "arch revisionist" for publicly distancing itself from Marxist-Leninism in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> and for supporting the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> in any form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbology">Symbology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Symbology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Registration_of_Political_Parties_Act_1998" title="Registration of Political Parties Act 1998">Registration of Political Parties Act 1998</a>, which regulated the use of symbols on ballot slips and electoral material, the Communist Party is the only British political party entitled to use a stand-alone <a href="/wiki/Hammer_and_sickle" title="Hammer and sickle">hammer and sickle</a> in such cases. The party tends to use the <a href="/wiki/Hammer_and_sickle#Variations" title="Hammer and sickle">hammer and dove</a> (adopted when the party was re-established in 1988) in conjunction with the hammer and sickle in publications and on other material, with the hammer and dove normally taking primacy. The party's official flag consists of a golden-outlined, five-pointed red star above and slightly to the left of a hammer and sickle design in red with a golden outline in the flag's canton. The words "Communist Party" appear in gold along the bottom of the flag.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dd><i>See also the <a href="/wiki/Young_Communist_League_(Great_Britain)" title="Young Communist League (Great Britain)">Young Communist League</a></i></dd>

<p>The YCL is the autonomous youth group of the Communist Party, with its own internal organisation. It is a growing organisation, having reorganised several districts with growing branches and has also increased its autonomous activity and events, as well as playing more of an active role in the Communist Party and political campaigns such as the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Assembly_Against_Austerity" title="People's Assembly Against Austerity">People's Assembly Against Austerity</a>.</p>

<p>The Communist Party describes itself as a "disciplined and democratic organisation" and operates on a model of <a href="/wiki/Democratic_centralism" title="Democratic centralism">democratic centralism</a>.</p>
<p>The basic party body is the branch. These are normally localities (towns or counties, for example), although workplace branches also exist. In England, branches are grouped into coherent geographical areas and send delegates to a biennial District Congress which elects a District Committee for its area. Similarly, the Welsh and Scottish branches send delegates to their own national congresses where each elects an Executive Committee. These congresses also decide the broad perspectives for party activity within their districts and nations.</p>
<p>The all-Britain national congress is also held biennially. Delegates from districts, nations and branches themselves decide the party's policy as a whole and elect an Executive Committee (EC) that carries out a <a href="/wiki/Presidium" title="Presidium">presidium</a>-like function, including decision-making and policy-formation whilst congress is not in session.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The EC also elects a Political Committee (PC) to provide leadership when the EC is not meeting. Advisory Committees also exist to provide in-depth information on an array of subjects, including committees dedicated to women, industrial workers, pensions, public services, education workers, economics, housing, rails, science technology and the environment, transport, Marxist-Leninist education, LGBT rights, anti-racism, anti-fascism, civil service and international affairs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Size_and_electoral_information">Size and electoral information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Size and electoral information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_election_results">Other election results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other election results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party runs candidates in elections on the local, national and European level.</p>
<p>In the 2010 Mayoral Election in the London Borough of Hackney, Communist candidate Monty Goldman secured 2,033 votes (2.17%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/London_Assembly" title="London Assembly">London Assembly</a>, the party ran on its own<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> and won 536 votes in <a href="/wiki/London_Assembly_election,_2000" title="London Assembly election, 2000">2000</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GLA_results_2002-2004_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-GLA_results_2002-2004-68">[68]</a></sup> and 1,378 votes in <a href="/wiki/London_Assembly_election,_2004" title="London Assembly election, 2004">2004</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GLA_results_2002-2004_68-1"><a href="#cite_note-GLA_results_2002-2004-68">[68]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/London_Assembly_election,_2008" title="London Assembly election, 2008">2008</a> the party supported the Unity for Peace and Socialism alliance, which won 6,394 votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> In local elections in 2008 the party gained one councillor, Clive Griffiths, a former Labour councillor who joined the party and was re-elected unopposed to <a href="/wiki/Hirwaun" title="Hirwaun">Hirwaun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penderyn,_Rhondda_Cynon_Taf" title="Penderyn, Rhondda Cynon Taf">Penderyn</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Community_Council" title="Community Council">Community Council</a> as a communist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2009_(United_Kingdom)" title="European Parliament election, 2009 (United Kingdom)">2009</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2014_(United_Kingdom)" title="European Parliament election, 2014 (United Kingdom)">2014</a> European Parliament elections the party supported the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No_to_EU_%E2%80%93_Yes_to_Democracy" title="No to EU – Yes to Democracy">NO2EU alliance</a> led by the <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Rail,_Maritime_and_Transport_Workers" title="National Union of Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers">RMT union</a>. The party also ran in the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_for_Wales" title="National Assembly for Wales">Welsh Assembly</a> elections in 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> and 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament_general_election,_2011" title="Scottish Parliament general election, 2011">2011 Scottish Parliament election</a> the party stood Marc Livingstone as a candidate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party publishes a wide variety of literature and material.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communist_Review"><i>Communist Review</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Communist Review">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>This is a theoretical and discussion journal published on a quarterly basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> It takes its name from the old journal published by the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Great_Britain" title="Communist Party of Great Britain">CPGB</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> and current issues cost <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>2.5. The content of the journal covers book reviews, feature articles, letters and sometimes poetry. The editor is Martin Levy.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Challenge"><i>Challenge</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Challenge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Challenge_(Communist_journal)" title="Challenge (Communist journal)">Challenge (Communist journal)</a></div>
<p>This is a magazine published by the Young Communist League. It mainly covers news, feature articles and political reports. It runs a Back 2 Basics series which explains the basic foundations of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marxism-Leninism" title="Marxism-Leninism">Marxism-Leninism</a> in an accessible way. Occasionally it publishes music, film or video game reviews alongside other light content such as comic strips. It's aimed at young people and tends to be less academic than <i>Communist Review</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communist_News_.26_Views"><i>Communist News &amp; Views</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Communist News &amp; Views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is an email bulletin which summarises the party's recent statements, resolutions, reports and policies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> It also brings attention to campaigns and events being promoted by the party. It is open to the public and can be subscribed to on the party website, if someone makes an enquiry to join the party they can choose to be subscribed to the email list.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Country_Standard"><i>Country Standard</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Country Standard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A journal for rural communities, produced since 1935.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> It is produced annually and is run by an editorial collective of Communist and Labour members, environmentalists and trade unionists. The paper supports the Countryside Charter with the following aims:</p>
<p>• Restore the Agricultural Wages Board • Restore the Commission for Rural Development • Unite to save local schools, post and health services - build housing • Break with EU Common Agricultural Policy • Extend the Gangmaster’s Act • Tax super profits of the giant food retailers • The land to those who work it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manifesto_Press">Manifesto Press</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Manifesto Press">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party publishes books under the Manifesto Press imprint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> It has a total catalogue of 8 titles and also sells 2 titles which are published separately by Hetherington Press. The books cover historical, political and social topics and are edited by Nick Wright.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unity.21_and_Solidarity"><i>Unity!</i> and <i>Solidarity</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Unity! and Solidarity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Unity!</i> is a short booklet focused around labour issues and often distributed for free at trade union events. <i>Solidarity</i> is a bulletin published by the international department of the party, it covers the party's foreign policy and the activities of the <a href="/wiki/Co-ordinating_Committee_of_Communist_Parties_in_Britain" title="Co-ordinating Committee of Communist Parties in Britain">Co-ordinating Committee of Communist Parties in Britain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> The editor is <a href="/wiki/Anita_Halpin" title="Anita Halpin">Anita Halpin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morningstaronline1_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-morningstaronline1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to this the party publishes many miscellaneous pamphlets under its own name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> The <i>Classics of Communism</i> series are reprints of classic works such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Communist_Manifesto" title="The Communist Manifesto">The Communist Manifesto</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/%22Left-Wing%22_Communism:_An_Infantile_Disorder" title='"Left-Wing" Communism: An Infantile Disorder'>"Left-Wing" Communism: An Infantile Disorder</a></i>. The <i>Our History</i> series aims to re-tell 'history from below' and covers historical events from a working class perspective. The party also publishes congress reports, the party programme, briefing notes and other documents.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Headquarters">Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the beginning of November 2004, the party and its youth organisation, the <a href="/wiki/Young_Communist_League_(Great_Britain)" title="Young Communist League (Great Britain)">YCL</a>, moved out of its temporary headquarters in <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Camden" title="London Borough of Camden">Camden</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_London" title="North London">North London</a> after receiving notice to quit because of redevelopment. The building was owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AKEL" title="AKEL">AKEL</a>, the Cypriot communist party. <a href="/wiki/Ruskin_House" title="Ruskin House">Ruskin House</a> in Croydon was chosen as the new party headquarters, with its long history in the progressive movement as centre of the Anti-Apartheid Movement and also local Labour Party and co-operative groups. The party rents the top floor of four offices at Ruskin House which also allows it plenty of room to hold its congresses and other important meetings, including an annual industrial cadre school and the Communist University of Britain.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences_and_festivals">Conferences and festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Conferences and festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Congress">Congress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Congress">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party holds a biennial congress with delegates from districts, nations and branches. The last congress as the Communist Party of Great Britain was the 43rd congress and was held in 1991. The 44th congress, as the Communist Party of Britain, was held in 1997. Since 2000 the congress has been held every two years apart from a special congress held in February 2004. The 29 member governing Executive Committee (EC) of the party is elected at congress.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Communist_Party_of_Britain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2004 the party organised Communist University events in Wales and England, these were further developed to form a national three-day event which ran annually from 2005 to 2010. This was accompanied by regional weekend universities in Wales,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> Scotland and the Midlands. Among the speakers at the Communist University at Ruskin House in November 2006 were <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> MP <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_McDonnell_(politician)" title="John McDonnell (politician)">John McDonnell</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Rail,_Maritime_and_Transport_Workers" title="National Union of Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers">RMT</a> general secretary <a href="/wiki/Bob_Crow" title="Bob Crow">Bob Crow</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CND" title="CND">CND</a> chair <a href="/wiki/Kate_Hudson_(activist)" title="Kate Hudson (activist)">Kate Hudson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_USA" title="Communist Party USA">Communist Party USA</a> vice-president <a href="/wiki/Jarvis_Tyner" title="Jarvis Tyner">Jarvis Tyner</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Communist_Party" title="French Communist Party">French Communist Party</a> economist Paul Boccara and <a href="/wiki/Palestine_Liberation_Organization" title="Palestine Liberation Organization">Palestine Liberation Organization</a> ambassador Dr Noha Khalef.</p>
