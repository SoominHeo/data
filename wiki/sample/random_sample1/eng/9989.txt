<p><b>British North America</b> refers to the former territories of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> in mainland <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. The term was first used informally in 1783, but it was uncommon before the <a href="/wiki/Report_on_the_Affairs_of_British_North_America" title="Report on the Affairs of British North America">Report on the Affairs of British North America</a> (1839), called the Durham Report. These territories today form modern-day <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. British colonization of North America (including colonization by both the English and the Scots), began in 1607 in <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown, Virginia</a> and reached its peak when colonies had been established throughout the Americas.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_divisions">Political divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Political divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1775, on the eve of the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>, the British Empire included 20 territories in the Western Hemisphere northeast of <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>. These colonies were:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Floridas" title="The Floridas">the Floridas</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_East_Florida" title="British East Florida">East</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_West_Florida" title="British West Florida">West Florida</a>, administered separately)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_Colony" title="Newfoundland Colony">Newfoundland</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/History_of_Nova_Scotia#Eighteenth_century" title="History of Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Province_of_Quebec_(1763%E2%80%931791)" title="Province of Quebec (1763–1791)">Quebec</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rupert%27s_Land" title="Rupert's Land">Rupert's Land</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/History_of_Prince_Edward_Island#British_Colony" title="History of Prince Edward Island">St. John's Island</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a> (each one administered separately)</li>
</ul>
<p>Britain had acquired Quebec from France and East and West Florida from Spain by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1763)" title="Treaty of Paris (1763)">Treaty of Paris (1763)</a>, which ended the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a>.</p>
<p>Spain gained West Florida and regained East Florida, and the United States of America, upon acknowledgement of their sovereignty, acquired the part of Quebec south of the Great Lakes by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1783)" title="Treaty of Paris (1783)">Treaty of Paris (1783)</a>.</p>
<p>Nova Scotia was split into modern-day <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> in 1784. The part of Quebec retained after 1783 was split into the primarily French-speaking <a href="/wiki/Lower_Canada" title="Lower Canada">Lower Canada</a> and the primarily English-speaking <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a> in 1791.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_1818" title="Treaty of 1818">Treaty of 1818</a> established the <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">49th parallel</a> as the United States–British North America border from Rupert's Land west to the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>. Britain gave up <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Country" title="Oregon Country">Oregon</a> south of the 49th parallel, which was part of the <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Columbia_District" title="Columbia District">Columbia District</a>, under the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Treaty" title="Oregon Treaty">Oregon Treaty</a> of 1846.</p>
<p>The boundary of British North America with <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> was finally determined by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Webster-Ashburton_Treaty" title="Webster-Ashburton Treaty">Webster-Ashburton Treaty</a> of 1842.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Canadas" title="The Canadas">The Canadas</a> were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Upper_and_Lower_Canada" title="Union of Upper and Lower Canada">united</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Canada" title="Province of Canada">Province of Canada</a> in 1841.</p>
<p>On 1 July 1867, an act of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">British Parliament</a> called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_North_America_Act" title="British North America Act">British North America Act</a> formed the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Dominion of Canada</a> from the Province of Canada, New Brunswick, and Nova Scotia. The former Province of Canada was split back into its pre-1841 parts, with Canada East (Lower Canada) renamed <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, and Canada West (Upper Canada) renamed <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. These were the original four provinces of Canada.</p>
<p>In 1870, Rupert's Land was annexed to Canada as the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Territories" title="Northwest Territories">Northwest Territories</a> (NWT), and a part of the NWT, <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, became a province of Canada. The west-coast British colony north of the 49th parallel, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, joined Confederation in 1871, and Prince Edward Island joined in 1873. The boundary of British Columbia with <a href="/wiki/Washington_Territory" title="Washington Territory">Washington Territory</a> was settled by arbitration in 1872, and with <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> by arbitration in 1903. In 1905, large parts of the Northwest Territories became the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>.</p>
<p>In 1907 the sole remaining British North American colony, <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Newfoundland" title="Dominion of Newfoundland">Newfoundland</a>, was granted <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">dominion</a> status, although starting in 1934 it was administered by the non-representational <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commission_of_Government" title="Commission of Government">Commission of Government</a>, a body chaired by British officials. In 1949 the island of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, and its associated mainland territory of <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>, joined Canada as the <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">tenth province</a>.</p>
<p>Although semi-independent from 1867, and fully sovereign on foreign affairs with the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster 1931</a>, the last vestiges of Canada's constitutional dependency upon Britain remained until Canadians agreed on an internal procedure for amending the Canadian Constitution. This agreement was implemented when the British Parliament passed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitution_Act_of_1982" title="Constitution Act of 1982">Constitution Act of 1982</a> at the request of <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Canada" title="Parliament of Canada">Parliament of Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maton98_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maton98-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maton95_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Maton95-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="British_North_America_colonies">British North America colonies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: British North America colonies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><a href="/wiki/Columbia_District" title="Columbia District">Columbia District</a>/<a href="/wiki/Oregon_Country" title="Oregon Country">Oregon Country</a> (shared with the United States)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maton98_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Maton98-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maton95_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Maton95-1">[1]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Besides the local colonial governments in each colony, British North America was administered directly via London.</p>
<p>From 1783 to 1801 it was administered by the Home Office and by the <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a>, then from 1801 to 1854 under the <a href="/wiki/War_Office" title="War Office">War Office</a> and Secretary of State for War and Colonies. When the <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Office" title="Colonial Office">Colonial Office</a> was re-established it was under the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_the_Colonies" title="Secretary of State for the Colonies">Colonial Secretary</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maton98_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Maton98-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maton95_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Maton95-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
