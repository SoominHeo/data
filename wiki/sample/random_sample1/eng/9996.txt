<p><b>Yeungnam University</b> is a private research university, located in <a href="/wiki/Gyeongsan" title="Gyeongsan">Gyeongsan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Gyeongsang" title="North Gyeongsang">North Gyeongsang</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. The university's predecessors, Taegu College and Chunggu College, were founded in <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a> in 1947 and 1950 respectively. In 1967, the two colleges merged by President <a href="/wiki/Park_Chung-hee" title="Park Chung-hee">Park Chung-hee</a> to form the degree-granting Yeungnam University. In 1972, the university's new main campus opened in <a href="/wiki/Gyeongsan" title="Gyeongsan">Gyeongsan</a> east of <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a>. The university includes colleges of Law and Medicine and a teaching hospital.</p>

<p>Yeungnam University was founded in 1967 through the merger of Daegu College and Chunggu College which were founded in 1947 and 1950 respectively. Mr. Lee, Dong Nyoung inaugurated as the first Chairperson of the Board of Trustees. In the same year Yeungnam University approved as starting with 6 Colleges by the Ministry of Education: College of Liberal Arts, College of Engineering, College of Law and Political Science, College of Commerce and Economics, College of Pharmacy, and Evening Programs, with 35 undergraduate departments and 12 graduate departments. In 1968 January 1, Dr. Shin, Ki Suck inaugurated as the first President of the University and in 1969 it has started the construction of the Main Campus in <a href="/wiki/Gyeongsan" title="Gyeongsan">Gyeongsan</a>.</p>
<p>In the early 1970s, the Graduate School of Management and Graduate Departments of Korean Language and Literature, Law, and Economics were established afterward the Main Administration Offices, College of Liberal Arts, College of Law &amp; Political Science and College of Commerce and Economics were relocated from the Daegu campus. During the mid-1970s the 21-story main library-cum-faculty office building and the main administration building also accomplished. During the 1980s, Yeungnam University hospital, Foreign Language Institute and University Museum opened.</p>
<p>In 1996, Yeungnam University is approved as an <i>Excellent University</i> in Undergraduate and Graduate Schools, and also as the "Best University" in the fields of business and International Trade, by the Korean University Education Committee. In 1997, Yeungnam University was chosen as a "Government-Sponsored Program for Science Education" in the area of basic science and laboratory education and selected as the supervising university for the model <i>Technopark Project</i>. Following the year Yeungnam University was chosen as a Supervising University in Machinery field and as an Attending University in Information Technology field for the program of rearing local universities in the <i>Brain Korea Project</i> as well as the university was selected by the Ministry of Science and Technology as Research Information Center for Textiles and Apparel Science.</p>
<p>In the year 2000, Yeungnam University established the graduate department of <a href="/wiki/Architectural_Design" title="Architectural Design">Architectural Design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Multimedia" title="Multimedia">Multimedia</a> Communications, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bio-technology" title="Bio-technology">Bio-technology</a> and graduate departments of Sino-Korean and Textile Engineering in Doctor's degree. In 2001 University changed the School of <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">Commerce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">Economics</a> to the Schools of <a href="/wiki/Finance" title="Finance">Finance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">Business</a> with the School of <a href="/wiki/Finance" title="Finance">Finance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">Economics</a> and the School of International <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">Economics</a> and Business. In 2003 the Graduate School of Clinical <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">Pharmacy</a> approved, starting with the graduate department of Clinical <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">Pharmacy</a>. The establishment of the Graduate School of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sports_Science" title="Sports Science">Sports Science</a> approved, starting with the graduate department of Sports Science.</p>
<p>Each year many International students join at Yeungnam University for their Masters or Ph.D. degree and proceed on their research work. Most of the students are from <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and other different countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yeungnam University has two campuses, Gyeongsan is the main campus and other is <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a> campus. Located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Gyeongsan" title="Gyeongsan">Gyeongsan</a> in the south-eastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a>, the city is adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a> metropolis, the third largest city in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, and it takes one and a half hours from <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, Capital of Korea by train, and one hour from <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, the 2nd largest city in Korea.</p>
<p>Yeungnam University has 22,000 undergraduate students, 3,500 graduate students, and 1,000 faculty members and staff with fine facilities and academic organizations. There are also 11 auxiliary organizations, including the Center for <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">International</a> Programs, the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_language" title="Foreign language">Foreign Language</a> Institute, and 38 academic research institutes on the scenic 900-acre (3.6 km<sup>2</sup>) campus. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_Programs"><i>Research Programs</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Research Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Brain Korea (BK21) Program</b></i></li>
</ul>
<p>The Research Manpower Development Center for Mechanical Part Industry, which was initiated and is supported by the Ministry of Education &amp; Human Resources Development as the second phase of Brain Korea 21(BK21), plays an important role of specializing and enhancing the research capacity of regional graduate schools, thereby tries to develop regional R&amp;D personnel clusters in local industry. The School of Display and Chemical Engineering(DsChE) and The Department of Information and Communication Engineering at Yeungnam University has been selected as one of the second phase BK21 (Brain Korea 21) programs in the Chemical Engineering and Telecommunication areas respectively, which was supposed to educate the graduate students to provide the creative research resources for the regional IT industries. The DsChE is also going to navigate its program intentionally to construct the prototype industry-university cooperation system by benchmarking <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_University" title="Kyushu University">Kyushu University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Due to the selection of the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> program of the BK21 regional program in Daegu/Gyeongbuk area in 2006 the DsChE has become the only <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> department to perform the ABEEK program, NURI program, and the BK21 program in the nation simultaneously, and for this many foreign students from various countries like <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> receive <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a> and join in the <a href="/wiki/Laboratory" title="Laboratory">laboratory</a> like <a href="/wiki/Process_Systems_Design_and_Control_Laboratory" title="Process Systems Design and Control Laboratory">PSDC</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/WINLAB_(Yeungnam_University)" title="WINLAB (Yeungnam University)">WINLAB</a> into the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Information_and_Communication_Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Information and Communication Engineering (page does not exist)">Information and Communication Engineering</a> department to pursue their <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a> and higher study.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>NURI Program</b></i></li>
</ul>
<p>The main purpose of NURI or New University for regional innovation program is to train specialists of embedded technology to lead ubiquitous era and contribute regional economics combining practical ability with international competitiveness. School of Materials Science and Engineering of Yeungnam University receives 1.3 million dollars annually to enhance the quality of teaching and learning environment for undergraduate students to provide top quality materials engineers to regional materials and devices industries under Association of Human Resources Initiative for Advanced Materials and Devices(AHRIAMD) which is supported by the Ministry of Education and Human Resources Development through the New University for Regional Innovation (NURI) program. The School of Display and Chemical Engineering(DsChE) at Yeungnam University was selected as one of the participating institutions of NURI (New University for Regional Innovation) program sponsored strategically by the Korean Ministry of Education and Human Resources. The DsChE aims at educating and cultivating chemical engineers specifically suited for regional display industry. The school's mission shifted from the conventional petrochemical industry oriented education to the new display and IT industry oriented education, to achieve the goals set for the NURI program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Polymer Gel Research Cluster Center</b></i></li>
</ul>
<p>The Polymer Gel Research Cluster Center (PGRC) at Yeungnam University is a project to be executed for nine years for establishing a new material R&amp;D cluster for fusion, type high, tech organic polymer gel in Gyeongsangbuk, do as one of “local R&amp;D cluster projects” with the support of the Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>RIC</b></i></li>
</ul>
<p>The Regional Innovation Center(RIC) for Wi-Media was started the part of Wireless Multi Media System Technology. The RIC set up an industry-University research institute cooperative systems to provide support to technology foundation and technology innovation for IT industry cluster at Daegu-Gyeongbuk. A strong regional base provides opportunities for ideas and products to springboard beyond the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Library"><i>Library</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg/200px-Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg/300px-Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg/400px-Yeungnam_University_Central_Library.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Yeungnam University Central Library</div>

<p>Yeungnam University library was originally established on the Daegu campus in 1967 by merging the two college libraries of Daegu and Chunggu, which were founded in 1947 and 1950, respectively. The present University Library moved to the <a href="/wiki/Gyeongsan" title="Gyeongsan">Gyeongsan</a> main campus in 1974. Now, it comprises a main <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">library</a> and two branches. The university central library has a gigantic and diverse collection of books, journals, international papers and periodicals. YU central library is the tallest building of the campus as a <a href="/wiki/Landmark" title="Landmark">landmark</a> of the university. The 21-story main library has five reading rooms by subjective areas, a <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Multimedia" title="Multimedia">multimedia</a> lab, two general reading rooms, 4 group study rooms, and 24 carrells. The science library branch has a reading room for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pure_science" title="Pure science">pure science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, a periodicals room, a computer and multimedia lab, and four general reading rooms. The number of the seats of the science library is 2,077. The Daegu campus has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical" title="Medical">medical</a> library branch which has 224 seats. As of February 2007, the University Library holds more than 1,478,491 books including bound ones, and carries 2,830 titles of domestic and foreign <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Periodicals" title="Periodicals">periodicals</a> along with 24,048 titles of E-<a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">Journals</a>. It also stores personal collections donated by 28 private collectors. The books are classified according to the 3rd edition of the Korean <a href="/wiki/Decimal" title="Decimal">Decimal</a> Classification system.</p>

<p>Freshmen are admitted to undergraduate programs once a year. The students who have graduated from accredited high schools or who have passed the Ministry of <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">Education</a> administered High School <a href="/wiki/Graduation" title="Graduation">Graduation</a> <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/qualification" title="wikt:qualification">Qualification</a> Examination or the students who have completed a minimum of twelve years of formal education in foreign countries are considered as qualified for those mentioned above under the pertinent education law. <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">International</a> applicants who have completed or will complete a bachelor's degree from regular colleges prior to admission and have a record that indicates good potential for advanced study and <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a> in one of the disciplines at Yeungnam University are encouraged to apply for admission to graduate study.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Programs"><i>International Programs</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/International" title="International">International</a> Programs are open to embrace the interests of foreign scholars who intend to study, to do research, or to teach at Yeungnam University as well as of students and faculty at Yeungnam University who want to experience the educational system outside <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. One of the major goals is to inspire the faculty and the students of Yeungnam University to cultivate global perspectives for their academic and intellectual growth and also is to give the scholars from foreign institutions the opportunities to join the international programs and to benefit from the process of educational reforms of the University. The principal responsibilities of the <i>Center of International Programs</i> are to</p>
<ul>
<li>Develop and manage exchange programs with foreign institutions; student exchange programs, faculty exchange programs.</li>
<li>Arrange joint seminars, international symposiums, special lectures or other international events to promote quality international education.</li>
<li>Provide administrative support for faculty members and students of Yeungnam University and of foreign institutions.</li>
<li>Advise on visas, studying abroad, University Referral Program and publishing English certificates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">Computer</a> Center and <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">Library</a></b></li>
</ul>
<p>Yeungnam University provides facilities for the students. University <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">library</a> is equipped with books, journals, periodicals of both domestic and foreign. The Institute of <a href="/wiki/Information" title="Information">Information</a> and Computing Systems(IICS) is supporting the academic and administrative activities by providing the state-of-art <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> such as efficient group-wares, gigabit <a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">network</a>, high capacity servers, and mobile environment for the wireless voice and data communications. It plays a major role in planning for the advanced networks, telecommunications, and <a href="/wiki/Computing" title="Computing">computing</a> systems to build a futuristic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intelligent" title="Intelligent">intelligent</a> <a href="/wiki/Campus" title="Campus">campus</a> named as <b>i-campus</b>. University also provides high speed internet service and <a href="/wiki/Wi-Fi" title="Wi-Fi">Wi-Fi</a> into the campus area.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Dormitories</b></li>
</ul>
<p>The University opened <a href="/wiki/Dormitory" title="Dormitory">dormitory</a> facilities in May 1973, for those who need on campus housing. These facilities are opened for undergraduates(mainly) and <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_education" title="Postgraduate education">graduate</a> students. At present six residence halls, including one self-boarding apartment type hall, accommodate 1,200 students. A modern style dining hall in which more than 500 people can eat at a time, a video room with a big screen TV and a video player, study rooms, <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> labs, resting rooms, ping-pong tables, <a href="/wiki/Snack" title="Snack">snack</a> stores and exercise rooms are facilities among others provided for <a href="/wiki/Dormitory" title="Dormitory">dormitory</a> residents.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Health Service Center</b></li>
</ul>
<p>The University Health Service Center was established in December 1967, for the purpose of undertaking the health care for students, faculty members, and administration staff. The Center provides annual physical examination for students, <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a> <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> on the campus, and preventive inoculation if required. It also takes charge of health counseling for students and university members.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Center for International Programs</b></li>
</ul>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/Foreign_language" title="Foreign language">Foreign Language</a> Institute and the former Office of <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">International</a> Affairs has been merged since March, 2002. The new merged organization is named as "<i>Center for International Programs</i>", and it has two teams, one is "<i>International Education</i>", and the other is "<i>International Cooperation</i>".</p>
<p>The main functions of the <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">International</a> Education Team are to offer the foreign language classes such as English, Chinese, Japanese, etc., as well as the Korean Language Program.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>University <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">Museum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">Folklore</a></b></li>
</ul>
<p>The University Museum was founded in May 1968 on the Daegu Campus with the collections of the Institute of <a href="/wiki/Silla" title="Silla">Shilla</a>-<a href="/wiki/Gaya_confederacy" title="Gaya confederacy">Gaya</a> culture. The University Museum performs to survey, collect, educate and preserve both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeological" title="Archaeological">archaeological</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropological" title="Anthropological">anthropological</a> fields. The displays arranged within the Museum are in great part the results of excavation and research both Shilla and Gaya Culture. In addition to them, the Museum has made great effort to have important collections including old maps, documents, paintings, and <a href="/wiki/Calligraphy" title="Calligraphy">calligraphy</a>. It stores over 13,000 historically important items. Among them, the collection of old maps is the richest one in Korean museum. The University Museum is not only for students but also open to the public. To maximize its function, the Museum started a public education programs from kids to senior citizens. With its various programs, the Museum become a cultural core of the community.</p>
<p>Also the University <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">Museum</a> has a <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a> park. Folklore Garden which covers area of 70,000㎡ is famous and unique place in Korean universities. Folklore Garden of Yeungnam University consists of 6 traditional houses located by the east to <a href="/wiki/Mirror" title="Mirror">Mirror</a> Lake and near the <a href="/wiki/Cherry_blossom" title="Cherry blossom">cherry blossom</a> promenade (so-called love road) on the hill. The folklore park consists of 7 traditional buildings inside the park as examples of a traditional tile-roof house, a traditional house of the middle-class, a private school of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Josun" title="Josun">Josun</a> period and a pavilion which was being used as a cottage to upper class scholars. These buildings are frequently used as lecture rooms for the university students and kids summer school program running by the museum.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_activities">Student activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Student activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Foundation-Day and CHUNMA Festival</li>
</ul>
<p>The Chunma Festival in memory of Yeungnam University's Foundation Day is held in mid May. For the unity of students, faculty, staff and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alumni" title="Alumni">alumni</a>, this festival holds various university-wide events such as interdepartmental contests, and other activities on a campus filled with the breath of young and lively <i>Yeungnamians</i>.</p>
<ul>
<li>SAE Mini Baja <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KOREA" title="KOREA">KOREA</a> at Yeungnam University</li>
</ul>
<p>SAE Mini Baja KOREA is an event which is aimed at creating an opportunity for college students to apply their classroom <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> knowledge to designing and producing an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">automobile</a>. In 1996, the school of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mechanical_Engineering" title="Mechanical Engineering">Mechanical Engineering</a> started this contest, named "The Nation-Wide Contest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobiles" title="Automobiles">Automobiles</a> made by College Students" which is held annually to inspire college students' desire for creation; to let them produce an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">automobile</a> based on stated regulations; and to have them learn to accept the results after doing their best in competition.</p>
<ul>
<li>Student-Governed Activities</li>
</ul>
<p>Student activities in many fields are supported and promoted in my university. There are many self-governing student organizations such as General Student Council and other student organizations. The General Student Council, which represents all 20,000 students of my university, tries to enhance and support both academic and cultural activities of students to obtain their personal development while they are in college. These activities can help them to prepare contribution to our society in the future.</p>
<ul>
<li>University <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">Newspaper</a></li>
</ul>
<p><i>The Yeungdae Shinmun</i> (campus vernacular newspaper) was first published in January 1968, by merging the Daegu and Chunggu College’s newspapers which were first published in June and December, respectively, in the year of 1954. The campus newspaper is currently published weekly during the academic term, and carries university <a href="/wiki/News" title="News">news</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information" title="Information">information</a> on new development in learning here and abroad, scholarly <a href="/wiki/Article_(publishing)" title="Article (publishing)">articles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Creative_writing" title="Creative writing">creative writings</a> by the faculty and students.</p>
<p><i>The Yeungnam Observer <a class="external autonumber" href="http://observer.yu.ac.kr/" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></i> had published a four-page <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a> <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> monthly since 1969. Then it changed its format from a <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a> to a magazine since 1994. It was originally named The Yeungnam <a href="/wiki/Chronicle" title="Chronicle">Chronicle</a>, however in 1982 the monthly was renamed <i>The Yeungnam Observer</i>. The papers, both in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, are edited by students under the supervision of faculty members.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/University" title="University">University</a> Broadcasting System</li>
</ul>
<p>Like many other large universities, Yeungnam University has its own <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> broadcasting station. University Broadcasting System(UBS) was established in 1965 and has provided music, news and other features on academic affairs, both on and off campus.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alumni_Association">Alumni Association</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yeungnam_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Alumni Association">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Yeungnam University <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alumni_Association" title="Alumni Association">Alumni Association</a> was organized in 1969, February 16. At present there are more than 27 branch offices throughout the country, including <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masan" title="Masan">Masan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Changwon" title="Changwon">Changwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gyeongsan" title="Gyeongsan">Gyeongsan</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju_island" title="Jeju island">Jeju island</a>; and some branches in foreign soil making a list of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> (the USA), <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The YUAA can be catalogued even according to the alumni's colleges, majors, and their work places. The YUAA Office Hall was purchased in 1972 at Daemyung-Dong, <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a>, which was the first among all college alumni associations in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> to be used only for the purpose of alumni services. The YUAA built a New Office Hall on the building site of 462-pyung at Shinchun-Dong, <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a>, in February 1995, of the size of 5-story building including the basement. In September 1996 it became to own an Annex of 4-story building with the basement that made the YUAA Office Hall own the real estate larger than any other alumni association in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> might have.</p>
